## Step 1 — Voice & Tone Check

Read the email against the guidelines above.

- Does the opener sound like Travis or like a generic sales email?
- Is the sentence rhythm consistent with the voice? Flag anything that sounds stiff, over-formal, or like a template left in.
- Are there filler phrases or corporate buzzwords that should be cut?
- Is the CTA appropriately direct without being pushy?
- Does it read at a reasonable pace — nothing that would make someone skim past it?

---

## Step 2 — Audience & ICP Fit

Check who this email is going to and whether the framing matches.

Reference `/shared/icp.md` for buyer context.

- **If Foundations / SMB:** Buyer is typically the person who owns employee comms — could be an office manager, ops lead, HR, or founder. Lead with escaping Outlook/Gmail, ease of getting started, no cost/no contract. Do not pitch Omni AI or enterprise features.
- **If Foundations+ target (501–2,000 employees):** Frame around growing out of volume limits, needing more seats, or adding SSO.
- **If Omni AI / enterprise:** Buyer is usually an Internal Comms Director, SVP, or HR/People leader. IT is often a co-buyer. Lead with complexity, channel chaos, deskless reach, personalization, or analytics maturity. Avoid self-serve framing.

Flag mismatches between audience and message framing.

## Step 3 — Fact Check

Verify all factual claims against `/shared/company-info.md`. Flag anything that is wrong, outdated, or unsupported.

### Pricing & Plan Claims
- Foundations is **free forever** — no credit card, no contract, no trial expiration
- Foundations includes: 5,000 sends/month, 3 communicator seats, 1 instance/domain
- Overages: $0.09/email
- Foundations+ adds: unlimited sends, unlimited seats, calendar invites, API, SSO, 3 instances
- Omni AI adds: AI personalization, SharePoint/Teams/Slack/mobile channels, enterprise implementation, 6 instances
- Paid plans are audience-based and annual — **not** per-seat or per-message

### Product & Feature Claims
- Integrations — only cite systems actually listed: Workday, SAP, PeopleSoft, ADP, Paycor, Active Directory (HRIS); SharePoint, Teams, Slack, Outlook, Gmail, mobile, microsites (channels); Azure AD, Google Workspace, Okta, PingIdentity, OneLogin, Workspace ONE, JumpCloud, Duo, CyberArk (SSO/IdP)
- Do not overstate AI features for Foundations accounts — AI personalization (MyNews/Omni AI) is a paid/Omni AI capability
- Insights/analytics redesign released May 12, 2026 — confirm timing before citing as "new" or "coming soon"

### Positioning Claims
- Core positioning: "delivery and measurement layer across the channels you already use" — not a replacement intranet, not a marketing tool
- Do not position Cerkl as an intranet or digital workplace suite
- "Low IT lift" and "quick rollout" are fair claims
- "Client-driven roadmap" is a fair claim

## Step 4 — Competitive Claims

Reference `/shared/competitors.md` for head-to-head context.

- Do not make claims Cerkl cannot support — avoid superlatives ("the only," "the best") unless they can be defended
- Do not disparage competitors by name in cold outreach — reframe as "vs. patching together Outlook/Gmail" or "vs. a full intranet replacement"
- If the email mentions a specific competitor, verify the positioning matches the head-to-head guidance in `competitors.md`
- Safe competitive framing: self-serve free entry, no procurement required, no channel replacement, faster launch, lower IT lift

**Common mistakes to flag:**
- Claiming Foundations competes with full enterprise suites feature-for-feature (it doesn't; that's Omni AI)
- Saying Cerkl "replaces your intranet" (incorrect positioning — Cerkl sits on top of existing channels)
- Citing ContactMonkey, Workshop, or Staffbase without checking current positioning guidance first

---

## Step 5 — Final Polish

After steps 1–4:

- Is the subject line compelling and specific? Flag vague or generic subjects.
- Is the email an appropriate length for the channel and audience? Cold outreach should be short. Warm nurture can be longer.
- Does the email have one clear ask? Flag if there are multiple competing CTAs.
- Any spelling, grammar, or punctuation issues?

---

## Output Format

When returning review feedback:

1. **Overall assessment** — 1–2 sentences: is this ready, needs minor edits, or needs significant revision?
2. **Flagged issues** — numbered list, each with: what's wrong, where it is, and a suggested fix
3. **Optional:** one revised version of the full email if significant edits were needed and Travis asked for it
