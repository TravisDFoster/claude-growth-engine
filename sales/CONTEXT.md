# Sales Function Context

## Sales motions in play

**Self-serve (Foundations):** Free sign-up, no sales touch needed. Marketing drives this. Product drives conversion to paid.

**Guided/assisted (Foundations+, Omni AI):** Inbound leads from demo requests, trial activity signals, or partner referrals. Sales engages mid-market and enterprise orgs (typically 500+ employees) who are evaluating Broadcast for a more complex comms stack.

## Deal profile (paid pipeline)

- **Champion:** Internal Communications Manager/Director, or HR/People Experience leader
- **Co-buyer:** IT (SSO, data sync, channel rollout)
- **Signer:** Senior Comms/HR leader; executive sponsorship for larger rollouts
- **Company size:** 2,000–12,200+ employees for Omni AI; 501–2,000 for Foundations+
- **Common trigger:** outgrown Outlook/Gmail, fragmented channel stack, new comms hire, compliance/change initiative, deskless workforce comms problem

## Competitive context in deals

Primary competitors encountered in sales cycles: Staffbase, ContactMonkey, Workshop, Simpplr, LumApps.

Cerkl wins on: time-to-value, self-serve free entry, "layer on top of existing channels" story, low IT lift, faster launch.

Cerkl loses when: buyer wants a fuller intranet/home-base platform, not a delivery-and-measurement layer.

## What good looks like
- Outbound that resonates with internal comms and HR leaders at the right company sizes
- Discovery that quickly surfaces whether a prospect fits Foundations+ or Omni AI
- Clear objection handling for common pushback (vs. Staffbase, vs. doing nothing, vs. Outlook/Gmail)
- Enablement that arms the right positioning for each deal context

## What to avoid
- Treating all inbound leads as Omni AI prospects — many will be Foundations+ fits
- Feature-first messaging in any sales motion
- Conflating marketing's subscriber growth goal with sales pipeline goals
