# Objection Handling Playbook

> Living document. Add objections as they surface in the field.

---

## "We already use Outlook/Gmail for internal email — it works fine."

**Acknowledge:** Makes sense — most teams start there.

**Reframe:** The question isn't whether Outlook works, it's whether it gives you what you need to run internal comms well. Can you segment by department, location, or role without manually managing distribution lists? Can you see who's opening and clicking — by employee, not just aggregate? Can you schedule, retarget, or manage sends from a shared communicator workflow? Those are the gaps Foundations closes — for free.

**Proof point:** [St. Elizabeth Healthcare — 54% open rate. Flagger Force — 2,000 employees across 10+ states.]

---

## "We're looking at Staffbase / Simpplr / [other competitor] too."

**Acknowledge:** Those are legitimate options in this space.

**Reframe:** Depends on what you're optimizing for. Staffbase is a fuller intranet platform — if your goal is to replace your digital workplace from scratch, they compete there. Cerkl is different: we sit on top of the channels you already have (email, Teams, Slack, SharePoint) rather than replacing them. That means faster to launch, less IT lift, and no forcing employees to go somewhere new. If you're evaluating us side by side, the question worth asking Staffbase is: how long until you're live and sending? For Cerkl, most teams are sending their first blast within days.

**Differentiator:** Self-serve, free entry point vs. sales-first competitors. Faster time-to-value.

---

## "Our IT team will push back."

**Acknowledge:** IT involvement is real — especially for SSO and data sync.

**Reframe:** The Foundations plan is designed for exactly this. You can start with email without touching IT — no SSO required, no HRIS sync needed to get going. When you're ready to connect Workday, ADP, or Active Directory, or add SSO, that's a paid upgrade you pursue when the value is already proven. You don't need IT buy-in to start.

**If they're on paid already:** Walk through the integration options. WorkOS-backed SSO covers Azure AD, Okta, Google Workspace, and more. HRIS sync is REST API, SFTP, or native connector — IT scope is usually smaller than they expect.

---

## "We don't have budget for another tool."

**Reframe:** Foundations is free forever. No credit card, no contract. You don't need budget to start — and if the team stays on Foundations, you never will. Paid plans (Foundations+, Omni AI) exist for when complexity actually shows up: more scale, more seats, more channels. You make that call after value is proven, not before.

---

## "We're too small for this."

**Acknowledge:** Plenty of tools in this space are built for enterprise — that's a fair concern.

**Reframe:** Foundations is specifically built for teams that are too small for enterprise tools but too sophisticated to keep using Outlook. If you're sending internal emails to more than 20 people and want audience segmentation, analytics, and a communicator workflow, Foundations is the right fit. And it's free — you're not taking a bet on software, you're just getting a better workflow.

---

## "We just went through a big platform change — our team has change fatigue."

**Acknowledge:** Totally valid — change fatigue is real, especially post-implementation.

**Reframe:** The reason Cerkl rolls out faster than most tools is that it doesn't replace existing channels — it layers on top of them. Your team keeps using Outlook or Gmail. IT doesn't have to migrate anything. Employees don't learn a new system. For the communicators, it's a better sending experience. That's a much lighter lift than another platform replacement.

---

## "We can build this ourselves / we have someone who can do this."

**Acknowledge:** If you have someone with the time and the skills, that's a real option.

**Reframe:** The thing to weigh is what "building it yourself" actually means in practice — managing distribution lists manually, patching together Outlook and a spreadsheet, losing visibility into who's reading what. Cerkl Foundations replaces that with a purpose-built system. Free forever, no contract. The question isn't whether you *could* build it — it's whether that's the best use of your team's time.
