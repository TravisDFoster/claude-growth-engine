# Pressure Prospecting — Phase 1 Methodology

> Outbound motion targeting Tech/SaaS + Healthcare companies (enterprise/midmarket) where organizational pressure has the internal comms function under stress. Output of the motion: a handoff list of named accounts + suggested angles for Marc Fregoe and Josh Mandelman. Complementary to [Competitor Dissatisfaction Mining](../../personal-assistant/projects/competitor-dissatisfaction-mining.md) — different signal, different opener. Phase 1 = methodology only. Pilot (20–40 companies) runs in Phase 3.

---

## How this works

1. **Detect** a pressure signal at a target-fit company (right vertical, right size).
2. **Confirm** the signal — cross-check against an authoritative source and assign a confidence level. Reject Low-confidence Tier-S signals until corroborated.
3. **Tier** the signal (S/A/B) based on recency, severity, public visibility, and how directly it activates the comms function.
4. **Bucket** the account (Acute Crisis / Aftermath / Transition Shock / Sustained Strain) — the bucket determines outbound *posture*, not the tier.
5. **Pick an angle** from the bucket's playbook.
6. **Fill a handoff row** with the schema below and hand to Marc/Josh.

**Tier ≠ bucket.** Tier is how hot the *signal* is. Bucket is how to *talk to* the company. A Tier-S layoff puts an account in Acute Crisis (don't pitch yet). A Tier-S CHRO exit puts the same account in Transition Shock (pitch now, lead with the audit angle). Get this distinction right before assigning.

## 1. Signal Taxonomy

Each signal has a default tier (S/A/B). Recency, severity, visibility, and comms-relevance modify it.

| Signal | Default tier | Why it's a *comms* signal |
|---|---|---|
| **Layoffs / RIF** (workforce reduction, WARN filing) | **S** | Comms drafts the announcement, manages internal narrative, fields manager questions, runs retention messaging in the aftermath. Most direct trigger we have. |
| **Stock drop** (>15% single-day or >25% in 30 days, public co.) | **S** | Triggers all-hands explanations, exec memos, "stay the course" cascades. Often precedes layoffs. |
| **CHRO / CCO / Chief Comms exit** | **S** | Direct decision-maker for our category. Successor will audit the comms stack. |
| **Regulatory enforcement** (FTC action, SEC enforcement, DOJ inquiry, FDA warning letter, state AG action) | **S** | Reputational crisis mode. Comms is in firefight with Legal. Healthcare especially: FDA, HHS, OIG, state DOH. |
| **M&A announced** (deal closed or signed) | **S** | Comms owns retention messaging, culture integration, brand transition. 30–180-day window post-announcement. |
| **CEO / CFO change** | **A** | New strategy = new internal narrative. Comms stack often gets audited within 6 months. |
| **Missed earnings / guidance cut** (public co.) | **A** | Pattern of pressure. Triggers "what is leadership saying" cascades. Less acute than a stock crash because partially telegraphed. |
| **Restructuring announced** (re-org, division spin-out, headcount realignment) | **A** | Comms owns the explanation to employees. Ambiguous solo; escalates when paired. |
| **RTO mandate** (new or tightened, esp. with backlash coverage) | **A** | Polarizing message comms is being asked to sell. Sustained internal drag. |
| **Union activity** (NLRB filing, organizing campaign, strike, public letter) | **A** | Comms is contested ground. Tier-S in healthcare (nursing unions especially active). |
| **Cultural / employer-brand collapse** (Glassdoor rating drop, viral negative employee post, leaked internal letter) | **B** | Soft signal — symptom of pressure, not source. Escalates to A when paired with another signal. |
| **Exec departures cluster** (3+ VP-or-above exits in 90 days) | **B** | Soft signal of dysfunction. Escalates with other signals. |

### Tier modifiers

A signal's *default* tier shifts based on:

| Modifier | What boosts the tier | What drops it |
|---|---|---|
| **Recency** | ≤30 days = full weight | 30–90 days = hold weight; 90–180 = drop one tier; >180 = drop two tiers or remove |
| **Severity** | Layoff >10% of workforce; stock drop >35%; multiple-state WARN filings; >$50M regulatory penalty | Layoff <3%; one-off filing; symbolic regulatory action |
| **Visibility** | National coverage (WSJ/NYT/CNBC/Reuters), trade press (HRDive, Becker's), front-page Reddit thread | Local press only; obscure SEC filing without coverage |
| **Comms relevance** | Signal directly activates internal narrative work (layoffs, exec change, RTO) | Signal is operational/financial but not comms-load-bearing (e.g., factory closure that doesn't affect office workforce) |

**Working rule:** ≥2 modifiers boosting → tier up. ≥2 modifiers dropping → tier down. Stay honest; don't tier-stuff to pad the list.

### Signal stacking

When 2+ signals hit the same company within 90 days, escalate to the highest tier present and prefer the **most recent** signal as the angle anchor. Note all signals in the 1-line context — they compound the story for the AE.

Example: Company has stock drop 2026-03-10 (S) + layoffs 2026-04-20 (S) + new CHRO 2026-05-02 (S). Anchor on CHRO arrival (most recent + opens the audit window). Bucket: Transition Shock, not Acute Crisis — the new exec is the open door.

### Source confidence & cross-checking

Tier says how hot the signal is. **Confidence says how reliable the source is.** Both go on the handoff row. A Tier-S signal sourced only from a Reddit rumor is not actually Tier-S.

| Confidence | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **High** | Official filing (WARN, SEC 8-K, NLRB filing, court docket, FDA warning letter) **or** confirmed by ≥2 independent reputable outlets (WSJ + Reuters; HRDive + Becker's). |
| **Medium** | Single reputable outlet, trade press, or single official press release. Not yet cross-confirmed but the source is trustworthy. |
| **Low** | Layoffs.fyi without a filing cross-check, single Reddit/LinkedIn post, single Glassdoor entry, leaked memo without corroboration. |

**Cross-check rule:** Before adding a row to the handoff list:

- **Tier-S signals require High confidence.** If only Medium, hold and look for cross-confirmation. If only Low, do not add.
- **Tier-A signals require Medium or High.** Low gets held.
- **Tier-B signals can ship at Low** but the confidence level goes in the row so the AE knows what they're working with.

**Quick cross-check checklist:** If Layoffs.fyi reports a layoff, verify against the state DOL's WARN page. If a press release announces an exec change, verify on LinkedIn or the company's own newsroom. If a regulator action is reported, link to the actual filing on the agency's site. *One unverified secondary source is not a signal — it's a lead to verify.*

## 2. Bucket Model

Four buckets. Each defines the outbound *posture* and *window*.

### Acute Crisis — "In the fire"
- **Signals that trigger it:** Tier-S layoffs ≤30 days; stock crash ≤30 days; regulatory enforcement ≤30 days; M&A announced ≤14 days.
- **Posture:** **Hold. Do not pitch.** Comms lead is in firefight mode and not taking demos.
- **Action:** Bookmark account. Schedule re-evaluation date (+60 days from signal). Optional: one warm, no-ask touch (useful resource, no meeting request).
- **Window:** Re-enter at Aftermath (60–90 days post-signal).

### Aftermath — "What broke, and how do we fix it?"
- **Signals that trigger it:** Tier-S signal 30–120 days old; missed-earnings + restructuring combo at any recency.
- **Posture:** **Prime pursuit window.** Comms lead has bandwidth, leadership saw comms mattered during the crisis, vendor conversations open up.
- **Action:** Pursue now. Lead with measurement, retention, trust-rebuilding.
- **Window:** Hot for 30–60 days, then decays.

### Transition Shock — "New boss, new audit"
- **Signals that trigger it:** New CEO/CFO/CHRO/CCO in last 6 months; M&A 30–180 days post-close; major restructuring 30–120 days old.
- **Posture:** **Pursue with platform/consolidation angle.** New leader is auditing the stack and looking to consolidate.
- **Action:** Position Broadcast as the answer to "why do we have 4 tools doing comms?" Reference peers who consolidated.
- **Window:** 30–180 days after new leader arrives or transition event.

### Sustained Strain — "The slow burn"
- **Signals that trigger it:** Active RTO mandate; union organizing/strike activity; missed earnings + guidance cut without other Tier-S signals; cultural collapse signals stacking.
- **Posture:** **Tactical pursuit, feature-led.** Lead with measurement of unpopular messages, pulse surveys, audience segmentation.
- **Action:** Hook on "are people actually reading this?" — prove or disprove the comms work is landing.
- **Window:** Open-ended while signals persist.

## 3. Emotional Read per Bucket

Drives the outbound copy angle. Read first, then write.

### Acute Crisis

- **What the head of comms is feeling:** Adrenaline, 6 hours of sleep, fear of saying the wrong thing publicly, worried about own job. Likely owns Legal and HR are in their calendar more than their own team.
- **What their boss (CEO/CHRO) is asking for:** "Get me the all-hands draft by 4pm. Have we briefed managers? Are we tracking what employees are saying back? What's hitting Glassdoor?"
- **What their week looks like:** Back-to-back drafting. Crisis comms tag-team with HR + Legal. Working weekends. Inbox is on fire. Not opening vendor emails.
- **Outbound implication:** **No pitch.** Don't ask for a meeting. If you reach out at all, send a useful no-ask resource (e.g., "5 questions every CHRO will ask their comms lead after layoffs — happy to send our notes"). Tag for revisit +60 days.

### Aftermath

- **What the head of comms is feeling:** Exhaustion turning to reflection. Survivor energy. "We got through it. What's broken in how we communicate that I want to fix before the next time?" Newfound budget conversation possible because leadership saw comms matter.
- **What their boss is asking for:** "How do we make sure this never lands this badly again? Can we measure whether our reset message is landing? What's our retention pulse? Are we sure managers are reinforcing the message?"
- **What their week looks like:** Strategic planning meetings, retros on what failed, early vendor evals, drafting next-quarter comms plan. Calendar has space again.
- **Outbound implication:** **Strongest window.** Lead with measurement, retention, and how peer comms leads rebuilt trust post-crisis. Bring an artifact (1-pager, peer mini-case) — don't just ask for time. Opener anchors on the *aftermath*, not the original event ("Three months after [event], what are you tracking to know it's working?").

### Transition Shock

- **What the head of comms is feeling:** Cautious optimism mixed with insecurity ("will my new boss replace me?"). Wants to make a good impression. Open to fresh approaches — old vendors associated with old regime.
- **What their boss (new CHRO or new CEO) is asking for:** "What's our comms stack? Why do we have 4 tools? What's our employee experience metric? Show me the analytics. Where are we overspending?"
- **What their week looks like:** Tool audits, vendor reviews, "how I'd modernize comms" memos for new leadership, briefing decks, lots of intro coffees.
- **Outbound implication:** Lead with **consolidation + platform** story. "If your new CHRO asks for one tool that replaces Outlook + your intranet announcements + the Slack/Teams duplicate-paste — here's the 1-pager." Reference peer companies that consolidated. Be the easy answer to the audit memo.

### Sustained Strain

- **What the head of comms is feeling:** Tired. Cynical. Burned out from defending unpopular positions. "We've been asked to sell another mandate nobody wants." Open rates are flat and they can't tell if the message landed.
- **What their boss is asking for:** "Are people actually reading the RTO updates? Are managers reinforcing this? Why is engagement so flat? Can we prove the comms team is having impact?"
- **What their week looks like:** Drafting yet another version of the same kind of message. Watching open rates flatline. Fielding manager complaints. Trying to defend their own headcount.
- **Outbound implication:** **Tactical, feature-led.** Lead with pulse surveys, read receipts, audience segmentation by location/role, comprehension tracking. Bring a 2-minute loom or a sample dashboard. Hook: tools that *prove* the comms work is having impact (or surface that it isn't, so they can refocus and defend their team).

## 4. Suggested Angles per Bucket

Direction, not final copy. Marc/Josh write the line; this gives them the lane.

### Voice & format rules (apply to every bucket)

These come from how outbound that actually gets replies reads. Same rules apply across all four buckets — bucket changes the *angle*, not the voice.

- **Subject lines: 2–4 words, lowercase, internal-looking.** "comms reset" beats "Following up regarding your recent organizational changes." No emojis, no urgency tricks, no prospect first name in the subject.
- **The signal IS the personalization.** If you could remove the reference to the layoff/exec change/etc. and the email would still make sense, the personalization isn't working. The signal opens the door; the bucket angle is what walks through it.
- **Calibrate by seniority:**
  - **SVP+ / Chief** — ultra-brief, peer-level, understated. 60–80 words max. No feature talk.
  - **Director / Senior Manager** — more specific value, slightly more detail, can include one concrete artifact reference. 100–130 words.
- **One ask, low friction.** Interest-based CTAs ("worth a look?" / "useful?") beat meeting requests in first touch. Save the calendar link for touch 2 or 3.
- **Lead with their world, not Cerkl's.** "You/your" should outnumber "I/we." Don't open with who Cerkl is.

For the full underlying framework, see [cold-email SKILL.md](../../marketing/skills/cold-email/SKILL.md) — routed in [sales/CLAUDE.md](../CLAUDE.md).

### Acute Crisis (warm-touch only, no meeting ask)
- "Saw the news. Not pitching anything — just wanted to share something useful from the last few clients who navigated similar moments. [Link]. Will check back in a couple months."
- Optional: send a single relevant artifact (post-layoff comms checklist, all-hands template), no CTA.

### Aftermath
- "Three months after [event], a lot of internal comms leads we work with start asking the same question: are we sure the reset message is actually landing? Couple of approaches I've seen work — open to comparing notes?"
- "Post-[event], leadership tends to suddenly care about engagement metrics. If you're getting that ask, here's a quick read on what other [vertical] comms teams put in their next-quarter scorecard."
- Angle hook: **measurement, retention, rebuilding trust.**

### Transition Shock
- "Saw [new CHRO/CEO]'s announcement. New leaders typically audit the comms stack in their first 90 days. Quick 1-pager on how peer [vertical] companies consolidated Outlook + intranet + Slack-announcement into one — useful if the audit memo is coming up."
- "Congrats on [name]'s arrival. If you're getting asked 'why do we have 4 tools doing comms?' — happy to share how [peer company] answered that without an 18-month implementation."
- Angle hook: **consolidation, platform simplification, easy answer to the audit.**

### Sustained Strain
- "Curious if you have a way to measure whether the RTO comms are actually being read, by location. Couple of [vertical] comms leads I work with are using pulse-survey + acknowledgment data to bring receipts into the next leadership review."
- "If your CEO is asking why engagement is flat on the [mandate] updates — happy to show you a 2-min loom on how [peer] segments by role and tracks comprehension."
- Angle hook: **prove the work is landing. Receipts.**

## 5. Handoff Schema

One row per account. Default format: **Google Sheet for pilot** (collab + filter), **HubSpot import after Phase 3** if Marc/Josh validate the schema. Doc is not recommended — multi-row review is painful.

| Field | Required? | Notes / example |
|---|---|---|
| `account_name` | yes | Company legal name |
| `account_domain` | yes | Primary domain |
| `industry` | yes | `Tech/SaaS` \| `Healthcare` |
| `headcount_band` | yes | `501–2k` \| `2k–5k` \| `5k–12k` \| `12k+` |
| `plan_fit` | yes | `Foundations+` \| `Omni AI` (per `shared/icp.md` upgrade path) |
| `bucket` | yes | `Acute Crisis` \| `Aftermath` \| `Transition Shock` \| `Sustained Strain` |
| `signal_tier` | yes | `S` \| `A` \| `B` |
| `source_confidence` | yes | `High` \| `Medium` \| `Low` — per the cross-check rule in §1. Tier-S rows must be High. |
| `signals` | yes | List w/ type + date; e.g. `layoff 2026-04-15 (8% workforce); new CHRO 2026-05-02` |
| `latest_signal_date` | yes | YYYY-MM-DD — used for window timing |
| `one_line_context` | yes | Plain English sentence the AE can paste into a sequence. e.g. *"Laid off 8% in April, new CHRO arrived May 2, stock down 22% YTD."* |
| `recommended_action` | yes | `Pursue now` \| `Hold (revisit YYYY-MM-DD)` \| `Warm-touch only` |
| `suggested_angle` | yes | One sentence keyed to bucket. e.g. *"Aftermath — measurement/retention; reference how peer SaaS comms teams rebuild trust after layoffs."* |
| `comms_contact_name` | when findable | LinkedIn + title; e.g. `Jane Doe — Director, Internal Communications` |
| `comms_contact_linkedin` | when findable | URL |
| `hr_contact_name` | when findable | Fallback if no comms contact |
| `hubspot_status` | yes | `Net new` \| `Existing contact` \| `Existing account` |
| `source_links` | yes | URLs to news/filing/SEC docs (≥1) |
| `added_by` | yes | Researcher name + date |

### Source evidence storage

`source_links` in the schema points at the public URL. But public URLs rot, get paywalled, and disappear behind login walls. So we also keep a local dated archive alongside the row.

**Directory layout** (relative to `sales/pressure-prospecting/`):

```
sales/pressure-prospecting/
├── methodology.md          ← this file
└── raw/
    └── <account-slug>/     ← lowercase, hyphenated (e.g. acme-health-systems)
        └── YYYY-MM-DD/     ← date the signal was captured (not the date the event happened)
            ├── articles/   ← one .md per source article (scraped/cleaned)
            ├── filings/    ← PDFs or .md of WARN, SEC 8-K, NLRB, FDA letters, etc.
            └── notes.md    ← researcher notes; signal summary; cross-check confirmations
```

**Rules:**

- **Never overwrite a prior date folder.** When an account's signal picture changes (e.g., new layoff round 60 days later, or Acute → Aftermath transition), create a new dated folder. The history is the audit trail.
- **One company can have multiple snapshots over time.** A company may sit in Acute Crisis at one snapshot and Aftermath 60 days later. Both folders coexist; the handoff row reflects the *latest* snapshot.
- **`notes.md` records the cross-check.** "Verified WARN filing matches Layoffs.fyi entry — both report 240 cuts at the Atlanta site." This is what makes confidence levels defensible.
- **Lightweight is fine.** Save the source as plain markdown — strip ads, ship the article. PDFs OK for filings. Don't over-engineer.

Pattern borrowed from [competitor-profiling SKILL.md](../../marketing/skills/competitor-profiling/SKILL.md) — same dated-snapshot logic.

## 6. Pilot rules (Phase 3 — not active yet)

When the pilot runs (20–40 companies, Tech/SaaS + Healthcare, enterprise/midmarket):

- **Bucket mix target:** at least 5 accounts per bucket (excluding Acute Crisis, which is "hold" — surface up to 5 of these so AEs can plan the +60-day re-entry).
- **Vertical split:** roughly 60/40 Tech/SaaS / Healthcare to start; rebalance after first feedback.
- **No double-counting with CDM.** If an account is already on the CDM list, flag it but don't duplicate the row — note the cross-signal in `one_line_context`.
- **Contact-first, not just account-first.** A pressure signal without a findable comms/HR contact gets de-prioritized for the pilot; we want Marc/Josh able to send something, not just see a logo.
- **Track which angles get replies.** Add a `response` column in the Sheet for pilot retro — bucket-by-bucket reply rate is the key Phase 4 input.

## 7. When to update this methodology

- **Per-pilot retro** (after Phase 3): which buckets drove replies, which angles converted, which signals were noise. Update tier defaults and angle copy.
- **When a new signal type emerges** (e.g., a wave of AI-displacement layoffs distinct from cost-cut layoffs): add a row to the taxonomy with its own tier default.
- **If Marc/Josh report a bucket is mis-calibrated** (e.g., Aftermath is hitting too early — comms leads still in firefight at day 30): adjust the bucket windows.
