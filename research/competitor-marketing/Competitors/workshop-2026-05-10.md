# IC Deep Dive — Workshop

**Date:** 2026-05-10   |   **Category:** Competitor

---

## TL;DR

Workshop (useworkshop.com, Omaha NE) has spent the last 12 months building out from "modern internal email" toward a full email-first IC platform — shipping Journeys (automation, Oct 2025), four AI features (Nov 2025), and Cici, their agentic AI assistant for internal communicators (Q1 2026). They are growing fast (>100 employees, Dublin office opening, ~600 customers), priced from $250/month entry with no free tier, and rank #1 on G2 for usability in enterprise + frontline internal newsletters. They are now Cerkl's most direct head-to-head competitor for the Foundations tier, and their AI-first push is the most decision-relevant 2026 shift to react to.

## What it is

Workshop is an email-first internal communications platform founded in 2020 in Omaha, Nebraska by Derek Homann, Ben Stevinson (ex-Median), Dusty Davidson, and Rick Knudtson (ex-Flywheel) ([PR Newswire — $12M Series A, 2023-11-16](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/omaha-based-startup-workshop-raises-12m-to-streamline-internal-email-and-communications-301991425.html)). Funding to date: $3.5M pre-seed (Apr 2021), $5.2M seed (Jul 2022), $12M Series A led by McCarthy Capital (Nov 2023) — roughly $20M+ raised. Crossed 100 employees in October 2025; ~15–20% of customers are in Europe and a Dublin office officially launches in 2026 ([Silicon Prairie News, 2025-12](https://siliconprairienews.com/2025/12/workshop-surpasses-100-employees-as-it-nears-five-year-anniversary/)). Customers include Panera Bread, FanDuel, Cargill, Jim Beam, Sprouts Farmers Market. Positioned as "The #1 internal communications email software" — homepage frame is *the fastest way to improve your internal communications, starting with email and extending to Teams, Slack, SMS, and more* ([useworkshop.com](https://useworkshop.com/)).

## What it does / claims

Workshop is a drag-and-drop email builder + distribution-list manager + analytics layer + (newer) cross-channel re-post layer, all priced as a single org-wide subscription rather than per-seat:

- **Email builder** — drag-and-drop, branded templates, no-code; the most-praised surface in G2 reviews ([G2](https://www.g2.com/products/workshop/reviews)).
- **Distribution + sync** — auto-updating lists, HRIS sync, segmentation.
- **Analytics** — opens, clicks, read-time, engagement benchmarks; performance summaries surfaced inside Cici.
- **Multichannel re-post** — cross-post email to Microsoft Teams, Slack, SharePoint; two-way SMS for frontline/deskless employees ([SMS product page](https://useworkshop.com/product/sms/)); explicitly framed as "reinforce in Slack/Teams, follow up by SMS only to non-openers" ([multi-channel blog](https://useworkshop.com/blog/multi-channel-comms-strategy/)).
- **Journeys (automation)** — onboarding, anniversary, reminder, birthday email sequences; HRIS-triggered; included on every plan ([PR Newswire, 2025-10-24](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/workshop-launches-journeys-automate-employee-comms-with-ease-302593958.html)).
- **AI / Cici** — agentic AI assistant pre-trained on Workshop's IC playbooks, templates, benchmarks, and tone rules; suggests subject lines, rewrites for skim, plans multi-step campaigns, answers engagement-benchmark questions, drafts full emails (the last "coming soon" as of launch); permission-scoped, private chat history ([Cici page](https://useworkshop.com/cici/); [launch press release](https://useworkshop.com/press/workshop-launches-cici-an-agentic-ai-assistant-built-for-modern-internal-communications/)).

## Who it's for

- **Primary user / ICP overlap:** Internal comms owner at SMB → lower mid-market (~200–3,000 employees), email is the dominant channel, IT has minimal involvement, buyer wants to launch in days not quarters. Strong fit for HR / People / Comms managers without a dedicated email-ops engineer.
- **Strong fit when:** Email is the primary IC channel; buyer is escaping Outlook/Mailchimp/homegrown; values design polish + drag-and-drop + good analytics; can clear a $3k–$15k+/yr software line item; Teams/Slack/SMS exist but are secondary reinforcement channels, not primary delivery.
- **Weak fit when:** Org has 5,000+ deskless employees and email isn't the primary reach channel; buyer needs a content-personalization engine (per-employee curated digests, not one-to-many sends); IT mandates SSO/SCIM at a tier the buyer can't afford; org needs a real intranet / home-base; budget is "$0 to start" (no free tier exists).

## How it slots into Cerkl's competitive set / positioning

Workshop is **the most direct head-to-head competitor for Cerkl Foundations**. `shared/competitors.md` already states this correctly: *"Workshop is email-first for SMB. Cerkl competes directly here with Foundations. Cerkl's advantage: free forever (Workshop is not), plus a clearer upgrade path to omni-channel if the org grows."* That framing still holds in 2026 — but Workshop's product trajectory has moved enough that the head-to-head needs updating:

1. **Free-forever is still the sharpest commercial wedge — and 2026 makes it sharper.** Workshop's entry plan is **$250/month for 250 employees**, ~$3,000/year minimum, with no permanent free tier (only a 14-day trial) ([SelectSoftware Reviews, 2026](https://www.selectsoftwarereviews.com/reviews/workshop); [free trial page](https://useworkshop.com/resources/free-trial/)). For the Cerkl Foundations buyer — SMB IC owner, ops lead, chief of staff, founder — that is a budget conversation; Cerkl Foundations is not. AEs should lead with this every time Workshop is in the deal: *"Workshop is $3k/year minimum, no free tier. Cerkl Foundations is free forever. You can launch in Cerkl this afternoon without a budget approval."*

2. **Workshop has quietly closed the multichannel gap — Cerkl's "upgrade path to omni-channel" story needs a sharper edge.** As of 2026, Workshop has cross-post to Teams/Slack/SharePoint, two-way SMS for frontline, and Journeys automation across channels ([multi-channel blog](https://useworkshop.com/blog/multi-channel-comms-strategy/); [SMS product](https://useworkshop.com/product/sms/)). The old line "Workshop is email-only; if you grow, you'll outgrow it" is weakening. Cerkl Omni AI's differentiator is no longer *channels* — it is **personalization** (MyNews Engine: per-employee curated digests in the inbox, not the same blast to everyone) and **measurement attribution across channels**. AEs should reframe the upgrade story: *"Workshop adds channels by reposting. Cerkl Omni AI personalizes what each employee receives, then measures which channel each employee actually read it in."* This is the right line for 2,001–5,000-employee deals where Workshop will increasingly show up.

3. **Cici is the 2026 product move Cerkl needs to react to.** Workshop is making an aggressive "AI for internal comms" claim — Cici is pre-loaded with IC playbooks, benchmarks, tone rules; drafts subject lines, rewrites for skim, plans campaigns, will soon draft entire emails ([Cici launch](https://useworkshop.com/press/workshop-launches-cici-an-agentic-ai-assistant-built-for-modern-internal-communications/)). This is a *content-creation* AI play, not a *content-distribution* AI play. Cerkl's AI story is the inverse — MyNews Engine *personalizes what each employee receives*, Workshop's Cici *helps the communicator write faster*. Both are valid, both are real, and they don't compete head-on — but the buyer will conflate them. Cerkl needs a clean one-liner: *"Cici writes your email. Cerkl decides what each employee should actually see."*

4. **ICP overlap is heavy and growing — and there is no live "vs. Workshop" SEO page.** Workshop's ICP — SMB → lower mid-market IC owners — is exactly Cerkl Foundations + the lower edge of Foundations+ (501–2,000 employees). `shared/competitors.md` lists live "vs." pages for Simpplr, LumApps, Firstup, Workvivo, Poppulo — but **not Workshop**. Given Workshop's G2 visibility (4.6 stars, 161+ reviews, #1 ranks in four Spring 2026 G2 categories), and that Workshop has invested in SEO content (their `/blog` and `/landing/` pages outrank Cerkl for several IC keywords), **Cerkl should commission a `cerkl.com/comparisons/workshop-vs-cerkl-broadcast/` page**. The angle: *free-forever Foundations + native inbox personalization + measurement layer across the channels you already have + a real upgrade path when you grow past email-only.*

**Counter-positioning lines for AEs (use when Workshop is in the deal):**
- **Price:** *"Workshop's floor is $250/month — about $3,000/year minimum, no free option. Cerkl Foundations is free forever. You can be sending today without a PO."*
- **Channel architecture:** *"Workshop reposts your email to Slack and Teams. Cerkl delivers each employee a personalized digest in their inbox or app, then measures which channel each person actually reads."*
- **AI:** *"Workshop's AI helps your team write the email faster. Cerkl's AI decides which content each employee should receive in the first place. Different problems."*
- **Growth path:** *"If you scale past 2,000 employees, frontline, or multiple regions, Cerkl moves you to Omni AI on the same platform. Workshop's path past email is repost-and-track."*

## Gotchas

- **No free tier.** 14-day trial only. Entry plan is **$250/month for 250 employees** (~$3k/year floor) ([SelectSoftware Reviews](https://www.selectsoftwarereviews.com/reviews/workshop)). For SMB IC owners on a $0 budget, this is the buying decision.
- **Tier-gated features.** SSO, advanced segmentation, full integrations, and higher admin-seat counts sit in upper tiers. Multiple G2 reviewers flag the **4-admin cap on lower tiers** as the most-cited frustration ([G2](https://www.g2.com/products/workshop/reviews)).
- **Pricing is not fully public.** Only the Essential tier ($250/mo, 250 employees) is published; Enhanced / Premium / Enterprise are quote-driven. Mid-market buyers will need a sales call before they see the real number.
- **Cross-channel is *re-post*, not native delivery.** Teams/Slack integration cross-posts a copy of the email. This is not the same as personalized native delivery to a Teams or Slack DM — important when stacking against Cerkl Omni AI or Firstup in 2,001+ employee deals.
- **Mobile is browser-first / no native employee app.** No deskless-worker employee app the way Staffbase or Workvivo have. SMS is the deskless play. For deeply deskless orgs (manufacturing, hospitality, healthcare), this is a gap.
- **No intranet / home-base.** Workshop is a sender, not a destination. If the buyer's actual problem is "we need an intranet," Workshop is not the answer (and neither is Cerkl — both lose this conversation to Staffbase/Simpplr/LumApps).
- **"#1 leader in AI for internal communications" is marketing, not analyst.** Workshop's Nov 2025 press release uses this framing in its own headline ([PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/workshop-debuts-four-new-ai-features-positioning-itself-as-the-clear-leader-in-ai-for-internal-communications-302615113.html)) — it is a self-declared claim, not a Gartner/Forrester position. Worth fact-checking if it appears in a competitive deal.

- **What would change my mind (escalate to "Critical — sales response"):**

  - Workshop adds a **personalization engine** (per-employee content curation, MyNews-style). Cici today is communicator-side; the moment Workshop ships recipient-side personalization, the differentiation collapses.
  - Workshop launches a **free or freemium tier**. Cerkl's #1 commercial wedge in the head-to-head disappears overnight.
  - Workshop is named in a major analyst report (Gartner, Forrester) as a category leader — moves the buyer conversation upstream.
  - Cerkl Foundations starts losing 2+ named SMB deals per quarter to Workshop specifically.
