01What it is & what it does

What it is

Workshop is an email-first internal communications platform founded in 2020 in Omaha, Nebraska by Derek Homann, Ben Stevinson (ex-Median), Dusty Davidson, and Rick Knudtson (ex-Flywheel) (PR Newswire — $12M Series A). Funding to date: $3.5M pre-seed (Apr 2021), $5.2M seed (Jul 2022), $12M Series A led by McCarthy Capital (Nov 2023) — roughly $20M+ raised.

Crossed 100 employees in October 2025; ~15–20% of customers are in Europe and a Dublin office launches in 2026 (Silicon Prairie News). Customers include Panera Bread, FanDuel, Cargill, Jim Beam, Sprouts Farmers Market.

Positioned as "The #1 internal communications email software" — homepage frame is the fastest way to improve your internal communications, starting with email and extending to Teams, Slack, SMS, and more (useworkshop.com).

What it does / claims

Drag-and-drop email builder + distribution-list manager + analytics layer + (newer) cross-channel re-post layer, priced as a single org-wide subscription rather than per-seat.

  • Email builder — drag-and-drop, branded templates, no-code; most-praised surface in G2 reviews.
  • Distribution + sync — auto-updating lists, HRIS sync, segmentation.
  • Analytics — opens, clicks, read-time, engagement benchmarks; performance summaries surfaced inside Cici.
  • Multichannel re-post — cross-post to Teams, Slack, SharePoint; two-way SMS for frontline (SMS; multi-channel blog).
  • Journeys — HRIS-triggered automations: onboarding, anniversary, reminder, birthday; included on every plan (launch PR).
  • AI / Cici — agentic assistant pre-trained on Workshop's IC playbooks; suggests subject lines, rewrites for skim, plans campaigns, drafts emails (last "coming soon") (Cici; launch PR).

02Who it's for

Primary user / ICP overlap

Internal comms owner at SMB → lower mid-market (~200–3,000 employees). Email is the dominant channel, IT has minimal involvement, buyer wants to launch in days not quarters. Strong fit for HR / People / Comms managers without a dedicated email-ops engineer.

Strong fit when

Email is the primary IC channel; buyer is escaping Outlook / Mailchimp / homegrown; values design polish, drag-and-drop, good analytics; can clear a $3k–$15k+/yr software line; Teams / Slack / SMS exist but as secondary reinforcement channels, not primary delivery.

Weak fit when

Org has 5,000+ deskless employees and email isn't the primary reach channel; buyer needs a content-personalization engine (per-employee curated digests); IT mandates SSO/SCIM at a tier the buyer can't afford; org needs a real intranet / home-base; budget is "$0 to start" (no free tier exists).

03How it slots into Cerkl's competitive set

Workshop is the most direct head-to-head competitor for Cerkl Foundations. shared/competitors.md already states this correctly: "Workshop is email-first for SMB. Cerkl competes directly here with Foundations. Cerkl's advantage: free forever (Workshop is not), plus a clearer upgrade path to omni-channel if the org grows." That framing still holds in 2026 — but Workshop's product trajectory has moved enough that the head-to-head needs updating.

1. Free-forever is the sharpest commercial wedge — 2026 makes it sharper

Workshop's entry plan is $250/month for 250 employees, ~$3,000/year minimum, with no permanent free tier (only a 14-day trial) (SelectSoftware; free trial). For the Foundations buyer — SMB IC owner, ops lead, chief of staff, founder — that is a budget conversation; Cerkl Foundations is not.

2. Workshop has quietly closed the multichannel gap

As of 2026, Workshop has cross-post to Teams / Slack / SharePoint, two-way SMS for frontline, and Journeys automation across channels. The old line "Workshop is email-only; you'll outgrow it" is weakening. Cerkl Omni AI's differentiator is no longer channels — it is personalization (MyNews Engine) and measurement attribution across channels.

3. Cici is the 2026 product move to react to

Workshop is making an aggressive "AI for internal comms" claim — Cici is pre-loaded with IC playbooks, benchmarks, tone rules. This is a content-creation AI play, not a content-distribution AI play. Cerkl's AI story is the inverse — MyNews personalizes what each employee receives; Workshop's Cici helps the communicator write faster. The buyer will conflate them.

4. ICP overlap is heavy and growing — no live "vs. Workshop" SEO page

Workshop's ICP is exactly Cerkl Foundations + the lower edge of Foundations+ (501–2,000 employees). shared/competitors.md lists "vs." pages for Simpplr, LumApps, Firstup, Workvivo, Poppulo — but not Workshop. Given 4.6 G2 stars, 161+ reviews, and four Spring 2026 #1 G2 ranks, Cerkl should commission the comparison page.

05Gotchas

No free tier
14-day trial only. Entry plan is $250/month for 250 employees (~$3k/year floor) (SelectSoftware). For SMB IC owners on a $0 budget, this is the buying decision.
Tier-gated features
SSO, advanced segmentation, full integrations, and higher admin-seat counts sit in upper tiers. Multiple G2 reviewers flag the 4-admin cap on lower tiers as the most-cited frustration (G2).
Pricing is not fully public
Only the Essential tier ($250/mo, 250 employees) is published; Enhanced / Premium / Enterprise are quote-driven. Mid-market buyers will need a sales call before they see the real number.
Cross-channel is re-post, not native delivery
Teams / Slack integration cross-posts a copy of the email. This is not the same as personalized native delivery to a Teams or Slack DM — important when stacking against Cerkl Omni AI or Firstup in 2,001+ employee deals.
Mobile is browser-first / no native employee app
No deskless-worker employee app the way Staffbase or Workvivo have. SMS is the deskless play. For deeply deskless orgs (manufacturing, hospitality, healthcare), this is a gap.
No intranet / home-base
Workshop is a sender, not a destination. If the buyer's actual problem is "we need an intranet," Workshop is not the answer (and neither is Cerkl — both lose this conversation to Staffbase / Simpplr / LumApps).
"#1 leader in AI for internal communications" is marketing, not analyst
Workshop's Nov 2025 press release uses this framing in its own headline (PR Newswire) — it is a self-declared claim, not a Gartner / Forrester position. Worth fact-checking if it appears in a competitive deal.
