01What it is

Origin & corporate

Hamburg, Germany-headquartered employee communications and employee experience platform created by the 2022 merger/rebrand of three companies: COYO (Hamburg, founded 2010 by Jan Marquardt and Marko Ilic), Smarp (Helsinki, employee advocacy), and Jubiwee (Paris, people analytics / engagement surveys).

Backed by Marlin Equity Partners (acquired 2020, pre-merger) and Nauta Capital; ~$33M total funding. ~300 employees across Atlanta, London, Hamburg, Berlin, Paris and Helsinki. Positioned today as "Employee Communications & Intranet Software" combining intranet, multichannel comms, employee advocacy, and engagement insights.

02What it does / claims

Haiilo sells a unified suite that bundles three historically separate products under one brand:

COYO LINEAGE

Employee Communications / Intranet

Social-feed intranet, multichannel publishing (web, mobile, email), communities and interest groups, 135+ integrations, customizable layouts. overview · Capterra

SMARP LINEAGE

Employee Advocacy / Amplify

Standalone module: employees connect personal LinkedIn/Facebook/X to share company content externally, with leaderboards, points, reach/click analytics. Headline case study: 2,033% ROI on Salesforce's advocacy program. page · App Store

JUBIWEE LINEAGE

Insights / Engagement

Engagement surveys and analytics, originally a separate French SaaS now folded in. Business Wire

AI LAYER

Haiilo AVA

Content drafting, summarization, hashtag suggestion, in-search Q&A grounded in platform content, event cover-image generation, AI-generated social sharing messages for advocates. BYOK: OpenAI, Azure OpenAI, or Google Vertex AI. AI features · AVA overview

03Who it's for

Primary user / ICP overlap

EU-headquartered mid-market and enterprise orgs (2,000+ employees) where the buyer wants a unified comms + intranet platform and employee advocacy / external social amplification is a stated goal. Strong industries: large transportation (Deutsche Bahn), telecom (Deutsche Telekom), healthcare (Asklepios), CPG (Ritter Sport), professional services (KPMG). Plans typically start at 500 user licenses (G2 pricing).

Strong fit when

Buyer wants intranet + advocacy bundled; marketing/comms wants employees as a distribution channel for external content; EU data residency / German-engineering preference is a procurement factor; social-feed UX is preferred over structured-publishing UX.

Weak fit when

Buyer wants deep audience segmentation and personalization (per LumApps: "Haiilo lacks audience segmentation, making targeted communication impossible" — LumApps vs Haiilo); compliance- or governance-heavy environment; org wants a delivery + measurement layer on top of existing channels rather than a new home base; sub-500 employees; no appetite for employee advocacy as a use case.

04How it slots into Cerkl's competitive set

Haiilo is already in the main competitor list in shared/competitors.md but has no live "vs. Haiilo" SEO page and no head-to-head positioning paragraph. That's a gap worth closing — Haiilo is structurally different from Staffbase/Simpplr/LumApps and Cerkl's existing competitive narrative does not address it cleanly.

1. Cerkl's core counter-frame applies — sharper here than against Staffbase

"We're the delivery + measurement layer; the intranet is the destination, not the comms strategy."

Haiilo is more intranet-as-destination than Staffbase, because Haiilo's center of gravity is a social-feed home base where employees go to read, react, and share. If the buyer's actual job is "make sure the right message reaches every employee through whatever channel they actually open," Haiilo is the wrong architecture — it presumes employees come to Haiilo. Cerkl's pitch (sync audiences from your HRIS, deliver into Outlook/Gmail/Teams/Slack/intranet you already own, measure cross-channel) is the inverse.

2. Employee advocacy is a Haiilo differentiator — but not a Cerkl threat

Employee advocacy (employees sharing company content to external social networks) is a marketing-team motion, not an internal-comms motion. It's the Smarp lineage and Haiilo's most distinctive surface area vs. Staffbase/Simpplr/LumApps/Firstup (Sociabble).

For Cerkl this is a dilution risk for Haiilo, not a strength: when Haiilo shows up in a Cerkl deal, the IC buyer is being sold a tool whose ROI case study (the 2,033% Salesforce number) is a marketing program metric, not an internal-comms metric. AEs can use that.

3. ICP fit is narrower than it looks

Haiilo's customer base skews EU-headquartered enterprise (Deutsche Bahn, Deutsche Telekom, Ritter Sport, KPMG-DE, Asklepios) with 500+ user minimums and German-engineering provenance. Cerkl's paid ICP (2,000–12,200+ employees, US-anchored, distributed/frontline/deskless) overlaps in headcount but not in geography or buying motion.

Where it does overlap is mid-market US orgs with EU operations — and there, Haiilo's documented weaknesses (no audience segmentation, limited personalization, weaker governance per LumApps; structural rigidity and customization limits per Sociabble) are exactly the things Omni AI's segmentation engine is built for.

06Gotchas

Three-product seams still visible
The COYO + Smarp + Jubiwee merger is four years in but Employee Advocacy ships as a standalone module on a separate knowledge base (advocacy-knowledgebase.haiilo.com) from the main intranet (support.haiilo.com). Buyers expecting a fully unified product may be surprised.
Audience segmentation gap
Competitors (LumApps, Sociabble) and review-aggregator summaries consistently call out limited segmentation and personalization vs. peers (LumApps vs Haiilo; Sociabble roundup). For Cerkl's Omni AI ICP, this is the most important functional gap.
No public pricing; 500-seat minimum
Custom quote-driven sales motion; no free trial; no free plan (G2; TrustRadius; Connecteam review). Multiple Capterra reviewers cite pricing as a deal-breaker.
Employee-advocacy fit-or-not-for-buyer
The Salesforce 2,033% ROI case study and the Amplify module are external-social-sharing plays. If the IC buyer doesn't have a marketing-led advocacy program (or doesn't want one), they're paying for a module they won't use.
Governance / compliance is light
Haiilo functions as a communications + social hub more than a structured-governance intranet (LumApps). Compliance-heavy buyers (healthcare, finance, regulated manufacturing) will gravitate to Staffbase or LumApps.
No surfaced analyst placement
No Gartner Magic Quadrant or Forrester Wave placement appeared in 2025–2026 searches. Haiilo has Gartner Peer Insights presence but did not appear as a named leader/challenger in the analyst placements I searched. May simply be a search-coverage gap; worth verifying with a direct source if it ever matters in a deal.
Integration + AI deployment friction
Slack integration reported broken by at least one Capterra reviewer who chose the platform specifically for that (Capterra reviews). AVA is provider-agnostic but not zero-config — customers either pay Haiilo for turnkey AI or bring their own OpenAI/Azure/Vertex keys (AVA setup). Flexible — but IT is in the AI deployment loop.
