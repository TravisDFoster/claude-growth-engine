## Pain-point cross-cut

> The buyer problems competitors are leading with this week, derived across positioning + ads + content. Most actionable section for Cerkl's marketing.

### Pain point 1: "Our employees are already using AI tools we haven't approved" — shadow AI moves from content to ad headlines

- **Who's claiming it:** Staffbase (homepage + paid LP creative), LumApps (hero sub-head + ad creative), Simpplr (lead blog post 2026-06-03), Firstup ("AI paradox" / frontline equity, ongoing).
- **How they're framing it (verbatim):**
  - Staffbase: *"Our employees are already using AI tools we haven't approved"* + *"79% of organizations suspect employees are misusing approved AI tools"* ([staffbase.com](https://staffbase.com/en/))
  - LumApps: *"Cut shadow IT, reduce complexity"* now elevated to hero ([lumapps.com](https://www.lumapps.com/))
  - Simpplr: *"Three strategies to stop shadow AI without killing productivity"* — half of corporate workers use unsanctioned AI tools; governance, not bans, is the lever ([Simpplr](https://www.simpplr.com/blog/govern-shadow-ai/))
- **Cerkl's current angle:** Cerkl has no shadow-AI positioning today. Omni AI is sold on personalization + reach, not on trust/governance.
- **Implication:** **Counter-position, don't chase — but this week.** "Shadow AI" is now a stat-led ad-copy pain claim used by 3+ of the 10 vendors. The dominant pitch is *"we govern your employees' AI for you"* (IT-co-buyer). Cerkl's IC-led ICP does not buy on those terms. Counter-frame: *"Shadow AI is a content problem disguised as a governance problem. The real fix isn't blocking the tools — it's making your sanctioned channel so much better that nobody needs to invent one. Foundations is free and live in an afternoon."* One short POV post + a homepage stat-card variant; not a campaign.

### Pain point 2: "Communication isn't reaching the right people" — fragmentation framing still dominant (4 vendors, holding from W22)

- **Who's claiming it:** Simpplr (*"Fragmented Knowledge Systems"* ad copy), LumApps (*"fragmented processes"*), Firstup (*"teams scattered across shifts, sites, and roles"*), Poppulo (*"right information to the right people"*). Plus Firstup's "75% miss critical updates" 2026 IC research published this week ([firstup.io](https://firstup.io/blog/internal-communications-failure-2026-research/)).
- **Cerkl's current angle:** Cerkl's positioning (*"delivery + measurement layer across the channels you already have"*) is directly the answer to this pain — but Cerkl is competing for the *segmentation* phrasing of it, not the *fragmentation* phrasing. Subtle but real difference: segmentation is what we sell; fragmentation is what they're solving for.
- **Implication:** **Lean in by adopting "fragmentation" as a hero word for at least one Cerkl LP variant.** Cerkl's Omni AI literally fixes this problem (sync segments, route by channel, prove read-through). Test *"Stop your IC fragmenting across 6 channels. One platform routes the right message to the right person — on the channel they actually read."* as a Foundations+ LP hero alongside the existing copy.

### Pain point 3: "We launched AI but can't see outcomes" — the execution-gap meme stalled (still 3 CEOs)

- **Who's claiming it:** Same three from W22 — Cates (ContactMonkey, W20), Black (Workvivo, W21), Fornell (Poppulo, W22). Sharma (Simpplr) and Böhringer (Staffbase) **did not adopt** the frame this week despite both publishing major AI content.
- **Implication:** **First-mover window is still open but narrowing.** Cerkl's W22 recommendation — *"AI writes the message. Cerkl makes sure the right employee actually reads it — and proves they did"* — still applies and still has no direct vendor competitor speaking it verbatim. If the homepage A/B test wasn't queued from W22, queue it now; the W24/W25 risk is that Simpplr or Staffbase claims a variant first, and then Cerkl's version reads as derivative rather than originating.

## Ideas to steal

> 3–5 specific tactics, formats, or angles a competitor is doing well that Cerkl could adapt. Each tagged with how Cerkl might use it.

- **Stat-led ad headlines (Staffbase + AxiosHQ + Firstup playbook).** Staffbase's *"79% of organizations suspect employees are misusing approved AI tools"* — ad copy as a research-stat reveal. AxiosHQ's *"92% / 90% / 82%"* triple-stat hero. Firstup's *"75% miss critical updates"* report-anchored creative.
  - *How Cerkl might use this:* Cerkl owns read-rate data on the platform every day. Lead one Foundations LP variant with a single one-screen stat: e.g., *"4 in 5 employee emails go unread. Cerkl knows because we measure every one."* Pair it with a 30-second "what 'read' actually means" tooltip explainer. Same Foundations LP, same offer, different hero — A/B against the current generic-platform creative.

- **Counter-positioned shadow-AI POV.** Cerkl has no answer to the W23 shadow-AI pitch — and the IC-team buyer doesn't care about IT-governance, so the right play isn't a Cerkl "AI Control Center."
  - *How Cerkl might use this:* One short POV post (~600 words) — *"Shadow AI is a content problem dressed as a governance problem. The real fix isn't blocking the tools — it's making your sanctioned channel so much better nobody needs to invent one."* Publish under a Cerkl exec byline. Tie to Foundations as the "live-in-an-afternoon" demonstration that the sanctioned-channel argument is real, not aspirational. Should ship within 14 days while Staffbase/Simpplr/LumApps are spending impressions on the pain.

- **Tit-for-tat conquest listicle preparedness.** Sociabble vs. ContactMonkey this week, plus ContactMonkey's now-confirmed monthly cadence (Workshop / PoliteMail / Poppulo / Staffbase = 4 in 14 days), means *somebody is going to publish "Best Cerkl Alternatives 2026"* eventually. Defense first.
  - *How Cerkl might use this:* (1) Run a SERP probe for `"Cerkl alternatives 2026"` weekly going forward — flag the moment one publishes. (2) Own the Cerkl-alternatives SERP defensively: publish *"Cerkl vs. the alternatives — when to pick what"* on the Cerkl blog now, with honest "if you want X, pick Y" sections. Owning your own conquest SERP is cheaper than reclaiming it after a competitor takes it. (3) Offensively: pick the two cheapest wins from `shared/competitors.md` — *Best Workshop Alternatives 2026* (most direct Foundations head-to-head) and *Best Axios HQ Alternatives 2026* (cheapest competitive SEO win on the list per `shared/competitors.md`). 4-week sprint.

- **Sustained-program signal: one wedge → 3+ assets across 14 days (Simpplr's AI-governance playbook).** Simpplr did this exact thing — AI Control Center launch (5/22) → "manager enablement for AI" (5/28) → "stop shadow AI" (6/3). Three different angles on one wedge, three weeks in a row. That's how a wedge stops reading as a launch and starts reading as a category position.
  - *How Cerkl might use this:* Pick ONE wedge for the next 3-week sprint. Suggested wedge: *"AI writes; Cerkl delivers and proves."* Week 1: exec POV post (the "execution problem, sharper variant"). Week 2: a stat-led customer micro-case (1 logo, 1 number, 1 chart — read-rate before/after). Week 3: a Foundations onboarding-flow video demonstrating the claim. No new gated assets needed — the sprint *is* the asset.

- **AxiosHQ June 23 "Smart Brevity Open House" event format.** Not a webinar, not a demo — an open community event as a marketing surface, anchored on training methodology. Smart Brevity isn't a product feature, it's a content meme they're scaling via training.
  - *How Cerkl might use this:* Lower-priority idea, but worth watching: the open-community format scales engagement with limited product touch. Cerkl's equivalent would be a *"Read-rate clinic"* — bring your worst-performing internal email, Cerkl team + a guest IC lead diagnoses live what's wrong and what to test. 60-min Zoom, monthly, recorded. Test once in Q3 before committing.
