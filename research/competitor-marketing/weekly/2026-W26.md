## This week in one paragraph

The measurement story matured this week — and the nuance is the headline. On the **homepage hero layer it looks like measurement is receding**: Staffbase swapped to a new consolidation H1 (*"Finally, one platform to reach every employee. Wherever they work."*) and pulled its shadow-AI stat back out, and "one platform + AI" is now the dominant hero frame across 7 of 10 fetchable vendors. But on the **proof layer it's surging**: measurement/outcomes ad creative grew to **7 of 10 and is still climbing** (LumApps newly added *"deep analytics prove what's working"*; Workshop sharpened to *"say goodbye to guesswork"*), and the week's gated assets are almost entirely ROI-substantiation — **Firstup published a Forrester Total Economic Impact study claiming 398% ROI**, ContactMonkey re-pushed its **Internal Email Benchmark Report 2026**, and Simpplr built a case-study roundup entirely on adoption metrics. Read together: **measurement is migrating from positioning tagline into the down-funnel proof/substantiation layer** — the category is now building the *evidence* infrastructure (TEI studies, benchmark reports, analytics features) behind a claim Cerkl already delivers natively. A second new thread emerged: an **AI-governance/"trust over AI" contrarian POV** (Staffbase *"AI won't save your employee experience. Trust will."* + Poppulo *"fix your data governance before agentic AI"*, near-identical takes days apart). The *"execution problem"* CEO frame stayed **stalled at 3 for a 6th week** even as the broader *"we can't prove impact"* narrative hardened industry-wide. And 0/10 still offer free-forever (week 8) — with pricing gating now near-total (7/10 gated, ContactMonkey + Workvivo's W25 gate held).

## Pain-point cross-cut

> The buyer problems competitors are leading with this week, derived across positioning + ads + content. Most actionable section for Cerkl's marketing.

### Pain point 1: "You can't prove your comms work" — migrated from hero claim to PROOF layer (the week's key move)

- **Who's claiming it (W26):** Ad creative 7/10 (Firstup, AxiosHQ, ContactMonkey, Simpplr, Poppulo, Workshop, + **LumApps newly added**). Gated proof assets: Firstup, ContactMonkey, Simpplr.
- **How they're framing it now (verbatim):**
  - Firstup: *"Don't just communicate. Get outcomes."* + **new Forrester TEI study: *"398% ROI"* / *"$8.9M in savings"*** ([firstup.io/blog](https://firstup.io/blog))
  - ContactMonkey: *"Prove the impact of every send"* + **Internal Email Benchmark Report 2026** ([contactmonkey.com](https://www.contactmonkey.com/))
  - LumApps: **newly added** *"deep analytics prove what's working"* ([lumapps.com](https://www.lumapps.com/)); Workshop: *"say goodbye to guesswork"* (sharpened from W25's *"less guesswork"*); AxiosHQ: *"92% streamlined / 90% engagement / 82% faster feedback"* ([axioshq.com](https://www.axioshq.com/))
- **Cerkl's current angle:** Cerkl's *"delivery + measurement layer… delivered, opened, read, by employee"* IS this. But the competitive game has shifted: vendors are no longer just *claiming* measurement — they're **substantiating** it with third-party ROI studies, benchmark reports, and analytics-feature proof. Cerkl has the real proof (per-employee read attribution) but lacks the proof *assets* (a Cerkl ROI study, a Cerkl benchmark report) to compete in the substantiation round.
- **Implication: This is the most important strategic shift this quarter.** The measurement message is commoditizing at the claim level and the contest is moving to *evidence*. **Two moves:** (1) **Out-specify on substance** — competitors prove with aggregate ROI (398%, 282%, 92%); none proves at the *employee* level. Cerkl's wedge sharpens to *"They publish a 398% ROI study. We show you which employees opened it — by name. One is a marketing model; the other is your actual data."* (2) **Build a proof asset** — a Cerkl "Internal Comms Benchmark Report" (read rates by industry/channel) would meet the category on the substantiation layer where Cerkl's data is genuinely differentiated, and double as a lead-gen gated asset and an SEO play on "internal communications measurement" (a SERP now crowded 5-deep).

### Pain point 2: "AI hype is outrunning trust/governance" — NEW contrarian thesis (2 vendors, near-simultaneous)

- **Who's claiming it:** Staffbase (*"AI won't save your employee experience. Trust will."* POV) and Poppulo (*"Before You Deploy Agentic AI, Fix Your Data Governance First"*) — published within days of each other ([staffbase.com](https://staffbase.com/blog/ai-wont-save-employee-experience-trust-will), [poppulo.com/blog](https://www.poppulo.com/blog)).
- **Cerkl's current angle:** None. This is the mirror image of the shadow-AI fear hook — instead of *"employees are using rogue AI,"* it's *"don't trust AI output until the data/governance is right."*
- **Implication: This is a contrarian lane Cerkl can ride authentically.** Two enterprise vendors just validated "AI skepticism" as a marketable POV. Cerkl's honest version: *"AI can write the message. It can't make employees read it. The unsexy part — getting the right message to the right person and proving they saw it — is still the whole job."* Ties the AI-trust thread directly back to Pain Point 1 (measurement as the trust mechanism). Ship this as the long-recommended POV that has now sat unwritten for 4 weeks.

### Pain point 3: "Fragmentation → consolidate on one platform" — now the dominant HERO frame (7/10)

- **Who's claiming it:** Staffbase (new H1 *"one platform to reach every employee"*), Workvivo (*"One digital headquarters"*), LumApps (*"where work comes together"*), Simpplr (*"one unified experience"*), Poppulo (*"one AI-powered experience platform"*), Haiilo, ContactMonkey.
- **Cerkl's current angle:** Direct counter — Cerkl's anti-destination frame (*"we meet employees in the inbox they already have — the delivery and measurement layer, not the destination"*).
- **Implication: Counter-position, don't follow — the contrast is sharpening in Cerkl's favor.** The whole set is converging on *"come consolidate onto our one platform/hub."* Cerkl's *"don't rip-and-replace; we layer on top of what you already run"* reads cleaner every week the field crowds the consolidation message. Pair with the speed/IT-light proof points.
