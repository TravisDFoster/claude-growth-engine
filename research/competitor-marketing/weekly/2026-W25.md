## This week in one paragraph

The headline this week is **convergence onto Cerkl's home turf**: the measurement/outcomes pain hook jumped from 3 vendors to **6 of 10** — Firstup (*"$1T turnover cost"*), AxiosHQ (*"92% streamlined / 90% engagement / 82% faster feedback"*), ContactMonkey (*"demonstrate clear internal communications impact"*), Simpplr (*"282% ROI"*), Poppulo (*"analytics to derive really important insights"*), and Workshop (*"less guesswork"*) all now lead creative with some version of *"you can't prove your comms work."* That is the exact ground Cerkl's *"delivery + measurement layer"* positioning owns — but competitors are claiming it with stat-led marketing while their own G2 reviews show none deliver per-employee read attribution. **Staffbase reversed both of its W24 retreats**: it fully recovered its −4 SERP collapse (3 → 6 P1 slots, now tied with ContactMonkey for the lead) *and* re-promoted the shadow-AI *"79%/69%"* stat back into the homepage hero. **Pricing gated harder, not softer** — ContactMonkey (was tiered-public) and Workvivo (was per-employee public) both pulled pricing back to *"Request pricing,"* and 0/10 still offer a free-forever tier (week 7) — Foundations' moat strengthens as peers retreat behind quote-walls. **Workvivo repositioned its hero** to *"One digital headquarters for every employee"* (a destination/consolidation frame). And the *"execution problem"* CEO frame stayed **stalled at 3 for a 5th straight week** — Cerkl's first-mover window is now effectively a ship-or-kill decision.

## Pain-point cross-cut

> The buyer problems competitors are leading with this week, derived across positioning + ads + content. Most actionable section for Cerkl's marketing.

### Pain point 1: "You can't prove your comms work" — measurement/outcomes went DOMINANT (3 → 6 of 10)

- **Who's claiming it (W25):** Firstup, AxiosHQ, ContactMonkey, Simpplr, Poppulo, Workshop — up from just Firstup/AxiosHQ/ContactMonkey in W24.
- **How they're framing it (verbatim):**
  - Firstup: *"Don't just communicate. Get outcomes."* + *"Employee turnover costs U.S. businesses over $1 trillion each year"* ([firstup.io](https://firstup.io/))
  - AxiosHQ: *"92% say Smart Brevity streamlined internal processes / 90% improved staff engagement / 82% made feedback faster"* ([axioshq.com](https://www.axioshq.com/))
  - ContactMonkey: *"Eliminate guesswork and demonstrate clear internal communications impact"* ([contactmonkey.com](https://www.contactmonkey.com/))
  - Simpplr: *"282% Return on investment"* ([simpplr.com](https://www.simpplr.com/)); Poppulo: *"analytics to derive really important insights"* ([poppulo.com](https://www.poppulo.com/))
- **Cerkl's current angle:** Cerkl's core positioning IS measurement (*"delivery + measurement layer… delivered, opened, read, by employee"* per `shared/competitors.md`). This is home turf — but Cerkl is not currently *fighting* for the measurement hero-word; competitors are now planting flags on it with borrowed stats.
- **Implication: This is the week's most important signal — and it cuts both ways.** The category is validating Cerkl's thesis (good: the buyer now expects measurement). But six competitors crowding onto a measurement message means Cerkl can no longer assume it owns the word by default. **Counter-move: out-specify them.** Their claims are aggregate ROI/percentage stats (282%, 92%, $1T); none deliver *employee-level read attribution*, and their own reviews complain about exactly the customization/analytics ceilings that undercut the claim. Cerkl's wedge sharpens to: *"Everyone advertises measurement. We deliver it per employee — delivered, opened, read, by name. Ask the others for that and watch the demo end."*

### Pain point 2: "Shadow AI / tool sprawl" — Staffbase rejoined the hook; now 2-of-10 hero (reversing W24)

- **Who's claiming it:** **Staffbase re-promoted the *"79% suspect employees misusing approved AI tools; 69% suspect prohibited public GenAI"* stat back into hero creative** (W24 had softened it out). LumApps holds *"Cut shadow IT, reduce complexity"* ([lumapps.com](https://www.lumapps.com/)). So the lone-voice status of LumApps in W24 is over — 2 vendors lead with the AI-misuse fear again.
- **Cerkl's current angle:** Still none. The W23/W24 recommendation (counter-positioning POV: *"the sanctioned-AI promise dies the moment employees don't read the message"*) has not shipped.
- **Implication:** The frame is oscillating, not dying — Staffbase pulled it in W24 and re-added it in W25, suggesting active A/B testing rather than conviction. **Don't chase the oscillation.** The durable version for Cerkl folds shadow-AI into Pain Point 1: governance/sanctioned-AI is meaningless without proof the message landed. Ship the POV tied to measurement, not to the AI-fear hook.

### Pain point 3: "Fragmentation / disconnected channels" — steady, now wearing a "digital HQ" costume

- **Who's claiming it:** Simpplr, LumApps, Firstup, Poppulo, and now **Workvivo's repositioned hero** *"One digital headquarters for every employee… One place where people show up, communication happens, and knowledge lives"* ([workvivo.com](https://www.workvivo.com/)).
- **Cerkl's current angle:** Cerkl's anti-destination frame is the direct counter (*"We meet employees in the inbox they already have — the delivery and measurement layer, not the destination"* per `shared/competitors.md`).
- **Implication:** **Counter-position, don't follow.** Workvivo's "digital HQ" / Poppulo's "one platform" / LumApps' "AI Employee Hub" are all the *destination* play — asking employees to come to a new place. Cerkl's whole differentiation is the inverse. The more competitors converge on "come to our hub," the cleaner Cerkl's *"we come to them"* contrast reads. This is a gift, not a threat.

## Ideas to steal

- **Out-specify the measurement crowd with the one proof they can't show.** Six vendors now advertise measurement with aggregate stats (282% ROI, 92%, $1T). None delivers per-employee read attribution, and their G2 reviews complain about analytics/customization ceilings. ([axioshq.com](https://www.axioshq.com/), [firstup.io](https://firstup.io/))
  - *How Cerkl might use this:* Ship a measurement-hero LP variant whose proof point is a *per-employee* read-receipt visual — *"Not '282% ROI.' This: Sarah in Ops opened it. Marcus on the floor didn't — yet."* Let the specificity expose the competitors' aggregate hand-waving. This is the highest-leverage idea on the board this week.

- **Pricing transparency as an active wedge while competitors gate harder.** Two vendors retreated behind "Request pricing" this week; 0/10 are free-forever. ([contactmonkey.com/pricing](https://www.contactmonkey.com/pricing))
  - *How Cerkl might use this:* Lead a Foundations campaign with the transparency contrast directly — *"Free. Public pricing. Live this afternoon. No quote required."* The category is moving the opposite direction; make the gap loud.

- **Defensive "what you'd be giving up" conquest framing (ContactMonkey playbook).** ([contactmonkey.com/blog](https://www.contactmonkey.com/blog))
  - *How Cerkl might use this:* Pair the long-recommended *"Cerkl vs. the alternatives — when to pick what"* defensive page with a *"What you'd give up leaving Foundations"* retention asset for free-tier users — owns the SERP AND reduces churn at the upgrade boundary.

- **G2 category-badge as hero material (Workshop now Spring + Summer 2026).** Workshop repeated its #1 Internal Newsletter win into the G2 Summer 2026 reports — two consecutive seasons of badge ammunition. ([useworkshop.com press](https://useworkshop.com/press/workshop-named-best-for-internal-and-frontline-communications-in-g2-summer-reports-2026/))
  - *How Cerkl might use this:* The W24 G2-audit recommendation is now urgent — Workshop will press this badge in every Foundations head-to-head. Identify the 1–2 G2 categories Cerkl can win for Fall 2026 and run an in-product review-velocity push before the Q3 Foundations window.

- **Customization/template-ceiling complaint is near-universal (8 of 10 vendors).** Confirmed again this week across Staffbase, Simpplr, LumApps, Firstup, Poppulo, AxiosHQ, Haiilo, ContactMonkey. Workshop is the lone holdout.
  - *How Cerkl might use this:* Durable wedge — *"You don't need to redesign your channels. Cerkl personalizes the message inside the channels you already have."* One-screen LP with honestly-attributed G2 complaint fragments as the backdrop.
