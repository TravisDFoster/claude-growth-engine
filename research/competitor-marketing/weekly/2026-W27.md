## This week in one paragraph

After a month of near-weekly hero churn, the **positioning layer went quiet** — zero H1 swaps among reachable vendors, and Staffbase held its "one platform" H1 for a second straight week (the A/B testing looks settled). The action moved to the **content/proof layer, and the axis rotated: frontline/deskless is the new gated-research battleground.** Firstup dropped a brand-new report — *"Communications crisis in manufacturing: what 1,000 frontline workers revealed"* (06-30) — and Workvivo continues featuring its *"Frontline AI Gap"* report, shifting the substantiation contest away from generic "prove IC value" toward "reach the deskless worker." That maps straight onto Cerkl's Omni AI ICP and is corroborated by this week's daily-trend signal (two deskless case studies: Sav-A-Tree, Goodwill). Meanwhile the **measurement hook grew again to 8/10 ad creative** (now the category default), **shadow-AI fear vanished from the hero layer** (Staffbase's 79%/69% stat dropped off), and **Staffbase turned conquester** — surging to a 7/10 SERP-presence tie with ContactMonkey and shipping "Simpplr Alternatives" + "ContactMonkey Alternatives" pages. Free-forever holds at 0/10 (week 9), and no new mid-market (2K–10K) logo shipped anywhere this window — the contested zone is quiet and open.

## Pain-point cross-cut

> The buyer problems competitors are leading with this week, derived across positioning + ads + content. Most actionable section for Cerkl's marketing.

### Pain point 1: "Your frontline/deskless workers never get the message" — NEW gated-research axis (the week's key move)

- **Who's claiming it (W27):** Firstup (new 1,000-frontline-worker survey, [firstup.io/blog](https://firstup.io/blog)), Workvivo (*"Frontline AI Gap"* report, [ai.workvivo.com]), plus the persistent frontline hero framing at Staffbase, LumApps, ContactMonkey (*"40–50% of deskless workforce now reached"*).
- **How they're framing it (verbatim):**
  - Firstup: *"Communications crisis in manufacturing: what 1,000 frontline workers revealed"* (06-30) — reframes the buyer pain from "IC is unmeasured" to "IC never arrives at the frontline."
  - ContactMonkey ad/LP: *"40–50% of deskless workforce now reached."*
  - Staffbase: *"From the frontline to the office… across every channel."*
- **Cerkl's current angle:** Frontline/deskless reach is core to the Omni AI ICP (distributed, frontline, deskless workforces) but Cerkl has **no gated frontline research asset** and its frontline story is a feature claim, not a substantiated one.
- **Implication: Lean in — this is Cerkl's ICP and the competition just made it a content axis.** The gated-research contest is rotating from "prove IC value" (W26) to "prove frontline reach" (W27). Cerkl's per-employee read attribution answers *"did the frontline actually get it AND read it?"* — a sharper claim than "reached." Pair the long-recommended benchmark report with a frontline cut (read rates for deskless vs. desk-based).

### Pain point 2: "You can't prove your comms work" — measurement now the category default (8/10)

- **Who's claiming it:** Ad/LP creative 8/10 (up from 7) — Firstup (*398% ROI / $8.9M*), Simpplr (*282% ROI*), AxiosHQ (*92/90/82*), ContactMonkey (*40–50% reached*), LumApps (*deep analytics prove what's working*), Workshop (*create, send & measure*), Poppulo (*analytics*), Haiilo. Only Staffbase + Workvivo lead with reach over a number.
- **How they're framing it:** Same aggregate-stat substantiation as W26 — no vendor has moved to employee-level proof.
- **Cerkl's current angle:** Cerkl's *"delivered, opened, read, by employee"* is the real version. The gap remains the *proof asset*, not the capability.
- **Implication: Hold the W26 plan — the measurement message keeps commoditizing at the claim level while the employee-level substantiation lane stays empty.** The benchmark-report idea only gets more valuable as the hook approaches 10/10.

### Pain point 3: "AI, but built-in / done right" — POV frame replacing the shadow-AI fear hook

- **Who's claiming it:** Staffbase (*built-in vs. bolted-on AI* buyer's guide, [staffbase.com/blog/ai-powered-employee-experience-platform]), Poppulo (IT-investment→outcome POV). Meanwhile the shadow-AI *fear* hook (Staffbase 79%/69%) **dropped off the hero entirely** this week.
- **Cerkl's current angle:** None explicit. The "trust/governance over AI hype" lane (flagged W26) is still unwritten.
- **Implication: The AI conversation is maturing from fear ("shadow AI!") to architecture ("is your AI real?").** Cerkl's honest entry stays *"AI can write the message; it can't make employees read it — or prove they did."* Ship the POV; the fear-hook fade means the lane is quieter and cleaner than a month ago.
