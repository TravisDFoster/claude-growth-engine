Cerkl's current angle
Cerkl has no shadow-AI positioning today. Omni AI is sold on personalization + reach, not on trust/governance.

One short POV post + a homepage stat-card variant; not a campaign.

4 · StealIdeas to steal

3–5 specific tactics, formats, or angles a competitor is doing well that Cerkl could adapt. Each tagged with how Cerkl might use it.

Stat-led ad headlines (Staffbase + AxiosHQ + Firstup playbook)

Staffbase's "79% of organizations suspect employees are misusing approved AI tools" — ad copy as a research-stat reveal. AxiosHQ's "92% / 90% / 82%" triple-stat hero. Firstup's "75% miss critical updates" report-anchored creative.

How Cerkl might use this Cerkl owns read-rate data on the platform every day. Lead one Foundations LP variant with a single one-screen stat: e.g., "4 in 5 employee emails go unread. Cerkl knows because we measure every one." Pair it with a 30-second "what 'read' actually means" tooltip explainer. Same Foundations LP, same offer, different hero — A/B against the current generic-platform creative.

Counter-positioned shadow-AI POV

Cerkl has no answer to the W23 shadow-AI pitch — and the IC-team buyer doesn't care about IT-governance, so the right play isn't a Cerkl "AI Control Center."

How Cerkl might use this One short POV post (~600 words) — "Shadow AI is a content problem dressed as a governance problem. The real fix isn't blocking the tools — it's making your sanctioned channel so much better nobody needs to invent one." Publish under a Cerkl exec byline. Tie to Foundations as the "live-in-an-afternoon" demonstration that the sanctioned-channel argument is real, not aspirational. Should ship within 14 days while Staffbase/Simpplr/LumApps are spending impressions on the pain.

Tit-for-tat conquest listicle preparedness

Sociabble vs. ContactMonkey this week, plus ContactMonkey's now-confirmed monthly cadence (Workshop / PoliteMail / Poppulo / Staffbase = 4 in 14 days), means somebody is going to publish "Best Cerkl Alternatives 2026" eventually. Defense first.

How Cerkl might use this (1) Run a SERP probe for "Cerkl alternatives 2026" weekly going forward — flag the moment one publishes. (2) Own the Cerkl-alternatives SERP defensively: publish "Cerkl vs. the alternatives — when to pick what" on the Cerkl blog now, with honest "if you want X, pick Y" sections. Owning your own conquest SERP is cheaper than reclaiming it after a competitor takes it. (3) Offensively: pick the two cheapest wins from shared/competitors.md — Best Workshop Alternatives 2026 (most direct Foundations head-to-head) and Best Axios HQ Alternatives 2026 (cheapest competitive SEO win on the list). 4-week sprint.

Sustained-program signal: one wedge → 3+ assets across 14 days (Simpplr's AI-governance playbook)

Simpplr did this exact thing — AI Control Center launch (5/22) → "manager enablement for AI" (5/28) → "stop shadow AI" (6/3). Three different angles on one wedge, three weeks in a row. That's how a wedge stops reading as a launch and starts reading as a category position.

How Cerkl might use this Pick ONE wedge for the next 3-week sprint. Suggested wedge: "AI writes; Cerkl delivers and proves." Week 1: exec POV post (the "execution problem, sharper variant"). Week 2: a stat-led customer micro-case (1 logo, 1 number, 1 chart — read-rate before/after). Week 3: a Foundations onboarding-flow video demonstrating the claim. No new gated assets needed — the sprint is the asset.

AxiosHQ June 23 "Smart Brevity Open House" event format

Not a webinar, not a demo — an open community event as a marketing surface, anchored on training methodology. Smart Brevity isn't a product feature, it's a content meme they're scaling via training.

How Cerkl might use this Lower-priority idea, but worth watching: the open-community format scales engagement with limited product touch. Cerkl's equivalent would be a "Read-rate clinic" — bring your worst-performing internal email, Cerkl team + a guest IC lead diagnoses live what's wrong and what to test. 60-min Zoom, monthly, recorded. Test once in Q3 before committing.
