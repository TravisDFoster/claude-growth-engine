Competitor Marketing Digest — Week of 2026-06-17

Week: 2026-W25 Captured: 2026-06-17 (Wed) Baseline: 2026-W24 (2026-06-10) Vendors tracked: 10

Cerkl's current angle

Cerkl's core positioning IS measurement ("delivery + measurement layer… delivered, opened, read, by employee" per shared/competitors.md). Home turf — but Cerkl is not currently fighting for the measurement hero-word; competitors are now planting flags on it with borrowed stats.

Implication — the week's most important signal, cuts both ways

The category is validating Cerkl's thesis (the buyer now expects measurement), but six competitors crowding the message means Cerkl can no longer assume it owns the word by default. Counter-move: out-specify them. Their claims are aggregate ROI/percentage stats (282%, 92%, $1T); none deliver employee-level read attribution. Cerkl's wedge sharpens to: "Everyone advertises measurement. We deliver it per employee — delivered, opened, read, by name. Ask the others for that and watch the demo end."

04Ideas to steal

Out-specify the measurement crowd with the one proof they can't show.

Six vendors now advertise measurement with aggregate stats (282% ROI, 92%, $1T). None delivers per-employee read attribution, and their G2 reviews complain about analytics/customization ceilings. (axioshq.com, firstup.io)

How Cerkl might use this: Ship a measurement-hero LP variant whose proof point is a per-employee read-receipt visual — "Not '282% ROI.' This: Sarah in Ops opened it. Marcus on the floor didn't — yet." The highest-leverage idea on the board this week.

Pricing transparency as an active wedge while competitors gate harder.

Two vendors retreated behind "Request pricing" this week; 0/10 are free-forever. (contactmonkey.com/pricing)

How Cerkl might use this: Lead a Foundations campaign with the transparency contrast directly — "Free. Public pricing. Live this afternoon. No quote required." The category is moving the opposite direction; make the gap loud.
