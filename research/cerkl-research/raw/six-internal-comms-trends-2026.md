# Six internal comms trends every communicator should know in 2026

**Source URL:** https://www.prdaily.com/six-internal-comms-trends-every-communicator-should-know-in-2026/
**Publisher:** PR Daily (presented by Workshop)
**Author:** Mayela Hernandez (Marketing & Events Specialist, Workshop)

**Fetched:** 2026-05-11

## The Six Trends

### 1. Managers are still the biggest priority (and the biggest gap)

- **56%** say manager communication is their top focus for 2026
- **Only 4%** believe managers are very effective at cascading messages
- Insight: employees trust managers more than other sources
- Recommended solutions: toolkits, leader digests, clearer cascade expectations

### 2. AI has moved from experiment to everyday

- **42%** of communicators use AI every day
- Another **31%** use it several times a week
- Use cases: content creation, summarizing employee feedback, supporting planning, adapting content across channels
- Framing: AI helps communicators move faster on repetitive work rather than replacing them

### 3. Email isn't going anywhere

- **81%** of communicators named email their most effective channel
- vs in-person events: **35%**; intranets: **31%**
- Advantages: reaches everyone, clear data, consistency, predictability

### 4. 'Do more with less' is finally getting some pushback

- **Only 44%** of communicators say they have the resources they need to execute their 2026 strategy
- Response strategies: intake forms, clearer timelines, reusable templates, boundary-setting
- Framing: protecting team time enables consistent, high-quality communication

### 5. Intranets are hard, but teams aren't giving up

- **29%** of communicators say intranet is the hardest channel to manage
- More than half are still prioritizing improvements this year
- Strategy shift: focusing on evergreen content (policies, FAQs, how-to guides) rather than attempting comprehensive functionality

### 6. Employee influencers are gaining real traction

- **41%** of communicators say this is an area they're actively investing in
- Approach: identifying natural influencers rather than creating formal ambassador programs
