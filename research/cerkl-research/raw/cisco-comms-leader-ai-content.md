# Cisco comms leader on the real problem with AI-generated content

**Source URL:** https://www.prdaily.com/cisco-comms-leader-on-the-real-problem-with-ai-generated-content/
**Publisher:** PR Daily
**Author:** Isis Simpson-Mersha

## Interviewee

**Austin Roth-Eagle** — Head of AI Acceleration Office at Cisco; formerly CEO communications strategist at Cisco and VMware.

## Core Thesis

The "real problem" with AI-generated content is that it lacks **taste and voice**. AI excels at efficiency and workflow optimization, but produces "AI slop" without human judgment, unique perspective, and authentic voice guiding the process at every stage — not just at publication review.

## Key Direct Quotes — Austin Roth-Eagle

> "If you have an idea for content and need to get from point A to point B, a well-built workflow broken into small steps… can produce exactly the output I'm looking for."

> "The biggest thing AI is still missing is taste… taste, judgment and voice are still critical."

> "AI-generated content without voice or taste is bad… Your voice is the most important element."

> "It's less about a checklist at the end and more about breaking the workflow into small, specific steps."

> "The best results come from people with deep domain expertise and strong AI fluency."

## Recommendations

- Break workflows into small, specific steps rather than relying on end-stage human review
- Implement human oversight **at every step**, not just final approval
- Combine deep domain expertise with AI fluency
- Use AI as a formatting/amplification tool for pre-existing ideas and outlines, not as a voice replacement
- Develop a sense of what good, mediocre, and bad content looks like (taste development)
