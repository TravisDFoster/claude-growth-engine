# How leaders can communicate AI-related changes as routine for employees

**Publication:** Ragan Communications
**Author:** Sean Devlin
**Date:** October 9, 2025
**URL:** https://www.ragan.com/ai-hr-comms-gartner-2026/

---

## Article Body

As 2026 approaches, internal communications and HR professionals should prepare for AI to dominate organizational priorities. Gartner recently highlighted AI as a top concern for CHROs, with insights that directly apply to communicators across functions.

Mark Whittle, vice president of advisory at Gartner, emphasized that while uncertainties exist, "these uncertainties and stresses create opportunities for us like never before."

### Rethinking Change Messaging in Leadership

HR and communications professionals must help executives lead discussions around AI transformation. Whittle drew parallels to the 2008-09 financial crisis, noting that leaders who failed to communicate left employees anxious about their futures.

"Effective leadership today is change leadership," Whittle stated. He recommended a three-step process for managing change:

- **Acknowledge the journey:** Recognize constant change as normal and update employees regularly
- **Regulate discomfort:** Use transparent communication to address employee concerns about transformation
- **Train intuition:** Build organizational reflexes through comprehensive change communications that help employees see themselves in the narrative

Leaders should discuss how specific teams can use automation to improve processes. "When change intensity spikes, so do the expectations of our leaders," Whittle noted.

### AI's Growing Role in Employee Experience

Generative AI will expand beyond current applications like chatbots to reshape HR functions and daily work experiences. Whittle emphasized that organizations must intentionally embed company culture into AI implementations, connecting automation to organizational mission and values.

"Whatever set of cultural attributes you're using to drive performance, employees need to understand the organization's culture," he explained.

### Addressing Job Replacement Concerns

Some organizations are using AI to reduce headcount. Communicators should prepare talking points for managers explaining how roles will evolve. One-pagers and FAQs can help reduce employee anxiety by showing day-to-day job changes.

Gartner's position holds that AI will create more net jobs than it eliminates, though rapid advances—"Things that weren't possible even 90 days ago are now happening with AI"—will reshape work fundamentally.

When layoffs occur, internal communicators should communicate transparently within company values, emphasizing who is affected and the organization's direction. Such messages significantly impact employer brand perception during major transitions.
