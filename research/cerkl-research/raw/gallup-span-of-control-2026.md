# Span of Control: What's the Optimal Team Size for Managers?

**Published:** January 14, 2026
**Author:** Jim Harter
**Contributors:** Sangeeta Agrawal, Mary Page James, Sabrina Yu
**URL:** https://www.gallup.com/workplace/700718/span-control-optimal-team-size-managers.aspx

---

## Introduction

Organizations are restructuring management hierarchies by expanding team sizes and reducing middle management layers. This "Great Flattening" trend reflects efforts to lower costs and accelerate decision-making, but fundamentally alters what effective leadership requires.

---

## Average Span of Control in the U.S. Is Growing

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, approximately one manager exists for every 11.5 employees. Gallup data reveal:

- **2013:** 10.9 direct reports per manager
- **2024:** 10.9 direct reports per manager
- **2025:** 12.1 direct reports per manager

This represents nearly a 50% increase since 2013, largely driven by a two-percentage-point increase in teams with 25+ employees.

> ⚠️ **Internal inconsistency in source.** The 10.9 → 10.9 → 12.1 time-series does not support a "+50% increase since 2013" headline. Most likely the +50% refers to the share of teams with 25+ employees, not the average span itself. Flagged here for re-source before any published citation.

**Distribution of current team sizes:**
- 37% of managers oversee fewer than 5 people
- 66% manage fewer than 10 employees
- 22% have 10-24 direct reports
- 13% oversee 25+ employees

Despite the rising average, the median team size remains steady at approximately five to six employees per manager.

---

## Four Questions That Shape the Right Span of Control

### 1. How Engaged Is the Team?

Gallup conducted a global meta-analysis examining 92,252 teams across 104 organizations and 26 industries in 46 countries, studying the relationship between employee engagement and three outcomes: productivity, turnover, and wellbeing.

**Team size categories:** small (4–9), medium (10–19), large (20+).

For small and medium-sized teams, higher engagement correlates with increased productivity, improved wellbeing, and lower turnover. For large teams, the relationship varies significantly by industry. In services, transportation, and healthcare sectors, large highly-engaged teams demonstrate greater productivity than lower-engagement counterparts.

> "Highly engaged teams of 12 or more workers who are supported by effective management — double the current median of six workers per team — can thrive, while poorly managed teams struggle."

### 2. How Much Time Do Managers Spend on Individual Contributor Work?

- 97% of U.S. managers report some individual contributor responsibilities
- Median time spent on non-managerial work: 40%
- Managers exceeding this threshold tend to lead smaller teams
- Managers spending less than 40% on IC work maintain higher engagement (37%) regardless of direct reports
- Those exceeding 40% show lower engagement, worsening with larger teams
- Overall manager engagement has declined compared to previous years

### 3. Do Managers Have the Right Talent for the Role?

Five key management traits predict higher team engagement and performance: motivation, workstyle, initiation, collaboration, thought process.

**Manager talent effects:** Highly talented managers maintain higher engagement when managing very large teams (25+) with heavy IC loads (>40%). Managers with medium or low talent show declining engagement as team size and workload increase.

**Work location considerations:** Remote and hybrid managers face engagement challenges as team size grows, regardless of talent level. On-site managers show minimal engagement decline with larger teams.

> "Manager talent trumps span of control. When organizations use scientific methods to identify the right talent in managers, those managers can successfully lead larger teams and stay engaged."

### 4. Do Managers Give Employees Meaningful, Regular Feedback?

Gallup research identified one critical habit: providing meaningful feedback to each employee at least weekly. **This practice nearly triples the percentage of engaged employees.**

**Current state:** Only 16% of employees reported their last manager conversation was extremely meaningful.

**Meaningful feedback includes:** recognition, discussion of collaboration, review of goals/priorities, focus on strengths; 15–30 minutes done consistently.

**Engagement outcomes across seven studies (44,025 responses):** Employees were highly engaged (~70%) regardless of team size when they strongly agreed they received meaningful weekly feedback. When they disagreed or were neutral, only 25% were engaged.

> "In today's workplace, where employees feel increasingly disconnected from their organizations, managers' open communication with employees is more important than the number of direct reports."

---

## How Many Direct Reports Should a Manager Have?

Three core findings: (1) most managers remain responsible for small teams, (2) the median span of control stays at six employees, (3) larger teams become more common and viable only when managers possess appropriate talent and organizational support.

> "Increasing span of control alone is not a performance strategy. Larger teams succeed only when organizations invest in the conditions that allow managers to lead them well."

---

## Five Reflection Questions Before Flattening Further

1. Distribution awareness — do we understand team-size distribution across the org?
2. Talent identification — which managers can lead larger teams?
3. Engagement measurement — at team level, including risk-tracking as spans widen?
4. Player-coach optimization — IC work over 40% limits coaching capacity.
5. Feedback culture — is weekly meaningful feedback an expected practice?

---

## Recommended Actions

- Identify managers with talent for larger teams using validated tools
- Measure engagement at team level
- Establish weekly meaningful feedback as standard

## Survey Methodology

### Individual Employee Analysis
- **Sample:** 44,025 U.S. adults, ages 18+, working full or part-time
- **Period:** 2022–2024
- **Sampling error:** ±0.7 pp (95% CI)
- **Method:** Self-administered web surveys via Gallup Panel (probability-based)
- **Adjustments:** Weighted to match CPS demographics

### Manager Analysis
- **Sample:** 16,442 U.S. managers and leaders, ages 18+
- **Period:** 2022–2024
- **Sampling error:** ±1.1 pp (95% CI)
- **Historical data points:** 2013: n=2,402 | 2019: n=2,933 | 2022: n=7,199 | 2023: n=3,971 | 2024: n=4,355 | 2025: n=8,795
- **Error ranges for mean estimates:** ±0.48 to ±0.78

### Meta-Analysis of Teams
- **Studies reviewed:** 312 research studies
- **Organizations:** 104 | **Industries:** 26 | **Countries:** 46
- **Teams analyzed:** 92,252 | **Total employees:** 897,971
- **Outcomes measured:** Turnover, wellbeing, productivity
- **Methodology:** Hunter-Schmidt meta-analysis methods
