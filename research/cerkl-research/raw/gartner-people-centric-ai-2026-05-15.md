# IC Deep Dive — Gartner: 50% of Enterprises Without a People-Centric AI Strategy Will Lose Their Top AI Talent by 2027

**Date:** 2026-05-15   |   **Category:** Analyst report

---

## TL;DR
Gartner predicts that by 2027, half of enterprises that fail to build a people-centric AI strategy will lose their top AI talent — and names CHROs (in partnership with CIOs) as owners of governance, decision rights, and tool UX. The data underneath the prediction is the IC-relevant part: 19% of employees report *zero* time saved from AI, 88% with enterprise AI access also use personal AI tools, and the gap between "rollout" and "transformation" — what Gartner calls the **enablement illusion** — is the problem. Analyst-grade validation for Cerkl's "delivery + measurement layer" pitch: the issue isn't AI capability, it's that orgs can't get the right message about AI to the right employee, with read-through proof and feedback.

## What it is

A Gartner press release (2026-05-13) tied to the **Gartner Global Labor Market Survey 1Q26** (12,004 employees and managers across 40 countries) plus a December 2025 survey of 197 CxOs. Authored as commentary by **Swagatam Basu** (Senior Director Analyst, Gartner HR practice) and **Diana Sanchez** (Senior Director Analyst, Gartner HR practice). The release frames a single bold prediction — *by 2027, 50% of enterprises without a people-centric AI strategy will lose their top AI talent to competitors prioritizing workforce enablement* — and unpacks four workforce dynamics CHROs and CIOs must address together.

## What it does / claims

The release lands the headline prediction and then names four dynamics that determine whether an enterprise is on the wrong side of the 50%:

1. **Move beyond time-saved metrics.** Implement a "True ROI Index" tracking depth and diversity of AI use; establish a central repository for AI use cases. Time-saved is the basic adoption metric leaders are currently mistaking for transformation.
2. **Shadow AI and data governance.** CIOs and CHROs must partner to **audit and improve the user experience of enterprise AI tools** to reduce shadow AI, protect data, and retain talent. CHROs should clarify AI governance and decision rights, with HR represented in governance bodies.
3. **Close the leadership gap.** 73% of highly productive AI users are managers or executives — front-line and individual contributors are underserved. Provide targeted tools and training; empower managers to integrate AI into daily workflows and encourage experimentation.
4. **Build psychological safety.** Transparent communication about job evolution; explicit human-AI collaboration norms; regular trust pulse surveys monitoring workforce sentiment.

The verbatim core quote (Basu): *"The survey revealed that in the shift to an AI-powered workforce, most leaders are mistaking basic access or adoption metrics for transformation. This 'enablement illusion' is hiding risks and draining ROI."*

And on shadow AI (Sanchez): *"Eighty-eight percent of employees with enterprise AI access also use personal AI tools for business tasks, often to save time. While hybrid AI users are 1.7 times more likely to report significant time saved over those using only enterprise solutions, this behavior increases corporate data risk and also drives attrition risks with critical talent."*

The numbers that matter:

- **19%** of employees report *no time saved* from AI (1Q26)
- **88%** of employees with enterprise AI access also use **personal AI tools** for business tasks — i.e., shadow AI is the norm, not the edge case
- **73%** of highly productive AI users are managers or executives — the front line is underserved
- **27%** of executives have a comprehensive AI strategy (Dec 2025, n=197 CxOs)
- **20%** of leaders believe their workforce is truly AI-ready
- Employees with a positive AI outlook are **3.4×** more likely to be highly productive
- Proficient multi-use-case AI employees: **2×** more productive, **2.3×** more likely to deliver quality work, **3.2×** more likely to drive process improvements

## Who it's for

- **Primary audience:** CHROs, CIOs, Chief AI Officers, CEOs at enterprises with formal AI initiatives. Gartner is explicitly putting CHROs in the seat alongside CIOs — historically a Gartner CIO-first frame.
- **Strong fit when:** the org has rolled out AI tools (Copilot, ChatGPT Enterprise, Glean, etc.) but can't show ROI; leadership is debating who owns AI governance; HR is on the outside of an IT-led AI program; "shadow AI" has been flagged by security or legal.
- **Weak fit when:** the org hasn't yet rolled out AI tools (the dynamics described don't exist yet), or the org is a pre-product AI-native startup where the labor distinctions don't map.

## Gotchas

- **The prediction is squishy by design.** Gartner predictions ending in "by 2027, 50% of enterprises…" are forecast-language. Don't cite it as a fact — cite it as analyst guidance backed by survey data. The defensible numbers are the 1Q26 survey figures (19%, 88%, 73%, 3.4×), not the prediction itself.
- **The press release is behind a 403 for direct fetch.** Gartner is blocking automated access to its newsroom URLs. Cite the three independent re-publishers (IT-Online, AppleWorld Today, ITPro) when sourcing — they each independently lift the same Basu/Sanchez quotes and stats, which is corroboration enough.
- **"People-centric AI strategy" is a Gartner-coined frame.** It will become a buzzword. Cerkl shouldn't adopt the exact phrase — let prospects use it; instead, translate it operationally: "rollout comms, decision rights, and tool UX." That maps to Cerkl's actual product surface.
- **Shadow AI as a *comms* problem is contested.** Most security and IT trade press treats shadow AI as a tooling/governance problem (block ChatGPT, deploy enterprise AI, enforce DLP). Gartner is reframing it as primarily a UX-and-communication problem. That reframe is the IC-relevant insight; it's also the part most likely to be misunderstood by IT buyers. Be explicit when citing.
