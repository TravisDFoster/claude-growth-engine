# AI Adoption Gap

## Definition

The observed gap between IC / intranet vendor *investment* in AI features (search, agents, content generation, governance, admin tooling) and actual *adoption* of those features by customers. As of 2026, vendors are heavily marketing AI but only roughly half of reference customers report using it.

## Why It Matters

The AI adoption gap is a 2026-current critique that complicates the "AI is transforming IC" narrative dominating vendor marketing. It's analyst-tier evidence ([[forrester]] reference-customer interviews) that customer behavior lags the vendor pitch deck. For IC blog writing: this is a useful counter-frame to vendor-hype content — backed by a primary-research analyst source.

## How It Works

In [[forrester]]'s Q2 2026 Intranet Platforms Wave reference-customer interviews ([[forrester-wave-intranet-platforms-q2-2026]]):

- **35 customers interviewed** across the 13 evaluated vendors.
- **17 of 35 (~49%)** are actively using their vendor's AI features.
- Despite "significant investment in these areas by vendors since our 2024 Wave" ([[cheryl-mckinnon]]).

The gap reflects some combination of: AI features that don't yet earn their place in IC workflows, customer-side change-management lag, IT/security gating, and feature parity with existing tools customers already use (Copilot, ChatGPT Enterprise).
