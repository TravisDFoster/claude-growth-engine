# Measurers vs. Builders

## Definition
[[matthew-prince]]'s ([[cloudflare]]) public framing that AI makes "measurers" obsolete while "builders and sellers" remain essential. In his 2026 WSJ op-ed justifying layoffs made "despite record growth," Prince placed builders/sellers at the top of a value hierarchy and explicitly named marketing and finance — "teeming with measurers" — as the functions AI replaces.

## Why It Matters
This is the sharpest *public, CEO-level* attack on the measurement discipline in the 2026 cycle — and measurement is the spine of IC's value case ([[ic-measurement-and-roi]]) and Cerkl's positioning ("delivery + measurement layer"). If "measurer" becomes a slur for automatable overhead, every IC team that justifies itself with open/read analytics is rhetorically exposed. It's a counter-narrative to neutralize, not ignore.

## How It Works
The framing collapses two different things under one word:
- **"Measurer" as bureaucratic overhead** — people who *report on* work without doing it (the strawman Prince is cutting).
- **Measurement as decision infrastructure** — knowing whether a message reached and landed with the right employees (what IC measurement actually is).

The Cerkl counter is to refuse the conflation: measurement that *changes the next send* (segmented read-through, retargeting, did-it-land receipts) is builder-work, not measurer-overhead. The dashboard that nobody acts on is the measurer Prince is right to cut; the delivery-and-measurement loop that improves outcomes is not.
