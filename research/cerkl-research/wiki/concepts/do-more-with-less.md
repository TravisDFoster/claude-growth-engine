# Do More With Less

## Definition

The persistent IC-team operating assumption that headcount, budget, and tooling will not grow proportionally with the scope of comms work expected. The 2026 development noted in Workshop's trends data ([[six-internal-comms-trends-2026]]): IC teams are *pushing back* on this assumption rather than absorbing it silently.

## Why It Matters

"Do more with less" is the single most-shared IC-practitioner complaint and the implicit framing of most IC-platform vendor marketing ("automate, scale, reach more with less effort"). The 2026 shift — that only 44% of communicators say they have the resources they need, and that teams are *responding* with process changes — is a more interesting story than the perennial complaint itself. For blog writing: pair the 44% stat with named tactics (intake forms, templates, boundary-setting) for a piece that goes beyond "IC teams are stretched."

## How It Works

The Workshop framing of the 2026 pushback ([[six-internal-comms-trends-2026]]):

- **Intake forms** to throttle ad-hoc requests
- **Clearer timelines** to set expectations
- **Reusable templates** to reduce repeated effort
- **Boundary-setting** with internal stakeholders

The implicit thesis: protecting team time enables consistent, high-quality communication. The alternative — absorbing every request and degrading output quality — is no longer being treated as professional.
