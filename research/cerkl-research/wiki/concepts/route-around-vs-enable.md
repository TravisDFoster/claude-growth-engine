# Route-Around vs. Enable

## Definition

The vendor-positioning split in 2026 IC: **route-around managers** (deliver high-stakes messages direct to employees and use cross-channel measurement to verify landing) vs. **enable managers** (give managers AI-augmented tools, role-targeted content, and feedback loops to make the cascade work). The two stances make different assumptions about whether the [[manager-cascade]] is fixable via tooling or structurally broken at the role-design level.

## Why It Matters

The manager-cascade is the [[manager-cascade]] topic's load-bearing failure mode: 56% of communicators name it their #1 priority; only 4% rate managers "very effective" at cascading. Vendors are converging on two opposite responses, and the choice has direct implications for Cerkl's positioning. Cerkl is structurally on the **route-around** side (personalized cross-channel delivery + measurement), but the route-around lane is crowded — and a one-sided stance leaves the [[manager-burnout-structural-thesis]] (Karian, June 2026) underleveraged. **The strongest Cerkl position holds both: enable where managers can, route around where they can't, with the same delivery measurement layer on both paths.**

## How It Works

### The route-around stance

Premise: managers are bandwidth-constrained ([[gallup-span-of-control-2026]] + [[gartner-managers-working-harder-2026-04-29]]) and the cascade is structurally broken ([[karian-middle-manager-burnout-2026-06-04]]). Don't fix the cascade — bypass it for the messages that can't afford to fail. Use cross-channel personalized delivery (email + mobile + intranet + signage) with read-attribution measurement.

Vendors leading here:
- **Firstup** — strongest route-around posture. *"Manager-as-communicator model was built for a different era."* Backed by 6,200-employee 2026 research showing managers overextended, untrained, miss messages. See [Firstup 2026 IC engagement research](https://firstup.io/blog/internal-communications-failure-2026-research/).
- **Workvivo** — cites 84% reliance on managers / 60% rated below expectations; positions exec-direct video + targeted messaging as the fix. See [Workvivo IC benchmarks](https://www.workvivo.com/internal-communications/benchmarks/).
- **Staffbase** — hybrid framing. Corporate message goes direct; manager *contextualizes* but isn't the cascade carrier. [Staffbase AI tools post](https://staffbase.com/blog/ai-tools-for-internal-comms).
- **ContactMonkey, Workshop** — route-around by *product design* — both bypass manager relay via direct send infrastructure. Neither has a major manager-enablement narrative.
- **Cerkl** — structurally here (Foundations, Omni AI). Distinguishing claim: same delivery infrastructure regardless of whether the path is cascade, direct, or both.

### The enable-managers stance

Premise: managers are the trust layer that survives even when leadership comms doesn't ([[why-employees-right-not-to-trust-ic]] / [[ic-credibility-deficit]]). Fix the cascade by reducing manager friction — centralized knowledge, role-targeted delivery, AI-augmented summarization, feedback loops.

Vendors leading here:
- **Simpplr** — most explicit. *"Why manager enablement is key to successful AI initiatives"* (2026-05-28) frames managers as "transformation leaders" given the right tools. [Simpplr post](https://www.simpplr.com/blog/manager-enablement-ai-initiatives/).

### The Karian / structural-redesign stance (third axis)

Premise (per [[karian-middle-manager-burnout-2026-06-04]]): both vendor stances treat the cascade as a *tooling* problem. The real problem is the *role* — responsibility without authority, hybrid load, admin bloat, emotional absorption, outdated design. Tooling without role redesign just accelerates burnout. Karian's prescription is structural — reduce span, strip admin, real decision rights, fewer + clearer priorities. **No vendor sits cleanly on this axis** because vendors sell tooling, not org redesign.

## Tensions / Criticisms

- **The route-around-only critique:** if managers are structurally needed for context translation (per [[ic-credibility-deficit]] / [[context-delivery]]), routing around them on high-stakes messages costs trust. Karian would say managers are *still* the context layer; cascade just shouldn't be the *delivery* layer.
- **The enable-only critique:** layering enablement tools on top of a structurally broken role (Karian's thesis) accelerates burnout. Adoption metrics will look good ([[enablement-illusion]]); cascade effectiveness won't move.
- **The both-sides synthesis critique:** "we do both" is a positioning move, not an operational claim. Hard for buyers to act on without a clear product-level distinction between cascade and direct paths. Worth pressure-testing whether Cerkl's product genuinely supports the both-sides claim or whether it's an aspirational frame.

## Open Questions

- Does Cerkl have a *product-level* feature distinguishing cascade-pre-brief (manager-context layer) from direct delivery? If not, the both-sides positioning is rhetorical.
- Will the route-around lane consolidate (Firstup + Workvivo + Staffbase + ContactMonkey + Workshop + Cerkl all crowding the same pitch) and force differentiation on measurement or pricing? Likely.
- Is there a credible enable-side answer to Karian's structural critique, or does the enable stance require an implicit assumption that the role is fixable?
