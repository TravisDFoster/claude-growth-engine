# Internal Email Benchmarks

## Definition

Aggregate performance metrics for internal-corporate-communications email — open rate, click rate, click-to-open rate — used as comparison baselines by IC teams to evaluate their own program performance.

## Why It Matters

Internal email is the largest single comms channel in most enterprises, yet there is no standardized industry-wide benchmark source. The most-cited datasets are vendor-published ([[contactmonkey]], Staffbase, PoliteMail) and therefore reflect each vendor's customer base, not the broader IC population. This creates a citation hazard: a "76% open rate" benchmark might be a perfectly fair number — but if cited without naming the vendor and methodology, it overstates the typical IC team's reality.

## How It Works

Vendors aggregate platform telemetry across their customer base and publish industry / company-size / interactive-feature splits. The 2026 ContactMonkey benchmark ([[contactmonkey-internal-email-benchmark-2026]]) is the most recent large-sample data point:

- **76%** average open rate
- **9%** average click rate
- Sample: **255,000 campaigns** across 20+ industries

## Tensions / Criticisms

- Single-vendor data: any benchmark from one platform reflects that vendor's customer base, not the IC market overall.
- Open-rate measurement is fraught generally (pixel tracking, image blocking, MS Outlook safelinks) — but internal email is somewhat shielded vs marketing email.
- No standardized cross-vendor benchmark exists. Anyone citing a number should name the source.
