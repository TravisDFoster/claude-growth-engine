# Corporate Wallpaper

## Definition

[[tony-kihl]]'s name for the failure mode of an employee value proposition (EVP) or employer-brand promise that exists only as words — present on the wall and in the deck, but not lived by employees. "We did not want it to feel like corporate wallpaper. We wanted it to feel real and human." The gap between the experience an org *markets* (externally and internally) and the experience employees *actually have*.

## Why It Matters

It's a sharp, portable label for the most common EVP/internal-branding failure, and it operationalizes the [[engagement-to-experience-pivot]]: an EVP only counts if it's a lived experience, not a message. For IC, it reframes employer-brand work from a launch deliverable into an ongoing experience-design and measurement problem — which is squarely Cerkl-adjacent (did the message reach people, and did behavior/experience actually move?).

## How It Works

The antidote, per the [[lhh]] case ([[ragan-evp-corporate-wallpaper-lhh-2026-06-10]]):

- **Treat the EVP as a campaign / business transformation**, not a single communication.
- **Listen first** — surveys + localized roundtables (60 countries) to ground it in what employees already value.
- **Connect to the whole employee journey** — onboarding, career, recognition, manager conversations — so it's built into the experience, not announced over it.
- **Enable leaders** with cascade scripts ("say this, ask this, listen for this, do this") — a [[manager-cascade]] enable-side move.
- **Reinforce past launch** — "the launch mattered, but the reinforcement mattered more"; sustaining energy is the hard part.
- **Measure multi-signal** — comms metrics *and* people metrics ([[ic-measurement-and-roi]]).

## Tensions / Criticisms

- Single-company, self-reported metrics (town-hall 90–100%, +20–30% survey movement) — a persuasive case, not generalizable proof.
- The antidote is resource-intensive (multi-channel, multi-month, leader enablement) — feasible for a global firm's flagship EVP, harder for the [[do-more-with-less]] team on a routine message.
- Survivorship: we hear from the launch that worked; the base rate of EVP launches that stayed wallpaper is unknown.
