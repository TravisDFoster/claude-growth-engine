# Employee Engagement Three-Bucket Model

## Definition

Gallup's classification of employees into three psychological states based on responses to the Q12 instrument:

- **Engaged** — psychologically committed to their work and contributing positively.
- **Not Engaged** — psychologically detached; doing time, not energy.
- **Actively Disengaged** — psychologically disconnected and undermining the work of engaged colleagues.

## Why It Matters

The three-bucket model is the lingua franca of engagement reporting in the IC industry. Almost every benchmark, vendor study, and analyst report eventually gets translated into "X% engaged." Most readers assume "engaged" means a single binary state; the three-bucket framing reminds you that the *unengaged middle* is the largest group — typically 60%+ of any workforce.

## How It Works

Gallup measures the buckets via the Q12 instrument (12 survey items, each rated 1–5). Scores combine into the three classifications. In Gallup's 2025 global data ([[state-of-the-global-workplace-2026]]):

- Engaged: 20%
- Not Engaged: 64%
- Actively Disengaged: 16%

The ratio of engaged to actively disengaged (the "engagement ratio") is sometimes used as an organizational-health metric — Gallup's best-practice organizations operate at ratios approaching 9:1 versus the global ~1.25:1.

## Tensions / Criticisms

- The model is proprietary to Gallup's Q12 instrument. Other vendor instruments (Glint, Qualtrics, Perceptyx) use different definitions and produce different numbers. Cross-vendor comparisons are not apples-to-apples.
- "Engaged" is a high bar in Gallup's instrument — one reason headline engagement numbers stay in the 20–35% range globally and rarely exceed ~50% even in best-in-class organizations.
