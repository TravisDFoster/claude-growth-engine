# Engagement-to-Experience Pivot

## Definition

The 2026 reframing of internal comms' success construct *away from* employee engagement (clicks, open rate, sentiment, "good vibes") *toward* employee experience (the lived, business-outcome-oriented experience of working at the org). Engagement asks "how do employees feel?"; experience asks "can employees do their best work, and did communication measurably help?"

## Why It Matters

It's the dominant trade-press narrative of mid-June 2026 — [[ragan-communications]] / [[pr-daily]] ran both a feature and a reader survey ("The New Employee Experience Mandate") inside one week. For Cerkl, the pivot is double-edged: it elevates **measurement and business outcomes** (Cerkl's strength — delivered/opened/read by employee, behavior change) *and* it's the banner the intranet/EX-suite vendors march under to justify destination platforms. The opportunity is to own the concrete proof layer under the abstract word "experience"; the risk is letting "experience" become a synonym for "buy a bigger intranet."

## How It Works

- **The engagement critique:** engagement scores have been "flat for 20 years and even declining" (vendor claim, [[ragan-engagement-to-experience-2026-06-11]]); the link from engagement to ROI is indirect ("the R is farther away from the I than people want to see").
- **The reframe:** measure behavior change and business results, not surface metrics ([[open-rate-inflation]] critique, voiced by practitioners).
- **The arrow reverses:** rather than engagement → better outcomes, the claim is better work outcomes (via less friction) → higher engagement.
- **In practice:** an EVP activation that connects to the whole employee journey and is measured on multi-signal outcomes ([[corporate-wallpaper]], [[ragan-evp-corporate-wallpaper-lhh-2026-06-10]]) is the pivot enacted.

## Tensions / Criticisms

- **Vendor-motivated.** The loudest voices are EX/intranet vendors ([[interact]]) for whom "experience" justifies a bigger platform purchase. The engagement→experience move can be destination-framing in disguise.
- **"Experience" is fuzzier than "engagement."** Engagement at least had Gallup Q12. EX measurement is multi-signal and less standardized — which is why [[tony-kihl]] says "there is no one perfect dashboard."
- The "20 years flat" engagement claim is uncited and broader than [[gallup]]'s actual series (which shows a 2023→2025 decline, not two flat decades).
