# AI-Integrated Comms Function

## Definition

A corporate-communications function in which AI is embedded into how the team *operates* — culture, leadership modeling, governance, workflow, and measurement — rather than bolted on as a set of tools. Anchored by [[institute-for-public-relations|IPR]]'s 2026 nine-item enterprise-adoption checklist and a 22-month proof case at [[new-york-life]] ([[ragan-9-lessons-ai-integrated-comms-2026-06-11]]). Core premise: "AI success is less about tools and more about how organizations operate and continuously evolve along with AI."

## Why It Matters

It's the practitioner-tier operating-model counterpart to the vault's analyst frames ([[analyst-trio-comms-as-ai-gate]], [[enablement-illusion]], [[people-amplification]]). Where Gartner/Forrester/McKinsey describe *what* goes wrong with AI rollouts at the org level, this names *how* an IC team gets it right at the function level. It also supplies a constructive answer to [[ai-anxious-buyer]]: structured, human-centric, leadership-modeled adoption converts displacement anxiety into agency (NY Life reports 78% higher job satisfaction *because of* AI).

## How It Works

The IPR nine-item checklist, as applied at NY Life:

1. **Learning culture** — embed AI in daily work; track and celebrate use (→ 90% use AI multiple times/day).
2. **Human-centric** — AI augments, humans define the final product ([[co-piloting]]).
3. **Unified change narrative** — only 37% of orgs have one; position AI inside enterprise strategy.
4. **Peer learning & champions** — adoption scales through peers, not mandates.
5. **Leadership modeling** — engagement from the top predicts adoption ([[manager-as-ai-champion]] one level up).
6. **Responsible/ethical use** — governance as a *trust* issue; Editorial Guidelines GPT in the workflow.
7. **Workflow integration** — the inflection point; redesign how work gets done, not just speed tasks.
8. **Measure for improvement** — outcomes (time reallocated, capacity, client satisfaction), not tool-usage ([[measurers-vs-builders]] rebuttal).
9. **Build the right team** — align talent + external expertise; "Blue Sky" initiatives.

Three meta-lessons: culture > technology; workflow integration is the inflection point; discipline (accuracy/credibility/brand) is unchanged. Treat the checklist as a recurring quarterly diagnostic, not a one-time setup.

## Tensions / Criticisms

- **Single-company, self-reported.** The NY Life percentages are internal-survey figures with undisclosed denominators — an inspiring case, not benchmark data.
- **Survivorship/selection.** A Fortune-100 insurer with a dedicated CCO and budget is not the median IC team; the checklist may under-serve under-resourced functions (the [[do-more-with-less]] majority).
- The IPR research's full methodology isn't visible in the article — only the checklist and the 37% stat surface.
