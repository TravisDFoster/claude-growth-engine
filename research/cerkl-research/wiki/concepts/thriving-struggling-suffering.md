# Thriving / Struggling / Suffering

## Definition

Gallup's three-tier classification of overall wellbeing — distinct from engagement. Based on the Cantril Self-Anchoring Striving Scale, which asks respondents to rate their current and expected future life on a 0–10 ladder.

- **Thriving** — strong, consistent, progressing life situation.
- **Struggling** — uncertain or negative current life situation.
- **Suffering** — at high risk on multiple wellbeing dimensions.

## Why It Matters

Wellbeing and engagement are distinct measures and often diverge. In Gallup's 2025 data ([[state-of-the-global-workplace-2026]]), thriving rose to 34% (first uptick in three years) while engagement fell to 20%. For internal-communications strategy, this distinction matters: communications that boost wellbeing (recognition, belonging, transparency) don't necessarily move engagement; communications that drive engagement (purpose, clarity, growth) don't necessarily lift wellbeing.

## How It Works

Respondents rate current life situation and expected life in five years on a 0–10 ladder. Scores classify them into one of the three tiers. This is independent of employer relationship — it's life-overall, not work-specifically.

Gallup's 2025 global distribution:

- Thriving: 34%
- Struggling: 56%
- Suffering: 9%

## Tensions / Criticisms

- Wellbeing is a life-level construct; attributing changes to employer or workplace action requires care. Improvements in thriving might reflect macroeconomic or geopolitical factors, not employer effort.
