# Evergreen Content Strategy

## Definition

Prioritizing communications content that retains relevance over long time horizons — policies, FAQs, how-to guides, reference documentation — rather than continuously producing time-bound news items. Particularly applied to intranet content management in 2026.

## Why It Matters

Workshop's 2026 trends data ([[six-internal-comms-trends-2026]]) reports that **29% of communicators name intranet the hardest channel to manage**, yet more than half are still prioritizing intranet improvements this year. The strategic shift surfaced in the data: rather than trying to make the intranet a comprehensive everything-tool, teams are narrowing scope to **evergreen reference content** — policies, FAQs, how-to guides — and accepting that timely news flows through other channels (email, mobile apps, manager cascade).

For blog writing: this is a useful counter-frame to "intranets are dying" narratives. Intranets aren't dying; they're being scoped down to what they're actually good at.

## How It Works

The reframe in practice:

- **In:** Policy archives, benefits documentation, IT how-to guides, onboarding-reference content, organizational charts, brand-asset libraries.
- **Out (or de-prioritized):** Time-sensitive announcements, leadership newsletters, recognition content, event-driven communications — these move to email, mobile apps, or chat.

The implicit measurement question: does your intranet investment generate value through *recurring* lookups, or only at publication time?

## Tensions / Criticisms

- Vendors who sell intranet platforms have an incentive to *broaden* the intranet's role, not narrow it. Vendor-tier sources may under-represent the evergreen strategy.
- The narrowed-scope intranet can feel deprioritized to executives who expect the intranet to be the "front door of the company."
