# Execution Gap

## Definition

[[justin-black]]'s framing of the chronic weakness of the employee-listening category: organizations collect feedback but fail to take visible action on it, breaking employee trust in the listening process. Coined / popularized in the launch narrative for Workvivo's [[seer]] (2026-05-06).

## Why It Matters

The execution gap is a useful frame for IC blog writing because it names a concrete behavioral failure that's widely observable: surveys distributed, results presented to leadership, no visible follow-through, employees disengage from the next survey. The frame is sympathetic to practitioners (who often agree the gap is real) without blaming any specific vendor — making it portable across listening-vendor coverage.

## How It Works

The launch survey behind Seer (4,736 frontline + desk workers via TrendCandy, cited in [[workvivo-seer-2026-05-11]]) quantified the gap:

- **62%** comfortable giving feedback
- **49%** see meaningful change happen as a result
- **42%** believe leaders are held accountable

The 62 → 49 → 42 ladder is the empirical anchor: comfort > visible change > accountability. Each step down represents the trust eroded by the execution gap.

## Tensions / Criticisms

- The framing benefits [[workvivo]]'s commercial interest — practitioners and competing analysts may discount it as marketing.
- The 4,736-respondent survey was Workvivo-commissioned (via TrendCandy), not independent. Methodology details are not public.

## Open Questions

- Independent measurement of the execution gap (Gallup, Forrester, Mercer, Aon, Willis Towers Watson)?
- Does closing the gap measurably improve trust / engagement, or is it correlation with already-healthier orgs?
