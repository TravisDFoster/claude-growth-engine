# Enablement Illusion

## Definition

[[gartner]]-coined (May 2026, attributed to Senior Director Analyst [[swagatam-basu]]): the mistake leaders make when they treat basic AI access or adoption metrics as evidence of *transformation*. The illusion is that rolling out AI tools, training, and seats equals an AI-powered workforce — when in fact the underlying behavior, productivity, and value creation often haven't moved.

## Why It Matters

The frame is the cleanest articulation of why 2026's AI rollouts aren't producing reported ROI even as enterprise AI seat-licenses scale. It supplies IC and HR leaders with analyst-grade language to push back against CIO/CEO narratives that "we shipped Copilot to 12,000 people, the rollout's done." For Cerkl positioning, it validates the [[ai-rollout-comms]] thesis directly: rollout messaging is part of the value, not a launch event.

## How It Works

Gartner's "True ROI Index" (named in the same release) is the operationalization — it suggests measuring **depth and diversity of AI use**, not seat-counts or time-saved-per-task. The signals that distinguish transformation from rollout:

- Are employees using AI on *multiple* job tasks, or one? (Proficient multi-use-case users are 2× more productive.)
- Is AI use concentrated in managers/executives, or spread across the front line? (73% of highly productive AI users are managers — front-line gap.)
- Are employees combining enterprise + personal AI tools? (88% are; suggests UX gap with sanctioned tools.)
- Is the org tracking quality, process improvement, and behavior shift, or just hours saved?

The implied IC job: pre-launch awareness, role-specific rollout messages, manager enablement scripts, ongoing pulse — the [[ai-rollout-comms]] playbook.

## Tensions / Criticisms

- "Enablement illusion" is a Gartner-coined framing — by design, it favors Gartner's research subscriptions. Discount accordingly.
- The prediction (50% lose top AI talent by 2027) is forecast-language, not data. Cite the underlying survey numbers (19%, 88%, 73%, 3.4×) for defensibility.
- The frame risks being used to attack any AI rollout in progress — useful as critique, less useful as prescription. The "True ROI Index" details remain paywalled.
