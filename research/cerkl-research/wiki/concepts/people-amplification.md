# People Amplification

## Definition

[[helen-poitevin]]'s framing (Gartner Distinguished VP Analyst, May 2026): organizations get AI ROI not by *eliminating* the need for people but by *amplifying* them — investing more in skills, roles, and operating models that let humans guide and scale autonomous systems. The frame is the analyst-grade rebuttal to the 2026 CEO playbook of cutting headcount to fund AI.

## Why It Matters

Three reasons this concept is high-leverage:

1. **It is empirically backed.** Poitevin's frame sits on Gartner's Q3 2025 survey of 350 enterprise execs — ~80% of orgs piloting autonomous business cut headcount; cuts show **no correlation** with AI ROI. The high-ROI cohort invested *more* in people, not less.
2. **It is convergent across the analyst landscape.** BCG (*Closing the AI Impact Gap*), Forrester (boomerang-rehire prediction), McKinsey (6.4 hrs/week saved per AI-using seat as augmentation), and MIT Sloan all align on the same diagnosis. Rare clean alignment.
3. **It is the structural Cerkl positioning frame.** Broadcast doesn't replace the IC team — it makes a 2-person team look like a 10-person one through personalization, segmentation, cross-channel delivery, and measurement. Omni AI extends that. "Amplification, not replacement" is the citation-ready version of Cerkl's product story.

## How It Works

The mechanism in the high-ROI cohort (Gartner data):

- **Aggressive skills investment** — reskilling, role redesign, AI-fluency training across the org, not just IT.
- **Role redesign** — new operating models where humans guide / supervise / steer agentic AI rather than being substituted by it.
- **Looking beyond workforce** — Poitevin: *"Those who only look to the workforce tend to be the 'laggards,' because they're not going after the broader set of value."* Process redesign and product reinvention matter more than headcount math.
- **Net-positive jobs forecast** by 2028–2029 (Gartner projection, contested).

The IC corollary: AI-rollout comms ([[ai-rollout-comms]]) is the operational mechanism that turns intent ("amplify our people") into reality (employees know which AI tools, for which jobs, with manager backing, with feedback loops).

## Tensions / Criticisms

- Gartner's sample is ≥$1B revenue enterprise; not directly transferable to SMB / Foundations buyer.
- "Workforce reduction tied to AI initiatives" is self-reported — some are real AI-driven; some are AI-washed (Sam Altman acknowledged February 2026). Gartner doesn't separate.
- The net-positive-jobs-by-2028 forecast is contested; cite the empirical no-correlation finding, not the forward-looking number.
- Vendor messaging that adopts the phrase risks sounding rehearsed — let prospects use it organically.

## Open Questions

- Does Gartner's underlying report break down cuts by function (IC, marketing, IT, customer service)? If IC is heavily cut, the "amplification layer for the IC team" pitch sharpens.
- ROI distribution shape (median, IQR, outliers) — "nearly identical returns regardless of cut depth" is the soundbite; the actual distribution would let citations be more specific.
