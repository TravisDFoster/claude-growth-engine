# Employee Influencers

## Definition

Trusted internal voices — typically not formal leadership — whose informal authority within the organization makes their endorsement of a message disproportionately persuasive to peers. The IC tactic involves identifying natural influencers and equipping them to amplify priority comms, rather than running formal "ambassador" programs.

## Why It Matters

Employee influencers cross from internal communications into external employer-brand territory: the same employees who carry credibility internally are often the most-trusted external advocates on social media. For 2026 IC strategy, the tactic is moving from niche to mainstream: **41% of communicators say they're actively investing** in this area ([[six-internal-comms-trends-2026]]), one of the higher "actively investing" numbers in the Workshop trends data.

## How It Works

The Workshop framing distinguishes two approaches:

- **Identify natural influencers** — observe who other employees already listen to (engagement on posts, referrals, peer-mentioned trust signals); equip them with messaging and content.
- **Create formal ambassador programs** — train and credentialize a chosen subset; more structured but more artificial.

The 2026 shift favors the first approach. Natural-influencer programs are lighter weight and less corporate-feeling, which preserves the credibility that made the influencer valuable in the first place.

## Tensions / Criticisms

- Risk of co-option: turning trusted peers into corporate mouthpieces erodes the trust that made them trusted.
- Equity concerns: which employees get tapped as influencers may amplify existing privilege/power gradients.
- Single-vendor data; cross-validation needed for the 41% figure.
