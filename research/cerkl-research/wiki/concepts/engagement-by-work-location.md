# Engagement by Work Location

## Definition

A breakdown of employee engagement rates by the employee's physical work arrangement: exclusively remote, hybrid, on-site remote-capable, and on-site non-remote-capable.

## Why It Matters

This breakdown reveals the structural disadvantage of frontline / non-remote-capable workers, which underpins the entire frontline-comms vendor narrative (Workvivo, Staffbase, Beekeeper, Firstup, Cerkl). The 2025 Gallup data shows a **13-point engagement gap** between the most-engaged cohort (exclusively remote, 30%) and the least (on-site non-remote-capable, 17%).

For blog writing: this is the cleanest single statistic to cite when defending the "frontline workers are underserved by IC" position — primary-research, large-sample, named-publisher.

## How It Works

Gallup classifies respondents into four work-location categories:

| Category | 2025 Engagement |
|---|---|
| Exclusively remote | 30% |
| Hybrid | 25% |
| On-site, remote-capable | 24% |
| On-site, non-remote-capable | 17% |

Source: [[state-of-the-global-workplace-2026]].

The "non-remote-capable" cohort encompasses frontline industries — manufacturing, healthcare, retail, hospitality, field service, warehousing.

## Tensions / Criticisms

- Self-selection: who *gets* to work remotely is correlated with role type, seniority, and industry. Engagement differences may partly reflect role differences rather than location differences alone.
- The "exclusively remote" advantage may be a Gallup-instrument artifact (Q12 items index workplace clarity and recognition, both of which knowledge workers may report more reliably than frontline workers).

## Open Questions

- Does the 13-point gap close with frontline-targeted communication investment, or is it structural?
