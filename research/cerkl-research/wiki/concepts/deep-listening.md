# Deep Listening

## Definition

[[forrester]]'s category-naming move (February 2026, [[david-brodeur-johnson]] + [[james-mcquivey]]): AI that turns the "digital exhaust" of Teams chats, channel conversations, and meeting transcripts into real-time sentiment, emotion, and friction signals across the workforce — **without surveys**. Forrester positions it as the AI-native evolution of "continuous listening," with vendors "one to two years away from full-featured offerings."

## Why It Matters

Deep listening is a category-naming event, not a think piece. It will likely become the new evaluation lens for the EX/Comms category by 2027 (next Forrester Wave expected Q2 2027 or Q2 2028). For Cerkl, the frame is **simultaneously a threat and an opportunity**:

- **Threat:** enterprise buyers reading the Forrester piece may conclude any EX tool worth buying does sentiment NLP on collaboration exhaust. Cerkl doesn't.
- **Opportunity:** Forrester is implicitly validating "raw collaboration channels are the new EX measurement surface" — which is the channel-native frame Cerkl already pitches on the *delivery* side.

The strategic move (Travis-directed): **complement-not-compete**. Cerkl owns the *response* half of the listen-and-act loop. Sentiment NLP on Teams is better done by Microsoft Viva, [[workvivo]] [[seer]], or Qualtrics — integrated alongside Cerkl. The frame: *"We don't compete with the listening layer. We make sure the response to what you hear actually reaches the right employees."*

## How It Works

Forrester defines the stack with five capabilities:

1. **Passive sentiment detection** across digital channels (Teams chats, meeting transcripts) via privacy-focused preprocessing and NLP.
2. **Emotional granularity** beyond engaged/disengaged — Forrester: *"tension from fear, cynicism from frustration, or burnout from ordinary stress."*
3. **Topic categorization at scale** — auto-categorizes conversations into themes (workload imbalance, dissatisfaction with organizational changes).
4. **Real-time change-impact measurement** — *"in days rather than quarters"*, the operational headline.
5. **Privacy-by-design as table stakes** — co-author McQuivey's framing positions consent + transparency as prerequisite.

The vendors structurally best-positioned (per the deep-dive analysis):

- **[[workvivo]] / [[seer]]** — Seer launched 2026-05-06 with framing almost verbatim to Forrester's; will cite this blog in sales decks within 30 days.
- **Microsoft Viva Glint** — already owns the data plane (Teams chat + Viva Insights + Glint sentiment). If anyone hits "full-featured" first, it's Microsoft.
- **Qualtrics / Medallia** — survey-platform incumbents pivoting hard; will brand themselves "deep listening leaders" within 90 days.

## Tensions / Criticisms

- **Cerkl's complement-not-compete position (Travis):** Cerkl ships read-through analytics across email + cross-channel delivery — *delivery measurement.* Forrester's deep listening is passive NLP on collaboration exhaust — a different pipeline, a different job. Pretending Cerkl is in this category dilutes the actual wedge.
- **"Privacy by design" is doing a lot of work** — workplace surveillance pushback is at a multi-year high ([ExpressVPN 2026](https://www.expressvpn.com/blog/workplace-surveillance-trends-us/) — 73% monitoring; [Meta no-opt-out backlash](https://www.businesstoday.in/technology/story/meta-staff-protest-over-activity-tracking-tool-amid-privacy-concerns-531282-2026-05-13)). The category's adoption gate is consent + transparency, not capability.
- **The frame favors incumbents with the data, not new entrants.** Microsoft (Viva + Teams), Workvivo (Zoom-bundled + Seer), Qualtrics (survey corpus). A Cerkl-sized vendor cannot reach the Teams transcript firehose without a Microsoft/Zoom partnership.
- **No vendors named in the public blog.** That's analyst hygiene; buyers will Google. The vendor narrative race is on.
