# People Intelligence

## Definition

[[workvivo]]'s preferred term for the next-generation employee-listening category, distinguishing from "employee listening" ([[glint]], [[culture-amp]], [[peakon]], [[qualtrics]]). The asserted differentiator is real-time signal integration across communication, collaboration, and engagement, paired with in-product action.

## Why It Matters

"People intelligence" is an attempted category-reframe by Workvivo for its [[seer]] launch. Whether the term sticks or fades is itself a market signal — successful category-reframes (HR Tech → "People Tech," recruiting → "Talent Acquisition") have reshaped buyer behavior; unsuccessful ones get absorbed back into the incumbent category. As of T+5 days post-Seer launch, the term has not yet been picked up by analysts ([[forrester]], Gartner, Josh Bersin) or trade press beyond UC Today.

## How It Works

Per Workvivo's framing:

- **Listening** = surveys, comments, eNPS — explicit feedback.
- **People intelligence** = listening + passive signals from communication and collaboration + in-product action.

The category claim depends on the integration depth (how much non-survey signal actually flows in) and on whether competitors stay anchored to "listening" vocabulary or adopt their own re-frame.

## Tensions / Criticisms

- Category-reframe attempts often fail. Practitioners may continue calling this "listening" or "engagement surveys" regardless of vendor preference.
- The "real-time signals" claim depends on integration depth Workvivo has not yet publicly substantiated for non-Workvivo / non-Zoom data sources.
