# Comms as Knowledge Steward

## Definition

The thesis that the internal-communications leader's future job is **stewardship of institutional knowledge** — ensuring organizational information is accurate, current, non-conflicting, and policy-compliant wherever it lives — so the organization is AI-ready. Premised on the claim that AI has solved the content-*production* problem, making content *trustworthiness* the new scarce good: "the future of the intranet is not about more content, it's going to be about trusted action."

## Why It Matters

It reframes IC's value in the AI era from *producing* messages to *governing the knowledge AI draws on*. If employees (and agents) increasingly query systems rather than browse them, the payload that matters is whether the underlying knowledge is reliable. This is upstream of Cerkl's delivery surface — but it pairs with delivery: stewarded knowledge is worthless if it doesn't reach the right employee, and reach is unprovable without measurement. It also reframes the governance narrative ([[shadow-ai-comms-problem]], analyst "AI quality layer" pitches) as a *comms-owned* responsibility rather than purely an IT/security one.

## How It Works

- AI removes the content-scarcity constraint → undifferentiated content volume *increases confusion* unless curated.
- The comms leader becomes the steward ensuring knowledge is accurate, up-to-date, non-conflicting, and on-guideline — "the foundation to AI readiness" ([[daren-jennings]], [[ragan-engagement-to-experience-2026-06-11]]).
- Operationalized in practice as embedding standards into the workflow — e.g. New York Life's "Editorial Guidelines GPT" ([[ragan-9-lessons-ai-integrated-comms-2026-06-11]]).

## Tensions / Criticisms

- **Vendor-motivated, and destination-framing.** Voiced by an intranet vendor ([[interact]]) for whom "the intranet is the knowledge layer" justifies the platform. The knowledge-steward role is real, but it doesn't require the intranet to be the destination.
- **Scope creep risk.** "Own all organizational knowledge" is a bigger mandate than most IC teams are resourced for; in practice it competes with HR, IT, and knowledge-management functions.
- Overlaps with the 3-vendor "governance layer" cliché (Staffbase Navigator / Simpplr AI Control Center / LumApps Agent Hub) — knowledge stewardship can be the same pitch with a friendlier noun.

## Open Questions

- Is "knowledge steward" a durable IC role or a temporary land-grab before knowledge-management/IT reclaim it?
- Where does Cerkl's measurement surface fit — does proving *which* knowledge was delivered and read make Cerkl part of the stewardship stack?
