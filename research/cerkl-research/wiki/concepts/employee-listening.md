# Employee Listening

## Definition

The systematic collection and analysis of employee feedback — via surveys (engagement, pulse, lifecycle), polls, open-ended comments, and increasingly passive signals (sentiment from communication and collaboration tools) — used to inform leadership decisions and intervention design.

## Why It Matters

Employee listening is the dominant category for capturing employee voice at scale. The category includes [[glint]] (now Microsoft Viva Glint), [[culture-amp]], [[peakon]] (now Workday), [[qualtrics]] EmployeeXM, and as of 2026-05 [[seer]] from [[workvivo]]. Categorically distinct from IC delivery — listening tools collect signals; IC tools deliver messages.

## How It Works

Traditional flow: survey (annual or quarterly) → analysis (scoring, segmentation) → reporting (to executives, managers) → action plan (separate process, often via HR business partners) → next survey cycle.

The 2026 shift, exemplified by [[seer]]: continuous listening + in-product action (post updates, launch initiatives, create spaces directly from the listening tool) — closing the loop between feedback and visible outcome ([[execution-gap]]).

## Tensions / Criticisms

- Survey fatigue: employees who don't see action taken on feedback stop engaging — the central pain point Seer's launch narrative exploits.
- Vendor moats are increasingly about data fusion (HCM, comms, productivity tools), not the listening instrument itself.
