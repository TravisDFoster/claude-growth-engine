# Leadership Communications

## Overview

How executives and senior leaders communicate with the workforce — cadence, channels, authenticity, and trust. Distinct from [[manager-cascade]], which covers the relay of leadership messaging through line managers.

## Current Thesis

The 2026 picture, anchored to Workshop's trends data and the Cisco/PR Daily AI interview:

- **Manager cascade is the headline gap** — 56% of communicators prioritize manager communication, only 4% believe managers are effective at cascading ([[six-internal-comms-trends-2026]], [[manager-cascade]]). Leadership comms doesn't break down at the executive layer; it breaks down at the relay layer.
- **AI augmentation in leadership comms requires taste preservation** — Roth-Eagle's framing ([[cisco-comms-leader-ai-content]], [[ai-slop]]) applies most strongly to leadership comms, where voice is the differentiator. AI-drafted CEO notes that read like every other AI-drafted CEO note are worse than no note.
- **Employee trust in managers vs other sources** — the underlying reason manager-cascade matters is that employees report higher trust in their direct managers than in distant executives, vendors, or HR comms. This puts the operational burden of leadership comms on line managers.
