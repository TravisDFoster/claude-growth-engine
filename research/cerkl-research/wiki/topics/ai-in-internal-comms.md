# AI in Internal Comms

## Overview

How AI is reshaping internal-communications practice — content drafting, audience targeting, sentiment analysis, summarization, search, agentic workflows — and where vendors, practitioners, and analysts are placing bets.

## Current Thesis

The 2026 picture has two related stories. The first (existing thesis, by source authority):

- **Analyst-tier (Forrester):** Only ~49% of customers actively use their *vendor's* AI features despite heavy vendor investment ([[ai-adoption-gap]], [[forrester-wave-intranet-platforms-q2-2026]]).
- **Vendor-tier (Workshop):** **73% of communicators use AI at least weekly** (42% daily + 31% several-times-a-week) — measured against *any* AI tool, not just vendor-platform features ([[six-internal-comms-trends-2026]]).
- **Practitioner-tier (Cisco / Roth-Eagle):** The bottleneck isn't tool adoption — it's **taste and voice** ([[ai-slop]]). AI workflows that preserve human judgment at every step produce useful output ([[cisco-comms-leader-ai-content]]).

Synthesis: communicators are using AI a lot, but mostly through general tools (ChatGPT, Copilot) rather than vendor-platform AI features. The quality bottleneck is workflow design — taste-preservation at every step rather than at the end.

The second story (May 2026 addition, the org-level frame):

- **Gartner-tier (rollout side):** Most AI rollouts are stuck in the **[[enablement-illusion]]** — leaders mistake adoption metrics for transformation. 88% of employees with enterprise AI also use personal AI tools ([[shadow-ai-comms-problem]]); 19% report zero time saved. The fix is not better tools — it's better **rollout comms** ([[gartner-people-centric-ai-2026-05-15]]).
- **Gartner-tier (response side):** ~80% of orgs piloting autonomous business cut headcount; zero correlation between cuts and AI ROI. The high-ROI cohort invests *more* in people — **[[people-amplification]]** ([[gartner-ai-layoffs-no-returns-2026-05-15]]).
- **Forrester-tier (listening side):** **[[deep-listening]]** — AI on Teams/meeting collaboration exhaust producing real-time sentiment, replacing surveys. One-to-two years out; structurally favors incumbents with the data (Microsoft, Workvivo-via-Zoom, Qualtrics) ([[forrester-ai-rewrite-ex-deep-listening-2026-05-15]]).

The unifying thesis lives at [[ai-rollout-comms]] — the 2026 chain where bad rollout → shadow AI → no ROI → AI-attributed layoffs → choreography failure → deep listening as new measurement lane. IC is the binding constraint at multiple links.

The AI-feature categories that [[forrester]] explicitly evaluates remain the 2026 buyer checklist for intranet platforms:

- AI-enabled search
- AI assistants and agents (content generation, transformation, governance, process automation)
- AI admin options
- Dashboards and analytics
- Natural language reporting

[[workvivo]]'s [[seer]] launch (2026-05-06) adds AI-powered manager dashboards and theme detection on open-ended feedback ([[workvivo-seer-2026-05-11]]) — and is now the vendor most likely to capture Forrester's deep-listening framing in market.

## Contradictions & Tensions

- 49% vendor-AI adoption (Forrester) vs 73% weekly+ AI use (Workshop). Not contradictory — different denominators. Worth surfacing when citing either.
- Forrester's deep-listening frame (listen passively on Teams) puts the EX/Comms category's measurement value upstream of Cerkl's delivery-side analytics. Cerkl's response is **complement-not-compete**, not redefine.
- Gartner's reframe of shadow AI as a *comms* problem contradicts the dominant IT/security framing as a *tooling/governance* problem. Be explicit when citing.
