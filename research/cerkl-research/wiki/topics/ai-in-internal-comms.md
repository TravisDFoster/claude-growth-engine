# AI in Internal Comms

## Overview

How AI is reshaping internal-communications practice — content drafting, audience targeting, sentiment analysis, summarization, search, agentic workflows — and where vendors and practitioners are placing bets.

## Current Thesis

The 2026 picture stratifies by source authority and asks slightly different questions:

- **Analyst-tier (Forrester):** Only ~49% of customers actively use their *vendor's* AI features despite heavy vendor investment ([[ai-adoption-gap]], [[forrester-wave-intranet-platforms-q2-2026]]).
- **Vendor-tier (Workshop):** **73% of communicators use AI at least weekly** (42% daily + 31% several-times-a-week) — measured against *any* AI tool, not just vendor-platform features ([[six-internal-comms-trends-2026]]). These two stats aren't contradictory; they answer different questions.
- **Practitioner-tier (Cisco / Roth-Eagle):** The bottleneck isn't tool adoption — it's **taste and voice** ([[ai-slop]]). AI workflows that preserve human judgment at every step produce useful output; AI workflows that rely on end-stage review produce slop ([[cisco-comms-leader-ai-content]]).

Synthesis: communicators are using AI a lot, but mostly through general tools (ChatGPT, Copilot) rather than vendor-platform AI features. The quality bottleneck is workflow design — taste-preservation at every step rather than at the end.

The AI-feature categories that [[forrester]] explicitly evaluates remain the 2026 buyer checklist for intranet platforms:

- AI-enabled search
- AI assistants and agents (content generation, transformation, governance, process automation)
- AI admin options
- Dashboards and analytics
- Natural language reporting

[[workvivo]]'s [[seer]] launch (2026-05-06) adds AI-powered manager dashboards and theme detection on open-ended feedback ([[workvivo-seer-2026-05-11]]), positioning AI as an action-enabling layer rather than a content-generation surface.
