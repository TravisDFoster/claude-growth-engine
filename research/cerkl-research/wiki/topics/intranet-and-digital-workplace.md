# Intranet and Digital Workplace

## Overview

The platform layer of internal communications and employee experience — intranets, mobile employee apps, digital-workplace tools. Vendors compete on features (publishing, search, social/feed, recognition, surveys), AI integration, integration with the broader workplace stack (Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, HRIS, Slack/Teams), and frontline support.

## Current Thesis

[[forrester]]'s Q2 2026 Wave evaluated 13 vendors and produced a tiered leaderboard (4 Leaders / 6 Strong Performers / 3 Contenders) ([[forrester-wave-intranet-platforms-q2-2026]]). Three analytical themes for the 2026 cycle:

1. **AI investment outpaces adoption.** Vendors invested heavily in AI since the 2024 Wave, but only ~49% of customers actively use vendor AI ([[ai-adoption-gap]]). The AI-feature checklist is now table-stakes for vendor evaluation, but customer pull is uneven.
2. **Frontline capability is now a named evaluation criterion.** Shift management integration, personal device apps, do-not-disturb, task management — explicit on the buyer checklist ([[frontline-communications]]).
3. **Intranets are hard, and practitioners know it.** 29% of communicators name intranet the hardest channel to manage ([[six-internal-comms-trends-2026]]); only 31% name it among their most-effective channels — far behind email at 81%. But more than half still prioritize intranet improvements — they're not abandoning the channel, they're **narrowing scope** to [[evergreen-content-strategy]] (policies, FAQs, how-to guides) while routing time-sensitive content through email and mobile.

[[workvivo]] (owned by [[zoom]]) is a Wave Leader in the 2024 cycle; positioning in the 2026 Wave is not stated in the blog summary (gated report). Workvivo's recent product launch [[seer]] (2026-05-06) post-dates the Wave evaluation period — it strengthens the EX-suite framing without affecting the Wave placement ([[workvivo-seer-2026-05-11]]).
