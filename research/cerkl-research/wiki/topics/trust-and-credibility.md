# Trust and Credibility

## Overview
Whether employees *believe* internal communication — and whether leaders are credible when they communicate. Distinct from [[employee-engagement]] (psychological investment) and [[culture-and-belonging]] (identity/meaning): this topic is specifically about the trust contract between the workforce and the comms it receives. It cuts across [[leadership-communications]], [[change-management-communications]], and [[ic-measurement-and-roi]] (because trust is measurable, and because "can we prove what was communicated?" is now an enterprise-risk question).

Lazy-created 2026-05-29 once three sources converged on it in a single day's ic-trends recap.

## Current Thesis
A clean three-part picture emerged in the 2026 cycle:

1. **Institutional trust is high; communication trust is not.** [[edelman-trust-barometer-2026]]: employers are the *most-trusted* institution (78%), and 81% say the employer should bridge divides — but there's a 75%/44% CEO credibility gap and trust is turning insular (only 22% trust someone meaningfully different). Employees trust "the employer" more than they trust "the CEO" or the comms they send ([[trust-brokering]]).

2. **Employee distrust of IC is rational, not cynical.** [[why-employees-right-not-to-trust-ic]] ([[ghassan-karian]], on [[ipsos-karian-and-box]] data): IC erodes trust through euphemism, fake neutrality, and bad timing ([[ic-credibility-deficit]]). The cure is [[context-delivery]] — radical honesty, "context not reputation management." Crucially, this is *measurable*: candour↔trust is a quantified relationship, and where comms feels authentic, leadership-trust scores hit the 80s–90s. Trust is a **performance variable**, reconciling this topic with [[delivery-as-comms-strategy]].

3. **Information integrity is now an enterprise-level risk.** [[gartner-information-integrity-risk-2026-05-13]]: AI-generated/unverified content is the #1 emerging risk among 337 senior risk leaders (Q1 2026). When risk officers say "can we trust what's communicated, and prove it?" is the top threat, trustworthy + *verifiable* internal messaging becomes a governance requirement, not a nicety.

**The synthesis:** trust is the 2026 battleground — and it favors Cerkl's lane. The counter to [[measurers-vs-builders]] (Cloudflare's claim that measurement is automatable overhead) is precisely that *verifiable, candid, well-targeted delivery* is how trust is built and proven. Governance-by-gatekeeping (Simpplr's AI Control Center, per `shared/competitors.md`) answers the integrity risk with control; Cerkl answers it with proof — delivered, opened, read, by employee.
