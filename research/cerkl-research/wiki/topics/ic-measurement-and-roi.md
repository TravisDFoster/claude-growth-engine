# IC Measurement and ROI

## Overview

How internal-communications teams quantify their impact — readership, engagement, sentiment, business outcomes — and defend budget against finance scrutiny.

## Current Thesis

Benchmark sources for IC measurement stratify by authority tier:

- **Primary-research tier (most citable):** [[gallup]] *State of the Global Workplace* — n=141k+ employed respondents in 2025 ([[state-of-the-global-workplace-2026]]). Defensible headline numbers: 20% engaged globally, 17% engaged frontline, 34% thriving.
- **Analyst tier:** [[forrester]] Wave reports — vendor evaluations and reference-customer interviews ([[forrester-wave-intranet-platforms-q2-2026]]). Strong on category dynamics; weaker on universe-level outcome metrics.
- **Vendor tier (multiple):** [[contactmonkey]] (annual *Internal Email Benchmark Report*), [[workshop]] (annual *Internal Comms Trends Report*), Staffbase, PoliteMail. Large samples but single-vendor data. Strong on channel-specific benchmarks and practitioner-priority signals; should be cited with vendor name and methodology caveat. Sample sizes vary — ContactMonkey gives an n (255k campaigns); Workshop does not.

For blog citation discipline: anchor headline stats with primary-research where possible. Use vendor-tier benchmarks for channel-specific numbers (email open rates, channel-effectiveness ratings) with the vendor explicitly named. Cross-vendor numbers measuring different denominators are not contradictory and shouldn't be presented as such (see [[ai-in-internal-comms]] for an example: 49% vendor-AI adoption (Forrester) vs 73% weekly+ AI use (Workshop)).

### Cross-source patterns worth surfacing in a blog

- **Channel performance gap = engagement gap.** Frontline-heavy industries land at the bottom of *both* Gallup engagement (17% non-remote-capable) and ContactMonkey email opens (Trucking 63%, Aviation 65%). Two views of one structural disadvantage.
- **AI denominator trap.** Forrester 49% (vendor AI) vs Workshop 73% (any AI) — both real, both citable, easy to misrepresent. Useful sidebar in any measurement-themed post.
- **Engagement and wellbeing are diverging in 2025.** Thriving up, engagement down. Most dashboards track only one — argument for measuring both as separate constructs.
