# Frontline Communications

## Overview

Reaching the deskless / non-remote-capable workforce — manufacturing, retail, healthcare, hospitality, field service, warehousing. These workers rarely have corporate email or scheduled screen time, and historically receive a fraction of the communications investment that knowledge workers do.

## Current Thesis

The frontline-engagement gap is real, quantified, and now analyst-validated as a category-defining buyer requirement:

- **Engagement gap (Gallup primary research):** on-site non-remote-capable workers sit at **17% engagement** vs 30% for exclusively-remote workers — a 13-percentage-point structural disadvantage ([[state-of-the-global-workplace-2026]]).
- **Buyer-requirement validation (Forrester analyst-tier):** the Q2 2026 Forrester Wave for intranet platforms names **frontline capabilities as an explicit evaluation criterion**, listing shift management integration, personal device apps, do-not-disturb features, and task management tools as feature expectations ([[forrester-wave-intranet-platforms-q2-2026]]).
- **Email-channel mirror:** in ContactMonkey's 2026 internal-email benchmarks, frontline-heavy industries cluster at the bottom of open rates — Trucking 63%, Aviation 65%, Logistics 67% — vs Legal/Real Estate 86% and Insurance 84% ([[contactmonkey-internal-email-benchmark-2026]]).

For IC blog writing: the frontline gap is the most-defensible single argument in the field, with primary-research, analyst-tier, and vendor-tier triangulation. Don't use older or weaker sources when this trio exists.
