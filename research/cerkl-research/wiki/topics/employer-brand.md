# Employer Brand

## Overview

How organizations define, communicate, and operationalize their value proposition to employees — the employer value proposition (EVP), internal-branding activation, and the alignment between the external talent-market promise and the internal lived experience. Where [[employee-experience]] is the full lived reality of working somewhere, employer brand is the *stated promise* about that reality — and the topic's central problem is the gap between the two.

## Current Thesis

The defining 2026 frame for this topic is [[corporate-wallpaper]] — an EVP fails when it's words employees don't actually live. The first source in the cluster ([[ragan-evp-corporate-wallpaper-lhh-2026-06-10]], [[lhh]] / [[tony-kihl]]) argues the antidote is to run the EVP as a **business transformation / campaign**, not a messaging exercise: listen first, connect to the entire employee journey, enable leaders with cascade scripts, reinforce well past launch, and measure on multi-signal outcomes. This puts employer-brand work inside the [[engagement-to-experience-pivot]] — the EVP only counts if it becomes lived experience, which makes it a delivery-and-measurement problem (Cerkl-adjacent), not a tagline problem.

Open question for the cluster: employer brand currently rests on a single practitioner case. It needs an analyst or primary-research anchor (e.g. EVP-effectiveness data) before any claim here is blog-citable beyond "one company's account."
