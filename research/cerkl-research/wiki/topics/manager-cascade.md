# Manager Cascade

## Overview

The practice of routing key organizational communications down through line managers, who relay messaging to their direct reports and provide local context. The dominant pattern for delivering high-stakes or change-related comms in mid-to-large organizations, because employees report higher trust in their direct managers than in distant executives.

## Current Thesis

Manager cascade is **the single largest priority-vs-effectiveness gap in 2026 IC practice**:

- **56%** of communicators name manager communication their #1 2026 priority.
- **Only 4%** believe managers are very effective at cascading messages.

Source: Workshop *Internal Comms Trends Report* via [[six-internal-comms-trends-2026]]. The 14:1 priority-to-effectiveness ratio is the kind of single-stat anchor that makes for strong blog framing — IC teams know cascade is critical and know it's failing.

The recommended interventions are well-rehearsed (toolkits, leader digests, clearer cascade expectations) but the gap persists, which suggests the problem is structural — managers' incentives, time, and skill development — not just IC tooling.
