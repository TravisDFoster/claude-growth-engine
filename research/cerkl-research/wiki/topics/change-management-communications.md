# Change Management Communications

## Overview

The IC sub-discipline focused on communicating organizational change — restructures, layoffs, M&A, leadership transitions, technology rollouts (including AI), strategy pivots — to employees and adjacent stakeholders. In 2026, "change comms" is dominated by AI-restructure and AI-rollout cycles, and the binding constraint is increasingly the **choreography of delivery** (channel, timing, two-way response, stakeholder acknowledgement) rather than the writing.

## Current Thesis

The May 2026 source cluster locks in three observations:

1. **The 2026 change-comms operating context is the AI-restructure memo cycle.** LinkedIn, GM, GitLab, Meta, Verizon, Klarna-rollback all hit in successive weeks. This is the dominant pattern through Q3 2026, not episodic news ([[ragan-week-in-comms-linkedin-gm-gitlab-2026-05-15]]).
2. **Choreography matters more than writing.** Per Ragan's three-case triangulation, *all three* memos got the framing roughly right; two of three broke at the delivery layer (channel, timing, audience). The thing communicators can fix is the choreography — not the words ([[layoff-comms-choreography]]).
3. **Layoff-as-AI-narrative is the analyst counter-frame.** Gartner's May 2026 survey punctures the "cut to fund AI" CEO playbook: ~80% cut; cuts show **no correlation** with AI ROI. Cite [[people-amplification]] as the analyst-grade pushback ([[gartner-ai-layoffs-no-returns-2026-05-15]]).

The two failure modes worth naming explicitly:

- **[[ai-washing]]** — attributing cuts to AI when the real driver is something else, OR disclaiming AI in a way that reads as defensive (the GitLab case). Anti-pattern.
- **Void-fill** — when the company doesn't name a driver, employees and coverage fill the void with "AI is replacing me" (the GM case). The channel and timing of the message *become* the message.

The Cerkl-positioning thread: **"the way the message is shared is often just as important as the message itself"** ([[ragan-communications]] verbatim) is verbatim "delivery layer not destination" framing. Cerkl's 15-minute-meeting story has been written by IC trade press, on a competitor's editorial real estate.

## Open Questions

- Is there a published quarterly choreography rubric (the way Forrester publishes Waves) — would be useful pressure on the discipline.
- What's the recovery template after a botched layoff? Salesforce's tiered cascade is widely cited as best practice; the May 2026 cases all departed from it in different ways.
- Vertical-specific layoff comms patterns — tech (AI-attributed) vs auto (restructure-attributed) vs retail (cost-attributed) — do the same choreography rules apply?

## Contradictions & Tensions

- Vendor narratives that AI agents enable headcount-replacement collide with the convergent analyst finding (Gartner, Forrester, BCG, McKinsey, MIT Sloan) that AI ROI comes from people investment, not substitution.
- Comms team doesn't always own channel/timing — legal/HR/IT often dictate. The discipline owns the *consequence* even when it doesn't own the *cause*; audit-log delivery (Cerkl's lane) matters precisely for that asymmetry.
