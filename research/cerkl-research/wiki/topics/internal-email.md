# Internal Email

## Overview

Internal-corporate-communications email — newsletters, leadership notes, HR announcements, change comms — sent over corporate email systems (Outlook, Gmail). The largest single comms channel in most enterprises and the most-measured.

## Current Thesis

Internal email is **the most effective single IC channel** as rated by practitioners: 81% of communicators name email their most effective channel, far ahead of in-person events (35%) and intranets (31%) ([[six-internal-comms-trends-2026]]).

**2026 benchmark substrate is paired vendor anchors, both single-channel and methodologically caveated.** Two competing vendor benchmarks dropped within 24 hours in May 2026:

- **[[politemail]] / [[ragan-communications]]**: 66% unique open, 7% click ([[ragan-email-benchmarks-report-2026-05-12]]; ~2B emails / ~11M employees / 10 S&P sectors; Outlook-only telemetry)
- **[[contactmonkey]]**: 76% open, 9% click ([[contactmonkey-internal-email-benchmark-2026]]; 255K campaigns across 20+ industries)

The 10pp open-rate gap between the two reflects methodology variance (Outlook-only vs Outlook+Gmail; preview-pane handling; DL hygiene) more than a real underlying performance gap. Both anchors carry the [[open-rate-inflation]] caveat — Outlook preview-pane "opens," automated security-scanner pixel hits, and stale-distribution-list noise all inflate the headline number. PoliteMail acknowledges this in its own content; ContactMonkey does not visibly. Either way, both numbers sit above external/marketing email's typical ~20–30% open / ~2–3% click — but the premium reflects audience captivity (employees obligated to open work email) more than message quality.

**Open rates decline with org size** (89% at 1–50 emp → 73% at 10,000+ emp), suggesting broad sends to large workforces lose effectiveness — tailwind for segmentation/personalization. **Frontline-heavy industries** (Trucking 63%, Aviation 65%, Logistics 67%) cluster at the bottom across vendors, consistent with the broader frontline engagement gap ([[engagement-by-work-location]], [[state-of-the-global-workplace-2026]]).

**Citation discipline caveat:** all 2026 benchmark data is single-vendor ([[politemail]], [[contactmonkey]], [[workshop]], Staffbase). No third-party independent benchmark exists yet. When citing in blog writing, name the vendor and methodology. Per the *Blog Post Research Mode* rule, competitor-sourced stats (ContactMonkey, PoliteMail) are excluded from external publication.

**The structural absence of a [[cross-channel-benchmark]] is the most important fact about IC measurement as of 2026.** Single-channel vendors structurally cannot publish one (no Teams/Slack/intranet/SMS data). A cross-channel benchmark would change the unit of measurement from "per-channel open rate" to "audience-level reach and engagement across the channels the employee actually uses" — the asymmetric content position for a delivery + measurement layer with HRIS sync.

## Contradictions & Tensions

- **PoliteMail 66% vs ContactMonkey 76% headline open rates.** Not a real contradiction — methodology variance (Outlook-only vs Outlook+Gmail; preview-pane handling; DL hygiene assumptions). Calling out as a tension because the gap is large enough that anyone citing "the 2026 IC email open rate" without naming the vendor will mislead.
- **Vendor-acknowledged methodology gaps coexist with the headline number being the most-cited metric.** PoliteMail's own content names the inflation problem; the headline 66% number remains the report's marketing anchor. [[open-rate-inflation]] is the resolution: cite the secondary metrics (open-attention, time-in-email, CTOR), not the headline.
