# Internal Email

## Overview

Internal-corporate-communications email — newsletters, leadership notes, HR announcements, change comms — sent over corporate email systems (Outlook, Gmail). The largest single comms channel in most enterprises and the most-measured.

## Current Thesis

Internal email is **the most effective single IC channel** as rated by practitioners: 81% of communicators name email their most effective channel, far ahead of in-person events (35%) and intranets (31%) ([[six-internal-comms-trends-2026]]). The benchmark data is consistent — internal email sits at much higher engagement than external/marketing email: ContactMonkey's 2026 data shows **76% average open rate** and **9% average click rate** ([[contactmonkey-internal-email-benchmark-2026]]), vs marketing email's typical ~20–30% open / ~2–3% click. The premium reflects audience captivity (employees obligated to open work email), not necessarily message quality.

Open rates **decline with org size** (89% at 1–50 emp → 73% at 10,000+ emp), suggesting broad sends to large workforces lose effectiveness — a tailwind for segmentation/personalization approaches. Industry variance is wide; frontline-heavy industries (Trucking 63%, Aviation 65%, Logistics 67%) cluster at the bottom, consistent with the broader frontline engagement gap ([[engagement-by-work-location]], [[state-of-the-global-workplace-2026]]).

**Citation discipline caveat:** most benchmark data is single-vendor ([[contactmonkey]], [[workshop]], Staffbase, PoliteMail). No third-party independent benchmark exists yet. When citing in blog writing, name the vendor and methodology.
