# Employee Engagement

## Overview

How organizations measure, sustain, and improve employees' psychological investment in their work and employer. Engagement is the most-cited IC outcome metric and the dominant framing for "is IC working?" conversations.

## Current Thesis

As of 2025 data ([[state-of-the-global-workplace-2026]]), global engagement is **declining** for the second consecutive year: 23% (2023) → 21% (2024) → 20% (2025). The post-COVID peak is over. The decline is most visible in the largest cohort — on-site, non-remote-capable / frontline workers at 17%. Regional dispersion is wide enough (US/Canada 31% vs Europe 12%) that any unqualified global engagement number is misleading.

For IC strategy: the decline rebuts the comfortable narrative that engagement programs are working — they're not, at least at the aggregate level. The opportunity is in the frontline cohort, where the gap to remote / hybrid workers (13 percentage points) is structurally addressable through better-targeted communications.

**2026 update — engagement-under-change is now contested.** [[qualtrics-change-engagement-2026-05-21]] claims employees experiencing more change are *more* engaged (n=33,831, [[goldilocks-zone-of-change]] framing). The dominant counter-frame is [[change-fatigue]] — Gallagher's State of the Sector 2026 ranks fatigue as the #2 IC barrier, [[perceptyx]]'s 20M-response data names leadership credibility (not change exposure) as the engagement driver, [[prosci]]'s [[change-saturation]] model says the relationship inverts above a threshold. The likely synthesis: Qualtrics' effect holds for *investment-coded* change with steady [[listening-cadence]] (87/44 engagement gap), inverts above saturation. **[[abhijit-bhaduri]]** layers in a methodological warning: constrained labor mobility in 2025–2026 may make engagement scores misleading.

## Open Questions

- Is the 2023→2025 decline real, or instrument-specific to Gallup Q12? Cross-check against Glint, Qualtrics, Perceptyx.
- Which countries / regions are driving the global decline most?
- Has any IC-specific intervention demonstrably reversed engagement decline in a published case study?
- Is there a longitudinal study that tracks the same employees across a multi-year change cycle and resolves the Qualtrics-vs-Gallagher directional disagreement?

## Contradictions & Tensions

- **Change-engagement direction is contested.** Qualtrics says more change → more engagement (within the Goldilocks zone, conditional on listening cadence). Gallagher, Perceptyx, Prosci say change fatigue is now the dominant driver. The likely synthesis is conditional, but no current study captures the full curve.
- **Engagement-score validity under constrained mobility.** [[abhijit-bhaduri]]: persistently low turnover may make engagement scores misleading — high scores may reflect lack of options, not psychological investment.
