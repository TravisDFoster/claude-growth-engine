## Summary

Gartner's Q1 2026 HR survey quantifies the manager-bandwidth squeeze that anchors the 2026 manager-cascade-is-broken thesis. Headline: **47% of managers say they're working harder than a year ago.** Pairs with two adjacent claims (66% of managers say people-management is primary duty; ~25% of manager time on employee personal/emotional issues). For IC research: explains *why* the [[manager-cascade]] is failing — cascade carriers are out of bandwidth — and quantifies what Karian narrates qualitatively in [[karian-middle-manager-burnout-2026-06-04]] and Gallup measures in [[gallup-span-of-control-2026]].

## Key Takeaways

- **The manager-bandwidth squeeze is analyst-tier confirmed.** Three independent 2026 sources (Gartner Q1 2026 HR survey + [[gallup-span-of-control-2026]] + Karian/IKB benchmark) converge on the same finding from different methodologies. The thesis (managers are out of bandwidth) is past the "single-vendor data point" stage.
- **People-management is the *named* primary duty for 2/3 of managers** — yet ~25% of their time goes to emotional-issue handling, ~40% (per Gallup) to individual-contributor work. The actual *coaching/feedback/decision-making* slice is shrinking even as the role's official mandate stays "people management."
- **The 47% is a comparison-to-self stat**, not a comparison-to-other-roles stat. Workload escalation is self-reported, which means the buyer-room rebuttal is "compared to what?" — but the consistency across 3 independent sources blunts that critique.
