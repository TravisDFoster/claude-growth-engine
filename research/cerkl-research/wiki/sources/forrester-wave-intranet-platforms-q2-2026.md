# The Forrester Wave: Intranet Platforms, Q2 2026 — Our Evaluation Of 13 Key Vendors

**Author:** [[cheryl-mckinnon]] (Principal Analyst, [[forrester]])  |  **Published:** 2026-04-14 (Wave report 2026-04-09)  |  **Source type:** Analyst-firm blog summary of gated Wave report  |  **URL:** https://www.forrester.com/blogs/the-forrester-wave-intranet-platforms-q2-2026-our-evaluation-of-13-key-vendors/

## Summary

Cheryl McKinnon's blog summary of Forrester's Q2 2026 Wave on Intranet Platforms. 13 vendors evaluated across 28 criteria, sorted into 4 Leaders / 6 Strong Performers / 3 Contenders. The blog **does not name individual vendors** (full report is client-gated), but it surfaces two analytically valuable findings: (1) **vendor AI adoption is lagging** — only ~half of reference customers (17 of 35) actively use their vendor's AI features despite heavy vendor investment since the 2024 Wave; (2) **frontline-worker capabilities are now a named criterion category**, reflecting the structural importance of the frontline cohort in intranet evaluation.

## Key Takeaways

- **AI investment is outpacing customer adoption.** Forrester's reference-customer interviews show **17 of 35 (~49%) actively use their vendor's AI** — the headline gap in the 2026 Wave.
- **The evaluation criteria themselves are the most-actionable read.** Forrester's named categories (AI-enabled search, AI agents for content generation/transformation/governance/automation, AI admin options, NL reporting, frontline) are a de-facto buyer checklist for intranet-platform evaluation in 2026.
- **Frontline-worker capabilities are now a top-line criterion** — shift management integration, personal device apps, do-not-disturb, task management. Vendors who lack this surface are explicitly down-weighted.
- **3 tiers, not 4** — no "Challengers" tier listed in this Wave (4 Leaders / 6 Strong Performers / 3 Contenders). A more concentrated leaderboard than typical.
- **No vendor names in the blog.** Full report is client-gated. For blog citations, Forrester's *findings* can be paraphrased from the blog but specific Leader/Strong-Performer attributions require the gated report.
