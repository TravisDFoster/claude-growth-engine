# How leaders can communicate AI-related changes as routine for employees

**Author/Publisher:** Sean Devlin / [[ragan-communications]]   |   **Published:** 2025-10-09   |   **Source type:** Trade-press article (analyst-quote pickup)   |   **URL:** https://www.ragan.com/ai-hr-comms-gartner-2026/

## Summary

Trade-press writeup of [[mark-whittle]]'s (VP Advisory, [[gartner]]) public framing of Gartner's *Predicts 2026: Top Predictions to Inform 2026 Comms Strategies* report (doc 7160430, paywalled). The article does not enumerate Gartner's five forward predictions verbatim — those live in other pickups (Sociabble, Stuart Bruce PR Futurist). What this article *does* anchor is Whittle's prescriptive framing: change-leadership-as-comms-discipline, a three-step process for managing change-comms, the 2008-09 financial-crisis analogy, and a closing connection between layoff-comms quality and employer brand. Surfaced via the 2026-05-27 [[ragan-gartner-2026-comms-predictions-2025-10-09]] [the source itself] and the same-day Cerkl deep-dive on the 75%-by-2028 chatbot prediction (the deep-dive lives at `../ic-trends/deepdives/gartner-chatbots-over-intranets-2026-05-27.md` and is not separately ingested here).

## Key Takeaways

- **Whittle's three-step change process** ([[change-leadership-three-step]]) — *Acknowledge the journey / Regulate discomfort / Train intuition* — is the central deliverable of this article. Promoted to its own concept page.
- **"Effective leadership today is change leadership"** is the verbatim quote that will get the most downstream re-citation in Cerkl writing about manager-cascade and leadership-as-comms-channel.
- **2008-09 financial-crisis analogy** — Whittle frames the current AI-restructure cycle as a parallel to the 2008-09 cycle: leaders who failed to communicate then left employees anxious about their futures. Useful historical anchor for any Cerkl piece framing AI-restructure as a comms-during-crisis problem.
- **Gartner's "net new jobs" stance is in tension with Gartner's own May 2026 AI-layoff finding** — this article holds the position that AI will create more net jobs than it eliminates, while [[gartner-ai-layoffs-no-returns-2026-05-15]] (Poitevin, 2026-05-05) finds 80% of AI-driven cuts deliver no ROI. Same firm, different posture; same period. Flagged in Contradicts.
- **Closing layoff-comms paragraph ties memo quality to employer brand** — *"Such messages significantly impact employer brand perception during major transitions."* Direct tie to [[layoff-comms-choreography]] and [[layoff-memo-subgenre-2026-05-21]]; Gartner is now on the record explicitly linking layoff-comms quality to brand consequence.

## Notable Claims

- **Whittle's three-step change framework** (verbatim from the article):
  - *Acknowledge the journey* — recognize constant change as normal and update employees regularly.
  - *Regulate discomfort* — use transparent communication to address employee concerns about transformation.
  - *Train intuition* — build organizational reflexes through comprehensive change communications that help employees see themselves in the narrative.
- **Whittle on leadership cadence:** *"When change intensity spikes, so do the expectations of our leaders."*
- **Whittle on culture-embedding into AI:** *"Whatever set of cultural attributes you're using to drive performance, employees need to understand the organization's culture."* Implies AI implementations must be intentionally connected to organizational mission and values.
- **AI velocity claim:** *"Things that weren't possible even 90 days ago are now happening with AI"* — used to justify the "reshape work fundamentally" framing.
- **Gartner net-jobs position:** AI will create more net jobs than it eliminates. (Stated as Gartner's position, no underlying data cited in the article.)
- **Layoff-comms-as-brand-driver:** Layoff messages communicated transparently within company values, emphasizing who is affected and the organization's direction, *"significantly impact employer brand perception during major transitions."*
- **Tactical prescription for managers:** "One-pagers and FAQs can help reduce employee anxiety by showing day-to-day job changes." Day-to-day-role-evolution comms artifact.
