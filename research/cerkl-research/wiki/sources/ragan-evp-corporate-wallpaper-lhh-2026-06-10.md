# Q&A: Building an employee experience that isn't just 'corporate wallpaper'

**Author/Publisher:** [[sean-devlin]] / [[ragan-communications]]  |  **Published:** 2026-06-10  |  **Source type:** practitioner Q&A  |  **URL:** https://www.ragan.com/employee-value-proposition-lhh/

## Summary

[[tony-kihl]], director of global internal communications at [[lhh]], walks through a ~year-long employee-value-proposition (EVP) launch built around the brand idea of "a beautiful working world." The thesis: an EVP fails if it's "corporate wallpaper" — a set of words employees don't actually live. Kihl's answer was to treat the EVP as a **business transformation / campaign**, not a messaging exercise: listen first, connect it to the whole employee journey (onboarding, career, recognition, manager conversations), enable leaders with cascade scripts, and reinforce across channels long after launch. A clean practitioner case for the [[engagement-to-experience-pivot]] in action, and a concrete [[manager-cascade]] *enable*-side example.

## Key Takeaways

- **"Corporate wallpaper" is the named failure mode:** the gap between the EVP an org markets externally and what colleagues actually experience internally. "We did not want it to feel like corporate wallpaper. We wanted it to feel real and human." See [[corporate-wallpaper]].
- **EVP as campaign, not announcement:** "We definitely treated it more as a campaign than a set of words or one communication." Reinforcement mattered more than the launch — explicitly rejecting "we sent an email, it is live, check the box."
- **Listen first for authenticity + buy-in:** surveys + English and localized roundtables across 60 countries to find what colleagues already valued and where the gaps were; participation in shaping the EVP drove adoption.
- **IC as "connector and translator":** partnered with HR (onboarding/career/recognition/manager enablement), IT/digital (access + the *Beautiful Working World Hub*), and leadership.
- **Leadership enablement via cascade scripts ([[manager-cascade]] enable-side):** "We gave them talking points and cascade decks… The idea was: say this, ask this, listen for this, do this." A working instance of equipping managers rather than routing around them.
- **Multi-signal outcome measurement ([[ic-measurement-and-roi]]):** comms metrics (town-hall participation, intranet/Viva Engage activity, video views) *and* people metrics (engagement-survey movement, willingness to recommend, internal mobility, onboarding feedback, employer reputation). "There is no one perfect dashboard."
- **Sustaining energy is the hard part:** the first moment gets attention; post-launch reinforcement "takes discipline" against competing priorities.

## Notable Claims

Single-company, self-reported figures — cite as a practitioner case, not a benchmark.

- Town-hall engagement rose from a typical **50–60%** to **90–100%** for the value-proposition-tied launch ([source](https://www.ragan.com/employee-value-proposition-lhh/)).
- Engagement-survey results moved **20–30% higher** in some areas (happier at LHH, more willing to recommend, can see their career staying).
- LHH supports colleagues in **60 countries**; localization/translation was a core listening requirement.
