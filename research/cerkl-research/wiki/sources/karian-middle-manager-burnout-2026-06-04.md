# Middle managers are becoming the most burned-out role in the western economy

**Author/Publisher:** [[ghassan-karian]] / isitworking (Substack) | **Published:** 2026-06-04 | **Source type:** opinion essay (in personal capacity) | **URL:** https://isitworking.substack.com/p/why-middle-managers-are-becoming

## Summary

[[ghassan-karian]] argues that middle management has become **structurally impossible** — a role-design failure, not a resilience problem. Backed by [[ipsos-karian-and-box]]'s employee-experience benchmark database (described in-article as "one of the largest in Europe") showing work-life-balance scores deteriorated for middle managers between 2023–2025 while executives' improved. Names five root causes (responsibility without authority, hybrid coordination load, emotional absorption, admin bloat, outdated role design) and prescribes redesign (reduce span of control, strip admin, real decision rights, AI for reporting/scheduling, fewer + clearer priorities), not wellness theatre. Coins the [[manager-burnout-structural-thesis]] frame within the wiki.

## Key Takeaways

- **The role itself is the problem, not the human in it.** Karian explicitly rejects "another wellbeing initiative aimed at individuals" — argues the fix is *fewer simultaneous priorities, cleaner decision rights, and protection of managerial capacity*. Aligns with the structural-redesign half of [[manager-cascade]] (the toolkit half is the surface-level fix that hasn't moved the 56/4 gap).
- **The 2023→2025 work-life-balance divergence between middle managers and executives** is Karian's load-bearing empirical claim. From the IKB benchmark: while executives' WLB scores improved, middle managers' deteriorated. The center is being squeezed structurally — not incidentally.
- **Hybrid + emotional + admin = the three workload-inflation vectors.** Hybrid multiplied coordination load (Karian's 2023 IKB research); emotional absorption is unpaid and untracked; admin is "eating their role alive." Together these account for why managers spend less than half their time actually managing.
- **The prescription is anti-resilience-training.** Karian explicitly names "pep talks" and "resilience training" as wrong answers. Worth pairing with the [[goldilocks-zone-of-change]] / [[change-fatigue]] tension — wellness initiatives without structural change just shift the failure onto the individual.
- **Personal capacity, not corporate publication.** This is Karian writing personally on isitworking — *not* citable as official Ipsos Karian and Box research. The corporate research surface is [ipsoskarianandbox.com](https://ipsoskarianandbox.com/) and the IoIC × Karian *IC Index*. Useful as "credible voice from the IC research industry," not as institutional research.

## Notable Claims

- [[ipsos-karian-and-box]]'s employee-experience benchmark database shows middle managers reporting significantly lower scores on **manageable workload, influence over decisions, and wellbeing** (https://isitworking.substack.com/p/why-middle-managers-are-becoming).
- "By 2025, while executives report marked improvement, the centre remains comparatively squeezed" on work-life-balance scores. (Source: IKB benchmark; same URL.)
- "Multiple HR and wellbeing studies show that managers handle the majority of employee wellbeing issues — despite lacking training, time, or organisational support." (Unattributed plural studies; pair with [[gartner-managers-working-harder-2026-04-29]] which puts ~25% of manager time on employee personal/emotional issues.)
- Karian directly names Bain & Co. and McKinsey as sources of "one-size-fits-all" span-of-control prescriptions; pushes back without naming a specific report. (Connects to the partial-contradiction tension with [[mckinsey-rewired-2-2026-05-21]] — *Rewired 2.0* prescribes manager-as-orchestrator-of-agents, which Karian would likely classify as more admin on top of an already-overloaded role.)
- **Provenance note:** the article does **not** contain the "12.1 direct reports / +50% since 2013" stat that circulated alongside it on 2026-06-04 ic-trends signal. That figure is from [[gallup-span-of-control-2026]]. The two are commonly conflated — keep separate when citing.

Karian would likely treat this as additional admin layered onto an already-collapsing role — unless McKinsey's prescription includes the structural redesign Karian demands. The two are theoretically compatible but rhetorically opposed.
- Partial tension with vendor "enable managers" stances (e.g., Simpplr — see [[route-around-vs-enable]]): Karian's prescription is *redesign the role first, then enable* — vendor enablement on top of a structurally broken role accelerates the burnout, not mitigates it.
