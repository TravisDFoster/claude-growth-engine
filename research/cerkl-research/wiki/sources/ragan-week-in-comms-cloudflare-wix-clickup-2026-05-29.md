# Cloudflare CEO says company doesn't need 'measurers'; Wix chief says job cuts are directly due to AI

**Author/Publisher:** [[sean-devlin]] / [[ragan-communications]] | **Published:** 2026-05-29 | **Source type:** article (Week in Comms column) | **URL:** https://www.ragan.com/the-week-in-comms-cloudflare-wix-clickup/

## Summary
The 2026-05-29 "Week in Comms" triangulates three CEO layoff messages that all rationalize cuts through AI. [[cloudflare]]'s [[matthew-prince]] (WSJ op-ed) draws an explicit "builders and sellers" vs "measurers" hierarchy and names AI as the thing making measurers — including most of his marketing and finance teams — obsolete. [[wix]]'s [[avishai-abrahami]] (~20% cut) frames the cut as a move to a flatter, faster org demanded by the AI "rewiring." [[clickup]]'s [[zeb-jones]] (22% cut "despite the business being the strongest it's ever been") lands the most human-centered framing — "the future is not fewer people… it's different work, new roles." Direct successor to [[ragan-week-in-comms-linkedin-gm-gitlab-2026-05-15]] in the [[layoff-comms-choreography]] reference set.

## Key Takeaways
- A CEO publicly declaring an entire job category ("measurers") obsolete is a tone-axis escalation beyond the euphemistic 2026 norm — and a direct rhetorical attack on the measurement discipline ([[measurers-vs-builders]]).
- AI-attribution of layoffs continues to harden as a convention; Wix and ClickUp both pin cuts to AI ([[ai-washing]], [[ai-restructure-memo-subgenre]]).
- "Flatter org / fewer layers" is becoming the structural justification language for cuts (Wix) — and it reshapes the manager-cascade layer comms relies on ([[manager-cascade]]).
- ClickUp's "different work, new roles, better rewards" tone is the closest of the three to [[people-amplification]] — optimism centered on the people doing the work.

## Notable Claims
- **Cloudflare:** layoffs occurred "despite record growth"; Prince's WSJ op-ed states "builders and sellers" are safe but AI is coming for "measurers." Verbatim: "We significantly reduced our marketing team, which, like in most companies, was teeming with measurers." Middle managers cut because "AI allows us to have more direct reports per manager." (https://www.ragan.com/the-week-in-comms-cloudflare-wix-clickup/)
- **Wix:** ~20% of staff cut; Abrahami (post on X) calls AI the largest "rewiring" in tech in decades; moving to "a structure with fewer levels… a smaller number of people"; "We are choosing to compete."
- **ClickUp:** 22% of workforce cut despite "strongest it's ever been"; Jones cites a "100x company" outlook and the emergence of new roles like "Agent Managers."
