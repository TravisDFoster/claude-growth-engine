# 9 lessons from building an AI-integrated comms function

**Author/Publisher:** [[paul-gennaro]] (CCO, [[new-york-life]]) / [[ragan-communications]]  |  **Published:** 2026-06-11  |  **Source type:** practitioner article  |  **URL:** https://www.ragan.com/9-lessons-from-building-an-ai-integrated-comms-function/

## Summary

[[paul-gennaro]], chief communications officer at [[new-york-life]] and advisor to Ragan's Center for AI Strategy, recounts 22 months of integrating AI into a Fortune-100 corporate-comms function, structured against a **nine-item enterprise-AI-adoption checklist** from [[institute-for-public-relations|IPR]]'s 2026 generative-AI research (presented by [[samantha-stark]] of [[phyusion]]). The throughline: "AI success is less about tools and more about how organizations operate." This is the strongest practitioner-tier operating-model account in the vault for [[ai-integrated-comms-function]] — and an outcome-measurement counterpoint to the [[measurers-vs-builders]] attack.

## Key Takeaways

- **The operating model beats the tool.** All nine lessons are about culture, leadership, governance, and measurement — not features. AI is "infrastructure enabling us to be more strategic, not a threat to creative work."
- **The change-narrative gap, quantified (IPR):** only **37% of organizations have clearly communicated a change story for AI adoption.** This is a near-twin of [[gallup-engagement-strategies-2026]]'s **26%** "clear AI plan" finding — two independent 2026 sources pinning the rollout-comms vacuum at roughly a quarter-to-a-third of orgs. Direct empirical support for the [[ai-rollout-comms]] / [[enablement-illusion]] chain from the IC practitioner side.
- **Measure outcomes, not tool usage (Lesson 8):** metrics should capture "time reallocated from drafting to strategic planning, increased capacity… internal client satisfaction." This is exactly the *action-driving measurement* that rebuts [[measurers-vs-builders]] — measurement as builder-work, not vanity dashboard.
- **Governance reframed as a trust issue (Lesson 6):** "this is not just a risk issue. It is a trust issue." NY Life built an *Editorial Guidelines GPT* embedding voice/accuracy/brand standards into the workflow so guardrails "make the work trustworthy" rather than slow it.
- **Peers beat mandates (Lesson 4):** "adoption scales faster through peers than through mandates" — champions/early-adopters as the accelerator. Echoes [[manager-as-ai-champion]]'s modeling mechanism one level up (leadership/peer modeling rather than manager-to-report).
- **Humans define the final product (Lesson 2):** "AI accelerates production, but humans define the final product" — the [[co-piloting]] prescription stated by a sitting CCO.

## Notable Claims

All statistics are NY Life internal quarterly-AI-survey figures unless attributed to IPR; sizes not disclosed. Cite as a single-company practitioner account, not benchmark data.

- **37%** of organizations have clearly communicated an AI-adoption change story (IPR 2026 research) ([source](https://www.ragan.com/9-lessons-from-building-an-ai-integrated-comms-function/)).
- **90%** of the NY Life comms team use AI multiple times a day (most recent quarterly survey).
- **56%** named custom-GPT / Claude Projects / Copilot Agents best-practices as their top 2026 training priority — "moved well past the basics and into… building."
- **76%** took on additional projects due to AI-driven efficiency; **78%** reported increased ability to focus on strategic work (Q1 survey).
- **78%** cited the AI implementation as the reason their job satisfaction increased.
- Five AI "Blue Sky Initiatives" underway; one team built an agentic media-monitoring → Copilot → crisis-comms-draft pipeline that "compresses monitoring, synthesis and drafting into minutes."

## Contradicts

- **Partial tension with [[ai-anxious-buyer]].** Gennaro's account is AI-optimistic from inside the comms team (78% more strategic, 78% higher job-sat *because* of AI) — the opposite valence to the displacement-anxiety frame ([[hr-reporter-ai-risks-ic-2026-05-18]], [[simpplr]] survey). Not a factual contradiction: the reconciliation is that *structured, human-centric, leadership-modeled* adoption converts anxiety into agency, while top-down/tool-first adoption produces the anxiety. The "finite amount of time to master AI" line shows the urgency that anxiety and agency share. Hold both; the difference is the operating model.
