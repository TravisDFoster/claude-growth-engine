# Ragan: The Week in Comms (LinkedIn, GM, GitLab)

**Author/Publisher:** Sean Devlin / [[ragan-communications]]  |  **Published:** 2026-05-15  |  **Source type:** Trade-pub editorial / case-study triangulation  |  **URL:** https://www.ragan.com/the-week-in-comms-linkedin-gm-gitlab/

## Summary

Ragan's weekly editorial puts three layoff/restructure memos from one week (LinkedIn under Daniel Shapero; GM IT; GitLab under Bill Staples) side by side. Read together, they show that *content* of the message matters less than the *channel and choreography* by which it lands: strategic framing can't survive a 15-minute-notice scripted call (GM), and even credible AI-era candor breaks if it doesn't acknowledge employees as stakeholders (GitLab). The Cerkl-relevance is direct: *"the way the message is shared is often just as important as the message itself"* — verbatim "delivery layer not destination" framing, on a competitor's editorial real estate. This source is the empirical anchor for [[layoff-comms-choreography]] and the case-study half of [[ai-rollout-comms]].

## Key Takeaways

- **The three cases differ on four IC-controlled axes:** tone, channel, timing, stakeholder-transparency. Reading them together is the point.
- **GM is the literal anti-Cerkl case study.** A 15-minute calendar invite to a scripted readout — the channel/timing *became* the AI-replacement narrative; the company didn't claim AI replacement at all.
- **GitLab's "this is not AI cost-cutting" disclaimer backfired.** Industry coverage called it "[[ai-washing]]" anyway. Anti-pattern: the disclaimer reads as defensive.
- **LinkedIn's memo was credibly framed but formulaic** — "reinvent how we work / agile teams" is now overused enough to read as template. Anti-pattern flagged by Ragan.
- **The 2026 AI-restructure memo cycle is the dominant change-comms operating context** through Q3 2026 (Verizon, Meta, LinkedIn, GitLab, GM, Klarna rollback all in weeks of dailies).

## Notable Claims

- **LinkedIn cut ~875 roles (~5% of 17,500)** — single all-employee memo at 7:00 AM Pacific, manager 1:1 cascade ([IBTimes UK reprint of Shapero memo](https://www.ibtimes.co.uk/linkedin-announces-major-layoffs-global-restructuring-1796682)).
- **GM eliminated ~500–600 IT roles in Austin + Warren** via email scheduling a virtual meeting **15 minutes out**, then a scripted HR readout, then disconnect ([CNBC 2026-05-12](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/12/gm-layoffs-ai-severance.html)).
- **GitLab cut ~7% of the workforce + 30% of country footprint** announced via public CEO blog ([GitLab Act 2](https://about.gitlab.com/blog/gitlab-act-2/)); shares fell ~8% after-hours.
- **GM employee reaction:** *"No appreciation or empathy. No questions. Nothing"* — data analyst with 10+ years at the company ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/12/gm-layoffs-ai-severance.html)).
- **Ragan's central diagnostic on GM:** *"When layoffs are tightly scripted and don't answer employee questions, they'll fill in the blanks themselves… interpreting vague messaging as 'you're replacing us with AI.'"*

## Contradicts

- **The "delivery-layer destinations" framing of Staffbase / Simpplr / LumApps** — Ragan's diagnosis (channel and timing are the message) is incompatible with a homepage-banner-as-resolution playbook.
- **Vendor narratives that AI agents enable layoffs without comms cost** — every case here proves the layoff comms moment is the most-read internal message of the year and demands receipt-confirmed delivery.
