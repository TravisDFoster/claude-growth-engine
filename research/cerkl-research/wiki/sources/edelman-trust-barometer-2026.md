# Edelman Trust Barometer 2026: employers most-trusted, but trust is turning insular

**Author/Publisher:** [[sean-devlin]] / [[ragan-communications]] (pickup of [[edelman]] Trust Barometer 2026) | **Published:** 2026-02-12 | **Source type:** survey pickup | **URL:** https://www.ragan.com/by-the-numbers-edelman-trust-barometer-2026/

> **Date note:** The recap that surfaced this item flagged the date as uncertain; on fetch it is a **2026-02-12** Ragan pickup, not a late-May item. Recorded accurately here.

## Summary
The 2026 [[edelman]] Trust Barometer finds employers remain the most-trusted institution (**78%** trust their employer to do the right thing — highest of any entity), and **81%** say the employer is responsible for bridging divides. But there's a **75%/44%** gap between expecting CEOs to bridge trust divides and believing they do it well, and overall trust is turning **insular** (only **22%** trust someone meaningfully different from them). The piece reframes trust as a **productivity** variable and positions IC as the org's de facto [[trust-brokering|trust broker]].

## Key Takeaways
- Employees distinguish "the employer" (trusted, 78%) from "the CEO" (29-point credibility gap) — leadership-comms credibility lives at the individual-leader layer ([[leadership-communications]]).
- Trust is *experienced and modeled*, not announced — "announcing trust into existence isn't a thing." A delivery/behavior claim, not a messaging-volume claim ([[delivery-as-comms-strategy]]).
- Trust now has a measurable productivity cost: 42% would put in less effort under a leader with different beliefs; 34% would switch departments to avoid one.
- Constructive-dialogue training (80% say it builds trust) is an IC-promotable lever — ties to [[manager-cascade]].

## Notable Claims
- **78%** trust their employer to do the right thing — the highest of any institution in the study. (https://www.ragan.com/by-the-numbers-edelman-trust-barometer-2026/)
- **75%** say CEOs are obligated to help bridge trust divides; only **44%** do it well — a **29-point** credibility gap.
- **81%** say the employer is responsible for bridging divides.
- **22%** currently trust someone meaningfully different from them (insularity); trust drivers = openness (49%) and transparency (46%) about differences.
- Trust-brokering datasets: 82% want orgs to promote a shared culture; 81% say mixed-values teams build trust; 80% say constructive-dialogue training promotes trust.
- Productivity cost: **42%** would put in less effort under a politically-different leader; **34%** would switch departments rather than report to a manager with different beliefs.
