# Cisco comms leader on the real problem with AI-generated content

**Author:** Isis Simpson-Mersha  |  **Publisher:** [[pr-daily]]  |  **Published:** 2026-05-08  |  **Source type:** Trade-publication interview  |  **URL:** https://www.prdaily.com/cisco-comms-leader-on-the-real-problem-with-ai-generated-content/

## Summary

PR Daily interview with [[austin-roth-eagle]], Head of AI Acceleration Office at [[cisco]] (formerly CEO communications strategist at Cisco and VMware). Roth-Eagle's central argument: the failure mode of AI-generated content isn't capability — it's **taste and voice**. AI workflows produce useful output when human judgment is inserted at every step, not just at final review. The piece anchors "AI slop" as IC-citable shorthand for the failure mode and argues the highest-leverage skill is *deep domain expertise + AI fluency together*.

## Key Takeaways

- **"AI slop" is what happens when taste is absent.** Roth-Eagle's headline framing — and a citable shorthand for the failure mode IC teams should worry about.
- **Workflow decomposition, not end-stage review.** The right pattern is breaking content production into small steps with human judgment at each one. Checklists at publication time are too late.
- **Voice is the differentiator that doesn't scale via AI.** "Your voice is the most important element" — useful as a counterweight to "AI will replace IC writers" narratives.
- **The highest-leverage IC operator combines domain depth + AI fluency.** Not just AI fluency, not just IC expertise. The combination.
- **No quantitative data.** Practitioner-tier source; cite for argument and quotes, not statistics.

## Notable Claims

All quotes from https://www.prdaily.com/cisco-comms-leader-on-the-real-problem-with-ai-generated-content/.

**On taste:** "The biggest thing AI is still missing is taste… taste, judgment and voice are still critical." — Austin Roth-Eagle

**On voice:** "AI-generated content without voice or taste is bad… Your voice is the most important element." — Austin Roth-Eagle

**On workflow design:** "It's less about a checklist at the end and more about breaking the workflow into small, specific steps." — Austin Roth-Eagle

**On who succeeds with AI:** "The best results come from people with deep domain expertise and strong AI fluency." — Austin Roth-Eagle
