# Gartner: AI Layoffs Don't Deliver Returns

**Author/Publisher:** Helen Poitevin / [[gartner]]  |  **Published:** 2026-05-05  |  **Source type:** Analyst press release  |  **URL:** https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2026-05-05-gartner-says-autonomous-business-and-artificial-intelligence-layoffs-may-create-budget-room-but-do-not-deliver-returns

## Summary

Gartner uses its own primary survey data to puncture the dominant 2026 CEO narrative ("cut headcount to fund AI / capture AI returns"): ~80% of organizations piloting or deploying autonomous-business capabilities cut headcount, but workforce reduction shows **no correlation with AI ROI** — the high-return companies cut at nearly the same rate as the low-return ones. Distinguished VP Analyst Helen Poitevin frames it as "**[[people-amplification]], not people elimination**" — ROI comes from investing in skills, roles, and operating models that let humans guide autonomous systems. For IC teams messaging AI-driven layoffs in this five-day window (LinkedIn / GM / GitLab / Meta), this is the cleanest counter-narrative on the table.

## Key Takeaways

- **Empirical, not forecast.** 350 enterprise execs surveyed Q3 2025; 80% cut, zero correlation between cut depth and ROI. Rare data; most analyst pieces in this space are forecast-driven.
- **"People amplification" is the citable frame.** Poitevin's quote travels — it's the analyst-grade version of the same thesis [[workshop]]'s "Cici" and Cerkl's "amplification layer" pitches use.
- **Convergent analyst landscape.** BCG, Forrester, McKinsey, MIT Sloan all point the same direction. The IC narrative is on unusually solid ground regardless of which analyst a buyer cites.
- **Net-positive jobs by 2028–2029** — but that's forecast. Cite the *empirical* finding (no-correlation), not the optimistic forward-looking number.
- Companion forecast: AI agent software spending hits **$206.5B in 2026** and **$376.3B in 2027** — the layoff-ROI miss is happening *while* spend is accelerating.

## Notable Claims

- **~80% of organizations** piloting or deploying autonomous business capabilities reported workforce reductions of **1–15%** tied to those initiatives (Q3 2025, n=350) ([State of Brand summary](https://www.thestateofbrand.com/news/ai-job-cuts-not-improving-returns)).
- **Layoff rate does not predict AI ROI.** *"Companies that cut the most showed nearly identical financial returns to the companies that cut the least. In several cases, the ones that cut less performed better."*
- **High-ROI cohort = people-amplifiers.** Poitevin: *"Organizations that improve ROI are not those that eliminate the need for people, but those that amplify them by aggressively investing more in skills, roles and operating models that allow humans to guide and scale autonomous systems."* ([People Matters](https://sea.peoplemattersglobal.com/news/workforce-planning/layoffs-wont-fix-ai-roi-problem-warns-gartner-49589))
- **Looking only at workforce is a laggard move.** Poitevin: *"Those who only look to the workforce tend to be the 'laggards,' because they're not going after the broader set of value."*
- **Net-positive jobs by 2028–2029** (forecast). Poitevin: *"AI is not leading to a jobs apocalypse, but it's unleashing job chaos, changing the shape of what people do."*
- **AI agent software spending: $206.5B in 2026, $376.3B in 2027** (Gartner forecast cited in [Fortune coverage](https://fortune.com/2026/05/11/ai-automation-layoffs-gartner-study-roi/)).
