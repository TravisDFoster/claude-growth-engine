# Why communicators are trading employee engagement for employee experience

**Author/Publisher:** Ragan Staff / [[ragan-communications]]  |  **Published:** 2026-06-11  |  **Source type:** article (vendor roundtable Q&A)  |  **URL:** https://www.ragan.com/why-communicators-are-trading-employee-engagement-for-employee-experience/

## Summary

A Q&A with [[interact]] execs [[cindy-knezevich]] (CMO) and [[daren-jennings]] (CSO), drawn from a London roundtable cohosted with [[ragan-communications]]. The argument: internal comms is moving past *engagement* (clicks, open rate, "good vibes") toward *employee experience* — a broader business initiative measured by behavior change and outcomes. Two adjacent claims do the real work: (1) the future of the intranet is "trusted action," not more content, and (2) the comms leader of the future is the **steward of institutional knowledge** that makes the org AI-ready. This is vendor thought-leadership (an intranet vendor arguing the intranet should be the "experience layer"), so treat the authority tier accordingly.

## Key Takeaways

- **Engagement vs experience, defined:** Knezevich — engagement is "how employees are feeling"; experience is "giving them the digital workplace they need to do the best work." EX is framed as the bigger, business-outcome construct.
- **The ROI-is-indirect admission:** Jennings — "engagement scores have been flat for 20 years and even declining"; "the engagement to ROI is indirect. The R is farther away from the I than people want to see." Reverses the causal arrow: better work outcomes (via less friction) → higher engagement, not the other way around.
- **Surface-level metrics called out:** the London group debated whether "click rate and open rate" are really indicative of impact, or whether it's "more about behavior change and business results" — a direct practitioner echo of [[open-rate-inflation]] and the surface-ROI critique.
- **Future of the intranet = trusted action:** "We don't have a content problem anymore… the future of the intranet is not about more content, it's going to be about trusted action." The EX platform is positioned as the "experience layer across all [systems of record] where reliable information results in trusted action."
- **Comms leader as knowledge steward:** the standout idea — comms leaders "will increasingly become the stewards of institutional knowledge to ensure that it's accurate, up-to-date, doesn't conflict, and adheres to organizational guidelines… as the foundation to AI readiness." See [[comms-as-knowledge-steward]].
- **Frontline = practical value + trust:** frontline workers want access to better shifts, growth/income mobility, and manager support — plus a trust guarantee that the app isn't surveilling them.

## Notable Claims

- "Engagement scores have been flat for 20 years and even declining, depending on the different cohorts" — Jennings ([source](https://www.ragan.com/why-communicators-are-trading-employee-engagement-for-employee-experience/)). Vendor-stated, no citation; directionally consistent with [[gallup]]'s primary-research decline (23%→20% 2023–2025, [[state-of-the-global-workplace-2026]]) but the "20 years flat" claim is broader than Gallup's series and uncited.
- "Employees have seven fundamental needs that need to be met to do their best work" — Knezevich, referencing an Interact framework (not enumerated in the article).
- Knowledge worker intranet adoption is "largely solved… through group policy" (forced homepage + behavioral habit); frontline adoption is a different, more practical problem.

## Contradicts

- **Intranet-as-experience-layer vs Cerkl's delivery-layer frame.** Interact argues the EX platform (intranet) should *be* "the experience layer across all systems of record." That's destination framing — the same move LumApps ("AI Employee Hub") and others make. Cerkl's counter-position is the delivery-and-measurement layer that meets employees in channels they already use, not a new destination they must visit. The "trusted action" language is appropriated delivery logic pointed back at the intranet.
