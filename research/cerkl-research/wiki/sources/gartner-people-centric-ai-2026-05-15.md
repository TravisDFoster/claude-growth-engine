# Gartner: People-Centric AI Strategy / 50% AI Talent Loss by 2027

**Author/Publisher:** Swagatam Basu + Diana Sanchez / [[gartner]]  |  **Published:** 2026-05-13  |  **Source type:** Analyst press release (with paywalled companion research)  |  **URL:** https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2026-05-13-gartner-predicts-by-2027-50-percent-of-enterprises-without-a-people-centric-ai-strategy-will-lose-their-top-ai-talent

## Summary

Gartner predicts that by 2027, 50% of enterprises without a people-centric AI strategy will lose top AI talent to competitors prioritizing workforce enablement. The press release names CHROs (with CIOs) as joint owners of AI governance, decision rights, and tool UX. The IC-relevant insight beneath the prediction: the gap between "rollout" and "transformation" — Gartner-coined as the **[[enablement-illusion]]** — is a *comms and UX* problem, not a tooling problem. 88% of employees with enterprise AI access also use personal AI tools, 19% report zero time saved, and 73% of highly productive AI users are managers/executives (the front line is underserved).

## Key Takeaways

- Gartner is putting AI ownership in CHRO/CIO co-buyer hands. This is a repositioning move — historically AI was a CIO frame.
- The "**enablement illusion**" frames the problem as confusing adoption metrics for transformation. Cited by every re-publisher; designed to travel.
- **[[shadow-ai-comms-problem]]:** 88% of employees with enterprise AI also use personal AI. Diagnosis = bad UX + unclear rollout comms, not bad governance. Reframes shadow AI as a *communications* problem with security consequences.
- The "True ROI Index" concept (measure depth + diversity of AI use, not time-saved) maps onto Cerkl's analytics surface; cite when positioning vs basic-open-rate vendor narratives.
- Direct counter-frame to Staffbase's *Navigator* governance-first narrative — Gartner's four dynamics (governance + decision rights + tool UX + comms) extend beyond what permission-aware AI search solves.

## Notable Claims

- **By 2027, 50% of enterprises without a people-centric AI strategy will lose their top AI talent to competitors** ([Gartner 2026-05-13](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2026-05-13-gartner-predicts-by-2027-50-percent-of-enterprises-without-a-people-centric-ai-strategy-will-lose-their-top-ai-talent)).
- **19% of employees report zero time saved from AI** (1Q26 Global Labor Market Survey, n=12,004) — basic adoption metric is masking lack of transformation.
- **88% of employees with enterprise AI access also use personal AI tools** for business tasks — shadow AI is the norm, not the edge case.
- **73% of highly productive AI users are managers/executives** — front-line and individual contributors are underserved.
- **27% of executives have a comprehensive AI strategy** (Dec 2025, n=197 CxOs); **20% of leaders** believe their workforce is truly AI-ready.
- **Hybrid AI users (enterprise + personal) are 1.7× more likely to report significant time saved** than enterprise-only users.
- **Employees with a positive AI outlook are 3.4× more likely to be highly productive.**
- Proficient multi-use-case AI employees are **2× more productive**, **2.3× more likely to deliver quality work**, **3.2× more likely to drive process improvements**.

## Contradicts

- **Staffbase's Navigator-as-governance narrative** — Gartner reframes governance as *one of four* dynamics, with rollout comms and tool UX coequal. Permission-aware AI search alone doesn't satisfy a "people-centric AI strategy."
- **Most security/IT trade press** treats shadow AI as a tooling/governance problem. Gartner reframes it as a UX-and-communications problem with security consequences (see [[shadow-ai-comms-problem]]).
