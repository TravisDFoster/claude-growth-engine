# Forrester: AI Will Rewrite EX — Deep Listening Shows How

**Author/Publisher:** David Brodeur-Johnson + James McQuivey / [[forrester]]  |  **Published:** 2026-02-06  |  **Source type:** Analyst blog (category-naming move; paywalled companion reports)  |  **URL:** https://www.forrester.com/blogs/ai-will-rewrite-employee-experience-deep-listening-shows-how/

## Summary

Forrester is naming a new EX category — **[[deep-listening]]** — defined as AI that turns the "digital exhaust" of Teams chats, channel conversations, and meeting transcripts into real-time sentiment, emotion, and friction signals across the workforce, no surveys required. Forrester says vendors are **one to two years away from full-featured offerings**; early movers will detect emerging issues earlier, intervene more effectively, and understand change-initiative impact in days rather than quarters. The Cerkl-relevance hook is sharp: Forrester is naming the lane Cerkl wants to own (measurable, channel-native delivery with read-through analytics) but its frame puts the value in *upstream sentiment*, not downstream delivery. Cerkl is on the right map but on the wrong axis — the strategic move is [[deep-listening|complement-not-compete positioning]].

## Key Takeaways

- **This is a category-naming event**, not a one-off think piece. Blog is the lead magnet for two paywalled reports (RES189386 *Deep Listening Is Your Next Employee Success Propellant*; RES189391 *For Deep Listening For EX, You Need A Viable AI Technology Stack*) and a client webinar.
- **Zero vendors named in the public blog.** Microsoft Viva Glint, Qualtrics, Medallia, Perceptyx, Workvivo Seer all unmentioned. Vendor narrative race starts now; the structurally best-positioned vendors own the data plane (Microsoft Teams, Zoom-Workvivo).
- **24-month build window before Forrester Wave likely re-evaluates the category.** Every EX/Comms vendor's roadmap shifts overnight if the Q2 2027 Wave includes deep-listening criteria.
- **Frame favors incumbents with the data, not new entrants.** Microsoft (Viva + Teams), Workvivo (Zoom-bundled + Seer launched 2026-05-06), Qualtrics (survey corpus). A Cerkl-sized vendor cannot get the Teams transcript firehose without a Microsoft or Zoom partnership.
- **Privacy-by-design is doing a lot of work** — practitioner pushback on workplace monitoring is at multi-year highs ([ExpressVPN trends 2026](https://www.expressvpn.com/blog/workplace-surveillance-trends-us/) — 73% monitoring; [Meta no-opt-out backlash](https://www.businesstoday.in/technology/story/meta-staff-protest-over-activity-tracking-tool-amid-privacy-concerns-531282-2026-05-13)). The category's adoption gate is consent + transparency, not capability.

## Notable Claims

- **Deep-listening capability spec (5 elements):** passive sentiment detection on Teams/meeting transcripts; emotional granularity beyond engaged/disengaged ("*tension from fear, cynicism from frustration, burnout from ordinary stress*"); topic auto-categorization; real-time change-impact measurement ("*in days rather than quarters*"); privacy-by-design preprocessing as table-stakes.
- **Forrester's headline claim:** *"Passive listening across digital channels produces exponentially more insight than employee surveys."*
- **"One to two years away"** from full-featured offerings — frames the 24-month build window for the entire EX/Comms vendor field.
- Early-mover EX benefits: "detect emerging issues earlier, intervene more effectively, understand the impact of change initiatives in days rather than quarters."

## Contradicts

- **Survey-only employee-listening orthodoxy** — Forrester is positioning passive NLP as exponentially more insightful than survey instruments.
- **Cerkl's read-through analytics as a *measurement* category** — Forrester frames the EX/Comms measurement layer through sentiment, not delivery. Cerkl's response cannot be "we measure too" (we measure a different thing); it has to be complement-not-compete.
