# Why employees are right not to trust Internal Communication

**Author/Publisher:** [[ghassan-karian]] / isitworking (Substack) | **Published:** 2026-05-26 | **Source type:** opinion essay | **URL:** https://isitworking.substack.com/p/why-employees-are-right-not-to-trust

## Summary
[[ghassan-karian]] (ex-director of IC at [[british-gas]]; founder of [[ipsos-karian-and-box]]) argues that employee distrust of internal communication is *rational*, not cynical — because the system forces IC to communicate in trust-eroding ways. Drawing on IKB's research across "millions of employee responses" and the Ipsos Global Trustworthiness Monitor, he names three things IC should never do (protect leaders from discomfort via euphemism; pretend neutrality while delivering spin; communicate on leadership's timetable not reality's). The prescription is **radical honesty** — treating IC as *context delivery*, not reputation management ([[context-delivery]]).

## Key Takeaways
- Employees trust peers and managers *often*; leaders and leadership comms *sometimes if at all* — because "IC is perceived as public relations with softer lighting." Echoes the manager-vs-executive trust split in [[leadership-communications]].
- Style is a **performance variable, not cosmetics**: where comms is experienced as authentic/warm, leadership-trust scores rise "into the eighties and nineties." Direct support for [[delivery-as-comms-strategy]] (how it lands *is* the strategy).
- The three credibility-killers ([[ic-credibility-deficit]]) — euphemism, fake neutrality, bad timing — are a clean diagnostic that maps onto [[boilerplate-layoff-language]] and the [[layoff-comms-choreography]] timing axis.
- Candour↔trust is *measurable* in IKB's data — a measurement argument that complements [[ic-measurement-and-roi]] (measure whether messages are *believed*, not just opened).

## Notable Claims
- IKB diagnostic work finds employees consistently describe leadership comms as "scripted," "insincere," "patronising," "cold," "evasive"; few choose "honest," "engaging," or "inspiring." (https://isitworking.substack.com/p/why-employees-are-right-not-to-trust)
- Among employees who *do* experience comms positively, confidence in leadership understanding and trust "soars into the eighties and nineties."
- Euphemism examples cited: cost-cutting → "strategic realignment"; layoff → "rebalancing of capacity"; failed initiative → "valuable learning cycle." (Maps to [[boilerplate-layoff-language]].)
- British Gas proof point: even when announcing the offshoring of 3,000 jobs, radical-honesty comms "secured high levels of trust in the weekly pulse research."
- IKB data "repeatedly shows a direct relationship between message candour and employee trust. When messages are overly curated, trust plummets."
