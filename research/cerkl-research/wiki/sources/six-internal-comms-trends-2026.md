# Six internal comms trends every communicator should know in 2026

**Author:** Mayela Hernandez (Marketing & Events Specialist, [[workshop]])  |  **Publisher:** [[pr-daily]] (sponsored by [[workshop]])  |  **Published:** 2026-04-14  |  **Source type:** Vendor-sponsored trends roundup  |  **URL:** https://www.prdaily.com/six-internal-comms-trends-every-communicator-should-know-in-2026/

## Summary

Workshop's annual IC trends roundup, surfaced through PR Daily. Six numbered trends, each anchored to a stat from Workshop's *Internal Comms Trends Report* (sample size not stated). Most useful for **practitioner-priority signals** — what comms teams say they're working on and where they say they're stuck — with vendor-tier authority caveat. Standout: the **manager-cascade gap** (56% prioritize, 4% effective) is one of the more memorable single stats in 2026 IC discourse.

## Key Takeaways

- **Manager-cascade gap is the headline:** 56% of communicators name manager comms as their #1 2026 priority, but only **4% believe managers are very effective** at cascading messages. The largest "priority vs effectiveness" gap in the report.
- **AI is mainstream, not experimental:** 42% use AI daily, another 31% several times a week. Roughly **73% use AI at least weekly**. Counter-narrative to [[ai-adoption-gap]] (which measures *vendor AI* adoption — Workshop's stat measures *any AI* use).
- **Email is still king:** 81% rate email their most effective channel vs intranet at 31% and in-person events at 35%. Reinforces [[internal-email]]'s status as the dominant IC channel.
- **Resource pushback is finally visible:** Only 44% have the resources they need for 2026 — and teams are *responding* with process changes (intake forms, templates, boundaries), not just absorbing the pressure. Framing shift worth noting.
- **Intranets remain the hardest channel** (29%) but teams continue investing; the strategy shift toward [[evergreen-content-strategy]] is the takeaway.
- **[[employee-influencers]] crossed the 40% line:** 41% actively investing — a tactic that's moved from niche to mainstream.

## Notable Claims

All statistics below cite https://www.prdaily.com/six-internal-comms-trends-every-communicator-should-know-in-2026/, attributed to Workshop's *Internal Comms Trends Report*.

**Trend 1 — Managers as priority and gap:**
- 56% say manager communication is their top focus for 2026.
- Only 4% believe managers are very effective at cascading messages.

**Trend 2 — AI adoption:**
- 42% use AI every day.
- 31% use AI several times a week.
- Use cases: content creation, summarizing employee feedback, supporting planning, adapting content across channels.

**Trend 3 — Email effectiveness:**
- 81% rate email their most effective internal channel.
- In-person events: 35%. Intranet: 31%.

**Trend 4 — Resource constraints:**
- Only 44% of communicators say they have the resources they need to execute their 2026 strategy.

**Trend 5 — Intranet difficulty:**
- 29% say intranet is the hardest channel to manage.
- More than half still prioritize intranet improvements this year.

**Trend 6 — Employee influencers:**
- 41% actively investing in employee-influencer programs.

## Contradicts

The 73% weekly+ AI use figure (Workshop) is **not strictly contradictory** with [[ai-adoption-gap]]'s ~49% finding ([[forrester-wave-intranet-platforms-q2-2026]]) — Workshop measures *any* AI use by communicators; Forrester measures *vendor AI feature* adoption by intranet customers. Different denominators, different question. Worth surfacing the distinction when citing either.
