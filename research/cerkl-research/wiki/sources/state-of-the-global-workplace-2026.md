# State of the Global Workplace: 2026 Report

**Publisher:** [[gallup]]  |  **Published:** April 2026 (2025 data)  |  **Source type:** Annual primary-research report  |  **URL:** https://www.gallup.com/workplace/697904/state-of-the-global-workplace-global-data.aspx

## Summary

Gallup's annual global workforce snapshot, based on the Gallup World Poll. The headline of the 2026 edition: **engagement is declining**, dropping for the second consecutive year to 20% globally (from 23% in 2023). The wellbeing measure ("thriving") ticked up 1pp to 34% — Gallup called this their key finding — but the engagement-vs-wellbeing divergence is itself the story. Frontline workers (on-site, non-remote-capable) sit at 17% engagement vs 30% for exclusively-remote employees, the widest cohort gap in the report.

## Key Takeaways

- **Engagement is declining, not flat** — 23% (2023) → 21% (2024) → 20% (2025). The post-COVID peak is over.
- **Frontline gap is 13 percentage points** — on-site non-remote-capable workers at 17% engagement vs exclusively-remote at 30%. The structural target for frontline-comms vendors.
- **Wellbeing diverged from engagement** — thriving up 1pp to 34% (first improvement in 3 years) while engagement declined. People feel slightly better about life overall while feeling less invested at work.
- **Massive regional dispersion** — US/Canada 31%, Europe 12%. Any global engagement number used without a regional qualifier misleads.
- **Daily emotional load remains high** — stress 40%, sadness 23%, anger 22%, loneliness 22%. These are wellbeing-state stats, not engagement stats; different framing for different blog angles.

## Notable Claims

All statistics below cite https://www.gallup.com/workplace/697904/state-of-the-global-workplace-global-data.aspx.

**2025 Global Engagement Distribution:** 20% engaged, 64% not engaged, 16% actively disengaged.

**Multi-year engagement trend:** 2009 12% → 2019 22% → 2023 23% → 2024 21% → 2025 20%. The trend is now a two-year decline from the post-2019 peak.

**Wellbeing (2025):** 34% thriving, 56% struggling, 9% suffering. Thriving rose by 1pp — first improvement in three years.

**Work-location engagement (2025):** exclusively remote 30%, hybrid 25%, on-site remote-capable 24%, on-site non-remote-capable 17%.

**Regional engagement (2025):** US/Canada 31%, Latin America 30%, Southeast Asia 25%, Post-Soviet Eurasia 25%, Australia/NZ 21%; Europe 12%; MENA 14%.

**Daily negative-emotion experience (2025):** stress 40%, anger 22%, sadness 23%, loneliness 22%.

**Job-market sentiment:** 52% globally say it is a good time to find a job.

**Demographic engagement splits:** female 21% / male 19%; under-35 19% / 35+ 21%; managers 22% / individual contributors 19%.

## Methodology

- Sample: 141,444 employed respondents in 2025 (263,810 total respondents).
- Historical scope: 2,616,488 employed respondents across 2009–2025.
- Source: Gallup World Poll, conducted across 160+ countries/areas since 2005.
- Margin of error (global): ±0.05 to ±0.08 percentage points at 95% confidence.
- **Methodology change in 2025:** regional data now based on three-year aggregated polling (prior reports used annual averages). Regional YoY comparisons across the 2024→2025 boundary are not apples-to-apples.
