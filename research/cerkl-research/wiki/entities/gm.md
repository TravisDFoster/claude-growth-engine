# General Motors (GM)

**Type:** Fortune-100 automaker  |  **Role (in wiki):** May 2026 layoff-comms anti-case (~500–600 IT roles, Austin + Warren)  |  **Website:** https://www.gm.com

## Overview

GM appears in the wiki as the **anti-Cerkl case study** of May 2026 — the layoff-comms moment where channel and timing failure *became* the message. Per [CNBC's primary reporting](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/12/gm-layoffs-ai-severance.html) (sourced through [[ragan-week-in-comms-linkedin-gm-gitlab-2026-05-15]]), employees received an email scheduling a virtual meeting **15 minutes out**, then sat through a scripted HR readout, then disconnected. No two-way channel. No follow-up Q&A space. Employee reactions (verbatim, CNBC): *"No appreciation or empathy. No questions. Nothing"* — a data analyst with 10+ years.

GM didn't claim AI was the reason. Employees filled the void. The void's voice was: "you're replacing us with AI."

Ragan's diagnosis: *"When layoffs are tightly scripted and don't answer employee questions, they'll fill in the blanks themselves. The company framed the layoffs as a way to 'position the company for the future' and without much more detail, employees are interpreting that as 'you're replacing us with AI.'"*

## Key Ideas / Contributions

- **The 15-minute meeting** — the timing axis of [[layoff-comms-choreography]] reduced to its starkest form.
- **Void-fill failure** — when the company doesn't name a driver, employees and coverage name AI.
- **The verbatim Cerkl pitch deck on a competitor's editorial real estate** — Ragan's "*the way the message is shared is often just as important as the message itself*" reading of the GM case is the cleanest public proof point Cerkl has in 2026 for the "delivery layer not destination" positioning.
