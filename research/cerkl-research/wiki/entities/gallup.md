# Gallup

**Type:** Analytics and advisory firm (primary-research tier)  |  **Founded:** 1935 (George Gallup)  |  **Role:** Primary-research source for workforce engagement and wellbeing  |  **Website:** https://www.gallup.com

## Overview

Gallup is a global analytics and advisory firm best known for the Gallup World Poll and the Q12 employee-engagement framework. For internal-communications research, Gallup is the most-cited single source of engagement and wellbeing benchmark data — its annual *State of the Global Workplace* report is the de-facto industry-baseline reference for "what % of employees are engaged" questions.

## Key Ideas / Contributions

- **Gallup Q12** — 12-item employee-engagement survey instrument; the field's most widely deployed engagement measurement tool.
- **Three-bucket engagement model** — engaged / not engaged / actively disengaged. See [[employee-engagement-three-bucket-model]].
- **Thriving / struggling / suffering** — Gallup's wellbeing classification, based on the Cantril Self-Anchoring Striving Scale. See [[thriving-struggling-suffering]].
- **State of the Global Workplace** — annual report; large-sample primary research; ~141k employed respondents in 2025 alone, ~2.6M historically.
- **US Manager Panel** — longitudinal sample (2013 n=2,402 → 2025 n=8,795) underwriting Gallup's [[span-of-control]] and manager-engagement research. The 2026 "Span of Control" piece is built on this panel + a 92,252-team global meta-analysis.
- **Manager-talent assessment** — Gallup's five-trait composite (motivation, workstyle, initiation, collaboration, thought process) underpins the "manager talent trumps span" finding.
- **Weekly meaningful-feedback finding** — Gallup's single-highest-leverage IC-relevant habit: weekly meaningful manager conversations triple the engaged-employee rate (from ~25% to ~70%); only 16% of employees say their last manager conversation was "extremely meaningful." Pairs with [[manager-cascade]] as the practical lever IC tooling can actually move.
