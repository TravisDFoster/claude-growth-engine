# British Gas

**Type:** UK energy / utilities company | **Role:** Radical-honesty IC proof-point case

## Overview
British Gas is a major UK energy supplier. In the IC research context it's the **proof-point case** in [[ghassan-karian]]'s trust argument: as its former director of internal communications, Karian reports that radical-honesty comms sustained high employee trust even while announcing difficult news (the offshoring of 3,000 jobs), as measured in weekly pulse research during change.

## Key Ideas / Contributions
- Demonstrates that candour can preserve trust *during* painful change — a counter-example to the euphemism reflex ([[ic-credibility-deficit]], [[context-delivery]]).
- The pulse-research-during-change practice is itself a measurement model ([[ic-measurement-and-roi]]).
