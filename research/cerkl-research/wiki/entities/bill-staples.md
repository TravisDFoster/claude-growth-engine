# Bill Staples

**Type:** Executive (person)  |  **Role:** CEO, [[gitlab]]

## Overview

GitLab CEO. His 2026-05-12 *Act 2* blog post ([about.gitlab.com/blog/gitlab-act-2](https://about.gitlab.com/blog/gitlab-act-2/)) announced ~7% workforce cut + 30% country-footprint reduction with an explicit pre-emption: *"This is not an AI optimization or cost cutting exercise."* Industry coverage and Ragan ([[ragan-week-in-comms-linkedin-gm-gitlab-2026-05-15]]) called it [[ai-washing]] anyway. Shares dropped ~8% after-hours. Staples' framing — *"Software will be built by machines, directed by people"* — was candid about the AI shift, but the memo's audience-of-record was investors rather than employees, which Ragan flagged as the failure point.

## Key Ideas / Contributions

- **Candor-with-philosophy framing** — *"Software will be built by machines, directed by people"* is one of the more memorable verbatim AI-era CEO lines of 2026.
- **Public CEO blog as primary channel** — consistent with GitLab's transparent-handbook culture, but in this case Ragan reads it as channel inversion: investors got it first, employees last.
- **Reinvestment commitment** — *"We intend to reinvest the vast majority of savings back into the business to accelerate our unique opportunity in the agentic era."*
