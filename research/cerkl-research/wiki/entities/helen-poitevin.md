# Helen Poitevin

**Type:** Industry analyst (person)  |  **Role:** Distinguished VP Analyst, [[gartner]]  |  **Coverage:** AI, workforce planning, autonomous business, HR technology

## Overview

Helen Poitevin is the Gartner Distinguished VP Analyst voicing the firm's May 2026 stance on AI-driven layoffs. Her empirical claim — that ~80% of organizations piloting autonomous business cut headcount but layoff rate shows **no correlation** with AI ROI — is the rare survey-based (not forecast-based) data point in the 2026 AI-ROI analyst landscape. Her framing of the high-ROI cohort as **"people amplification, not people elimination"** is now traveling through Fortune, CIO, HCAMag, Silicon Republic, People Matters coverage as the citable analyst-grade rebuttal to the "cut to fund AI" CEO playbook.

## Key Ideas / Contributions

- **[[people-amplification]]** — frame coined in the May 2026 release: AI ROI comes from investing in skills, roles, and operating models that let humans guide autonomous systems, not from headcount substitution.
- **Empirical no-correlation finding** — Q3 2025 survey, n=350 enterprise execs. Companies that cut the most showed nearly identical returns to those that cut the least; in several cases, the lower-cut cohort performed better.
- **"Laggards look only to the workforce"** — Poitevin's frame for which executives miss broader AI value (process redesign, product reinvention).
- **Net-positive jobs forecast (contested)** — AI creates more jobs than it displaces between 2028–2029 per Gartner forecast.
