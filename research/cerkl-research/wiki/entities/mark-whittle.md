# Mark Whittle

**Type:** Industry analyst (person)   |   **Role:** Vice President, Advisory at [[gartner]] — communications practice   |   **Coverage:** Internal communications, change leadership, AI-in-comms

## Overview

Mark Whittle is Vice President of Advisory at Gartner and the most-quoted public voice on Gartner's comms-practice predictions. He hosts the Gartner webinar series *"Gartner's Top Communications Predictions for 2026: What Every CCO Must Know"* and is the named lead in trade-press pickup of Gartner's *Predicts 2026: Top Predictions to Inform 2026 Comms Strategies* report (doc 7160430, 2026-02-13, paywalled). Whittle's public framing positions change-leadership-as-comms-discipline — the practitioner-level prescription that pairs with [[helen-poitevin]]'s organization-level *people-amplification* finding.

## Key Ideas / Contributions

- **[[change-leadership-three-step]]** — Whittle's three-step process for change comms: *Acknowledge the journey / Regulate discomfort / Train intuition*. The central deliverable of his 2026 public-facing framing.
- **"Effective leadership today is change leadership"** — Whittle's most-portable verbatim, framing change-comms as the defining leadership discipline of the AI-restructure era.
- **2008-09 financial-crisis analogy** — Whittle frames AI-restructure-cycle comms as a parallel to 2008-09 comms; *"leaders who failed to communicate left employees anxious about their futures."* Historical anchor for the 2026 cycle.
- **Culture-embedding into AI implementations** — Whittle argues organizations must "intentionally embed company culture into AI implementations, connecting automation to organizational mission and values." The leadership-side counterpart to Gartner's [[people-amplification]] frame.
- **Layoff-comms-as-employer-brand-driver** — Whittle's closing position in [[ragan-gartner-2026-comms-predictions-2025-10-09]] explicitly ties layoff-comms quality to employer brand perception during major transitions.
