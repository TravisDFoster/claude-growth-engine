# LinkedIn

**Type:** Microsoft-owned professional network and SaaS business  |  **Role (in wiki):** May 2026 layoff-comms case study (~875 cuts / ~5% of 17,500)  |  **Website:** https://www.linkedin.com

## Overview

LinkedIn appears in the wiki primarily as a change-comms case study from the May 2026 layoff cluster. CEO [[daniel-shapero]]'s 2026-05-13 layoff memo is the "credibly framed but formulaic" tone in Ragan's three-case triangulation ([[ragan-week-in-comms-linkedin-gm-gitlab-2026-05-15]]) — single all-employee memo at 7:00 AM Pacific, manager 1:1 cascade, named departing staff and called staying staff to support them. Ragan flagged the "reinvent how we work / agile teams" phrasing as the now-overused 2026 template.

Context: Shapero became CEO after Ryan Roslansky moved to a Microsoft AI role in April 2026 ([TechCrunch on Roslansky departure](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/22/linkedins-ceo-is-moving-on-please-hold-your-tearful-video-tributes/)). The 2026-05-13 memo is Shapero's, not Roslansky's — verify any Cerkl content attributions.

## Key Ideas / Contributions

- **Case study in adequate-but-formulaic layoff comms** — strategic framing, stakeholder acknowledgement, manager cascade all present; inauthenticity risk only.
- **Reuters reporting noted LinkedIn's cuts as not-AI-attributed** — "not about AI replacing jobs, but about reorganizing around growth areas." A rare 2026 tech-cut that didn't lean on the AI narrative, which Ragan framed as a comms upside.
