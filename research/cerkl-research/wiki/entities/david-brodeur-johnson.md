# David Brodeur-Johnson

**Type:** Industry analyst (person)  |  **Role:** Principal Analyst, [[forrester]] (Employee Experience research lead)  |  **Coverage:** EX, employee listening, AI in HR/EX, digital employee experience

## Overview

Forrester's Employee Experience research lead. In February 2026, Brodeur-Johnson (with co-author [[james-mcquivey]]) published the public blog naming **[[deep-listening]]** as Forrester's EX category for the next 24 months: AI on Teams chats, channel conversations, and meeting transcripts producing real-time sentiment, emotion, and friction signals. The blog is the lead magnet for two paywalled reports (RES189386, RES189391) and a client webinar — a category-naming move that will likely shape EX/Comms evaluation criteria through 2027.

## Key Ideas / Contributions

- **[[deep-listening]] category naming** — passive NLP on collaboration exhaust supplanting/extending survey-based [[employee-listening]].
- **"One to two years away from full-featured offerings"** — the 24-month window framing for the EX/Comms vendor field.
- **Emotional granularity as differentiator** — beyond engaged/disengaged: "tension from fear, cynicism from frustration, burnout from ordinary stress."
- **Privacy-by-design as table stakes** — not optional; the adoption gate is consent + transparency.
