# Ghassan Karian

**Type:** Person (practitioner / founder) | **Role:** Founder, [[ipsos-karian-and-box]]; ex-Director of Internal Communications, [[british-gas]]; author of the *isitworking* Substack

## Overview
Ghassan Karian is an internal-communications leader and researcher. He founded Karian and Box (now [[ipsos-karian-and-box]] after the Ipsos acquisition), one of the larger employee-research / IC-measurement practices, and previously ran internal communications at [[british-gas]]. He writes the *isitworking* Substack.

## Key Ideas / Contributions
- Argues employee distrust of IC is **rational**: IC erodes trust through euphemism, fake neutrality, and bad timing ([[ic-credibility-deficit]]).
- Prescribes **radical honesty** — treating IC as [[context-delivery]] ("context delivery, not reputation management").
- Backs the thesis with IKB data across "millions of employee responses" and the Ipsos Global Trustworthiness Monitor — making "style is a performance variable" a measurement claim, not an aesthetic one ([[ic-measurement-and-roi]]).
- British Gas proof point: radical-honesty comms sustained trust even while announcing the offshoring of 3,000 jobs.
- **2026-06-04 — middle-manager structural-burnout thesis.** Names the role as "structurally impossible" — responsibility without authority + hybrid coordination load + emotional absorption + admin bloat + outdated design ([[manager-burnout-structural-thesis]]). Backed by IKB's employee-experience benchmark database (described as "one of the largest in Europe") showing work-life-balance scores deteriorated for middle managers 2023–2025 while executives' improved. Prescription is structural redesign, not resilience training. **Extends [[context-delivery]] downstream — managers must have capacity to deliver the context.**
