# PoliteMail

**Type:** IC email measurement / Outlook plug-in vendor  |  **Role:** Internal-email tracking, segmentation, and benchmark research attached to Microsoft 365  |  **Website:** https://politemail.com

## Overview

PoliteMail is an Outlook plug-in product for internal-communications email tracking, segmentation, and measurement. Its commercial moat sits as much in its **annual benchmark research** as in the product itself — the company aggregates telemetry across customers (~2B emails / ~11M employees) and publishes an annual *Internal Email Communications Benchmarks* report. The 2026 edition is co-branded with [[ragan-communications]] and distributed as the "Ragan 2026 Internal Email Benchmarks Report" (see [[ragan-email-benchmarks-report-2026-05-12]]) — a co-branding pattern that obscures PoliteMail's authorship at the headline level.

## Key Ideas / Contributions

- **Annual *Internal Email Communications Benchmarks*** — the largest single-vendor IC email dataset (~2B emails / ~11M employees / 10 S&P sectors). Year-over-year comparisons since at least 2022. Most-cited in the space alongside [[contactmonkey]]'s benchmark report.
- **Co-branded distribution via Ragan / [[pr-daily]]** — PoliteMail's CEO Michael DesRochers bylines the PR Daily summary posts; Ragan hosts the gated PDF. The "Ragan benchmark" anchor is, on close read, the PoliteMail benchmark in editorial cover.
- **Outlook-native delivery + measurement positioning** — the closest *positioning* analog to Cerkl in the IC vendor set ("delivery + measurement layer attached to the channels you already have"). Differs from Cerkl in that PoliteMail is Outlook/M365-only — no cross-channel layer.
- **Self-acknowledged methodology gaps** — see [[open-rate-inflation]]; PoliteMail's own content flags that open-rate measurement is inflated by Outlook preview-pane "opens," automated security scans, and stale distribution lists.
