Marketing Team — 7-Day Rollup

Week ending: 2026-07-01 Action items: 5 Cadence: Wednesday
Headline of the week

The trend discourse and the competitor content pointed at the same target this week: the message isn't reaching — or being read by — the people who most need it (the frontline, and everyone being asked to adopt AI). On the trends side, a wall of analyst data reframed AI adoption as a communications failure: SHRM found only 33% of individual contributors were told about their org's AI adoption vs. 74% of directors; Karian's IC Index says just 35% of employees think their org applies AI to the right problems and 32% got clear guidance; McKinsey pegs fully-scaled AI at 7%; Forrester explicitly names IC the strategic lever. On the competitor side, the gated-research axis rotated from "prove IC value" to "prove frontline reach" — Firstup dropped a new 1,000-frontline-worker manufacturing survey, Workvivo keeps pushing its "Frontline AI Gap" report — and two Ragan case studies this week (Sav-A-Tree, Goodwill) hammered deskless reach.

This is Cerkl's ground twice over. Competitors can claim a worker was "reached" (40–50% of deskless, per ContactMonkey); only Cerkl's per-employee read attribution proves they read it — including the frontline. Every asset this quarter should sharpen from "we reach them" to "we prove they read it, by name, desk or no desk." The measurement-proof-asset play we've been queuing since W25 now gets a frontline cut, and it just got more urgent because the category is spending research budget to own the exact axis Cerkl can win on substance.
