## Headline of the week

**The IC category just crowded onto Cerkl's home turf — measurement.** The "you can't prove your comms work" pain hook jumped from 3 vendors to **6 of 10** this week ([W25 §Pain-point cross-cut](../../competitor-marketing/weekly/2026-W25.md)): Firstup (*"$1T turnover cost"*), AxiosHQ (*"92% / 90% / 82%"*), ContactMonkey (*"demonstrate clear impact"*), Simpplr (*"282% ROI"*), Poppulo, and Workshop all now lead creative with some version of *"you're guessing."* That's validation — the buyer now expects measurement, which is exactly the wedge Cerkl has been claiming. But it also ends Cerkl's default ownership of the word: six competitors are planting flags on "measurement" using **aggregate, borrowed stats** while none of them delivers *per-employee* read attribution (and their own G2 reviews complain about the analytics/customization ceilings that undercut the claim). The play this week is to stop *describing* measurement and start *out-specifying* it — make the proof point a named employee opening a message, not a percentage. The daily signal stacks neatly on top: two HBR pieces plus Gartner's anti-uniform-governance stance give Cerkl the empirical case that **trust + measurement beats control** — the exact counter to the Staffbase/Simpplr/LumApps governance frame.

## Action items (ranked)

### 1. Out-specify the measurement crowd — ship a per-employee read-attribution LP variant
**What it is:** Measurement/outcomes went from 3 → 6 of 10 vendors leading with it this week — the standout WoW shift in [W25 §Top takeaways #1](../../competitor-marketing/weekly/2026-W25.md) and [§(c) Advertising](../../competitor-marketing/weekly/2026-W25.md). Every claim is aggregate (282% ROI, 92%, $1T); none shows employee-level read receipts, and G2 complaint themes (analytics/customization ceilings, 8 of 10 vendors) expose the gap.
**Why it matters for Cerkl marketing:** "Delivery + measurement layer… delivered, opened, read, by employee" is Cerkl's stated core position (`shared/competitors.md`). When six competitors advertise measurement with hand-waving stats, specificity is the differentiator — Cerkl can win the word by being the only one that shows the receipt.
**Action:** Marketing (Travis + Tracy) — build a measurement-hero LP variant whose proof point is a *per-employee* read-receipt visual: *"Not '282% ROI.' This: Sarah in Ops opened it. Marcus on the floor didn't — yet."* Draft this week, live next. Highest-leverage item on the board.

### 2. Make the pricing-transparency gap loud while competitors gate harder
**What it is:** ContactMonkey (was tiered-public) and Workvivo (was per-employee public) **both pulled pricing behind "Request pricing" this week**; 0 of 10 vendors offer a free-forever tier for the 7th straight week ([W25 §Top takeaways #3](../../competitor-marketing/weekly/2026-W25.md), [§(f) Pricing](../../competitor-marketing/weekly/2026-W25.md)).
**Why it matters for Cerkl marketing:** The category is moving *opposite* to Cerkl Foundations. The free-forever + public-pricing moat is more differentiated than it was a month ago, not less — but only if marketing actively contrasts it.
**Action:** Marketing — lead a Foundations campaign on the transparency contrast directly: *"Free. Public pricing. Live this afternoon. No quote required."* Pair with the recurring "escape the manual stack" Foundations message. Scope this week; ties to the Q3 Foundations window (see watchlist).

### 3. Ship the governance-vs-trust POV — the daily research now arms it
**What it is:** Two HBR pieces this week — employees hide AI use when trust is low ([06-17 #1](../daily/2026-06-17.md)); judgment, not speed, is the scarce skill ([06-17 #3](../daily/2026-06-17.md)) — plus Gartner's "uniform AI-agent governance will fail" ([06-10, rolling index](../rolling-7day.md)) and Staffbase's own VOICES Awards reframing "trust and adoption" as the EX differentiators ([06-17 #2](../daily/2026-06-17.md)). The empirical case: you only *see* shadow AI when employees trust you — control doesn't surface it.
**Why it matters for Cerkl marketing:** This is the third-party evidence behind Cerkl's *"governance is what you market when you don't trust your AI; we market what we measure"* counter-position (`shared/competitors.md`). The W25 digest notes this POV has been recommended for three weeks and **still hasn't shipped** — and now the supporting data is strongest.
**Action:** Marketing/content — publish the counter-positioning POV (blog or LP) tying sanctioned-AI to measurement: *"The sanctioned-AI promise dies the moment employees don't read the message."* Anchor it to the HBR/Gartner data, not the AI-fear hook. Draft within 2 weeks.

### 4. G2 review-velocity push is now urgent — Workshop has a Spring + Summer badge
**What it is:** Workshop repeated its **#1 Internal Newsletter Software** win into G2 Summer 2026 — two consecutive seasons of badge ammunition it will press in every Foundations head-to-head ([W25 §Ideas to steal](../../competitor-marketing/weekly/2026-W25.md), [useworkshop.com press](https://useworkshop.com/press/workshop-named-best-for-internal-and-frontline-communications-in-g2-summer-reports-2026/)). Workshop is Cerkl's most direct Foundations-tier rival.
**Why it matters for Cerkl marketing:** Heading into the Q3 Foundations window, a competitor with a repeating G2 #1 badge and no recurring complaint theme is a sharp threat to the free-tier pitch. Cerkl needs its own badge before that window opens.
**Action:** Travis + Marketing — identify the 1–2 G2 categories Cerkl can realistically win for **Fall 2026**, then run an in-product review-velocity push (CSM-driven ask to happy Foundations users). Scope the category pick this week; the review push runs over the next 4–6 weeks.

### 5. Decide the "execution problem" frame — ship-or-kill
**What it is:** The "execution problem" CEO narrative (Cates/ContactMonkey, Black/Workvivo, Fornell/Poppulo) **stalled at 3 vendors for the 5th straight week** — no 4th adopter ([W25 §(d)](../../competitor-marketing/weekly/2026-W25.md)). The first-mover window to claim or counter-position it has decayed to a decision point.
**Why it matters for Cerkl marketing:** Cerkl flagged weeks ago that two-then-three vendors converging on identical language was a category-language shift to claim or counter before it ossifies. It hasn't ossified — but the window isn't free forever.
**Action:** Travis — make the call this week: either (a) claim/counter-position the "execution" frame in Cerkl messaging now, or (b) explicitly drop it and stop tracking. Don't leave it open a 6th week. Decision, not a project.

### 6. Refresh "vs. Workvivo" + commission "vs. Haiilo" — destination convergence + advocacy backlash
**What it is:** Two converging signals. (a) Workvivo repositioned its hero to *"One digital headquarters for every employee"* ([W25 §Top takeaways #4](../../competitor-marketing/weekly/2026-W25.md)) — the sharpest "destination" frame yet, and a cleaner foil for Cerkl's anti-destination contrast. (b) Ragan ran a CCO POV arguing forced employee advocacy backfires ([06-17 #7](../daily/2026-06-17.md)) — direct ammunition against Haiilo and the Poppulo+Sociabble advocacy bundle.
**Why it matters for Cerkl marketing:** The more competitors say "come to our hub," the cleaner Cerkl's *"we meet employees where they already are"* reads — but the live `versus/workvivo` page is stale (pre-digital-HQ), and there's **no live "vs. Haiilo" page** at all (counter-positioning writes itself per `shared/competitors.md`).
**Action:** Marketing — (a) refresh `cerkl.com/broadcast/versus/workvivo` with the digital-HQ + Zoom-bundle + Seer narrative; (b) commission a net-new "vs. Haiilo" page leaning on advocacy ≠ internal comms + post-merger seams. Queue both; vs-Workvivo refresh first.

### 7. Stand up the customization-ceiling wedge — it's now near-universal (8 of 10)
**What it is:** The template/customization-ceiling complaint is confirmed across 8 of 10 vendors in G2 themes ([W25 §(e)](../../competitor-marketing/weekly/2026-W25.md)); Workshop is the lone holdout.
**Why it matters for Cerkl marketing:** A durable, evergreen wedge that doesn't depend on a competitor's weekly move: *"You don't need to redesign your channels — Cerkl personalizes the message inside the channels you already have."*
**Action:** Marketing — build a one-screen LP with honestly-attributed G2 complaint fragments as the backdrop. Lower urgency than 1–4; queue behind the measurement and Foundations pushes.
