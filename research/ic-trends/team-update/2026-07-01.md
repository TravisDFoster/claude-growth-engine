## Headline of the week

**The trend discourse and the competitor content pointed at the same target this week: the message isn't reaching — or being read by — the people who most need it (the frontline, and everyone being asked to adopt AI).** On the trends side, a wall of analyst data reframed AI adoption as a *communications* failure: SHRM found only 33% of individual contributors were told about their org's AI adoption vs. 74% of directors; Karian's IC Index says just 35% of employees think their org applies AI to the right problems and 32% got clear guidance; McKinsey pegs fully-scaled AI at 7%; Forrester explicitly names IC the strategic lever. On the competitor side, the gated-research axis rotated from "prove IC value" to **"prove frontline reach"** — Firstup dropped a new 1,000-frontline-worker manufacturing survey, Workvivo keeps pushing its "Frontline AI Gap" report — and two Ragan case studies this week (Sav-A-Tree, Goodwill) hammered deskless reach. **This is Cerkl's ground twice over.** Competitors can claim a worker was *"reached"* (40–50% of deskless, per ContactMonkey); only Cerkl's per-employee read attribution proves they *read* it — including the frontline. Every asset this quarter should sharpen from "we reach them" to "we prove they read it, by name, desk or no desk." The measurement-proof-asset play we've been queuing since W25 now gets a frontline cut, and it just got more urgent because the category is spending research budget to own the exact axis Cerkl can win on substance.

## Action items (ranked)

### 1. Add a frontline read-rate cut to the "State of Internal Comms Read Rates 2026" benchmark
**What it is:** The competitor gated-research axis rotated to frontline reach this week — Firstup's new 1,000-worker manufacturing survey + Workvivo's Frontline AI Gap report ([W27 §Pain point 1](../../competitor-marketing/weekly/2026-W27.md)) — while the trend side ran two deskless-reach case studies (Sav-A-Tree, Goodwill) and quantified the AI-adoption comms gap ([daily 2026-07-01 §1, §8](../daily/2026-07-01.md)).
**Why it matters for Cerkl marketing:** This evolves the flagship benchmark idea (already queued from W26), it doesn't replace it. Competitors prove "reached"; Cerkl's per-employee read data proves "read" — and a deskless-vs-desk-based read-through segment is the one number none of them can publish. It's the un-fakeable version of the claim they're funding research to make.
**Action:** Marketing/Travis — when scoping the benchmark report, add a "frontline vs. desk-based read rates" section to the outline. Pull a defensible anonymized aggregate from platform data first. Keep it one report, not two. This quarter (anchors the Foundations campaign).

### 2. Ship the "AI adoption is a comms problem" POV — the week's strongest earned narrative
**What it is:** Five credible sources converged in seven days — SHRM (33% vs. 74% told about AI), McKinsey (7% scaled), Forrester (IC as strategic lever; 48% cut headcount to AI while deprioritizing change mgmt), HBR (middle managers overloaded), Karian (35%/32%) ([daily 2026-07-01 §1–5](../daily/2026-07-01.md)).
**Why it matters for Cerkl marketing:** This is analyst air-cover for elevating IC from cost center to strategic function — the exact budget argument Cerkl's buyers make internally — and Cerkl's read attribution is the proof mechanism: *the AI rollout plan doesn't matter if it's never read.* Sharper and better-sourced than the AI-backlash hook we've been sitting on since W23.
**Action:** Travis-bylined POV (Cerkular + LinkedIn) — *"Your AI rollout isn't failing on strategy. It's failing on comms."* + a stat-led LinkedIn carousel (one slide per source, Cerkl close on read-attribution as the adoption-visibility layer). This supersedes the older "AI backlash" framing of the queued AI POV. Ship this week — the sources are hot.

### 3. Get the vs. Workshop + vs. AxiosHQ pages into production — conquest pressure is now two-vendor
**What it is:** Staffbase joined ContactMonkey as an active conquester this week — surged to a 7/10 SERP-presence tie and surfaced "Simpplr Alternatives" + "ContactMonkey Alternatives" pages ([W27 §5, §f](../../competitor-marketing/weekly/2026-W27.md)). Meanwhile no competitor shipped a new mid-market logo, and the customization complaint held across 9/10 — the 2K–10K segment is contested but quiet.
**Why it matters for Cerkl marketing:** Two of the strongest players are now running conquest SEO; Foundations is the obvious next target. The two highest-leverage Cerkl comparison gaps remain unbuilt: **vs. Workshop** (most direct Foundations head-to-head; neutralize its back-to-back G2 #1 newsletter badge) and **vs. AxiosHQ** (cheapest win, free vs. ~$15k floor).
**Action:** Furqan/SEO + comparison-seo — move both briefs into the queue this week. (Editorial stance still bars *offensive* listicles; these are owned-comparison pages, which are in-scope.)

### 4. Press the Foundations free-forever contrast — the moat widened to week 9
**What it is:** 0/10 vendors offer free-forever (9th straight week); pricing gating is near-total and no one reverted ([W27 §f](../../competitor-marketing/weekly/2026-W27.md)).
**Why it matters for Cerkl marketing:** The single competitive fact with zero erosion over two months. As peers gate harder, "free, public pricing, live this afternoon, no quote" reads cleaner every week.
**Action:** Marketing — keep the Foundations transparency line in rotation on the homepage hero + Foundations LP; pair it with the benchmark report as the gated-value counterweight ("free to use, and here's the data to prove it works"). Evergreen; reinforce alongside #1.

### 5. Stand up the customization-ceiling wedge LP while the mid-market is quiet
**What it is:** The template/customization complaint held across 9/10 vendors again (Workshop the lone holdout), and no competitor shipped a new 2K–10K logo this window ([W27 §e](../../competitor-marketing/weekly/2026-W27.md)).
**Why it matters for Cerkl marketing:** Durable, evergreen wedge — and the segment is momentarily uncontested on new proof, so a wedge asset lands without noise.
**Action:** Marketing — one-screen LP: *"You don't need to redesign your channels. Cerkl personalizes the message inside the channels you already have,"* with honestly-attributed G2 complaint fragments as backdrop. Low lift; carries from the W26/W17 recommendation.
