## Headline of the week

**"Prove impact" just became the consensus 2026 IC problem on both sides of the market at once — and it's Cerkl's home turf.** The practitioner side is shouting it: Ragan's lead piece this week is literally *"Stop measuring activity, start measuring impact"* (measurement named IC's top unsolved gap), the 2026 Ragan Communications Benchmark found **43% of teams would spend extra budget on humans, not tools**, and the AI-productivity backlash got a number — **78% use AI, ~9% are satisfied** ([rolling-7day 06-18](../rolling-7day.md)). The vendor side is racing to *substantiate* the same message: this week Firstup dropped a **Forrester TEI study claiming 398% ROI**, ContactMonkey re-pushed an **Internal Email Benchmark Report**, and Simpplr built a case study entirely on adoption metrics — measurement migrated off the homepage hero and **down into the gated-proof layer** ([2026-W26 §Pain-point 1](../../competitor-marketing/weekly/2026-W26.md)). So the whole category — buyers and sellers — is converging on the exact thing Cerkl does natively (delivered, opened, read, *by employee*). The risk is that competitors now *own the proof conversation* with ROI studies and benchmark reports while Cerkl has the better data but no proof *asset* to point at. **The single most important marketing move this quarter is to plant Cerkl's flag on measurement with a proof artifact before the category commoditizes the claim.**

## Action items (ranked)

### 1. Build Cerkl's measurement proof asset — a "State of Internal Comms Read Rates 2026" benchmark report
**What it is:** The category turned measurement into a *substantiation* contest this week — Firstup's Forrester TEI (398% ROI), ContactMonkey's Internal Email Benchmark Report, Simpplr's adoption-metric roundup ([2026-W26 §b/§Pain-point 1](../../competitor-marketing/weekly/2026-W26.md)). Practitioners are simultaneously begging for impact measurement ([Ragan "stop measuring activity"](https://www.ragan.com/stop-measuring-activity-and-start-measuring-impact/)). Cerkl's anonymized per-employee read-rate data is the one dataset no competitor can replicate.
**Why it matters for Cerkl marketing:** Triple duty — flagship gated lead-gen asset, SEO play on the now-5-deep "internal communications measurement" SERP, and the *proof artifact* that finally backs Cerkl's core measurement claim. It converts Cerkl's biggest data advantage into a marketing weapon.
**Action:** **Travis + Marketing** — scope the report this sprint (data pull feasibility + outline). Target a Q3 drop to anchor the Foundations campaign. Highest-leverage item on the board.

### 2. Ship the AI-trust / "AI can't make them read it" POV (queued 4 weeks — now ship-this-week)
**What it is:** AI backlash is now a hard number on the practitioner side (78% use / 9% satisfaction; [HBR: trust beats policy for surfacing AI use](https://hbr.org/2026/06/why-employees-arent-transparent-about-their-ai-usage); 43% would hire humans) — and two enterprise vendors just validated AI-skepticism as a marketable lane: Staffbase *"AI won't save your employee experience. Trust will."* + Poppulo *"fix your data governance before agentic AI"* ([2026-W26 §Pain-point 2](../../competitor-marketing/weekly/2026-W26.md)).
**Why it matters for Cerkl marketing:** A contrarian lane Cerkl can ride authentically and tie straight back to measurement: *"AI can write the message. It can't make employees read it. The unsexy part — right message, right person, proof they saw it — is still the whole job."* It rides the backlash without being anti-AI.
**Action:** **Tracy / Content** — draft the POV this week (blog + LinkedIn). This has been recommended since W23; the market has now de-risked it.

### 3. Stand up a measurement-hero LP variant with per-employee read-receipt proof
**What it is:** Six vendors prove measurement with *aggregate* stats (398%, 282%, 92%); none shows per-employee delivery ([2026-W26 §c](../../competitor-marketing/weekly/2026-W26.md)).
**Why it matters for Cerkl marketing:** Specificity exposes the aggregate hand-waving — *"They publish a 398% ROI study. We show you which employees opened it, by name."*
**Action:** **Marketing / demand gen** — build one measurement-hero Foundations+ LP variant next sprint; A/B against current copy. Pairs with the proof asset (#1) as the conversion surface.

### 4. Pre-empt ContactMonkey's conquest motion — commission the overdue "vs." pages
**What it is:** Under Cates, ContactMonkey is now the most aggressive conquester in the set — new "Staffbase Alternatives" + "vs. Staffbase" pages, refreshed "Best Workshop Alternatives 2026," and actively poaching Staffbase (Chamberlain Group "switched from Staffbase") ([2026-W26 §5/§f](../../competitor-marketing/weekly/2026-W26.md)). That energy will turn toward the Foundations tier.
**Why it matters for Cerkl marketing:** Cerkl's existing comparison pages rank well, but the two highest-leverage gaps are still unbuilt: **vs. Workshop** (most direct Foundations head-to-head; neutralize its back-to-back G2 #1 newsletter badge) and **vs. AxiosHQ** (cheapest competitive SEO win per `shared/competitors.md`).
**Action:** **Travis / SEO** — commission both "vs." pages this sprint; brief includes the free-forever + per-employee-measurement contrast.

### 5. Lean the Foundations campaign hard on free-forever + transparent pricing while the field gates harder
**What it is:** 0/10 competitors offer a free-forever tier for the **8th straight week**, and pricing gating is now near-total — ContactMonkey and Workvivo both pulled public pricing in W25 and neither reverted ([2026-W26 §f](../../competitor-marketing/weekly/2026-W26.md)).
**Why it matters for Cerkl marketing:** The category is moving the *opposite* direction from Cerkl. The moat isn't just "free" — it's "free *and* you can see the price *and* you're live this afternoon." Make the gap loud.
**Action:** **Marketing** — make "Free. Public pricing. Live this afternoon. No quote required." a lead message in the Q3 Foundations push.

### 6. Counter-program the "one platform / digital HQ" hero convergence with the anti-destination contrast
**What it is:** 7/10 vendors now lead their hero with consolidation — Staffbase's new H1 *"one platform to reach every employee,"* Workvivo *"One digital headquarters,"* LumApps *"where work comes together"* ([2026-W26 §Pain-point 3](../../competitor-marketing/weekly/2026-W26.md)).
**Why it matters for Cerkl marketing:** The more the field crowds "come consolidate onto our hub," the cleaner Cerkl's *"we layer on top of the channels you already run — the delivery and measurement layer, not the destination"* reads.
**Action:** **Messaging** — refresh the homepage/category one-liner to sharpen the anti-destination contrast; reuse in the vs. Staffbase/Workvivo pages.
