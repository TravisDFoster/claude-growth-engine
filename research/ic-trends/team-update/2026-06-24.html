Marketing Team — 7-Day Rollup

Date: 2026-06-24 (Wednesday) Action items: 6 Watchlist: 5

"Prove impact" just became the consensus 2026 IC problem on both sides of the market at once — and it's Cerkl's home turf. The practitioner side is shouting it: Ragan's lead piece this week is literally "Stop measuring activity, start measuring impact," the 2026 Ragan Communications Benchmark found 43% of teams would spend extra budget on humans, not tools, and the AI-productivity backlash got a number — 78% use AI, ~9% are satisfied. The vendor side is racing to substantiate the same message: Firstup dropped a Forrester TEI study claiming 398% ROI, ContactMonkey re-pushed an Internal Email Benchmark Report, and Simpplr built a case study entirely on adoption metrics — measurement migrated off the homepage hero and down into the gated-proof layer. The whole category is converging on the exact thing Cerkl does natively (delivered, opened, read, by employee). The risk: competitors now own the proof conversation with ROI studies while Cerkl has better data but no proof asset. The single most important marketing move this quarter is to plant Cerkl's flag on measurement with a proof artifact before the category commoditizes the claim.
