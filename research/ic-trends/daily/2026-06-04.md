### 2. Karian: "middle managers are becoming the most burned-out role in the western economy" — a structural role-design argument
**Source:** isitworking Substack (Ghassan Karian, founder/now non-Exec Chairman of Ipsos Karian and Box, writing personally) | **URL:** [https://isitworking.substack.com/p/why-middle-managers-are-becoming](https://isitworking.substack.com/p/why-middle-managers-are-becoming) | **Date:** 2026-06-04 | **Category:** Community
Karian argues middle management has become "structurally impossible" — responsibility without authority, squeezed between layers, buried in admin. Backs the claim with Ipsos Karian and Box's employee-experience benchmark database showing work-life-balance scores deteriorated for middle managers 2023–2025 while executives' improved. Five root causes (responsibility-without-authority, hybrid-coordination tax, emotional absorption, admin bloat, outdated role design); fix the role, not the human.
**Why it matters for Cerkl:** Hardens the case for *direct-to-employee* personalized delivery as a complement to manager cascades — but the argument is role-design, not span-of-control. The often-quoted "12.1 reports / +50% since 2013" stat is **not** in this article; it's from a separate [Gallup span-of-control report](https://www.gallup.com/workplace/700718/span-control-optimal-team-size-managers.aspx) (8.2 → 12.1, 2013–2025). Pair the two carefully — see the [Karian deep-dive](../deepdives/karian-middle-manager-burnout-2026-06-04.md) for the median-vs-mean pressure-test (median span is still 5–6; the 12.1 mean is pulled up by a tail of mega-teams).
