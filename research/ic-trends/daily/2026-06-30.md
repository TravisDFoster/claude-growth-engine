# IC Trends — 2026-06-30

**Today's headline takeaway:** The category's measurement message has finished migrating into the *proof layer* — in a single week **two competitors dropped internal-email benchmark reports** (ContactMonkey's *Internal Email Benchmark 2026*, 255K+ campaigns/76% open rate; Workshop's *2026 IC Benchmarks*, 186M emails analyzed), exactly the gated-proof-asset move flagged in W26. Underneath that, analyst and practitioner signal converges on AI accountability — Gartner on the hidden workforce costs eroding AI ROI, HBR on employees *over-trusting* AI advice, and Poppulo's data showing "can't measure impact" is still IC's #1 unsolved pain.

## Top items

### 1. The benchmark-report arms race: two competitors publish internal-email benchmarks in one week
**Source:** ContactMonkey + Workshop   |   **URL:** https://www.contactmonkey.com/blog/internal-email-benchmark-report-2026 + https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uTadx_R2d9U   |   **Date:** 2026-06-25   |   **Category:** Competitor

**Why it matters for Cerkl:** This is the W26 "proof layer" prediction landing in real time — competitors are racing to own the benchmark dataset. The "State of Internal Comms Read Rates 2026" report built on Cerkl's read data (lead-gen + SEO + the artifact backing the measurement claim) is now an urgent, not optional, move.

### 2. Gartner: CHROs must identify hidden workforce costs to protect AI ROI
**Source:** Gartner   |   **URL:** https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2026-06-29-gartner-says-chros-must-identify-hidden-workforce-costs-to-protect-ai-roi   |   **Date:** 2026-06-29   |   **Category:** Analyst

### 4. HBR: Employees aren't questioning AI advice enough
**Source:** HBR   |   **URL:** https://hbr.org/2026/06/employees-arent-questioning-ai-advice-enough   |   **Date:** 2026-06-24   |   **Category:** Analyst

### 5. Poppulo data: measurement is still IC's #1 unsolved pain
**Source:** Poppulo   |   **URL:** https://www.poppulo.com/blog/employee-communications-in-2026-the-issues-senior-leaders-are-actually-grappling-with   |   **Date:** 2026-06   |   **Category:** Competitor
Poppulo's own survey: 63% can't satisfactorily measure sentiment and 31% name quantifying impact as their single biggest pain point.
**Why it matters for Cerkl:** A competitor is publishing the exact problem Cerkl solves better than they do. The stat is reusable in Cerkl's measurement positioning — and it underscores why aggregate-ROI claims (the W26 trend) don't actually close the practitioner's pain; per-employee read data does.

### 6. Forrester: AI will rewrite employee experience, and "deep listening" shows how
**Source:** Forrester   |   **URL:** https://www.forrester.com/blogs/ai-will-rewrite-employee-experience-deep-listening-shows-how/   |   **Date:** 2026-06   |   **Category:** Analyst
Forrester frames AI "deep listening" — turning Teams/meeting digital exhaust into anonymized real-time sentiment — as the next EX/IC measurement lever.
**Why it matters for Cerkl:** The measurement axis is broadening from read/engagement into passive sentiment. Worth tracking whether competitors (Workvivo Seer, Simpplr) lean into this; Cerkl's wedge stays "active, attributable read" vs. inferred sentiment.

### 7. Staffbase: "What is an AI-powered employee experience platform?"
**Source:** Staffbase   |   **URL:** https://staffbase.com/blog/ai-powered-employee-experience-platform   |   **Date:** 2026-06-24   |   **Category:** Competitor

**Why it matters for Cerkl:** Staffbase is trying to redefine the category around platform-depth/AI-nativeness, away from delivery + measurement. Counter-frame: buyers don't need an AI-native platform; they need their message read on the channels they already have.

### 8. Ragan: comms for deskless workers — "if they can't see it, it's not there"
