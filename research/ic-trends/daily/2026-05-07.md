# IC Trends — 2026-05-07

**Today's headline takeaway:** The 2026 wave of "State of IC" research has now fully landed (Gallagher, Simpplr, ContactMonkey, Ragan/Axios HQ on May 14), and they all point at the same wall: IC teams are being asked to be strategic, AI-fluent, and measurable, but most are still under-resourced, under-targeted, and trusted only narrowly by leadership and employees. The Cerkl-relevant signal underneath: targeting/segmentation, frontline reach, and credible measurement are now the named gaps in the category — exactly Foundations+/Omni AI's strike zone.

---

## Top items

### 1. Simpplr — 2026 State of Internal Communications report
**Source:** Simpplr   |   **URL:** https://www.simpplr.com/blog/state-of-internal-communications-2026/   |   **Date:** 2026-04-16
Year-over-year IC improvement dropped from 54% (2025) to 33% (2026); only 51% of orgs segment audiences and 45% blast everyone uniformly; 41% report half or more of their workforce lacks computer access; 26% don't measure IC's revenue impact despite 89% of execs saying they should. Six conditions for IC programs that scale: leadership engagement, clear ownership, credible measurement, audience targeting, frontline-ready delivery, right tech.
**Why it matters for Cerkl:** Three of the six "scale conditions" — audience targeting, frontline-ready delivery, credible measurement — are core Broadcast pitches. This is the highest-authority validation of our positioning the category has produced this year.

### 2. Gallagher — State of the Sector 2026: the "Readiness Gap"
**Source:** Gallagher Employee Experience   |   **URL:** https://www.ajg.com/employeeexperience/state-of-the-sector/   |   **Date:** 2026-03 (PR Newswire pickup 2026-03-13: https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/61-of-organizations-have-no-formal-change-communication-strategy-despite-ranking-it-as-their-most-pressing-need-302709190.html)
17th annual report, 1,300+ comms/HR pros. 73% of IC teams aim to operate strategically, only 18% have achieved it. 83% say information overload is growing; 61% have no formal change communication strategy despite ranking it the #1 need. AI confidence jumps from 35% to 63% when there's a framework + leadership endorsement.
**Why it matters for Cerkl:** "Readiness Gap" is now the industry-standard frame for "we know what to do, we don't have the structure/tools to do it." It's a clean wedge for Foundations → Foundations+ upsell narrative (segmentation, analytics, calendar invites = the structure they don't have).

### 3. Ragan Employee Communications & Culture Conference — top takeaways
**Source:** PR Daily   |   **URL:** https://www.prdaily.com/top-takeaways-from-ragans-employee-communications-and-culture-conference-2026/   |   **Date:** 2026-04-24
Boston conference recap. Themes: AI can distribute info but cannot build belonging; employees detect AI-generated messages and lose trust; manager enablement should fit into managers' existing workflow ("if it's homework, it fails"); repetition and context shape interpretation more than message structure. Notable quote — Marta Ravin: "AI can distribute information, but it can't foster a sense of belonging."
**Why it matters for Cerkl:** Reinforces the Cerkl angle that personalization/targeting (delivering the right message to the right person) is what humanizes AI-era comms — versus dumping AI-generated copy into a mass send.

### 4. ContactMonkey — Global State of IC 2026
**Source:** ContactMonkey   |   **URL:** https://www.contactmonkey.com/ebook/global-state-of-internal-communications-report-2026   |   **Date:** 2026-01-13
AI is the #1 topic of interest among IC teams but real implementation is still limited. Only 26–75% of employees consistently engage with internal emails. Lean IC teams spend most of their time on creation/formatting, not strategy. Only 9% of employees completely trust leadership communications.
**Why it matters for Cerkl:** Direct competitor research validating Foundations' core narrative ("get out of Outlook/Gmail and stop spending all your time on formatting"). The 9% trust stat is a strong opener for Cerkular and BoFu pages.

### 5. Gallup — Engagement crisis deepens, managers are the lever
**Source:** Gallup Workplace   |   **URL:** https://www.gallup.com/workplace/349484/state-of-the-global-workplace.aspx   |   **Date:** 2026 edition (referenced via UC Today recap 2026: https://www.uctoday.com/employee-engagement-recognition/hr-employee-engagement-trends-2026-industry-reports/)
Global engagement fell to 20% in 2025 (lowest since 2020); US sits at 31% (11-year low). Manager engagement dropped 5 points (27% → 22%), the largest YoY drop on record. Managers explain 70% of team-level engagement variance.
**Why it matters for Cerkl:** Manager-as-broadcaster is a real wedge. If managers drive 70% of engagement variance and are themselves disengaged, IC teams need delivery tools that *equip managers* — not just blast all-employees. Reinforces the "send-on-behalf-of-leadership" angle of Calendar Invites + targeted Blasts.

### 6. Staffbase — AI quality and intranet search are the new battleground
**Source:** Staffbase blog   |   **URL:** https://staffbase.com/blog/ai-quality-layer (also: https://staffbase.com/blog/ai-intranet-search)   |   **Date:** 2026 (specific dates not surfaced; recent blog wave)
Staffbase is publishing aggressively into "AI quality layer," "permission-aware AI," and "AI intranet search" — staking the intranet-platform claim that AI without governance/permissions/quality is dangerous in regulated industries.
**Why it matters for Cerkl:** Staffbase is leaning *harder* into the intranet/platform replacement narrative. Cerkl's counter — "we're the personalized delivery and measurement layer across the channels you already have" — gets sharper as Staffbase commits more deeply to platform-replacement positioning. Worth a watch on whether their "AI quality" framing migrates into RFPs.

### 7. RTO mandates as an IC challenge — Fidelity, BCE, PNC, Paramount
**Source:** Ragan Communications, multiple   |   **URL:** https://www.ragan.com/the-week-in-comms-paramount-pnc-deloitte/ and https://www.ragan.com/the-week-in-comms-sony-propublica-rto/   |   **Date:** 2026 spring
54% of Fortune 100 employees now under five-day RTO (up from 11% a year prior). Fidelity ended hybrid in 2026 with comms framed around "in-person collaboration." BCE fired dozens for "swipe and go" attendance fraud. 72% of employees believe RTO is attrition-by-policy, not collaboration.
**Why it matters for Cerkl:** RTO communications are a *high-stakes change comms moment* — exactly where Gallagher's "61% have no formal change comms strategy" stat bites. Talking track for AEs: "if you're rolling out RTO and your only tool is a Friday all-hands email, you're losing the trust war."

### 8. Gartner — task-specific AI agents in 40% of enterprise apps by 2026
**Source:** Gartner   |   **URL:** https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-08-26-gartner-predicts-40-percent-of-enterprise-apps-will-feature-task-specific-ai-agents-by-2026-up-from-less-than-5-percent-in-2025   |   **Date:** 2025-08-26 (still the operative 2026 prediction)
Up from <5% in 2025. Forrester echoes: 2026 is the breakthrough year for multi-agent systems where specialized agents collaborate under central coordination.
**Why it matters for Cerkl:** Frame for how Omni AI's News Digest/AI features should be discussed in pitches: not "we have AI" — everyone does — but "we have *task-specific* AI for the IC use case (personalization, subject lines, segmentation), inside your existing channels."
