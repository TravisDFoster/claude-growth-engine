# IC Trends — 2026-06-18

**Today's headline takeaway:** The AI-productivity backlash crystallized into hard numbers this week — Ragan's 2026 benchmark shows 78% of IC teams use AI but it's mostly producing *more* content, not better comms (frontline satisfaction sits at 9%), and given fresh budget 43% of communicators would hire humans over buying more tooling. The through-line across today's signal: volume is solved, *impact* isn't — measurement, targeting, and leadership alignment remain the top unsolved gaps, which is squarely Cerkl's wedge.

## Top items

### 1. The AI-productivity backlash gets a number: "AI may not be the comms engine we were promised"
**Source:** Ragan   |   **URL:** https://www.ragan.com/ai-is-here-to-stay-but-it-may-not-be-the-comms-productivity-engine-we-were-promised/   |   **Date:** 2026-06-17   |   **Category:** Community
78% of IC teams use AI, but it's generating more volume — not better comms. Half are shipping more content while employee satisfaction with that content sits at ~9%. This is the live practitioner backlash: AI overload, not AI adoption, is now the pain point.
**Why it matters for Cerkl:** Validates the personalization + measurement thesis — "more content" is the disease, not the cure. Cerkl's pitch ("right message, right person, measured per employee") is the antidote to volume-for-volume's-sake AI.

### 2. Ragan 2026 Communications Benchmark: given extra budget, 43% would hire humans — not buy tools
**Source:** Ragan   |   **URL:** https://www.ragan.com/press-releases/ragan-releases-2026-communications-benchmark-report/   |   **Date:** 2026-06-15   |   **Category:** Community
With a hypothetical budget bump, 43% of communicators would add headcount rather than more software. Counters the "AI replaces IC" narrative — demand for people persists even amid heavy AI adoption.
**Why it matters for Cerkl:** Buyers are tool-fatigued, not tool-hungry. The winning sell isn't "another AI tool" — it's a tool that makes the existing (human) team provably more effective. Lean into free Foundations + fast time-to-value, not feature-pile.

### 3. "Stop measuring activity, start measuring impact"
**Source:** Ragan   |   **URL:** https://www.ragan.com/stop-measuring-activity-and-start-measuring-impact/   |   **Date:** 2026-06-17   |   **Category:** Community
Measurement, audience targeting, and leadership alignment remain IC's top unsolved operational gaps — proving behavioral impact, not counting outputs.
**Why it matters for Cerkl:** Directly on the measurement axis. Per the 2026-06-17 competitive watch, 6 of 10 vendors now claim "measurement" but none deliver employee-level read attribution. This piece is the demand-side proof that the *practitioner* gap is real — Cerkl's "delivered, opened, read, by name" wedge answers the exact pain Ragan names.

### 4. A sudden federal directive makes your AI playbook vulnerable
