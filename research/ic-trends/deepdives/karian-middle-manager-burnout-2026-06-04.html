Karian's five root causes of structural burnout

CAUSE 1

Responsibility without authority

Own outcomes without the decision rights to deliver them.

CAUSE 2

Hybrid-work coordination tax

Distributed teams multiply scheduling, alignment, and rework load.

CAUSE 3

Emotional absorption

Buffer downward anxiety from frontline and upward pressure from execs.

CAUSE 4

Administrative bloat

Reporting, compliance, status, and tooling overhead eat manager hours.

CAUSE 5

Outdated role design

Built for a different operating model and never redesigned.

Headline evidence (in-house data)

  • Work-life-balance scores for middle managers deteriorated 2023–2025; in 2025 executives' scores improved while middle managers stayed “comparatively squeezed.” Source: Ipsos Karian and Box employee-experience benchmark.
  • Middle managers score significantly lower than other levels on manageable workload, decision influence, and wellbeing.

Prescription

Reduce span of control, strip admin load, grant real decision rights, deploy AI for reporting/scheduling, fewer + clearer priorities from the top, practical support tools over resilience training.

Provenance flag — important

The often-repeated “average manager has 12.1 direct reports (+50% since 2013); spends less than half their time managing” stat is not in this article. It originates from Gallup's span-of-control research: Gallup, “Span of Control” — 8.2 (2013) → 10.9 (2024) → 12.1 (2025).

The two are commonly conflated. Pair them deliberately, but cite Gallup for the stat.

05Gotchas

The 12.1 stat ≠ Karian's article

Conflating them creates a provenance problem if cited in published writing. Pair Karian (role-design argument) with Gallup (span-of-control data) deliberately.

Median vs. mean

Gallup's 12.1 mean sits on a long tail (13% of managers oversee 25+). The median is still 5–6 — the buyer's lived experience for most ICs and team leads. “Average manager has 12.1 reports” sounds compelling in a deck but invites pushback in a room of practitioners who oversee 6.

isitworking is personal, not corporate

Karian publishes in personal capacity — useful for “credible voice from the IC research industry” framing, not citable as official Ipsos Karian and Box research. The corporate research lives at ipsoskarianandbox.com/insights and the IoIC IC Index (ic-index.html).

UK-centric

Karian and Box's panel is largely UK; IC Index 2026 surveyed 5,000 UK employees. US/EU buyer rooms will ask for US data — pair with Gallup/Gartner for the US side.
