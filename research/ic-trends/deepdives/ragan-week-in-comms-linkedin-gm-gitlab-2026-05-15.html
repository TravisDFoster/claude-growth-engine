02What it does — three cases on four axes

The cases vary on tone, channel, timing, and stakeholder transparency. Reading them together is the point.

CASE 1 · 2026-05-13

LinkedIn — Shapero memo

~875 roles (5% of 17,500). Strategic framing, cuts bundled with marketing/vendor/office reductions. All-employee memo 7am PT + manager cascade. Acknowledges departing staff by function and calls on remaining staff to support them. Risk: "reinvent how we work" phrasing is now formulaic.

CASE 2 · 2026-05-11

GM — IT cuts

~500–600 IT roles, Austin + Warren. 15-minute notice email, scripted HR readout, hang up. No two-way channel. No acknowledgement. Employees filled the silence with "you're replacing us with AI" — even though AI wasn't the only driver. Outcome: public revolt; CNBC quoted employees by name.

CASE 3 · 2026-05-12

GitLab — Bill Staples "Act 2"

~7% workforce + 30% country footprint. Public CEO blog post. Pre-empts the AI cost-cutting read explicitly ("this is not an AI optimization"). Gap: spoke to investors, not employees. Shares slid ~8% after-hours anyway.

06Gotchas

"Reinvent how we work" is a tell, not a frame
Ragan explicitly flags it as inauthentic-sounding due to overuse in 2026. Any layoff memo using this exact phrase signals template-grade comms. If Cerkl's content team writes about change comms, do not deploy this phrasing without irony.
Comms teams don't always control channel
GM's 15-minute meeting was likely a legal/HR-driven decision. IC owns the consequence even when it doesn't own the cause — which is where Cerkl's audit-log story matters. When a 15-minute layoff comes back as a Glassdoor story, IC needs receipts.
"This is not AI cost-cutting" disclaimers backfire
GitLab said it explicitly; The Register and Byteiota called it "AI washing" anyway. The disclaimer reads as defensive. Better: name the AI shift, name the savings reinvestment specifically, name the employees affected by function.
Channel inversion is the 2026 mistake
Public blog for an internal layoff (GitLab) reaches investors first, employees second. Internal memo for an industry-shaping AI reorg (Meta's Bosworth) leaks within hours anyway. Channel sequencing matters: internal first, on a channel that confirms receipt, then public.
