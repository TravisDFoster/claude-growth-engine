Gartner: 75% of employees will use chatbots over traditional IC channels by 2028

Date: 2026-05-27 Category: Analyst report (Gartner Predicts 2026) Author: Mark Whittle, VP Advisory, Gartner Act on it — Yes

Gartner forecasts that by 2028, 75% of employees will rely on chatbots to obtain relevant internal communications, replacing traditional communications channels — one of five predictions in a Feb 13, 2026 paywalled report led by VP Advisory Mark Whittle. The 2028 chatbot stat anchors a broader 4-year arc: PR budgets 2× by 2027, comms data/analytics spend doubling to 6% by 2029, and IC teams using employee digital-footprint analytics for personalized delivery. The strongest practitioner pushback — that "chatbots fail without information governance" — is exactly the wedge Cerkl already plays in: the delivery + measurement layer that feeds both the intranet and the chatbot.

The chatbot prediction's mechanism

Per the report, via Sociabble

"Chatbots will improve information accessibility and provide personalized, relevant communications. For pull communications, employees will ask questions and get personalized, curated answers. For push communications, chatbots will send customized push alerts to specific employee segments."

Underpinning signal cited (per WebSearch surface of the report): 75% of employees already use AI tools at work — Gartner extrapolates from current AI-usage rates to channel substitution. A separate Gartner finding referenced alongside the chatbot prediction: employees reporting high information overload are 52% less likely to report high intent to stay and 30% less likely to report high strategic alignment.

Methodology gap
No sample size, survey instrument, or baseline year is disclosed in any public-facing pickup. Standard Gartner Predicts format — analyst extrapolation from internal panel surveys — but the 75%-by-2028 number doesn't come with showable math.

06Gotchas

Methodology is opaque

No sample size, survey instrument, baseline year, or extrapolation method is disclosed in public materials. The 75%-by-2028 number is analyst extrapolation, not measurement. Use the stat in positioning, but don't over-anchor strategy on the exact number — anchor on the directional reframe.

HR press hasn't picked this up

Pickup is concentrated in comms/PR trade press only. CHRO/HR audiences (Cerkl's adjacent buyer for Foundations+) are not yet exposed to this prediction. That's both a risk (it might not actually land in CHRO conversations) and an opportunity (Cerkl could be the first to bridge the Gartner comms-predictions framing into HR conversations).

"Replacing managers" is a provocative framing not in the Gartner text

The Ragan write-up (and the prompt that surfaced this deep-dive) glossed the prediction as employees turning to chatbots instead of intranets/newsletters/managers. The Gartner verbatim is "replacing traditional communications channels" — managers aren't named. Don't take the "managers" framing into customer-facing copy; it's a Ragan reframe and won't survive scrutiny.

Whittle's quoted lines are about change leadership, not the chatbot prediction

"Effective leadership today is change leadership" — these are useful but tangential. Don't cite them as proof of the chatbot prediction; they're orthogonal.

2028 is two years from now, not five

The 2028 date sounds far but is two budget cycles. If a Cerkl IC-leader buyer signs in 2026 expecting their 2028 stack to look like their 2025 stack, the Gartner stat is the argument for not renewing the legacy intranet they're paying for. Use accordingly.

09Open questions

What's the underlying Gartner survey behind the 75%?

Sample size, baseline year, panel composition all undisclosed. Worth a sales-team or analyst-relations ask if Cerkl has Gartner client access (or anyone in network does).

Will the chatbot side of "2028" be the org's existing IT-deployed chatbot or a vendor-shipped IC-specific chatbot?

Material to Cerkl strategy — if it's the former (Teams Copilot, Slack AI, intranet-native), Cerkl needs a "feeds your existing chatbot" integration story; if the latter, the destination war reshuffles to include AI-IC vendors not yet in the named set.

Does Cerkl's measurement story need a chatbot-side signal by 2028?

Email opens / link clicks / read-through don't map onto chatbot interactions. Either Cerkl federates from the chatbot, or measurement coverage drops as channel mix shifts. This is the most strategic open question.

Is anyone going to claim the IC-strategist "distribution advantage" frame (Gawlak quote) before Cerkl does?

Sociabble and Isentia already moved on the "validation" framing. The "we lose narrative control" framing is unclaimed.

Does the 75% include knowledge workers only, or frontline + deskless too?

Per Firstup's recent research, 60% of frontline workers have never used AI and 64% lack access — Gartner's number can't plausibly include them at 75% by 2028. A methodology note that doesn't exist would resolve this.
