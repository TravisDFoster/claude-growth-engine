The headline prediction

"By 2027, 50% of enterprises without a people-centric AI strategy will lose their top AI talent to competitors who prioritize workforce enablement over basic adoption."

The release lands the prediction, then unpacks four workforce dynamics CHROs and CIOs must address together. The "enablement illusion" — confusing access and adoption metrics for transformation — is the diagnosis. Tool UX, governance, leadership gap, and psychological safety are the prescriptions.

02Who it's for

Primary audience

CHROs, CIOs, Chief AI Officers, CEOs at enterprises with formal AI initiatives. Gartner is explicitly putting CHROs in the seat alongside CIOs — historically a Gartner CIO-first frame.

Strong fit when

The org has rolled out AI tools (Copilot, ChatGPT Enterprise, Glean) but can't show ROI; leadership is debating who owns AI governance; HR is on the outside of an IT-led AI program; "shadow AI" has been flagged by security or legal.

Weak fit when

The org hasn't yet rolled out AI tools (the dynamics described don't exist yet), or the org is a pre-product AI-native startup where the labor distinctions don't map.

Gartner's reframe
Shadow AI isn't a security problem — it's a rollout communications problem. 88% of employees with enterprise AI access are using personal AI anyway, to save time. Enterprise AI tools' UX is bad, employees don't know which tools are sanctioned for which jobs, managers don't know how to model behavior, and the org has no read-through on whether AI rollout messages are landing. That's not a security tooling problem. It's a comms problem with security consequences.

05Gotchas

Prediction is squishy by design
"By 2027, 50% of enterprises…" is forecast-language. Don't cite it as a fact — cite it as analyst guidance backed by survey data. The defensible numbers are the 1Q26 survey figures (19%, 88%, 73%, 3.4×), not the prediction itself.
Direct fetch is blocked (HTTP 403)
Gartner is blocking automated access to its newsroom URLs. Cite the three independent re-publishers (IT-Online, AppleWorld Today, ITPro) — they each independently lift the same Basu/Sanchez quotes and stats, which is corroboration enough.
"People-centric AI strategy" is a Gartner-coined frame
It will become a buzzword. Cerkl shouldn't adopt the exact phrase — let prospects use it. Instead, translate it operationally: "rollout comms, decision rights, and tool UX." That maps to Cerkl's actual product surface.
Shadow AI as a comms problem is contested
Most security and IT trade press treats shadow AI as a tooling/governance problem (block ChatGPT, deploy enterprise AI, enforce DLP). Gartner is reframing it as primarily a UX-and-communication problem. That reframe is the IC-relevant insight; it's also the part most likely to be misunderstood by IT buyers. Be explicit when citing.
