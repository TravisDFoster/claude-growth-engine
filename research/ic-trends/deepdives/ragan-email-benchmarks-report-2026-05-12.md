# IC Deep Dive — Ragan "2026 Internal Email Benchmarks Report"

**Date:** 2026-05-12   |   **Category:** Analyst report (vendor-funded research, co-branded)

---

## TL;DR

The "Ragan 2026 Internal Email Benchmarks Report" is **not Ragan research** — it's PoliteMail's annual vendor-data study, co-branded with Ragan and distributed via Ragan/PR Daily as a gated white paper. Headline numbers (66% open rate, 7% click rate, 14 emails/month, 33-min monthly read time, n=~2B emails / ~11M employees / 10 S&P sectors) are useful as buyer-side anchors, but the source is the IC email **measurement** vendor whose moat depends on those numbers staying interesting. Two competing 2026 benchmarks dropped within 24 hours (PoliteMail/Ragan 2026-05-07, ContactMonkey 2026-05-08) — vendors are racing to own the 2026 benchmark category, and **no neutral independent IC email benchmark exists in the space yet.**

## What it is

An annual internal-email benchmark report, **conducted by [PoliteMail](https://politemail.com)** and distributed through Ragan Communications / PR Daily as a gated white paper. PoliteMail is an Outlook plug-in product for IC email tracking — their dataset IS the benchmark. The report covers ~2 billion corporate internal emails sent to nearly 11 million employees across 10 S&P industry sectors (technology, healthcare, consumer, financials, industrials, materials, communications, energy, education/nonprofit, utilities), with year-over-year comparisons to 2024 and 2023. PR Daily summary post is bylined by **Michael DesRochers (PoliteMail)**, labeled "presented by PoliteMail," and ran on 2026-05-04, three days before the gated Ragan landing went live.

## What it does / claims

The report makes a measurable-channel argument for internal email — "treat internal email as a measurable channel, one guided by real data, not assumptions." Headline data (all citations: [PR Daily summary](https://www.prdaily.com/internal-email-benchmark-report-key-findings/)):

- **Open rate: 66% unique opens** ("one-third of employees aren't even getting past the subject line")
- **Open attention rate: 83.7% average** (PoliteMail proprietary metric — time-in-email)
- **Click rate: 7% of recipients click at least one link** | of openers, **10% will click on average**
- **Volume: ~14 corporate emails per employee per month** | **33 minutes total read time**
- **Skim behavior: 14% of openers skim** — skimmers spend max **36 seconds** (30% of the 2-minute baseline)
- **Email length: ~500 words avg, ~2-minute read time**

Methodology caveats are acknowledged inside PoliteMail's own broader content — open rates are **inflated by Outlook preview-pane "opens," automated security scans, and stale distribution lists** ([PoliteMail on metrics](https://politemail.com/5-internal-email-metrics-better-than-open-rates/)). This is buried in the marketing copy but matters: the 66% open-rate headline is the most-cited PoliteMail number and the least defensible one in technical terms.

## Who it's for

- **Primary user / ICP overlap:** Internal communications managers and directors building 2026 plans, slide decks, and CFO conversations. Particularly useful for *PoliteMail buyers* — the report is structured to convert the reader to a PoliteMail demo.
- **Strong fit when:** You need a defensible-sounding 2026 anchor stat for a slide ("benchmark IC open rate is 66%") and your audience won't fact-check the methodology.
- **Weak fit when:** You need rigorous cross-channel measurement, multi-vendor data, or methodology you can defend in a technical procurement review. Also weak for SMB/Foundations buyers (PoliteMail is enterprise-Outlook-shop oriented).

## Gotchas

- **The Ragan name is editorial cover.** The data and authorship are PoliteMail's. If you cite "the Ragan 2026 benchmark" in a deck, you're citing PoliteMail telemetry. Practitioners and analysts who know the IC space already know this; sales prospects often don't.
- **Open rate of 66% is the most-cited and least-defensible number.** PoliteMail itself acknowledges Outlook preview-pane and security-scan inflation in their own content. Use this when the number shows up in a buyer conversation.
- **The full report is email-gated** behind a Ragan form (name, email, employee count, company type, job function, ToS accept). The PR Daily summary post is ungated and contains the headline numbers — start there for any internal use.
- **"Independent" is a stretch.** Ragan/PR Daily editorial framing presents this as trade-publication research; the byline ("presented by PoliteMail," authored by PoliteMail's CEO) gives it away on close read.
- **2024 and 2023 YoY comparisons are PoliteMail-only longitudinal data.** They're internally consistent, but not industry-wide trend lines.
