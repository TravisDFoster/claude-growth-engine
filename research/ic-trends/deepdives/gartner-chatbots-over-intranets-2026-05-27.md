# IC Deep Dive — Gartner "Predicts 2026: Top Predictions to Inform 2026 Comms Strategies"

**Date:** 2026-05-27   |   **Category:** Analyst report

---

## TL;DR

Gartner forecasts that by 2028, **75% of employees will rely on chatbots to obtain relevant internal communications, replacing traditional communications channels** — one of five predictions in a Feb 13, 2026 paywalled report led by VP Advisory Mark Whittle. The 2028 chatbot stat anchors a broader 4-year arc: PR budgets 2× by 2027, comms data/analytics spend doubling to 6% by 2029, and IC teams using employee digital-footprint analytics for personalized delivery. The strongest practitioner pushback — that "chatbots fail without information governance" — is exactly the wedge Cerkl already plays in: the delivery + measurement layer that feeds *both* the intranet and the chatbot.

## What it is

A Gartner Predicts report — *"Predicts 2026: Top Predictions to Inform 2026 Comms Strategies"* (doc ID [7160430](https://www.gartner.com/en/documents/7160430), HTTP 403 to non-clients). Published **2026-02-13** per Sociabble's pickup; written by **Mark Whittle**, VP Advisory at Gartner — the same analyst who hosted [Gartner's CCO-targeted webinar](https://www.gartner.com/en/webinar/798291/1797036-gartners-top-communications-predictions-for-2026-what-every-cco-must-know) on the same predictions. The full report is paywalled to Gartner clients; trade-press pickup is concentrated in comms/PR outlets (Ragan, Sociabble, CommsCollectiv, Sword & the Script, CMOTech Asia, Stuart Bruce's PR Futurist) — **no SHRM, HR Brew, HR Executive, Forbes, or CIO Dive coverage** has surfaced, which is itself a signal (this prediction set hasn't hit HR press yet).

## What it does / claims

The report makes **five forward predictions**. Verbatim where available:

1. **By 2027** — *"Mass adoption of public LLMs as a replacement for traditional search will drive a 2x increase in PR and earned media budgets."*
2. **By 2028** — *"75% of employees will rely on chatbots to obtain relevant internal communications, replacing traditional communications channels."* *(The headline prediction.)*
3. **By 2029** — *"45% of CCOs will adopt narrative intelligence technologies to support reputation monitoring amid an intensifying disinformation landscape."*
4. **By 2029** — *"75% of Communications teams will use analyses of employee digital footprints to design and deliver personalized communications."*
5. **By 2029** — *"Communications' spending on data & analytics will double to 6% of the function's budget to amplify decision-making speed and business impact."*

The chatbot prediction's mechanism, per the report (quoted in Sociabble's pickup):

> *"Chatbots will improve information accessibility and provide personalized, relevant communications. For pull communications, employees will ask questions and get personalized, curated answers. For push communications, chatbots will send customized push alerts to specific employee segments."*

Underpinning signal cited (per WebSearch surface of the report): **75% of employees already use AI tools at work** — Gartner extrapolates from current AI-usage rates to channel substitution. A separate Gartner finding referenced alongside the chatbot prediction: employees reporting high information overload are **52% less likely to report high intent to stay** and **30% less likely to report high strategic alignment**.

**Methodology gap:** No sample size, survey instrument, or baseline year is disclosed in any public-facing pickup. Standard Gartner Predicts format — analyst extrapolation from internal panel surveys — but the 75%-by-2028 number doesn't come with showable math.

## Who it's for

- **Primary user / ICP overlap:** Chief Communications Officers, VP Comms, IC Directors at enterprises Gartner sells to. The webinar framing — "What Every CCO Must Know" — and the all-5-predictions framing (PR + IC + analytics) point to head-of-comms readers, not IC managers.
- **Strong fit when:** A Cerkl ICP buyer (IC Director, Senior Director of Comms, SVP of IC) is presenting 2026 strategy to a CCO/CEO and needs an analyst anchor. Gartner predictions carry RFP-level weight; this is the kind of stat that ends up in board decks.
- **Weak fit when:** SMB / Foundations buyers (office manager, chief of staff, operations lead, founder) — Gartner is not their reference set. For those buyers, the prediction lands abstract; the actionable framing is what their next vendor does, not what Gartner forecasts.

## How it slots into Cerkl's competitive set / positioning

This is the cleanest external validation the category has produced for Cerkl's **existing reframe** — *"the intranet is the destination, not the comms strategy"* (from the vs-Staffbase positioning in [`shared/competitors.md`](../../shared/competitors.md#L56-L58)). Gartner doesn't just say the intranet won't be the strategy; it forecasts that by 2028 **the intranet won't even be where 75% of employees go to find internal info**. The reframe gets sharper:

| Reframe (today) | Reframe (post-Gartner) |
|---|---|
| "We're the delivery + measurement layer across the channels you already have." | "We're the delivery + measurement layer across the channels your employees will actually use — and increasingly, that won't be the intranet." |

**Three direct implications for Cerkl positioning:**

1. **Sharpens every "vs. intranet vendor" page.** vs-Staffbase, vs-Simpplr, vs-LumApps, vs-Workvivo refresh-queue items in `competitors.md` can now cite this Gartner stat — *"Gartner forecasts the intranet stops being the destination by 2028; you're being sold a destination, not a strategy."* Highest-authority analyst stat available for that reframe.

2. **Introduces a real measurement gap Cerkl needs to think about.** Today, Cerkl measures email opens, link clicks, read-through. If 75% of employees route around traditional channels to chatbots by 2028, what does Cerkl measure then? Chatbot interaction logs are a different signal class. **This is the first prediction in this set that points at a Cerkl roadmap question, not just a marketing one.** Worth a 2026-Q4 strategy conversation: does Cerkl need a "chatbot-side" measurement story by 2028, or does Cerkl federate signals from the org's existing chatbot (Slack-side bot, Teams Copilot, intranet-native)?

3. **Validates the "delivery layer" architecture over the "destination" architecture.** Every competitor in `competitors.md` (Staffbase, Simpplr, LumApps, Firstup, Workvivo, Poppulo, Workshop) is investing in an intranet/portal that IS the destination — see today's recap: Poppulo just acquired Sociabble for advocacy + frontline + rewards; LumApps closed $650M Bridgepoint specifically to push "AI Employee Hub" further. **If Gartner is right, every one of those plays is investing in the wrong layer.** Cerkl's "feed the channels your employees use" architecture is structurally better positioned for the post-2028 landscape than any vendor in the named competitive set.

- Add a footnoted "Gartner forecast" sidebar to the next refresh of `cerkl.com/broadcast/versus/staffbase`, `versus/simpplr`, `versus/lumapps`.
- Lift the headline 75%-by-2028 stat into the next IC-leader cold outreach as the lead.

**Decision-relevant axis: stance on intranet survival.** Gartner says employees route *around* the intranet (chatbot replaces it). Forrester + every named intranet vendor say the intranet *absorbs* the chatbot (it becomes the chatbot's governed knowledge base). **This single axis tells Cerkl whether to frame Broadcast as the delivery layer feeding both surfaces, or pick a side.** The right answer for Cerkl is almost certainly *feed both* — the destination war is between Staffbase/Simpplr/LumApps/Workvivo and the chatbot, and Cerkl doesn't need to win it; Cerkl needs to be upstream of whoever does.

## Gotchas

- **Methodology is opaque.** No sample size, survey instrument, baseline year, or extrapolation method is disclosed in public materials. The 75%-by-2028 number is analyst extrapolation, not measurement. Use the stat in positioning, but don't over-anchor strategy on the exact number — anchor on the directional reframe.
- **HR press hasn't picked this up.** Pickup is concentrated in comms/PR trade press only. CHRO/HR audiences (Cerkl's adjacent buyer for Foundations+) are not yet exposed to this prediction. That's both a risk (it might not actually land in CHRO conversations) and an opportunity (Cerkl could be the first to bridge the Gartner comms-predictions framing into HR conversations).
- **"Replacing managers" is a provocative framing not in the Gartner text.** The Ragan write-up (and the prompt that surfaced this deep-dive) glossed the prediction as employees turning to chatbots instead of *intranets/newsletters/managers*. The Gartner verbatim is *"replacing traditional communications channels"* — managers aren't named. Don't take the "managers" framing into customer-facing copy; it's a Ragan reframe and won't survive scrutiny.
- **Whittle's quoted lines in Ragan are about change leadership, not the chatbot prediction.** *"Effective leadership today is change leadership"* — these are useful but tangential. Don't cite them as proof of the chatbot prediction; they're orthogonal.
- **2028 is two years from now, not five.** The 2028 date sounds far but is two budget cycles. If a Cerkl IC-leader buyer signs in 2026 expecting their 2028 stack to look like their 2025 stack, the Gartner stat is the argument for *not* renewing the legacy intranet they're paying for. Use accordingly.

## Open questions

- **What's the underlying Gartner survey behind the 75%?** Sample size, baseline year, panel composition all undisclosed. Worth a sales-team or analyst-relations ask if Cerkl has Gartner client access (or anyone in network does).
- **Will the chatbot side of "2028" be the org's existing IT-deployed chatbot (Teams Copilot, Slack AI, intranet-native), or a vendor-shipped IC-specific chatbot?** Material to Cerkl strategy — if it's the former, Cerkl needs a "feeds your existing chatbot" integration story; if the latter, the destination war reshuffles to include AI-IC vendors not yet in the named set.
- **Does Cerkl's measurement story need a chatbot-side signal by 2028?** Email opens / link clicks / read-through don't map onto chatbot interactions. Either Cerkl federates from the chatbot, or measurement coverage drops as channel mix shifts. This is the most strategic open question.
- **Is anyone going to claim the IC-strategist "distribution advantage" frame (Gawlak quote) before Cerkl does?** Sociabble and Isentia already moved on the "validation" framing. The "we lose narrative control" framing is unclaimed.
- **Does the 75% include knowledge workers only, or frontline + deskless too?** Per Firstup's recent research, 60% of frontline workers have never used AI and 64% lack access — Gartner's number can't plausibly include them at 75% by 2028. Methodology note that doesn't exist would resolve this.
