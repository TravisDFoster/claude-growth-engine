01What it is & what it does

Cork, Ireland-founded employee experience platform founded in 2017 by Joe Lennon and John Goulding (thinkbusiness.ie), raised a $16M Series A from Tiger Global in May 2020 (TechCrunch), acquired by Zoom April 2023 (TechCrunch).

2026 homepage tagline: "Empower your people, unlock knowledge, and deepen understanding. The AI-native employee experience platform bringing it all together." (workvivo.com). What it does / claims

Positions itself as "the world's best employee experience platform designed for engagement, built for intelligence, and powered by AI at every layer" (workvivo.com) — an all-in-one EXP combining internal comms, engagement, recognition, intranet, and measurement on one stack.

  • Social-first activity feed + Spaces + Pages — the original "Facebook for work" UX: feed, reactions/comments/shares, shout-outs, podcasts, live streams. Pages handle governed content; Spaces handle conversation (Changeengine).
  • Workvivo AI — launched April 2025, powered by Zoom AI Companion. Searches across Workvivo content, drafts updates/shout-outs/surveys; customizable Help Center. Included at no extra cost (blog, support).
  • Seer by Workvivo — standalone people-intelligence product launched 2026-05-06. Connects sentiment, communication, and performance data (GlobeNewswire).
  • Zoom-client integration — Workvivo runs as a tab inside the Zoom desktop client (blog).
  • Frontline mobile + Zoom Workplace for Frontline — push-to-talk, shift swapping, task management, AI shift-handoff summaries (Zoom news).

Zoom integration story (2026)
The post-acquisition narrative is no longer "Workvivo, now part of Zoom" — it's "Workvivo is one of five Zoom Workplace pillars" alongside Zoom Workplace, Zoom Phone, Zoom Revenue Accelerator, and Zoom CX (Zoom blog). Workvivo AI is Zoom AI Companion rebadged. Workvivo is now sold (and bundled) by Zoom's enterprise sales force, not just by the Cork team.

02Who it's for

Primary user

Internal comms, HR, and people teams at mid-market through enterprise organizations that want a culture-and-engagement platform employees actually open. Strong pull in retail, hospitality, transportation, and frontline-heavy industries. Published logos: Amazon, Ryanair, Bupa, Dollar General, Virgin Group, Lululemon, Mercedes, Telus, Motherson (customers).

Strong fit when

Buyer's #1 KPI is engagement/adoption/cultural connection; org is already a Zoom Workplace shop or migrating off Workplace from Meta (Workvivo is Meta's only named migration partner — GlobeNewswire 2024-05-14); workforce is deskless/distributed; budget supports ~$20k+/year floor.

Weak fit when

Buyer needs structured, governed corporate comms (compliance-driven announcements, formal HR notices, regulated-industry change comms); needs cross-channel delivery to channels Workvivo doesn't own (Outlook, Teams, existing SharePoint, SMS, signage) without replacing the home; needs deep email-newsletter authoring; is sub-250 employees or needs a free tier.

03How it slots into Cerkl's competitive set

Workvivo is already in shared/competitors.md with a live Cerkl "vs." page (cerkl.com/broadcast/versus/workvivo). The 2026 update is that Workvivo has visibly rotated its narrative from "social engagement platform" toward "AI-native EXP with people intelligence" — compressing the differentiation gap on the AI axis even though the underlying surface area is still engagement-first.

1. Counter-frame still wins — framing matters more

"We're the delivery + measurement layer; the intranet is the destination, not the comms strategy." Workvivo's homepage now claims intranet, engagement, and measurement in one product. That's a fat target if the buyer already has an intranet/SharePoint, an HRIS for measurement, and just wants disciplined cross-channel delivery. Reframe lands hardest when buyer is adding to an existing stack.

2. Engagement-first vs. structured comms collision

Workvivo wins "how do we get employees to feel something?" Cerkl wins "how do we make sure the right message reached the right people through the channel they actually read?" Different jobs that look similar in an RFP. Practitioner reviews flag notification overload and weak search — symptoms of feed-first design. Cerkl's pitch: predictable native-inbox delivery, segment-targeted, measurable open/read by audience.

3. Zoom acquisition pushed Workvivo upmarket

Sold by Zoom AEs into Workplace renewals. Business plan ~$20k/yr for 250–2,000 employees (eLearning Industry); Enterprise quote-driven for 2,000+. Structurally similar to Staffbase's ~$30k floor — both now enterprise-procurement deals with IT involvement. Cerkl's Foundations free tier remains the only wedge in this set that doesn't require procurement.

ICP-fit map for AEs

Foundations / 0–500

Workvivo is not in the conversation — pricing floor excludes them. Foundations wins by default; lead with free-forever, no procurement.

Foundations+ / 501–2,000

Workvivo Business is the price-comparable competitor. Cerkl wins on speed-to-launch, no-IT-required, and native email/inbox delivery vs. Workvivo's feed-first model. Lose when the buyer's KPI is "make work feel like community."

Omni AI / 2,001–12,200+ deskless

Workvivo is the most direct head-to-head, especially in retail/hospitality/transportation. Cerkl wins when buyer has SharePoint/Viva/Teams and wants to layer on personalized cross-channel delivery + measurement; loses on Workplace-from-Meta migrations or Zoom Workplace bundles.

Counter-positioning lines AEs can use

"Workvivo is the destination — we make sure your message gets read on the channels your employees already use, including Workvivo if you have it." (Reframes Workvivo as a channel, not a replacement.)

"Workvivo measures engagement with Workvivo. Cerkl measures engagement with your message — across email, intranet, mobile, and whatever else is in your stack."

"A feed is great for shout-outs. It's not great for the CEO town hall summary, the open-enrollment deadline, or the safety bulletin. That's what Cerkl does."

"If you're already paying Zoom for Workplace, Workvivo gets bundled in cheap — and you should take it for the social layer. We're the delivery and measurement layer that sits on top, regardless of what intranet you land on."

05Gotchas

Pricing floor
Business plan starts at ~$20,000/year for 250–2,000 employees (eLearning Industry); per-user equivalents cited as $5–6/user/month. Enterprise (2,000+) is quote-driven. Not as public as Workvivo claims — final price varies materially by user count and configuration.
Zoom dependency is now structural, not symbolic
Workvivo AI is Zoom AI Companion rebadged; the Zoom-client integration is a featured channel; Workvivo is sold/bundled in Zoom enterprise deals. Upside: scale and AI investment ride Zoom's roadmap. Downside: orgs not on Zoom Workplace (or de-emphasizing Zoom for Teams/Google Meet) get a thinner product story. Procurement may slow if Workvivo gets pulled into a multi-product Zoom MSA negotiation.
Search and governance gaps in large orgs
Reviewers repeatedly flag "the search function lacks robustness, making it hard to find specific content in large organizations" (review summary) and "limitations in scalability and customization... for very large enterprises or those with complex communication demands" (Research.com). Workvivo addresses governance via Theta Lake's compliance integration — but that's a partnership, not a native capability. Finance/healthcare/regulated buyers should sniff this.
Notification overload is the #1 recurring UX complaint
"Notifications cannot be personalized and users get pop ups for so many irrelevant posts" — the consumer-social UX is the brand, but it's also the operational risk for "did the safety bulletin actually reach the line?" use cases.
The "AI-native" claim is partly retrofit
Workvivo has been a social engagement platform since 2017. Workvivo AI launched April 2025; Seer launched May 2026. Real products, real shipping cadence — but the foundation is engagement, and the AI sits on top, not underneath. Same pattern as Staffbase. Buyers should ask whether they're getting AI-native architecture or AI-bolted-on UX.
Workplace-from-Meta migration is a one-time tailwind
Workplace shut at end of August 2025; Workvivo was the named migration partner (blog). Many 2025–2026 customer wins are migrations, not net-new wins against incumbents. That tailwind has now passed.

08Open questions

How much of Workvivo's 2025–2026 growth was Workplace-from-Meta migration vs. net-new?

The Meta partnership was named May 2024; Workplace shut down end of August 2025. If most of the customer logo growth is migration, the tailwind is now spent and 2026–2027 numbers will tell a different story. Worth checking Zoom's earnings calls for disclosure.

Does Workvivo AI / Seer actually read non-Workvivo content?

Like Staffbase's Navigator, the value proposition collapses if AI search is Workvivo-content-only. If Seer can ingest Workday, ServiceNow, HRIS, and other non-Workvivo signals into its "people intelligence," that's a meaningfully bigger product. If it's Workvivo data only, the moat is narrower.

What's the actual attach rate of Workvivo into Zoom Workplace deals?

Zoom's enterprise GTM has been pushing the bundled story for two years. Are renewals attaching Workvivo at 5%, 20%, 50% of accounts? This is the single most decision-relevant number for sizing the threat, and it's not public.
