# IC Deep Dive — Seer by Workvivo

**Date:** 2026-05-11   |   **Category:** Competitor (Vendor product launch — adjacent category)

---

## TL;DR
Seer is a standalone people-intelligence product launched by Workvivo on 2026-05-06, built in-house and led by Justin Black — the former head of Microsoft/LinkedIn's Glint. It competes in the **employee-listening / people-intelligence** category (Glint, Culture Amp, Peakon, Qualtrics), **not** in Cerkl's IC-delivery lane. The most consequential implication for Cerkl is *indirect*: Seer strengthens Workvivo's "full EX suite" framing in deals where Cerkl is already the delivery+measurement challenger to Workvivo-the-platform — but it does not warrant its own "vs." page, and Cerkl's positioning toolkit does not need to change.

## What it is
Seer is Workvivo's new people-intelligence offering, launched 2026-05-06 via a dedicated product page at [workvivo.com/seer/](https://www.workvivo.com/seer/) and a press release on Workvivo's newsroom ([Workvivo newsroom](https://www.workvivo.com/newsroom/workvivo-launches-seer/), [GlobeNewswire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/05/06/3288671/0/en/Workvivo-Launches-Seer-to-Advance-People-Intelligence-and-Close-the-Employee-Listening-Gap.html)). It is "available as a standalone solution or integrated into the broader Workvivo platform" ([workvivo.com/seer](https://www.workvivo.com/seer/)) — meaning Workvivo is selling Seer outside its own install base, not just to existing customers. Workvivo is owned by Zoom (acquired April 2023). Seer is **built in-house, not acquired**, and is staffed by an ex-Glint/Qualtrics leadership team: **Justin Black** (Head of Seer; former head of Glint at Microsoft/LinkedIn, founder of Glint's People Science team), **Phil Murphy** (Head of Growth, ex-Qualtrics), and **Jaime Gonzales** (Principal People Scientist, ex-Microsoft/LinkedIn/Glint) ([Workvivo newsroom](https://www.workvivo.com/newsroom/workvivo-launches-seer/)).

## What it does / claims
Seer "combines feedback with real-time signals from communication, collaboration, and engagement" ([press release](https://www.workvivo.com/newsroom/workvivo-launches-seer/)). Concretely, the buyer gets:
- **Listening surface** — customizable engagement surveys, pulse surveys, polls/quizzes spanning HQ and frontline ([seer page](https://www.workvivo.com/seer/)).
- **AI analysis** — automated theme detection and risk surfacing across thousands of open-ended comments.
- **Segmentation** — by region, team, tenure.
- **Auto-generated executive reports** for the people-strategy audience.
- **AI-powered manager dashboards** — personalized, mobile-first, with guided next steps for line managers ([press release](https://www.workvivo.com/newsroom/workvivo-launches-seer/)).
- **In-product action tools** — post updates, launch initiatives, create spaces directly from Seer (the differentiator vs. Glint/Culture Amp/Peakon, which require leaving the listening tool to act).
- **Progress tracking** on implemented changes — closing the feedback-to-outcome loop.

The positioning is a deliberate category reframe — verbatim:
- *"The industry doesn't have a listening problem, it has an execution problem."* — Justin Black
- *"Measurement alone does not create progress… When feedback does not lead to visible outcomes, it breaks trust."* — Justin Black
- *"The next phase of this category is about impact and accountability, not just insight."* — Justin Black
- *"People intelligence is the next evolution of this category… With Seer, we're focused on helping leaders turn insight into impact at scale."* — John Goulding, Workvivo Co-Founder/CEO
- *"Feedback without action isn't listening. It's just noise."* — workvivo.com/seer

Launch survey data (4,736 frontline + desk workers via TrendCandy, surfaced in the press release): 62% comfortable giving feedback, 49% see meaningful change, 42% believe leaders are held accountable. The "execution gap" is the launch narrative's empirical anchor.

## Who it's for
- **Primary user / ICP:** Mid-market and enterprise HR / people / EX leaders evaluating an employee-listening platform — **not** internal-comms managers. Manager-level use case is the secondary persona (manager-action dashboards).
- **Strong fit when:** Buyer already runs Workvivo and/or Zoom Workplace; buyer wants action-in-product rather than survey-and-export; buyer is dissatisfied with Glint cycle-based listening; buyer values an ex-Glint pedigree in the vendor team.
- **Weak fit when:** Buyer's HRIS gravity is Workday (Peakon's stronger fit); buyer needs heavyweight statistical/predictive modeling (Qualtrics's stronger fit); buyer wants engagement + performance + development in one suite (Culture Amp's stronger fit); buyer is **purely** evaluating IC delivery tooling (this is Cerkl's lane, not Seer's).

## How it slots into Cerkl's competitive set / positioning
**Seer is not a direct Cerkl competitor.** It plays in employee-listening / people-intelligence — a different category from Cerkl's IC-delivery-and-measurement lane. None of Seer's named alternatives (Glint, Culture Amp, Peakon, Qualtrics) appear in [`shared/competitors.md`](../../../../shared/competitors.md), which confirms the category separation.

The Cerkl-relevant implications are all *indirect*:

1. **Workvivo's "EX suite" framing strengthens marginally.** In deals where Cerkl already competes against Workvivo-the-platform, Workvivo can now point to a richer surface area — engagement feed + Workvivo AI + Seer people-intelligence — and pitch "one vendor for the full EX stack." Cerkl's reframe ("Workvivo is the destination; we are the delivery + measurement layer across the channels you already have") remains valid and unchanged. The reframe is in [`shared/competitors.md` vs. Workvivo](../../../../shared/competitors.md) — no edit needed.

2. **Watch the Zoom bundle.** If Zoom packages Seer free or discounted into Zoom Workplace renewals (mirroring the Workvivo AI bundling pattern), this becomes a procurement-channel issue, not a product issue. Cerkl loses no head-to-head on capability but risks losing renewal mindshare in shared accounts. The escalation criterion from the existing Workvivo deep-dive ("escalate only if Zoom packages Workvivo free") now extends to Seer.

3. **Pulse Surveys & Acknowledgments adjacency.** Cerkl's Pulse Surveys feature ([`features/pulse-surveys-acknowledgments.md`](../../../../shared/features/pulse-surveys-acknowledgments.md)) is the closest functional overlap — Cerkl already has employee listening at small scale, embedded inside the comms workflow. This is not a positioning weakness; it's an asset for the "listening-attached-to-delivery" story. If marketing wants a counter-narrative to the people-intelligence trend, the angle is: *"Listening only works if your message gets read first. We do delivery + measurement; lightweight listening is built-in. If you need heavyweight people-intelligence, layer Seer/Glint/Culture Amp on top — but get delivery right first."*

4. **No new "vs." page required.** The existing Workvivo "vs." page refresh (already queued in `competitors.md`) should mention Seer in passing — one line — without standing up a separate vs.-Seer asset. Seer is not a Cerkl ICP threat.

## Gotchas
- **"Standalone" but Zoom/Workvivo-flavored.** Workvivo positions Seer as standalone, but the differentiation story ("real-time signals from communication, collaboration, and engagement") leans heavily on passive telemetry from Workvivo and Zoom Workplace. For a buyer without either, the in-product action layer and the manager-dashboard guidance lose some of their differentiation against Glint/Culture Amp.
- **No public pricing.** Sales-led with custom quotes — buyers should expect Workvivo's procurement profile (~$20k/year floor as of 2026 per the existing [Workvivo deep-dive](workvivo-2026-05-10.md)). No published Seer-specific SKU or floor.
- **Integration surface not disclosed publicly.** The press release names "communication, collaboration, and engagement" signals but does not name Workday, ServiceNow, HRIS, Slack, MS Teams, or SSO providers. Buyers should pressure-test integration claims directly.
- **Reception is still mostly press release re-shares.** As of 2026-05-11 (T+5 days), no Reddit discussion, no Josh Bersin / RedThread / Gartner / Forrester commentary, no LinkedIn viral thread, no comparison content. Only UC Today produced editorial pickup. Treat the "Seer is taking the category" framing as Workvivo's narrative, not yet a market consensus.
- **Workvivo is keeping "Seer by Workvivo" branding.** This is a signal Workvivo wants halo from the parent brand more than a clean break — useful tell that this is positioned as a *category claim* (people intelligence) within a single vendor's portfolio, not a true product-line independence move.

## Open questions
1. **Integration surface.** Does Seer actually ingest Workday / HRIS / Slack / MS Teams / SSO directory signals, or is the "communication, collaboration, and engagement" framing referring only to Workvivo + Zoom telemetry? Critical for evaluating the moat.
2. **Pricing model.** Is Seer priced at Workvivo's ~$20k/year floor as a bundled add-on, or sold separately at a different floor? Does the "available standalone" claim come with a real standalone price list, or is it always quote-driven?
3. **Zoom Workplace bundling.** Has Zoom decided whether Seer rides the same bundling path Workvivo AI took (rebadged Zoom AI Companion inside Workplace renewals)?
4. **Migration playbook.** Is Workvivo actively sourcing Glint customers post-Microsoft-acquisition? Justin Black's hire and the "execution problem" framing read like a deliberate Glint-displacement play, but no migration program is publicly announced.
5. **Roadmap signaling.** Is Seer's manager-action surface (post updates, launch initiatives, create spaces) Workvivo-only today, or will it expand to Microsoft Teams / Slack / Outlook over time? Expansion would meaningfully widen the comp set.
