# IC Deep Dive — Ghassan Karian: "Middle managers are becoming the most burned-out role in the western economy"

**Date:** 2026-06-04   |   **Category:** Analyst report / Trend POV

---

## TL;DR

Ghassan Karian (founder, now non-Exec Chairman of Ipsos Karian and Box) argues that middle management has become "structurally impossible" — a role-design failure, not a resilience problem — and that the fix is redesign, not wellness theatre. Independently corroborated by Gallup, Gartner, McKinsey, HBR, and BCG data published in the last 60 days, but the load-bearing "average manager has 12.1 direct reports (+50% since 2013)" stat circulating with the piece is from Gallup, not Karian — and is distributionally misleading (median is still 5–6). For Cerkl, the headline finding (manager-cascade is structurally broken) hardens the *direct-to-employee delivery* argument, but the route-around lane is getting crowded — Firstup, Workvivo, Staffbase, and ContactMonkey are all positioning here. The cleaner buyer-language hook is the Ragan ECCC 2026 gap: **56% of communicators rank manager comms as a top-2026 focus; only 4% rate managers "very effective" at cascading.**

## What it does / claims

The article opens with: *"If you were asked to guess the most exhausted population in the modern workforce, you might aim high: CEOs, CFOs, founders, tech engineers. You'd be wrong."* The argument is five root causes, then a prescription:

**Five root causes of structural burnout (Karian's frame):**
1. **Responsibility without authority** — middle managers own outcomes without the decision rights to deliver them.
2. **Hybrid-work coordination tax** — distributed teams multiply scheduling, alignment, and rework load.
3. **Emotional absorption** — middle managers buffer downward anxiety from frontline and upward pressure from execs.
4. **Administrative bloat** — reporting, compliance, status, and tooling overhead eat manager hours.
5. **Outdated role design** — the role was built for a different operating model and hasn't been redesigned.

**Headline evidence (drawn from in-house data, no external URLs in article):**
- Work-life-balance scores for middle managers **deteriorated 2023–2025**; in 2025 executives' scores improved while middle managers stayed "comparatively squeezed." *Source: Ipsos Karian and Box employee-experience benchmark.*
- Middle managers score significantly lower than other levels on **manageable workload, decision influence, and wellbeing**.

**Prescription:** Reduce span of control, strip admin load, grant real decision rights, deploy AI for reporting/scheduling, fewer + clearer priorities from the top, practical support tools over resilience training.

**⚠️ Provenance flag — important.** The often-repeated "average manager has 12.1 direct reports (+50% since 2013); spends less than half their time managing" stat **is not in this article**. It originates from Gallup's span-of-control research: [Gallup, "Span of Control: What's the Optimal Team Size for Managers?"](https://www.gallup.com/workplace/700718/span-control-optimal-team-size-managers.aspx) — 8.2 (2013) → 10.9 (2024) → 12.1 (2025). The two are commonly conflated. Pair them deliberately, but cite Gallup for the stat.

## How it slots into Cerkl's competitive set / positioning

**The structural finding (manager-cascade is broken) is a tailwind for Cerkl's positioning** — direct-to-employee personalized delivery is increasingly viable as the channel-of-record, with manager cascade as a *contextualizing* layer rather than the *delivery* layer. But three nuances matter:

1. **The route-around lane is getting crowded.** Per Stage 2c fetch:
   - **Firstup** — strongest route-around stance. *"Manager-as-communicator model was built for a different era."* Backed by 6,200-employee research showing managers overextended, untrained, miss messages ([source](https://firstup.io/blog/internal-communications-failure-2026-research/)).
   - **Workvivo** — cites 84% reliance on managers / 60% rated below expectations; positions exec-direct video + targeted messaging as the fix ([source](https://www.workvivo.com/internal-communications/benchmarks/)).
   - **Staffbase** — hybrid framing; corporate message goes direct, manager contextualizes ([source](https://staffbase.com/blog/ai-tools-for-internal-comms)).
   - **ContactMonkey / Workshop** — route-around by product design (bypass manager relay via direct send).
   - **Simpplr** — counterpoint. The *only* major vendor explicitly positioning to **enable managers** as transformation leaders ([source](https://www.simpplr.com/blog/manager-enablement-ai-initiatives/)).

2. **The buyer-language hook is the Ragan ECCC 2026 gap, not the 12.1 stat.** 56% of communicators rank manager comms as their top 2026 focus, but **only 4% rate managers "very effective"** at cascading ([Ragan ECCC 2026 manager-comms coverage](https://www.ragan.com/ragan-insider-eccc-2026-manager-comms/)). That gap names the pain in IC-team language. The 12.1 mean is a credibility-risk if the buyer's lived experience is 6.

3. **Cerkl can hold both sides.** *"Enable managers with delivery measurement (so they're not the bottleneck), and route around them where they are."* That's a differentiated stance vs. the route-around-only chorus and the enable-only Simpplr counterpoint. The Cerkl pitch becomes: *we are the only platform that lets you do both — the same delivery infrastructure with the same measurement layer, channel-agnostic to whether the message goes via cascade, direct, or both.*

## Gotchas

- **The 12.1 stat ≠ Karian's article.** Conflating them creates a provenance problem if cited in published writing. Pair Karian (role-design argument) with Gallup (span-of-control data) deliberately.
- **Median vs. mean.** Gallup's 12.1 *mean* sits on a long tail (13% of managers oversee 25+). The median is still 5–6 — the buyer's lived experience for most ICs and team leads. "Average manager has 12.1 reports" sounds compelling in a deck but invites pushback in a room of practitioners who oversee 6.
- **isitworking is personal, not corporate.** Karian publishes in personal capacity — useful for "credible voice from the IC research industry" framing, *not* citable as official Ipsos Karian and Box research. The corporate research lives at [ipsoskarianandbox.com/insights](https://ipsoskarianandbox.com/insights/) and the IoIC IC Index ([ic-index.html](https://www.ioic.org.uk/knowledge-hub/thought-leadership-research/ic-index.html)).
- **UK-centric.** Karian and Box's panel is largely UK; IC Index 2026 surveyed 5,000 UK employees. US/EU buyer rooms will ask for US data — pair with Gallup/Gartner for the US side.
