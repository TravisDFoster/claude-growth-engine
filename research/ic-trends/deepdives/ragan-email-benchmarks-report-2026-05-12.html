Ragan "2026 Internal Email Benchmarks Report"

Date: 2026-05-12 Category: Analyst report (vendor-funded, co-branded) Worth profiling — Maybe

The "Ragan 2026 Internal Email Benchmarks Report" is not Ragan research — it's PoliteMail's annual vendor-data study, co-branded with Ragan and distributed via Ragan/PR Daily as a gated white paper. Headline numbers (66% open, 7% click, 14 emails/mo, 33-min monthly read time, n=~2B emails / ~11M employees / 10 S&P sectors) are useful buyer-side anchors, but the source is the IC email measurement vendor whose moat depends on those numbers staying interesting. Two competing 2026 benchmarks dropped within 24 hours (PoliteMail/Ragan 2026-05-07, ContactMonkey 2026-05-08) — vendors are racing to own the 2026 benchmark category, and no neutral independent IC email benchmark exists in the space yet.

01What it is

The report

An annual internal-email benchmark report, conducted by PoliteMail and distributed through Ragan Communications / PR Daily as a gated white paper. PoliteMail is an Outlook plug-in product for IC email tracking — their dataset IS the benchmark.

Coverage: ~2 billion corporate internal emails sent to nearly 11 million employees across 10 S&P industry sectors (technology, healthcare, consumer, financials, industrials, materials, communications, energy, education/nonprofit, utilities), with year-over-year comparisons to 2024 and 2023.

PR Daily summary post is bylined by Michael DesRochers (PoliteMail), labeled "presented by PoliteMail," and ran on 2026-05-04 — three days before the gated Ragan landing went live.

Methodology caveat (acknowledged by PoliteMail's own content)
Open rates are inflated by Outlook preview-pane "opens," automated security scans, and stale distribution lists (PoliteMail on metrics). Buried in marketing copy but it matters: the 66% open-rate headline is the most-cited PoliteMail number and the least defensible one in technical terms.

02Who it's for

Primary user

Internal-comms managers and directors building 2026 plans, slide decks, and CFO conversations. Particularly useful for PoliteMail buyers — the report is structured to convert the reader to a PoliteMail demo.

Strong fit when

You need a defensible-sounding 2026 anchor stat for a slide ("benchmark IC open rate is 66%") and your audience won't fact-check the methodology.

Weak fit when

You need rigorous cross-channel measurement, multi-vendor data, or methodology you can defend in a technical procurement review. Also weak for SMB / Foundations buyers (PoliteMail is enterprise-Outlook-shop oriented).

Both vendor benchmarks have the same flaw — single-vendor data, single-channel scope, vendor-funded. Cerkl's ~6M employees/month dataset is roughly half PoliteMail's volume — credible, but the asymmetric move is publishing a cross-channel benchmark (Cerkl sees email + Teams + Slack + intranet + SMS unified, which neither PoliteMail nor ContactMonkey can).

"Cross-channel benchmark" is a defensible content position that doesn't require beating PoliteMail at email-volume.

3. The 66% open-rate methodology issue is a positioning gift

Cerkl's "we measure delivery + read-through across channels, not just opens" message has a sharper edge when the benchmark anchor itself acknowledges (in PoliteMail's other content) that open rates are inflated by preview-pane behavior.

Direct ammunition for cerkl.com/broadcast measurement-page copy and any AE conversation that comes up against a PoliteMail benchmark slide.

05Gotchas

The Ragan name is editorial cover
The data and authorship are PoliteMail's. If you cite "the Ragan 2026 benchmark" in a deck, you're citing PoliteMail telemetry. Practitioners and analysts who know the IC space already know this; sales prospects often don't.
66% open rate is the most-cited and least-defensible number
PoliteMail itself acknowledges Outlook preview-pane and security-scan inflation in their own content. Use this when the number shows up in a buyer conversation.
Full report is email-gated
Gated behind a Ragan form (name, email, employee count, company type, job function, ToS accept). The PR Daily summary post is ungated and contains the headline numbers — start there for any internal use.
"Independent" is a stretch
Ragan/PR Daily editorial framing presents this as trade-publication research; the byline ("presented by PoliteMail," authored by PoliteMail's CEO) gives it away on close read.
YoY data is single-vendor longitudinal
The 2024 and 2023 comparisons are PoliteMail-only longitudinal data. Internally consistent, but not industry-wide trend lines.
