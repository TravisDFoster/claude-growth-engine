## How Broadcast Differentiates

Cerkl's positioning is not "we are the company intranet." It is: "We are the personalized delivery and measurement layer across the channels you already have."

- Less emphasis on replacing existing digital workplace tools
- More emphasis on audience sync, personalization, native inbox delivery, cross-channel measurement, low IT friction, and faster launch
- Free Foundations plan is a meaningful commercial differentiator vs. heavier enterprise suites

---

## What Customers Switch From

Cerkl more often replaces patched-together internal comms stacks than named enterprise suites:
- Outlook/Gmail newsletter workflows
- PDFs and spreadsheets / distribution lists
- Homegrown tools
- Disconnected email-plus-intranet processes

Recurring switch reasons: better design, better scheduling, dynamic targeting, personalization, cross-channel reach, measurable analytics. The dominant pattern is "escape the manual stack," not displacing an enterprise competitor.

## Head-to-Head Positioning

### vs. Staffbase
Staffbase is a fuller intranet platform — if a buyer wants to replace their entire digital workplace, Staffbase competes there. Cerkl's advantage: faster to launch, less IT lift, no channel replacement required, self-serve free entry point. Cerkl loses when the buyer's primary need is a home-base intranet, not delivery and measurement.

**Cerkl reframe (use this in deals where Staffbase is the alternative):** *"We're the delivery + measurement layer. The intranet is the destination, not the comms strategy."* Lean into speed-to-launch and IT-light implementation against Staffbase's ~$30k floor and multi-quarter rollouts. The 2026 Staffbase narrative is upstream — Navigator (permission-aware AI search), AI quality layer, intranet-as-content-source — which makes their pitch governance-heavy and longer to procurement; Cerkl's pitch lands inside the IC team's existing channels with measurable delivery from day one. (Reframe sourced from the 2026-05-10 deep-dive: [`Competitors/staffbase-2026-05-10.md`](../research/ic-trends/deepdives/Competitors/staffbase-2026-05-10.md).)

### vs. Simpplr
Simpplr crossed from "intranet competitor" into direct orchestration in **February 2026** with **Comms AI** — an AI workspace for IC covering campaign planning, multichannel delivery, and approvals. Stack that on top of Gartner MQ Leader (3rd year), Forrester Wave Q2 2026 Leader, and 4.6/5 G2 average, and Simpplr is the most material 2026 competitive shift in the space. **Cerkl reframe:** *"Comms AI is a comms workspace built on top of Simpplr's intranet. We're the delivery + measurement layer across the channels you already have — including any intranet you've already invested in."* Lean on free Foundations + self-serve onboarding against Simpplr's custom enterprise quote and IT-led implementation. Refresh `cerkl.com/broadcast/versus/simpplr` to address Comms AI specifically. (Sourced from [`Competitors/simpplr-2026-05-10.md`](../research/ic-trends/deepdives/Competitors/simpplr-2026-05-10.md).)

### vs. Firstup
Firstup is the competitor whose **frame** is structurally closest to Cerkl's — same "personalized delivery across the channels you already have" thesis, same skepticism of intranet-as-comms-strategy. The substantive difference is *execution*: Firstup is sales-led, custom-quote, IT-heavy, multi-quarter rollouts; Cerkl is self-serve with free Foundations and fast launch. **Cerkl reframe:** *"Free Foundations vs. custom enterprise quote. Launch in a sprint, not in quarters. Personalization at the employee level (MyNews + dynamic segments) vs. segment + journey orchestration. Delivery intelligence — right message, right person, right channel, right time — is Cerkl's lane. Task automation / agentic HR actions (PTO requests, audience builders) is Firstup's new lane. Different jobs."* Firstup's **March 2026 agentic AI suite** (Search, Content Creator, Insights, Actions, Audience Builder) plus new CEO/CRO/CMO/EMEA-MD lineup signals more aggressive 2026 outbound — refresh `cerkl.com/broadcast/versus/firstup` to address the agentic pillars within 60 days. (Sourced from [`Competitors/firstup-2026-05-10.md`](../research/ic-trends/deepdives/Competitors/firstup-2026-05-10.md).)

### vs. Workvivo
Workvivo is now Zoom's distribution arm for EX — bundled into Zoom Workplace renewals, Workvivo AI is Zoom AI Companion rebadged, **Seer** (people-intelligence) launched 2026-05-06, Forrester Wave Leader Q2 2026. Pricing floor ~$20k/year now matches Staffbase's procurement profile — Workvivo is no longer the cheaper, scrappier alternative. **Cerkl reframe:** *"Workvivo is the destination — we make sure your message gets read on the channels your employees already use, including Workvivo if you have it. A feed is great for shout-outs. It's not great for the CEO town hall summary, the open-enrollment deadline, or the safety bulletin. That's what Cerkl does."* Treat Workvivo as a channel, not a replacement, in deals where it's already in the stack. Refresh `cerkl.com/broadcast/versus/workvivo` with the Zoom-bundle + Seer + Workvivo AI narrative. Escalate only if Zoom packages Workvivo free into Workplace renewals. (Sourced from [`Competitors/workvivo-2026-05-10.md`](../research/ic-trends/deepdives/Competitors/workvivo-2026-05-10.md).)

### vs. Haiilo
Haiilo is the COYO + Smarp + Jubiwee merger (2022) — strong on employee advocacy / external social sharing (the Smarp lineage), documented weak on audience segmentation, personalization, and governance (per LumApps' and Sociabble's own comparison pages, plus G2/Capterra reviews). EU-anchored ICP. **Cerkl reframe:** *"Haiilo is three products in a trench coat — intranet, advocacy, and surveys merged in 2022. We're one product built for one job: getting your internal message read, on the channels your employees already use. Their flagship ROI case study is Salesforce's marketing-team employee advocacy program — if your KPI is internal read-through, not external social reach, you're being sold a different tool. 500-seat minimum, custom quote, sales-led — Cerkl Foundations is free and live in an afternoon."* No live "vs. Haiilo" SEO page exists — worth commissioning. (Sourced from [`Competitors/haiilo-2026-05-10.md`](../research/ic-trends/deepdives/Competitors/haiilo-2026-05-10.md).)

### vs. Axios HQ
Axios HQ is an **AI editor priced like a platform** (~$15k/year floor). The overlap with Cerkl is sharpest at **Foundations buyers** — the same writers, office managers, chiefs of staff, and operations leads that Smart Brevity courts. Mixing Board acquisition + 2026 State of Internal Communications report (with Ragan) signal investment in community/training, not in delivery or measurement infrastructure. **Cerkl reframe:** *"Axios HQ is an AI editor priced like a platform. Cerkl is a platform priced like a free tool. Smart Brevity is a style — we don't dictate the style, we make sure whatever you write gets read by the right person. Their Slack/Teams support is a post-send link-forward; ours is native cross-channel publishing with read attribution."* No live "vs. Axios HQ" SEO page exists — **cheapest competitive SEO win on the list**, framed for SMB/Foundations buyers (not enterprise). (Sourced from [`Competitors/axios-hq-2026-05-10.md`](../research/ic-trends/deepdives/Competitors/axios-hq-2026-05-10.md).)

### vs. ContactMonkey
More direct internal-comms comparison. ContactMonkey is sales-led with custom pricing. Cerkl wins on: free forever entry, no procurement needed to start, self-serve workflow. Cerkl may lose to ContactMonkey if the buyer is already deep in a ContactMonkey sales cycle.

### vs. Workshop
Workshop is email-first for SMB. Cerkl competes directly here with Foundations. Cerkl's advantage: free forever (Workshop is not), plus a clearer upgrade path to omni-channel if the org grows.
