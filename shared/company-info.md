## Ideal Customer Profile

Two distinct ICPs depending on the motion.

**Foundations / subscriber growth ICP:** Any team that owns internal employee communication and needs something better than Outlook or Gmail. Defined by need state, not headcount — includes SMBs and organizations of all sizes. Buyer is whoever owns employee communication at that org: a formal internal comms director down to an office manager or founder.

**Paid ICP (Foundations+, Omni AI):** Mid-market to enterprise organizations with messy internal comms environments — distributed, frontline, deskless, or global workforces; multiple channels to coordinate; need for segmentation, analytics, and IT-friendly data sync. Paid customers range from 2,000–12,200+ employees.

### Expected Upgrade Path by Company Size

| Company Size | Expected Behavior |
|---|---|
| 0–500 employees | Stay on Foundations. Low send volume, simple needs. Subscriber growth is the goal. |
| 501–2,000 employees | Likely to upgrade to Foundations+ (unlimited send volume). |
| 2,001–5,000 (complex comms stack) | Likely to upgrade to Omni AI. Trigger: multiple channels, diverse audiences, deskless/frontline. |
| 5,000+ employees | Likely to upgrade to Omni AI regardless of stack complexity. |

Most visitors create a free account due to low friction. Conversion to paid is Product's responsibility, not Marketing's.

## Positioning & Differentiation

Cerkl's clearest differentiation: it sits on top of the channels companies already use instead of forcing a channel replacement.

**Core product story:** Sync employee data from HRIS/IdP → build dynamic segments → publish once across email/Teams/Slack/SharePoint/mobile → personalize with MyNews/Omni AI → measure engagement in one place.

- Low IT lift and quick rollout
- Keeps email native to Outlook/Gmail
- Client-driven roadmap

**Paid buyers:** Internal Communications Managers/Directors, Senior Directors of Communications, SVPs of Internal Communications, HR/People Experience leaders. IT is often a co-buyer.

**Foundations buyers (includes SMB):** Whoever owns employee communication — a formal internal comms or HR leader at larger companies; an office manager, chief of staff, operations lead, or founder/CEO at smaller ones.

## Competitive Landscape

### Main Competitors

**Enterprise internal comms:** Staffbase, Simpplr, LumApps, Firstup, Poppulo, Workvivo, Haiilo, AxiosHQ

**Email-first / SMB:** Workshop, ContactMonkey

### How Broadcast Differentiates

Strongest positioning: "We are the personalized delivery and measurement layer across the channels you already have" — not "we are the company intranet."

- Less emphasis on replacing existing digital workplace tools
- More emphasis on audience sync, personalization, native inbox delivery, cross-channel measurement, low IT friction, faster launch
- Free Foundations plan is a meaningful commercial differentiator vs. heavier enterprise suites

### What Customers Switch From

Cerkl more often replaces patched-together stacks than named enterprise suites:
- Outlook/Gmail newsletter workflows
- PDFs, spreadsheets, and distribution lists
- Homegrown tools
- Disconnected email-plus-intranet processes

Dominant pattern: "escape the manual stack," not displacing an enterprise competitor. Recurring switch reasons: better design, better scheduling, dynamic targeting, personalization, cross-channel reach, measurable analytics.

### Where Competitors Win

More vulnerable when a buyer wants a broader intranet/home-base platform or stronger suite breadth. Staffbase is preferred when buyers prioritize roadmap fit. Intranet-centric or digital-signage-heavy use cases can favor LumApps or Poppulo. Known product friction: editor/design limitations, small editing windows.

## Goals & Metrics

**Marketing KPI: Foundations subscriber growth.**

Converting free subscribers to paid is Product's responsibility. Marketing's job is to grow the number of active Foundations users. Secondary experiments (e.g., AI Agents upsell) do not take priority over subscriber growth.

**Customer-facing metrics Cerkl emphasizes:** open rates, click rates, audience reach, personalization, engagement, proof of ROI.

## Pricing Model

- Foundations is free forever — no credit card, no contract, no seat or message pricing
- Paid plans (Foundations+, Omni AI) are audience-based and typically annual
- Not priced per communicator seat or per message on paid tiers

## Strategic Read

Cerkl sells **relevance, reach, and measurement for internal comms** — not a broad employee social network or a general marketing platform. The positioning is sharp. The tradeoff is that larger "digital workplace suite" competitors win when buyers want a fuller home-base/intranet platform rather than a delivery-and-measurement layer.
