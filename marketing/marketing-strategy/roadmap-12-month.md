# Broadcast Foundations — 12-Month Marketing Roadmap

> Built on the diagnosis and guiding policy in `diagnosis-and-guiding-policy.md`. Each phase has a clear objective and a go/no-go gate before the next phase scales.

---

## Objective

Grow Foundations sign-ups from ~4/week to 20+/week within 12 months. Find at least one paid channel operating at ≤$10 CPC with a clear scaling path. Build organic and community distribution that compounds independently of paid spend.

---

## Phase Structure

| Phase | Months | Objective | Gate |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — Find the channel | 1–3 | Identify which channel(s) can reach the Foundations ICP at ≤$10 CPC | At least one paid channel at ≤$10 CPC, or a clear organic channel showing growth |
| 2 — Prove the channel | 4–6 | Generate enough volume to confirm the channel is repeatable and scalable | 10+ sign-ups/week with attributable source |
| 3 — Scale what works | 7–9 | Increase budget and output on proven channels; let organic compound | 15+ sign-ups/week; cost per sign-up trending down |
| 4 — Compound and measure | 10–12 | Full flywheel; assess what to carry into next year | 20+ sign-ups/week sustained; strategy for following year drafted |

---

## Phase 1 — Find the Channel (Months 1–3)

**Objective:** Stop spending on channels that can't hit the CPC target. Test the most viable unproven channel (Meta). Build the organic surface area that will compound in Phase 3+.

### Paid
- **Exit Reddit paid.** Reallocate the full Reddit budget to Meta/Facebook.
- **Launch Meta/Facebook test.** Target HR managers, office managers, operations leads, chiefs of staff at companies with 50–500 employees. Lead with problem-aware messaging, not product features. CTA: free sign-up, no credit card. Budget: full $5,000/month redirected from Reddit. Run for 60–90 days before a go/no-go call.
- **Hold SEM.** Keep Google/Bing focused on competitor comparisons and Omni AI keywords where intent and deal size justify cost. Do not expand SEM to Foundations keywords at current CPCs.

### Organic / SEO
- **Build the Foundations keyword layer.** Audit current blog content for SMB/pre-evaluation intent gaps. Identify 10–15 target queries the current footprint is missing (e.g., "free internal newsletter tool," "internal email software small business," "Mailchimp alternative for employee communications"). Redirect a portion of the existing 1 post/week cadence to these topics.
- **Reframe AI blog content toward SMB pain points.** Posts should lead with the problem the pre-evaluation buyer is experiencing, not internal comms strategy for large organizations.

### Webinars
- **Reframe the program for the Foundations ICP.** Replace enterprise-positioned topics with practical, how-to content for small-team communicators. Example topics: "How to stop using Outlook for your company newsletter," "Building your first internal email workflow in under an hour." One webinar per month to start.

### Partnerships
- **Rework Crescenzo.** Propose a content-first model: a co-hosted webinar or co-branded guide for CC members who are still running internal email manually. Replace the discount offer with a distribution offer — get in front of the audience with something useful, not a coupon.
- **Assess Paycor.** One conversation with the Paycor partnership contact to understand whether a co-marketing or in-product placement is feasible. If there is no viable activation path, formally deprioritize.

### Community
- **Identify and enter 2–3 communities.** Start with r/humanresources, the IC Kollectif network, and one LinkedIn internal comms group. Objective in Phase 1 is presence and contribution — not promotion. Comment on threads, share useful content, establish the brand as a helpful participant.

### Phase 1 Gate
Before moving to Phase 2: Is Meta (or any other new channel) delivering clicks at ≤$10 CPC? Is organic traffic to Foundations-specific content growing? If neither is true at the end of Month 3, reassess channel mix before scaling anything.

---

## Phase 2 — Prove the Channel (Months 4–6)

**Objective:** Confirm that Phase 1's winning channel(s) are repeatable. Generate enough sign-up volume to show a pattern, not just a result.

### Paid
- **Double down on what hit the CPC target.** If Meta is at ≤$10 CPC, increase budget and test a second audience segment or creative variation. If Meta did not hit target, evaluate LinkedIn (mid-market, higher CPC but potentially higher conversion) or programmatic display.
- **Track cost per sign-up, not just CPC.** CPC is the proxy metric. Cost per sign-up is what actually matters. A channel at $8 CPC with 3% sign-up conversion ($267/sign-up) is worse than a channel at $12 CPC with 20% conversion ($60/sign-up).

### Organic / SEO
- **Measure Foundations-specific content performance.** Which posts from Phase 1 are generating impressions, clicks, and sign-ups? Double down on those topics and formats. Cut or deprioritize topics that get traffic but no sign-ups.

### Webinars
- **Measure sign-up attribution from webinars.** Are attendees converting to Foundations accounts? If yes, increase webinar frequency and invest in better promotion. If not, diagnose whether the ICP is wrong or the CTA is weak.

### Community
- **Begin soft promotion in communities.** After establishing presence in Phase 1, start sharing Foundations content and resources in relevant threads. Track referral traffic and sign-ups from community sources.

### Partnerships
- **Launch the reworked Crescenzo activation** (co-hosted webinar or guide) and measure sign-ups attributable to CC distribution. Set a clear 60-day window to evaluate.

### Phase 2 Gate
Before moving to Phase 3: Are sign-ups at 10+/week? Is there at least one channel with clear attribution and a cost per sign-up below $100? (At $10 CPC and 10% conversion rate, that's $100/sign-up — acceptable for a free-forever product with LTV from future paid conversion.)

---

## Phase 3 — Scale What Works (Months 7–9)

**Objective:** Increase budget and output on channels that have proven themselves. Let organic and community distribution begin to compound with lower marginal effort.

### Paid
- **Scale proven channel budget.** If a channel is at ≤$10 CPC and generating consistent sign-ups, increase monthly spend. Test budget ceiling — at what spend level does CPC start rising? That's the practical cap for the current targeting approach.
- **Consider a second paid channel** only if the primary channel is capped and budget headroom exists.

### Organic / SEO
- **Increase Foundations-specific content output** if Phase 1–2 content is generating sign-ups. Compound the keyword surface area. SEO takes 3–6 months to return results — content published in Phase 1 should be producing traffic here.

### Community
- **Deepen in 1–2 communities** rather than spreading to new ones. Authentic community presence compounds through reputation, not volume of posts.

### Webinars
- **Scale frequency if converting.** If webinars are driving sign-ups, move to twice monthly. Build an on-demand library from recordings to extend reach beyond live attendees.

### Phase 3 Gate
Before moving to Phase 4: Are sign-ups at 15+/week? Is the cost per sign-up trend flat or declining?

---

## Phase 4 — Compound and Measure (Months 10–12)

**Objective:** Sustain 20+/week. Evaluate what's compounding vs. what still requires ongoing investment. Plan next year's strategy based on real performance data, not hypotheses.

### Paid
- **Hold spend on proven channels.** No new channel experiments in Phase 4 — this is a compounding and measurement phase, not a discovery phase.

### Organic
- **Measure SEO compounding.** Content published in Phases 1–2 should be generating organic traffic without additional effort. Quantify this as a baseline for next year's SEO investment case.

### Community
- **Assess referral contribution.** What percentage of sign-ups are coming from community channels? Is this worth formalizing (dedicated community manager time, sponsored community participation)?

### Partnerships
- **Close or commit.** Any partnership that has not generated measurable sign-ups by Month 10 should be formally deprioritized or restructured. Don't carry dead weight into next year.

### End-of-Year Output
- Channel performance report: CPC, cost per sign-up, volume by channel
- Which channels compound (organic, community) vs. require ongoing spend (paid)
- Budget recommendation for following year based on proven channel ROI
- Draft of next year's strategy, built on real data

---

## Budget Allocation by Phase

| Phase | Paid Monthly Budget | Direction |
|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 | $5,000 | Full Reddit budget → Meta test |
| Phase 2 | $5,000–$7,500 | Scale proven channel; hold or cut underperformers |
| Phase 3 | $7,500–$10,000 | Scale further if CPC holds; add second channel if budget allows |
| Phase 4 | TBD by Phase 3 results | Hold proven channels; no new experiments |

Budget scaling is contingent on hitting the $10 CPC gate. If a channel doesn't hit the target, the budget does not scale — it reallocates.

---

## What This Roadmap Rules Out

- Running Reddit paid beyond Month 1 at current CPC
- Launching multiple paid channel tests simultaneously
- Adding new initiatives in Phase 4 before measuring what Phase 1–3 built
- Treating webinars or community as supplemental — they have measurable sign-up attribution goals, not just brand awareness goals
