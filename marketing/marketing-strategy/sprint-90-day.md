# Broadcast Foundations — 90-Day Sprint (Phase 1)

> Execution layer for Phase 1 of the 12-month roadmap. Covers Months 1–3. The objective is to find at least one channel that reaches the Foundations ICP at ≤$10 CPC. Everything here is in service of that gate.

---

## Sprint Objective

Stop spending on channels that can't hit target. Launch Meta as the primary new paid experiment. Build the organic and community surface area that will compound regardless of what paid does.

**Success criteria at Day 90:**
- Meta/Facebook paid at ≤$10 CPC, or a documented decision on which paid channel to test next
- 5 Foundations-specific blog posts published and indexed
- 2 webinars delivered with Foundations/SMB positioning
- Active presence in 2–3 practitioner communities
- Crescenzo partnership relaunched or formally closed
- Paycor activation assessed (go or no-go)

---

## Week-by-Week Priorities

### Weeks 1–2 — Stop, Start, Audit

**Paid**
- Pause Reddit paid ads. Redirect the full $5,000/month budget to Meta setup.
- Create Meta Business account and campaign structure if not already in place.
- Define targeting parameters: HR managers, office managers, operations leads, chiefs of staff, HR generalists. Company size: 50–500 employees. US geography.
- Write 2–3 ad creative variations. Each leads with the problem ("still sending company-wide email from Outlook?"), ends with a single CTA: start free, no credit card.

**SEO / Content**
- Audit existing blog posts for Foundations-relevant keywords. Flag any posts already ranking for SMB/pre-evaluation queries that could be updated or expanded.
- Build a keyword target list of 10–15 Foundations-specific queries. Prioritize by search volume and competition. Focus on pre-evaluation intent, not category-aware intent.
- Identify the next 4 blog post topics from that keyword list. These replace 4 of the next 8 AI-generated posts in the publishing queue.

**Community**
- Create or claim brand profiles on r/humanresources and relevant LinkedIn internal comms groups if not already present.
- Spend 30 minutes/week reading and identifying threads where Foundations is genuinely relevant. Do not post yet — just listen and map the conversation.

---

### Weeks 3–4 — Launch

**Paid**
- Launch Meta campaigns. Start with one audience segment and 2–3 creative variations.
- Set up UTM tracking so sign-ups from Meta are attributable.
- Daily CPC check for the first two weeks. Flag immediately if CPC is trending above $15 — adjust audience or creative before it burns budget.

**SEO / Content**
- Publish first two Foundations-specific blog posts.
- Ensure each post has a clear, frictionless CTA to the free sign-up page — not a gated asset, not a demo request.

**Webinars**
- Schedule first Foundations-reframed webinar for Week 6–8.
- Choose a topic that speaks to the pre-evaluation SMB buyer. Example: "How to send a better company newsletter (without Outlook)."
- Write a landing page and promotional copy that reflects the new framing. The audience for this webinar is not an internal comms director at a 3,000-person company.

**Partnerships**
- Send one email to Crescenzo proposing a co-hosted webinar for CC members. Offer to do the work — topic, format, promotion. Frame it as useful content for their audience, not a product pitch.
- Make one contact at Paycor to assess whether in-marketplace placement or co-marketing is feasible. Set a 2-week response deadline. If no response or no viable path, formally deprioritize.

---

### Weeks 5–8 — Run and Observe

**Paid**
- Let Meta run. Do not optimize prematurely. Give campaigns 3–4 weeks of data before making changes.
- Track weekly: CPC, CTR, click-to-sign-up conversion rate, cost per sign-up.
- First optimization checkpoint at Week 6: kill the lowest-performing creative, test one new variation.

**SEO / Content**
- Publish one Foundations-specific post per week (replacing standard cadence).
- Begin tracking impressions and clicks for these posts in Google Search Console. It is too early for ranking results — this is baseline data only.

**Webinars**
- Promote and deliver first Foundations-reframed webinar.
- Measure: registrations, attendance rate, and post-webinar sign-ups (trackable via UTM on the sign-up CTA in the follow-up email).

**Community**
- Begin participating in r/humanresources and LinkedIn internal comms groups. Contribute to relevant threads with helpful answers — no promotion yet. Goal: 2–3 genuine contributions per week across all communities.

---

### Weeks 9–12 — Assess and Adjust

**Paid**
- Full Meta performance review at Week 10. Decision framework:
  - CPC ≤$10 and sign-ups attributable → continue, begin planning budget increase for Phase 2
  - CPC $10–$20 and sign-ups attributable → optimize targeting and creative; one more 4-week run before go/no-go
  - CPC >$20 or no attributable sign-ups → exit Meta, evaluate LinkedIn or programmatic as next test
- Document results regardless of outcome. The data is the asset.

**SEO / Content**
- Publish 5th Foundations-specific post.
- Review early GSC data. Any posts generating impressions for target queries? Double down on those topic clusters.

**Webinars**
- Deliver second webinar.
- If Crescenzo co-hosted webinar was agreed, schedule it for Weeks 10–12.

**Community**
- After 6–8 weeks of contribution, begin light promotion in community. Share a Foundations-relevant post or resource in a thread where it's genuinely useful. Track any referral traffic.

**Partnerships**
- Close out Crescenzo and Paycor assessments. Document the outcome for each and update the strategy accordingly.

## Metrics to Track Weekly

| Metric | Target | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Foundations sign-ups | Trending toward 8–10/week by Day 90 | CRM / sign-up dashboard |
| Meta CPC | ≤$10 | Meta Ads Manager |
| Meta cost per sign-up | ≤$150 | Meta + CRM |
| Blog posts published (Foundations-specific) | 5 by Day 90 | Content calendar |
| Webinars delivered | 2 by Day 90 | Webinar platform |
| Community contributions | 2–3/week | Manual log |

---

## What to Ignore This Sprint

- LinkedIn paid (CPCs likely too high for $10 target; revisit in Phase 2 if Meta disappoints)
- SEM expansion to Foundations keywords (still too expensive; not a Phase 1 experiment)
- New gated assets or lead magnets (adds friction to a frictionless offer)
- Redesigning the website or sign-up flow (activation is Product's problem; focus on acquisition)
- Launching more than one paid channel simultaneously

---

## Day 90 Decision Point

At the end of the sprint, answer these three questions:

1. **Did we find a paid channel at ≤$10 CPC?** If yes, Phase 2 scales it. If no, which channel is next in line to test?
2. **Is organic showing early signs?** Are Foundations-specific blog posts generating impressions? Is community referral traffic measurable?
3. **Are partnerships viable distribution channels?** Has Crescenzo generated any sign-ups from the new model? Does Paycor have a real activation path?

The answers to these questions are the inputs to Phase 2. Do not skip this assessment — it is the mechanism that prevents the strategy from drifting back into a list of activities.
