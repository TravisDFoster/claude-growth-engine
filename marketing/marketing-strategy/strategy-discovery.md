# Marketing Strategy Discovery — Broadcast Foundations

> Working document capturing the Q&A and strategic synthesis from the initial strategy discovery session (April 2026). Use this as context when building or updating the marketing strategy.

---

## Current State of Foundations Growth

- **Weekly sign-ups:** ~4/week
- **Weekly activations:** 1–2/week
- **Activation benchmark:** Strong — product is working on improving time-to-first-value, but needs more users/data to do so
- **Growth goal:** 5x pipeline as fast as possible — target ~20 sign-ups/week
- **Primary KPI:** Foundations subscriber growth. Paid conversion is Product's responsibility, not Marketing's.

## Where Subscribers Are Coming From

- **Primary source:** Organic / no UTMs — likely SEO, even though SEO investment has been significantly reduced
- **Takeaway:** SEO equity built up over time is still driving growth despite defunding. This is the signal — not an accident.

## Targeting and ICP Prioritization

- **Priority:** Maximum volume of Foundations sign-ups. SMBs (0–500 employees) are preferred because there are more of them, but mid-market (501–2,000) is acceptable if they are easier and cheaper to reach.
- **CPC target:** <$10 on paid platforms
- **ICP for Foundations:** Anyone who owns internal employee communication and is still using Outlook/Gmail as a workaround. Defined by need state, not headcount.
- **Key buyer titles at SMB:** Office managers, chiefs of staff, operations leads, HR generalists, founders/CEOs
- **Key buyer titles at mid-market:** Internal comms managers/directors, HR/People Experience leaders

- **Pathway differentiation:** The website (cerkl.com) already differentiates between the free Foundations path and the paid/demo path. Two separate CTAs and journeys exist.
- **Gap:** Current SEO content is optimized for internal comms professionals aware of the category — not for SMB buyers still running email from Outlook. Missing: low-competition, pre-evaluation search intent queries.

## Key Strategic Observations

### 1. SEO is being abandoned at the wrong time
Organic is still the top source despite reduced investment. The real gap isn't investment level — it's keyword targeting. Current SEO footprint covers internal comms content for category-aware buyers. Missing: a Foundations-specific keyword layer targeting pre-evaluation, low-competition SMB queries:
- "free internal newsletter tool"
- "employee newsletter software small business"
- "internal email template for employees"
- "Mailchimp alternative for internal communications"

### 2. No paid channel currently hits the $10 CPC target for the Foundations ICP
- SEM: $35+ CPC on current keywords (targeted at paid buyers, not Foundations)
- Reddit: $50 CPC (5x over target)
- Meta/Facebook: Not yet tested — typically $3–8 CPC for B2B HR/ops at SMB company sizes. Most viable next paid experiment.
- LinkedIn: Likely $15–20+ CPC for precise targeting — viable for mid-market, not for ≤$10 CPC goal.

### 3. Webinars need a full reframe for Foundations
Previous webinar program targeted enterprise/paid buyers. With Foundations now live, the content and ICP need to flip to practical, how-to content for communicators still running internal email from a mailbox. Topic framing: "how to stop using Outlook for company newsletters," "setting up your first employee email workflow."

### 4. Community distribution is the highest-upside underdeveloped channel
The Foundations ICP gathers in practitioner communities where peer recommendations carry more weight than brand content. Near-zero media cost. Compounds over time. Currently approached ad hoc, not systematically. Relevant communities: IC Kollectif, SHRM, internal comms LinkedIn groups, r/humanresources.

### 5. Partnerships are zero-for-two
Neither Crescenzo nor Paycor is generating subscribers. Both need different activation models before they can count as distribution channels.

---

## Trade-offs the Strategy Must Make

| Decision | Option A | Option B |
|---|---|---|
| SEO investment | Keep defunded, experiment elsewhere | Rebuild Foundations-specific keyword layer at moderate investment |
| Primary paid channel | Keep iterating Reddit | Drop Reddit, test Meta/Facebook |
| Webinar positioning | Continue current positioning | Reframe entirely for Foundations / SMB ICP |
| Crescenzo | Continue discount model | Convert to co-hosted content partnership |
| Community | Keep ad hoc | Systematic program with dedicated time allocation |

## Resolved Pre-Strategy Questions

**Paid acquisition budget:** $5,000/month. Scalable if experiments hit target ROI — budget is not a hard ceiling, it's a starting point.

**Team capacity:** Available for community and content work. Currently active on Reddit (organic) and LinkedIn. Publishing 1 AI-generated blog post per week on cerkl.com.

**Budget math at target CPC:**
| CPC | Clicks/month ($5k) | Sign-ups (10% CVR) | Sign-ups/week |
|---|---|---|---|
| $50 (Reddit current) | 100 | 10 | ~2.5 |
| $10 (target) | 500 | 50 | ~12.5 |
| $5 (Meta potential) | 1,000 | 100 | ~25 |

At $10 CPC with a 10% click-to-sign-up rate, $5k/month gets close to the 5x goal (~20/week). At $5 CPC it exceeds it. The CPC target is the right lever to pull.
