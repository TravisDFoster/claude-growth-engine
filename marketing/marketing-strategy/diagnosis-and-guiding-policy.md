# Broadcast Foundations — Marketing Strategy Diagnosis & Guiding Policy

> Foundational strategy document. The roadmap and channel plans sit on top of this. If a decision conflicts with the guiding policy, resolve the conflict here first — don't bend the policy to fit the initiative.

---

## Diagnosis

Foundations subscriber growth is stalled at ~4 sign-ups per week. The obstacle is not a product problem — activation is strong and the free-forever offer is genuinely differentiated. The obstacle is a channel-market fit problem: **the existing marketing infrastructure was built for the paid ICP, and it cannot efficiently reach the Foundations ICP.**

This shows up in three specific ways:

**1. The channels are calibrated for the wrong buyer.**
SEM targets competitor and Omni AI keywords at $35+ CPC — those are enterprise evaluation queries, not SMB discovery queries. Comparison SEO pages, case studies, and most webinar content speak to buyers who are already in a formal evaluation. The Foundations ICP — an office manager or HR generalist still running company-wide email from Outlook — is not in a formal evaluation. They don't know a purpose-built tool exists. None of the current channels intercept them at that stage.

**2. The one channel that does reach the Foundations ICP is being defunded.**
Organic search is still the top source of Foundations sign-ups, despite reduced investment. But the SEO footprint targets internal comms professionals who are already category-aware — not the pre-evaluation SMB buyer searching for "how to send a company newsletter" or "internal email tool for small teams." There is untapped SEO surface area for the right buyer, and it's not being built.

**3. Paid channel experiments have not found a viable replacement.**
Reddit paid is at $50 CPC — five times the $10 target. At that cost, $5,000/month buys ~100 clicks and roughly 10 sign-ups. The budget is being consumed without generating meaningful signal. No other paid channel has been tested for the Foundations ICP specifically.

**The root cause in one sentence:** Every acquisition channel is aimed at a buyer who is already looking for software like Broadcast. The Foundations ICP is not looking yet — and no channel is designed to reach them before they are.

---

## Guiding Policy

**Intercept the pre-evaluation buyer where they already are, before they start a formal search.**

This means building acquisition around the moment the Foundations ICP is experiencing the problem — not the moment they're ready to evaluate solutions. The channels, content, and offers that follow from this policy are different from those that serve the paid pipeline.

### What this policy requires

- **Content speaks to problems, not products.** Blog posts, ads, and community contributions lead with the problem the buyer is living with ("your Outlook newsletter workflow is costing you three hours a week"), not the product category ("internal communications software").

- **Channels reach the SMB buyer, not the enterprise evaluator.** Paid investment goes to platforms where HR managers, office managers, ops leads, and chiefs of staff at 50–500 person companies spend time — not platforms optimized for enterprise B2B software evaluation.

- **The ask is frictionless.** Every acquisition touchpoint ends with one next step: start a free account, no credit card. Not "request a demo," not "talk to sales," not "download a guide." The Foundations offer has no friction at entry — the acquisition motion should reflect that.

- **Organic compounds, paid validates.** Organic channels (SEO, community, social) are built for long-term compounding. Paid channels are used to test whether a specific audience and message combination works — then scaled if they hit the $10 CPC target, cut if they don't. No paid channel runs indefinitely without proof.

### What this policy rules out

- **Continuing Reddit paid ads at $50 CPC.** The target is $10. At five times the target with no path to improvement, this is not a channel to iterate — it's a channel to exit and reallocate.

- **Using SEM budget for Foundations keywords.** Current SEM keyword costs ($35+) are structurally misaligned with the Foundations ICP and the $10 CPC target. SEM stays focused on paid/enterprise use cases where the intent and deal size justify the cost.

- **Partnership models built around discounts.** A discount on a free product is not an incentive. The Crescenzo and Paycor partnership models need to change from offer-based to distribution-based — getting Broadcast in front of the right audience through co-created content or ecosystem placement, not coupon codes.

- **Content that only reaches category-aware buyers.** Blog posts and webinars positioned for internal comms directors at 2,000-person companies serve the paid pipeline, not Foundations subscriber growth. New content investment should skew toward the SMB, pre-evaluation buyer — lower competition keywords, practical how-to framing, smaller-team contexts.

- **Splitting budget across too many unproven channels simultaneously.** The instinct to test everything at once produces noise, not signal. One new paid channel at a time, run long enough to generate real data, then a clear go/no-go.

## What Success Looks Like at 12 Months

- At least one paid channel operating at or below $10 CPC with a clear scaling path
- Foundations sign-ups at 20+/week (5x current baseline)
- Organic SEO rebuilt with a Foundations-specific keyword layer that compounds independently of paid spend
- Community distribution generating measurable referral traffic from 2–3 practitioner communities
- Partnership channels converted from zero-producing to active distribution, or formally closed
