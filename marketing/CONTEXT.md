# Marketing Function Context

## Primary objective
Foundations subscriber growth. Converting free subscribers to paid (Foundations+ or Omni AI) is Product's responsibility. Marketing's job is to grow the number of active Foundations users.

## ICP for marketing
Broader than Cerkl's paid ICP. Because the goal is Foundations subscriber growth, SMB teams are in scope — not just mid-market/enterprise. The ICP is any internal comms team or people leader who would benefit from a better internal email workflow and is likely to find ongoing value in the free product.

Companies 0–500 employees are expected to stay on Foundations long-term. This is acceptable — subscriber growth is the goal, not conversion. Companies 501–2,000 are natural Foundations+ candidates; 2,001+ are natural Omni AI candidates. Conversion at those sizes is Product's responsibility.

## What good looks like
- Foundations subscriber growth is the primary KPI
- Distribution reaches internal comms teams, HR/People leaders, and SMB communicators who are underserved by or priced out of enterprise tools
- Channels and messaging attract users likely to engage with and stick with the free product
- Experiments with upsell opportunities (e.g., AI Agents) are measurable and do not compromise subscriber growth focus

## What to avoid
- Treating Foundations as a conversion funnel — that framing belongs to Product, not this strategy
- Positioning Foundations as an enterprise product; the free tier needs to be accessible and appealing to smaller teams
- Channels or messaging that only reach buyers who need paid features to see value
- Duplicating or conflicting with Cerkl's existing content-led demand gen, which is already optimized for paid pipeline
