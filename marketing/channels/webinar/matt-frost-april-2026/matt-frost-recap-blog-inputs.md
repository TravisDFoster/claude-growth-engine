## Named frameworks to anchor on

Use **deck-canonical wording** ([matt-frost-deck-extract.md](matt-frost-deck-extract.md)), not Matt's verbal versions.

### From Compliance to Connection (Slide 6)
- **Compliance** — "We said what we had to. Boxes ticked. Written by the cold hand of a lawyer."
- **Clarity** — "We took time to explain things in ways to help people understand what, when and why it matters."
- **Relevance** — "We thought about the messaging from 'their' perspective — and personalized content to reflect their life and known needs."
- **Connection** — "We took the opportunity to connect to hearts and minds. Transparency builds trust — and language shows we care."

### Fight for Attention — the four challenges (Slide 11)
- **Attention deficit** ("Everyone else is shouting too")
- **Relevance fatigue** ("This doesn't apply to me")
- **Procrastination barrier** ("I'll figure it out if I ever need it")
- **Earned scepticism** ("I don't trust what I'm being told")

### Fight for Attention — the four moves (Slide 12, deck order)
1. Make it **interesting**
2. Make it **meaningful**
3. Make it **emotive**
4. Make it **findable**

### The 3-layer comms architecture (Matt verbal at ~47:21; not on extracted slide)
- **Evergreen layer** — always-on, findable answers
- **Constant drum beat** — surface stories, repeated exposures (people need to see something 7 times)
- **High-impact campaigns** — the Nana-style guerrilla moments

### The VALUE framework (Slide 23)
- **V**isibility
- **A**ccessibility
- **L**iteracy *(use this word — not "Understandable" as Matt verbally said)*
- **U**sage
- **E**xperience

## Recommended outline

**Length:** 1,800–2,200 words (pillar-leaning supporting post)

### Introduction (~150 words)
The visible cost vs. the hidden cost. Open with the BLS figure ($28,250 per employee per year) and the question Tarek asked Matt mid-session: is there a hidden cost on top of that, in the comms gap between what's offered and what's used? Frame the post: the visible cost is in the budget; the hidden cost is everywhere else.

### H2 — What the hidden cost actually is
Three forms it takes: wasted spend (the 12,000-employee / 3-user travel loan example), eroded trust (the viral pension comms example), and vendor-controlled narrative (the 90% vendor point — *but only if we get a citation; otherwise drop this third form and keep the section to two*). Anchor on Matt's "vendors are louder than you, they're getting the credit" quote.

### H2 — Why it happens: the four-stage ladder
Walk through compliance → clarity → relevance → connection using the deck-canonical descriptions. Position most internal comms work as stuck at clarity, with the move to relevance and connection blocked by time and prioritization. Quote Tarek's question: "How did we get to a state where something so important is reduced to checking a box?"

### H2 — The four moves to fight for attention
Make it interesting / meaningful / emotive / findable. One paragraph each, with at least one concrete example per move (the Nana pension campaign, the cancer-story comms, the searchable benefits hub). Quote Matt's "no one wakes up wanting to read the benefits guide" line as the section opener.

### H2 — Control the message year-round
The 350-days-of-silence-then-cacophony problem. Introduce the 3-layer architecture (evergreen / drum beat / high-impact). The 84M / 74M point — half the people receiving benefits and making decisions are dependents you've never communicated with.

### H2 — How to know it's working: the VALUE framework
Visibility / Accessibility / Literacy / Usage / Experience. One sentence per dimension. Close with Matt's "if you can't measure it, you can't defend it" — frame measurement as a budget-defense tool, not a vanity exercise. Tie the section to Foundations analytics through a single light reference.

### Recording CTA
Sit it here, between the measurement section and the conclusion. Frame it as "Want to hear two practitioners debate the diagnosis and the fixes for an hour? Watch the conversation between Matt Frost and Tarek Kamil at `<RECORDING_URL>`." One line. No more.

### Conclusion (~150 words)
Close with the unspoken bonus line from Slide 25 ("Everything spent on benefits that isn't understood is a dollar that bought you nothing. The cheapest ROI improvement you can make is communicating what you already have."). Use it as a callback to the opening cost frame. Forward-moving line: the data is already on the budget; the work is making sure it lands.
