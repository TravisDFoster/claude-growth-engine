## Structure / Outline (60 Minutes)

*Spine: the 5 questions, each paired with a live Insights show-and-tell. Three things to land throughout (Rachel): (1) what Insights 3.0 solves = **ease of use** (navigate with no analytics experience, speed, clarity of data + timeframe); (2) **audience-specific reporting** (right data, right visualization, right audience — leadership summaries, communicator content analysis); (3) **shrink the feedback loop** from insight → action.*

### 0–5 Minutes: Opening hook (Rachel)
"Great communicators do more than send messages — they become the source of truth for engagement and effectiveness." Most teams send and hope; leadership expects more. Promise: the 5 questions every communicator should be able to answer, with a first look at the new Insights.
**Live Poll:** *Where are you coming to us from?*

### 5–13 Minutes: Q1 — Who received the message?
Reach/delivery confidence before measuring engagement. "I never received that email" → answer with certainty (delivered / opened / clicked / ignored). Audience visibility starts *before* the send.
**Show & Tell:** Blast Audience Targeting · Blast Audience Report · Individual Subscriber Performance.
**Live Poll:** *What's the first metric leadership asks you for after a major communication?*

### 13–25 Minutes: Q2 — Who is engaged and who isn't?
Averages hide stories (a 60% open rate hides frontline vs. managers vs. new hires vs. locations). Compare groups to find meaningful differences. Example: Albany office vs. company-wide.
**Show & Tell:** Segments · Blast Audience Reports · Content Audience Reports · Custom Dashboards.

### 25–35 Minutes: Q3 — When should I send for maximum impact?
Best send time = audience behavior, not opinion. Example: filter to Buffalo, read delivery/open/click patterns and volume over time; place messages when employees actually consume.
**Show & Tell:** Blast Overview · Heatmaps · Filtering.

### 35–47 Minutes: Q4 — What content actually resonates?
Categories as "employee votes" — subscriptions, preferences, clicks signal interest. Surface evergreen content and long-term value (new Insights credits delayed opens/clicks). Reshape the editorial calendar around what employees return to.
**Show & Tell:** Content Trends · Category Performance · Long-Term Content Performance · Top Content Reports · Category Interest Trends.

### 47–55 Minutes: Q5 — What should I improve next?
Data isn't the destination — better communication is. Surface underperforming categories, overcommunicated audiences, high-performing communicators, untapped opportunities; benchmark across communicators to spread best practices. Move from message sender → strategic communication advisor.
**Show & Tell:** Creator Analysis · Benchmarking · Optimization Opportunities · Trend Analysis · Recommendations.

### 55–60 Minutes: "Yes, YOU can do this" + Call to Action
Land the punchline: leadership-worthy reporting, no analytics skills, free in Foundations. CTA = Foundations sign-up / demo (+ giveaway, if used).
**Live Poll:** *If you could improve ONE thing about your communications tomorrow, what would it be?*
