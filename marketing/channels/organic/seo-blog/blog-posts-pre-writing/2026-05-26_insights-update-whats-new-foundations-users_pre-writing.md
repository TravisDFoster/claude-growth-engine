### Introduction (75–100 words)

- Open with the challenge most small and mid-size internal comms teams face: they are sending emails into a void — no real feedback, no data, no way to know if anyone read the thing
- Acknowledge that most teams are not flying blind by choice — they are using Outlook or Gmail because that is what they had access to
- Frame the post: Cercl Foundations just updated its Insights module, and this post covers what changed, how to use it, and why it matters for teams that have never had real email analytics before

---

### Section 1: Why Analytics Matter More Than Most Small Teams Think (150–175 words)

- Frame the tension: smaller teams often assume analytics are for enterprise. They are not.
- Leadership at organizations of any size wants to know whether internal communications are actually working — benefits enrollment, safety updates, policy changes, culture messages
- Without measurement, internal comms is an act of faith. With measurement, it becomes a discipline you can improve and defend.
- The specific questions analytics helps answer:
  - Did the all-staff email about open enrollment actually reach people?
  - Which department or location has the lowest engagement — and why?
  - Is our send frequency too high? Are people tuning out?
- Transition: these are not enterprise-only questions. They are questions every communicator faces, regardless of team size. That is why Foundations includes Insights — and why the recent update makes it more useful.

---

### Section 2: What the Insights Update Adds (200–225 words)

- Context: Foundations has always included email analytics through the Insights module. The update expands what you can see and act on.
- Cover the key additions/improvements — frame each as a practical capability, not a feature spec:
  - **Improved open and click visibility**: Cleaner per-blast reporting so communicators can assess individual send performance at a glance
  - **Audience reach tracking**: See how many unique employees were touched, not just how many emails were opened — important distinction for proving program reach
  - **Engagement trend data**: Performance across sends over time, so you can spot patterns — improving open rates, declining click rates, engagement drops after a certain frequency
  - **Individual-level engagement detail**: Know who opened and who clicked, not just aggregate rates — critical for compliance sends, required reading, or high-stakes announcements
- Write this section in plain language. Do not over-spec it. Focus on what each improvement lets a communicator do or know that they couldn't before.
- Note: position the update as meaningful but not overwhelming — Foundations Insights is scoped to what internal comms teams actually need, not a full enterprise analytics suite

---

### Section 3: How to Use Insights in Practice (175–200 words)

- Give readers a simple framework for turning data into action:
  - **After every send**: Check open rate and click rate. Note anything that underperforms against your baseline.
  - **Monthly**: Review send history trends. Are rates stable, improving, or declining? What changed?
  - **When engagement drops**: Look at subject line, send time, content relevance, and frequency — data surfaces the symptom; the communicator diagnoses the cause
  - **For leadership reporting**: Pull audience reach and trend data. Show improvement over time. Use real numbers, not anecdotes.
- Brief benchmark context: internal email open rates typically run higher than external marketing email — because employees expect and are more invested in internal messages. Rates of 40–60%+ are achievable for well-run programs (reference St. Elizabeth Healthcare's 54% open rate or Paycor's 34% click rate if they fit naturally).
- Reinforce that good analytics is not about having perfect numbers — it is about having a feedback loop that lets you improve

---

### Section 4: What Foundations Gives You That Outlook and Gmail Don't (100–125 words)

- Tight, direct comparison — no hedging:
  - Gmail and Outlook: sent, delivered, bounced. No open tracking at scale. No click attribution. No audience-level reporting. No history.
  - Foundations Insights: open rates, click rates, audience reach, individual engagement detail, historical trends — all in one place, built for internal comms
- This is not about switching email platforms. Foundations works with Outlook and Gmail — it sits on top of them and adds the measurement layer that email clients were never designed to provide.
- One sentence on competitors: tools like ContactMonkey track similar metrics but require a sales conversation and custom pricing. Foundations gives you analytics free, forever, with no contract.

## Notes for Writing

- Tone: direct and practical. The audience includes people who have never had purpose-built analytics before — meet them where they are, do not assume sophistication.
- Do not oversell the update. Frame it as a meaningful improvement to an already-included feature, not a revolutionary product launch.
- Avoid: "unlock," "game-changer," "transform," "revolutionize." Use plain language.
- The post should feel informative first, promotional second. Readers should come away with a clear understanding of what changed and why it matters for their program — whether or not they use Foundations.
- Where relevant, reference real customer outcomes (St. Elizabeth 54% open rate, Paycor 34% click rate) to ground the analytics discussion in real-world results — but only where they fit naturally, not forced.
- The implicit message throughout: if you do not have this data right now, you should. And Foundations is the lowest-friction way to get it.
