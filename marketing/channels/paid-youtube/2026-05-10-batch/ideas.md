# Hook Batch — 2026-05-10

> 20 hook ideas for the YouTube paid ad opener (5–10s clip). Selection happens in `winners.md`.

## Batch parameters

- **Generated by:** `paid-youtube-hook-batch` skill
- **ICP focus:** Foundations
- **Angle mix:** skewed pain — 15 pain, 5 positioning
- **Seeded from:** `proven-hooks.md` (5 pre-launch promising patterns from 2026-05-08 dry run) + `shared/icp.md` + `shared/broadcast.md`
- **Notes:** First real batch. Built against the refined "land on the lived experience, not the feature" criterion. Every pain hook describes a specific psychological moment an IC lives through. All visuals are Flow-friendly: one subject, one setting, one action.

---

## Hooks

### H01
- **VO / on-screen text:** "Hit send forty minutes ago. Your phone hasn't buzzed once. No replies. No questions. No way to know."
- **Visual concept:** Phone face-up on a wooden desk, screen dark, untouched.
- **Angle:** pain — the experience of post-send silence
- **Pattern note:** POV / negative space (the buzz that doesn't come)

### H02
- **VO / on-screen text:** "You've opened the same draft seven times this morning. You still haven't figured out how to say it."
- **Visual concept:** Cursor blinking in an empty email body, same untouched subject line at the top.
- **Angle:** pain — the experience of drafting paralysis on a high-stakes message
- **Pattern note:** POV / quiet observation

### H03
- **VO / on-screen text:** "You are the only person at this company who knows which email distribution list is current."
- **Visual concept:** A spreadsheet titled "Distribution Lists" with handwritten margin notes, viewed at someone's desk.
- **Angle:** pain — the experience of being the only one who holds critical institutional knowledge
- **Pattern note:** Direct address / specific responsibility

### H04
- **VO / on-screen text:** "Three people Slacked you to say 'hey did you see the all-staff email?' That means three hundred didn't."
- **Visual concept:** A single Slack notification on a laptop screen reading "hey did you see the all-staff?"
- **Angle:** pain — the experience of inferring reach from the wrong signal
- **Pattern note:** Stat-led inversion / single object

### H05
- **VO / on-screen text:** "Your follow-up email exists because your first one had a typo. Your third exists because your second had a wrong link."
- **Visual concept:** An inbox with three sequential emails: "Update", "Correction", "Apologies — corrected link".
- **Angle:** pain — the experience of compounding small errors with no undo
- **Pattern note:** Pattern-break / visual escalation

### H06
- **VO / on-screen text:** "You time send for 9:14 AM because by 9:15 everyone is in a meeting."
- **Visual concept:** A clock at 9:13 and a finger hovering over the send button on a laptop.
- **Angle:** pain — the experience of engineering around your colleagues' calendars
- **Pattern note:** POV / specific timing

### H07
- **VO / on-screen text:** "Half your warehouse staff speaks Spanish as their first language. You're writing the email in English. Again."
- **Visual concept:** A draft email in English with a yellow sticky note on the monitor that says "Spanish?"
- **Angle:** pain — the experience of knowing your message won't fully land and sending it anyway
- **Pattern note:** Direct address / single artifact

### H08
- **VO / on-screen text:** "Your CEO just asked 'how engaged are our employees with our internal communications?' You opened your mouth. You closed it."
- **Visual concept:** An IC manager in a one-on-one meeting room, mid-pause, eyes down.
- **Angle:** pain — the experience of being asked a question you should be able to answer and can't
- **Pattern note:** POV / micro-moment

### H09
- **VO / on-screen text:** "Your 'comms calendar' is a Google Doc with comments from four people you haven't talked to in two weeks."
- **Visual concept:** A Google Doc with a stack of sidebar comments, all dated weeks ago, different colored avatars.
- **Angle:** pain — the experience of the planning artifact that has slowly stopped being a plan
- **Pattern note:** POV / single screen

### H10
- **VO / on-screen text:** "Someone printed your email and taped it to the break room wall, because half your staff doesn't check email."
- **Visual concept:** A printed email taped above a coffee maker in a break room.
- **Angle:** pain — the experience of your "digital" comms requiring a physical workaround
- **Pattern note:** Visual-first / single subject

### H11
- **VO / on-screen text:** "You're tracking who has and hasn't acknowledged the new policy in a spreadsheet. Manually. Row by row."
- **Visual concept:** A spreadsheet with rows of employee names, some columns checked, most empty.
- **Angle:** pain — the experience of manual labor that should not be manual
- **Pattern note:** POV / mundane reality

### H12
- **VO / on-screen text:** "Legal sent edits. HR sent edits. Compliance sent edits. The email was supposed to go out Monday. It's Thursday."
- **Visual concept:** An email draft on screen with track-changes markings in three different colors.
- **Angle:** pain — the experience of approval-chain ping-pong on time-sensitive comms
- **Pattern note:** Stat-led time / single screen

### H13
- **VO / on-screen text:** "You've written this same kind of subject line forty times this year. You're running out of ways to make it sound new."
- **Visual concept:** A sent folder showing the same recipient and similar subjects: "Quick update", "Quick reminder", "Friendly reminder", "Just a quick note."
- **Angle:** pain — the experience of voice fatigue and self-plagiarism
- **Pattern note:** POV / visual repetition

### H14
- **VO / on-screen text:** "The CEO doesn't read company-wide emails. So you also Slack his EA. Every single time."
- **Visual concept:** A Slack DM open on a laptop with the message "Hey — sending an all-staff in 10, can you make sure he sees it?"
- **Angle:** pain — the experience of building a private second channel because the first one doesn't work
- **Pattern note:** POV / negative space

### H15
- **VO / on-screen text:** "Forty-two new hires this quarter. You don't actually know which of them are on the all-staff list yet."
- **Visual concept:** An HR list of new hire names on a screen — no status indicator next to any of them.
- **Angle:** pain — the experience of onboarding gaps you only discover by accident
- **Pattern note:** Stat-led / single artifact

### H16
- **VO / on-screen text:** "Internal email tool. Free forever. We mean forever. Not 'free until you need a real feature.'"
- **Visual concept:** Black screen, white text. "We mean forever."
- **Angle:** positioning — counter to SaaS trial-trap expectations (speaks to the experience of being burned)
- **Pattern note:** Title card / negative space

### H17
- **VO / on-screen text:** "Outlook wasn't built to email your employees. Mailchimp wasn't either. Something is."
- **Visual concept:** Three icons appearing in sequence on a black background: Outlook, Mailchimp, then a third silhouette.
- **Angle:** positioning — purpose-built for internal comms
- **Pattern note:** Direct address / minimal

### H18
- **VO / on-screen text:** "Compare internal email tools. Skip the demo. Skip the contract. Skip the procurement review. It's already free."
- **Visual concept:** Black screen with three lines appearing one at a time, each crossed out: "Demo", "Contract", "Procurement". Then a final line: "$0".
- **Angle:** positioning — self-serve vs. sales-led tools (speaks to the experience of dreading procurement)
- **Pattern note:** Title card / negative space

### H19
- **VO / on-screen text:** "If you've ever called your internal email setup a 'system,' you don't have one. You have a workaround."
- **Visual concept:** An IC manager at her desk side-eyeing her own monitor.
- **Angle:** positioning — frames the current Outlook/Excel/sticky-note routine as a workaround, not a system
- **Pattern note:** POV / pattern-break framing

### H20
- **VO / on-screen text:** "There is now a free, purpose-built tool for sending company-wide email. There wasn't one when you started this job."
- **Visual concept:** A single desk with a closed laptop on it. Bright morning window light behind it.
- **Angle:** positioning — frames the moment as "the thing you needed years ago, now exists"
- **Pattern note:** Direct address / minimal
