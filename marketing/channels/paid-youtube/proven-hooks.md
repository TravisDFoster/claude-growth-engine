## Pattern observations

- **Land on the lived experience, not the feature.** Hooks that evoke a specific psychological moment (post-send uncertainty, hours of effort buried in an inbox, being the only person who knows the comms calendar) read as authentic. Hooks that name a product capability (read receipts, audience segmentation, sending speed) read as ad copy and lose the first 2 seconds. Travis-validated 2026-05-10 against the inaugural dry-run batch.
- **Specific moments beat general pains.** "You sent the email three days ago, no idea if anyone read it" > "you can't track open rates". The IC says "that's me" to the moment, not the metric.
- **Simple visuals win.** Single subject, single setting, single action. Flow renders these cleanly; complex visuals (conveyor belts, walls of objects, multi-screen montages) don't survive the generation step.
- **Title-card minimalism reads as confident.** Black screen + one line of text breaks ad expectations and forces the viewer to read.

## Pre-launch promising patterns (dry-run validated 2026-05-10)

Hooks from the first generated batch that Travis flagged as worth pursuing. **Not yet shipped — no performance data.** Listed here so future batches can bias toward these patterns until real performance data exists.

### dry-run-01 — post-send uncertainty
- **VO:** "You sent the all-staff email three days ago. You still have no idea if anyone read it."
- **Visual:** Manager refreshing her sent folder over and over at her desk.
- **Angle:** pain — the experience of post-send signal-blindness
- **Why it landed (per Travis):** describes the exact psychological moment, not the missing feature

### dry-run-02 — bewildered cross-client rendering
- **VO:** "Why does the same email look fine on your screen and broken on every other Outlook in the building?"
- **Visual:** Split-screen — same email, one renders clean, the other collapses into a wall of text.
- **Angle:** pain — the experience of "wait, it looked fine when I sent it"

### dry-run-03 — effort vs. signal
- **VO:** "It took you four hours to write the leadership update. It's already eight emails deep in their inbox."
- **Visual:** Time-lapse of an email scrolling down a mailbox until it disappears.
- **Angle:** pain — the experience of high effort, low reach

### dry-run-04 — blocked by IT, routing around it
- **VO:** "It's been eight weeks since you asked IT for an updated employee list. You're still pulling it from the intranet org chart."
- **Visual:** A dusty intranet org chart titled "Updated Q3 2024" being viewed in 2026.
- **Angle:** pain — the experience of being blocked, then permanently working around it

### dry-run-05 — title-card minimalism, positioning
- **VO:** "Stop using Outlook for internal comms. Start using something built for it. It's free. There's no version two."
- **Visual:** Title card. Black screen. White text. "It's free. There's no version two." Hold for two beats.
- **Angle:** positioning — speaks to the experience of being burned by SaaS upsell traps
- **Why it landed:** the negative-space format ("there's no version two") flips the usual ad pattern
