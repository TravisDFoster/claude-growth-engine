# Insights Update: What's New for Foundations Users

If you have been sending internal emails from Outlook or Gmail, you already know the problem: the message goes out, and then nothing. No data on who opened it. No way to tell whether the all-staff benefits reminder landed. No feedback loop of any kind. Most small and mid-size teams are not flying blind by choice. They are using the tools they had access to, and those tools were never built to measure internal communication. The Cerkl Foundations Insights module just received a meaningful update, and this post covers what changed, how to use it, and why it matters if your team has never had real internal email analytics before.

## Why Internal Email Analytics Matter More Than Most Small Teams Think

A common assumption is that analytics are an enterprise concern, that small and mid-size organizations running internal comms through a shared inbox or a distribution list do not need measurement dashboards. This is backwards. Leadership at organizations of any size wants to know whether communications are working. Benefits enrollment, safety updates, policy changes, culture messages: every one of these requires reach and comprehension to do its job, and "I sent it" is not proof that it worked.

Without internal communications reporting, you are making decisions on gut feel. Did the open enrollment email reach everyone? Which department has the lowest engagement? Is the weekly all-staff starting to wear out its welcome? These are practical questions with real consequences, and email clients do not answer them. Gmail tells you a message was delivered. Outlook tells you whether your mail server bounced it back. Neither gives you open rates, click rates, audience-level engagement trends, or anything you could put in front of a leadership team.

This is not a scale issue. It is a tool issue. That is why Foundations includes an Insights module by default, and why the recent update makes it more useful for teams new to this kind of data.

## What the Foundations Insights Update Adds

Foundations has always included email analytics as part of the core plan. The update expands what you can see and what you can act on. The changes are worth understanding at a practical level, not as a feature list.

**Per-blast reporting got cleaner.** You can assess individual send performance at a glance: open rate, click rate, and delivery metrics for each blast, without hunting through a dashboard designed for something else. If one send underperformed, you can identify it quickly and compare it against recent history.

**Audience reach is now reported separately from open count.** This distinction matters more than it might seem. Knowing that an email got 200 opens is one data point. Knowing that it reached 180 unique employees out of a 300-person audience tells you something different and more useful. For compliance sends, required-reading announcements, and high-stakes HR messages, audience reach is often the number leadership wants to see.

**Engagement trend data is now part of the module.** You can track performance across sends over time, which is where patterns become visible. If open rates have been stable for three months and then drop after you increased send frequency, the data surfaces that shift. You make the diagnosis. The module makes the signal visible instead of hiding it in a spreadsheet you have to build yourself.

**Individual-level engagement detail is now available.** You can see who opened and who clicked, not just aggregate rates. For compliance-driven communications, required training announcements, or any send where individual acknowledgment matters, this is a meaningful capability. It also helps when a manager asks whether their team saw a specific message.

The Foundations Insights module is not a full enterprise analytics suite, and it is not trying to be one. It is scoped to what internal comms teams at small and mid-size organizations need: clear, accessible data about how their email program is performing, without configuration overhead or a procurement process.

## How to Use Insights in Practice

Having the data is not the same as using it. Here is a simple framework that works for teams at any experience level with internal communications measurement.

After every send, check open rate and click rate and note anything that falls below your recent average. You are not looking for a single explanation after every email. You are building a baseline. Once you have sent six to ten blasts, you will have enough history to know what normal looks like for your audience and your organization.

On a monthly cadence, review your send history as a whole. Are engagement rates stable, trending up, or declining? Has your audience reach held steady? What changed in the sends where performance differed? The answers will not always be obvious, but asking the questions consistently is how measurement becomes a discipline rather than an afterthought.

When engagement drops, look at subject line, send time, content relevance, and frequency together. The data tells you there is a problem. The communicator diagnoses why. A drop in click rates after you added a new content section tells a different story than a drop in open rates across all messages. Insights gives you the symptom. Your knowledge of the audience helps you find the cause.

For leadership reporting, pull audience reach and trend data rather than leaning on individual send snapshots. Real numbers over time are more defensible than anecdotes, and they show improvement in a way that a single good send cannot. Internal email open rates for well-run programs regularly run 40 to 60 percent or higher. St. Elizabeth Healthcare, a Cerkl customer, achieved a 54 percent employee open rate. Paycor reached a 34 percent click rate. These are attainable benchmarks for teams that measure consistently and improve deliberately.

The goal is not perfect numbers. It is a feedback loop that makes your program improvable.

Outlook and Gmail tell you three things: sent, delivered, bounced. There is no open tracking at scale, no click attribution, no reporting by department or location, no engagement history. If you CC'd the whole company on a policy update last year, there is no record of how many people read it.

Foundations Insights gives you open rates, click rates, audience reach, individual engagement detail, and historical trend data, all in one place, built for internal comms workflows instead of adapted from a marketing campaign tool. Foundations does not replace Outlook or Gmail. It sits on top of them. Employees still receive email in their inboxes. You get the measurement layer that email clients were never designed to provide.

ContactMonkey tracks similar metrics and is worth considering, but requires a sales conversation and custom pricing before you can get started. Foundations gives you internal email analytics free, forever, with no contract and no credit card required.

Internal comms teams at every size deserve the same thing: a clear picture of whether their work is reaching people and doing something useful. The Foundations Insights update gives current and new users better visibility into what is working, where engagement is dropping, and how to make the case for the program with real data. The analytics are free, included in the plan, and accessible the moment you send your first blast. If your current setup gives you nothing after you hit send, that is worth changing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is included in Cerkl Foundations Insights?

Foundations Insights includes per-blast open rates and click rates, audience reach tracking, individual-level engagement detail (who opened and who clicked), and historical engagement trend data across sends. All of these features are included in the free Foundations plan with no upgrade required.

### How is internal email analytics different from what Gmail and Outlook provide?

Gmail and Outlook report on delivery: whether an email was sent, received, or bounced. They do not track whether recipients opened the message, what they clicked, or how engagement compares across your audience over time. Purpose-built internal communications reporting tools like Cerkl Foundations Insights add the measurement layer that email clients were not designed to provide.

### What are typical employee email open rates for internal communications?

Internal email open rates tend to run higher than external marketing email because employees have a direct relationship with the sender and an expectation of relevant content. Well-run internal email programs regularly achieve open rates of 40 to 60 percent or higher. Cerkl customer St. Elizabeth Healthcare achieved a 54 percent open rate across employee communications.

### Do I need a paid plan to access Insights in Foundations?

No. Insights is included in the free Foundations plan. There is no credit card required, no trial period, and no contract. Open rates, click rates, audience reach, and individual engagement detail are all available from the moment you create your account.

### How often should I review my internal email analytics?

A practical cadence is to check open rates and click rates after every send, review engagement trends monthly, and pull audience reach data whenever you are preparing a report for leadership. Reviewing consistently over time is more valuable than deep-diving after a single send.

### How does Foundations Insights compare to ContactMonkey?

Both tools track internal email metrics including open rates, click rates, and individual engagement. ContactMonkey is a more established tool in the space but requires a sales conversation and custom pricing. Foundations Insights is free forever, self-serve, and requires no procurement process or contract commitment.
