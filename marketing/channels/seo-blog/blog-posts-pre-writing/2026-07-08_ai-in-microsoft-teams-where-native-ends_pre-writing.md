## Outline for Writing

**H1:** AI in Microsoft Teams: Where Native Ends for Internal Comms
*(Primary keyword "measure internal comms in microsoft teams" lands verbatim in the intro's first 100 words and in the H2 below.)*

**[Direct answer up top]** — First two paragraphs: native Teams (Copilot, channel posts, announcements) is built for real-time collaboration and can draft a message well, but it can't tell you who actually read an internal comms message, whether frontline staff ever saw it, or how the same message performed across Teams plus email plus intranet. That gap is where native ends.

**H2: The reach gap: employees who never see the channel**
- Frontline, deskless, and non-desk staff outside active Teams use; distributed-workforce sibling link lands here.

**H2: The question native Teams can't answer**
- "Who actually read this, and did the people who needed it see it?" Frame this as the line where a purpose-built layer begins.

**H2: What a purpose-built IC layer adds on top of Teams**
- Targeted/segmented delivery, cross-channel send, per-employee read measurement, one view across Teams + email + SharePoint. Hub link + SharePoint sibling land here. Cite Cerkl Broadcast Omni AI.
