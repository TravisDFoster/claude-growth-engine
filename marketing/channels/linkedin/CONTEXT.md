# LinkedIn Channel Context

## What this channel does
Organic posts on LinkedIn — the primary professional network for reaching internal comms and HR buyers. Posts wrap other Cerkl assets (blogs, press releases, webinars, case studies) per the weekly content plan. Paid ads run separately and are out of scope for this channel.

## How this channel runs
- Posting cadence is decided at the [weekly content session](../../content-plan/weekly-content-process.md). The wraps are a menu, not a quota — a typical week has up to 3 posts wrapping a blog (theme, link, poll; carousels can substitute) and 1 short video that's **planned outside the content-plan system** (out-of-band).
- Short video out-of-band rule: the weekly short video is owned end-to-end by Furqan — topic, wrap, and copy are not planned at the weekly session. The Jira CSV still has a barebones short-video Task for capacity tracking. See [`../../content-plan/jira-csv-guidelines.md` §Short video — out-of-band](../../content-plan/jira-csv-guidelines.md#short-video--out-of-band).
- Furqan owns publishing in LinkedIn. This channel produces the copy + handoff into the week's Jira CSV.

## Audience
- Primary: VP / Director of Internal Communications; HR Communications leads
- Secondary: People Ops, employee experience, IC consultants

## What we avoid
- Engagement-bait ("comment YES if you...", "tag someone who...")
- Excessive emoji or lowercase-thought-leader voice
- Long, rambling captions (lead with the hook, keep it brief, use line breaks for scannability)
- Hashtags (skip them entirely)
- Burying the link in the first comment (keep everything the reader needs, including the link, in the caption)
