## Caption

Think about your last all-staff send. Can you name a single employee who read it?

Most internal comms teams can report an open rate. Far fewer can answer the question leadership asks next: did the people who needed this message actually read it?

That gap is a maturity-stage problem, not a discipline problem. Open rates tell you an email rendered in a preview pane. They don't tell you whether the second-shift crew saw the safety bulletin or which department is quietly ignoring everything leadership sends.

The teams that move past it get to per-employee read attribution: not an average, but a named list. (Illustrative: "94% of affected employees read the policy change in 48 hours, and here's the 6% who didn't, by department.") That's the difference between reporting that comms happened and proving the right people received it.

So here's the self-diagnosis. When leadership comes to you after a big send, what do they ask for first?
