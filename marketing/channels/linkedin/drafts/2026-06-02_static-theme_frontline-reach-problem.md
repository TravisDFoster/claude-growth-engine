## Caption

A typical mid-market manufacturer has 5,000 employees.

70 to 90 percent of them work on the plant floor. They don't have corporate email. They don't log into SharePoint. They aren't on Teams.

So when the internal comms team reports a 60 percent open rate on the latest safety bulletin, what does that actually mean? If the office is 20 percent of headcount, the real reach against the total workforce is closer to 12 percent.

That's the frontline reach problem most internal comms teams haven't named, let alone solved.

Gallup's 2026 State of the Global Workplace report puts on-site, non-remote-capable engagement at 17 percent. That's 13 percentage points behind exclusively-remote workers, and the widest cohort split in the report.

A program that defaults to email-first measurement is reporting on the office and calling it the company.
