## Caption

You send the all-staff message about the reorg. It goes clean to 240 inboxes. Then your VP asks, "Did people read it?" and you don't know that the one director whose team is most affected never opened it.

That gap between "we sent it" and "we can prove who engaged" is a measurement problem, not an effort problem. Gmail and Outlook were built to move external email, not to tell you whether your own employees engaged with it.

There are five questions every internal communicator should be able to answer about any message they send:

1. Who received the message?
2. Who is engaged and who isn't?
3. When should I send for maximum impact?
4. What content actually resonates with employees?
5. What should I improve next?

Question 2 is the one that separates a real measurement tool from a vanity dashboard. A percentage tells you something is wrong. A name tells you what to do about it. When you can see that Dana in the Cleveland warehouse and eleven colleagues missed the benefits reminder three days before the deadline, that's a follow-up you can still make.

The blog walks through all five. Rachel Folz and Maddy Rieman go deeper on July 9 in our free webinar, "Stop Guessing About Your Analytics," with the new Insights experience shown live.

Here's the part most communicators don't believe yet: you can answer all five yourself, no analytics skills required. Leadership-ready reporting is built into Foundations, free.
