## Caption

Every internal comms tool now says it does measurement. Watch the demos and they all land on the same screen: a dashboard of percentages. Open rate. Click rate. A number that went up or down since last quarter.

A percentage tells you something happened to a crowd. It cannot tell you it reached the person who needed it.

Here is the screen that changes the conversation. In a demo of Cerkl's new Insights last week, our team pulled up a single link in a single send and saw, by name, exactly who clicked it. Not 14%. Three people: two we expected, one we didn't. Then the inverse. The named list of who got the message and never opened it.

That is the unit of measurement IC has been missing. Not "the update performed well." "These eleven managers on the night shift never saw it, and here they are."

The same view runs on a pulse survey or an acknowledgment. You can filter to the people who said yes, and to the people who said nothing, then send only to the second group. As one of our product leads put it on the call: you didn't take action here, let's make sure you do.

Aggregate rates are for the board deck. Named attribution is for the work. It is the difference between reporting that a campaign happened and proving it landed with the people it was written for.

If "stop guessing about your analytics" sounds like your year, we're spending an hour on exactly this on July 9. Details in comments.
