## Outline for Writing

### H1: Mental Health Awareness Month: Communicating Wellness Programs with Segmentation

Opening (1–2 paragraphs): Mental Health Awareness Month is when most wellness comms get sent. The harder question than what to send is *who actually sees it and acts*. Segmentation is the practical mechanism. Measurement closes the loop.

### H2: The Problem: Wellness Emails Get Lost Because They're Written for Everyone

- All-staff wellness emails train people to skip
- Different cohorts have different mental health needs (new hires, remote, frontline, managers)
- Low utilization is often a comms problem, not a program problem

### H2: How Segmentation Helps the Right Employees See Wellness Benefits

- Define internal comms segmentation in one sentence
- Concrete segments and what each needs:
  - New hires (0–90 days): EAP basics, confidentiality
  - Remote/hybrid: virtual therapy, mental health apps, teletherapy coverage
  - Frontline/deskless: mobile-first, short-form
  - Managers: how to support their teams, conversation guides
- Build one core message, vary it — not five campaigns from scratch
- Dynamic segmentation from HRIS data keeps lists current without manual upkeep

### H2: Measuring Whether Your Wellness Comms Are Landing

- Four metrics: open rate, click rate, reach by segment, retargeting
- EAP scheduling click-through as utilization-intent proxy
- "We sent it" vs. "we reached 84% of employees across segments" — the second is a proof-of-impact story

### H2: A Simple Mental Health Awareness Month Comms Plan

- Practical six-step starting point
- Same logic applies to open enrollment, benefits reminders, every wellness send

### Closing

Forward-moving takeaway: send the right message to the right people, measure what happened. No CTA copy (Wix renders the newsletter footer).
