## Outline for Writing

### H1: Employee Wellness Comms: How Segmentation Helps You Reach the Right People

Opening (1–2 paragraphs): Concrete cold open — the all-staff wellness email lands, a few hundred opens, no enrollments, EAP utilization comes back at 4%. The problem is not the writing. The delivery model was never built for a workforce this varied.

### H2: Why Broad Wellness Emails Fall Short

- All-staff treats every employee as interchangeable
- Wellness programs are wildly varied (EAP, parental leave, FSA/HSA, fertility, chronic condition, mental health) — packing them together guarantees low relevance for most recipients
- Downstream credibility erosion when employees keep receiving benefits they cannot use

### H2: The Case for Segmentation in Wellness Outreach

- Define wellness email segmentation in plain terms (not 12 separate emails; using employee data to filter who gets what)
- Three dimensions do most of the work:
  - **Role and job type** — frontline vs. office, EAP access differences
  - **Location** — state-specific plans, regional carriers, clinic networks
  - **Eligibility** — full-time vs. part-time, HDHP enrollment for HSA, tenure thresholds
- Downstream effects: open rates rise, program enrollment increases, HR credibility builds

### H2: How to Build Wellness Segments Without Building a Manual Process

- Address the bandwidth objection head-on
- Dynamic segmentation driven by HRIS data eliminates manual overhead
- Concrete segment logic examples:
  - Full-time, 90+ days, not enrolled in EAP → EAP reminders
  - Employees in states with specific parental leave laws → targeted parental benefits
  - Frontline → mobile-optimized mental health pushes
  - HDHP enrollees → HSA contribution deadline reminders
- Tooling sentence: connect HRIS data to the comms platform. (Rewrite the original Cerkl-product sentence to peer-expert / generic framing — *"modern internal comms tools"* or *"tools like Cerkl Broadcast"* in a list, not as the primary recommendation.)

### H2: What Targeted Wellness Delivery Does for Engagement

- Open rate **by segment**, not just overall
- Click-through on program links as intent signal
- Unsubscribe spikes signal relevance problems, not message-quality problems
- The leadership story: 4% utilization isn't necessarily program failure — could be a reach failure
