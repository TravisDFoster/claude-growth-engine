## Outline for Writing

### H1: Communicating Inclusively Across Diverse Workforces

Opening (1–2 paragraphs): Teams say they want to communicate inclusively, then send the same email through the same channel to everyone. AAPI Heritage Month is a useful test case. The harder question is what the current infrastructure allows, and who it leaves out by default.

### H2: What Inclusive Internal Communications Actually Means

- The conversation gets stuck on language; the real bottleneck is structural
- Single-channel, single-format, single-cadence architecture excludes by design
- Language access, channel access, and cultural relevance are three distinct dimensions
- Citation: ~80% of global workforce is deskless (Emergence Capital / Workleap, widely cited)
- Timing and format are themselves inclusion decisions

### H2: How Segmentation Makes Inclusive Comms Possible

- Define why segmentation is the technical foundation
- Concrete moves segmentation unlocks:
  - Language-appropriate routing by region, primary language, role
  - Mobile-friendly or SMS-accessible content for deskless workers
  - Heritage month acknowledgments targeted to opted-in employees
  - Cadence and format adjusted by function
- Dynamic segments synced from HRIS update automatically — no manual list management
- Employee data accuracy is the prerequisite, not an IT nice-to-have

### H2: The AAPI Heritage Month Example: What Doing It Right Looks Like

- Most teams: graphic + all-staff email, no follow-up, no measurement
- Inclusive teams: co-create with AAPI ERG or council, target opted-in employees, use the month as a listening moment
- Generalizes to any heritage month, ERG initiative, or cultural moment

### H2: Measuring Whether Inclusive Comms Is Working

- Reach by segment
- Engagement rate by demographic or role group
- Opt-in / opt-out patterns
- Survey or pulse feedback on belonging + communication effectiveness
- Inclusive comms as both an effectiveness argument and an equity argument

### Closing: Build the Infrastructure First

Forward-moving takeaway. No CTA copy.
