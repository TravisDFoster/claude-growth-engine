## Search intent

Practitioner intent. The reader is an IC or HR comms owner whose all-staff email reaches the desk-based majority but misses a meaningful slice of the workforce (frontline, field, deskless, shift). They want a framework for matching channels to populations, not a feature list. Direct answer needed early: you reach 100% by mapping populations to channels, not by adding channels for their own sake.

## Angle / point of view

The strong opinion: "100% reach" is not a channel-count problem, it is a population-mapping problem. Most teams add channels reactively and still miss the same people, because they never inventoried who they were missing. The fix is to start from the population, work backward to the channel, and assign every employee a guaranteed-receipt channel. Honest take: more channels also means more maintenance and more fragmentation, so a channel mix needs a governance rule (one source of truth, published once, distributed everywhere) or it decays.

## Outline for Writing

- **H1:** Reaching Every Employee: A Practical Channel Mix
- Opening (2 paras): Most all-staff email reaches the people who already had the message. The hard 15–40% is deskless/frontline/field. Direct answer: reaching every employee is a population-mapping exercise, not a channel-count exercise. Name the framework: inventory populations, match each to a guaranteed channel, govern the mix so it does not fragment.

- **H2: Start with the population, not the channel**
  - Why teams add channels reactively and still miss people. The "who is missing" audit. List the population types (desk-based, deskless/frontline, field/remote, shift, no-corporate-email).

- **H2: What each channel is actually good at**
  - Honest strengths/limits of email, mobile app/SMS, Teams/Slack, intranet/SharePoint, digital signage, manager cascade. Channels are not interchangeable; each has a population it serves and a job it does well (push vs pull, urgent vs reference, individual vs ambient).
  - H3: Push channels vs. pull channels (the reach distinction that matters most)

- **H2: Building the channel mix: match populations to guaranteed-receipt channels**
  - The core method. Every employee should have at least one channel where receipt is near-certain. Deskless workers usually need a non-email primary (mobile, signage, manager cascade). Worked anonymized scenario (e.g., a 5,000-employee manufacturer: HQ on email, plant floor on signage + shift huddle + mobile).

- **H2: Govern the mix so it does not fragment**
  - The trade-off: more channels = more maintenance and version drift. Rule: one source of truth, author once, distribute everywhere. Where tools like Cerkl Broadcast (single, light list mention) and native channel tools fit. Measurement by population, not org-wide average.

- **H2: A simple test for whether your mix actually reaches everyone**
  - Forward-moving close: pick your most operationally critical message (safety, pay, closure) and trace whether each population has a channel that guarantees receipt. The gap is your roadmap.
