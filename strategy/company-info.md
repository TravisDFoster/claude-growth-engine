## Most Important First

### ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

There are two distinct ICPs depending on the motion.

**Foundations / subscriber growth ICP:** Any team that owns internal employee communication and needs something better than Outlook or Gmail. Defined by need state, not headcount — includes SMBs and organizations of all sizes. The buyer is whoever owns employee communication at that org, from a formal internal comms director to an office manager or founder.

**Paid ICP (Foundations+, Omni AI):** Mid-market to enterprise organizations with messy internal comms environments — distributed, frontline, deskless, or global workforces; multiple channels to coordinate; need for segmentation, analytics, and IT-friendly data sync. Paid customers range from 2,000–12,200+ employees.

### Expected Upgrade Path by Company Size

| Company Size | Expected Behavior |
|---|---|
| 0–500 employees | Stay on Foundations. Low send volume, simple needs. This is fine — subscriber growth is the goal. |
| 501–2,000 employees | Likely to upgrade to Foundations+ to unlock unlimited send volume. |
| 2,001–5,000 employees (complex comms stack) | Likely to upgrade to Omni AI. Trigger: multiple channels (email, internal content, Teams/Slack), diverse audiences (location, function, deskless/frontline). |
| 5,000+ employees | Likely to upgrade to Omni AI regardless of stack complexity. |

Most visitors are expected to create a free account due to low friction. Conversion to paid is Product's responsibility, not Marketing's.

### Differentiation

Cerkl's clearest differentiation is that it sits on top of the channels companies already use instead of forcing a channel replacement.

**Core product story:** Sync employee data from HRIS/IdP → build dynamic segments → publish once across email/Teams/Slack/SharePoint/mobile → personalize with MyNews/Omni AI → measure engagement in one place.

- Low IT lift and quick rollout
- Keeps email native to Outlook/Gmail
- Client-driven roadmap

### Current Channel State

Cerkl has a mature content-led demand engine:

- Active blog, newsletter, webinars and recaps, newsroom
- Gated asset library, customer case studies, documentation/help content
- YouTube videos and competitive comparison SEO pages
- Partnerships: Crescenzo Communications, Paycor Marketplace

Blasts can be built with drag-and-drop elements, pre-made templates, or HTML uploads. Foundations accounts have access to Blasts, Audience, and Insights.

Account note: subscription auto-renews monthly even at $0. Turning off auto-renew eventually freezes the account after a grace period — this is an account mechanic, not a trial expiration.

### What's Locked to Paid

**Foundations+** adds: unlimited sends, unlimited seats, calendar invites, API access, SSO included, 3 instances.

**Omni AI** adds: AI-powered personalization, SharePoint/Teams/Slack/Mobile/AI Newsletter channels, omni-channel analytics, content management system, enterprise implementation/support, 6 instances.

### The SMB Value Proposition

Foundations solves for SMB teams in a sequence:
1. **Stop using Outlook/Gmail as an internal comms hack**
2. **Start free with something purpose-built**
3. **Upgrade only when complexity actually shows up** (more scale, seats, domains, calendar invites, API, or omni-channel needs)

### Why Foundations vs. Alternatives

**vs. Gmail/Outlook:** Not built for internal comms. Neither provides audience segmentation, internal analytics, branded templates, or a communicator workflow. Foundations replaces mailbox hacks with a purpose-built internal email system.

**vs. free Mailchimp:** Mailchimp's free plan is built for external marketing — customer lists, campaign CRM logic, marketing automation. Foundations is built for employee communication: internal audience structure, communicator seats, read insights, and internal messaging workflows. Different product for a different job.

**vs. ContactMonkey:** A more direct internal-comms comparison, but ContactMonkey is sales-led with custom pricing. Foundations is self-serve and free forever — no procurement, no renewals, no budget defense, no contract pressure.

**Sharpest summary:** Foundations gives SMBs a real internal email system, not a workaround — free forever, purpose-built for employees, with a clear growth path.

## Company & Product Foundation

### What Cerkl Broadcast Does

Cerkl Broadcast is internal communications software. It helps companies create, target, deliver, and measure employee communications — starting with a better internal email workflow than Outlook/Gmail, then extending across other employee channels like Teams, Slack, SharePoint/intranet, web, and mobile.

### Key Features (Most Differentiated)

**Dynamic audience sync and segmentation**
Pulls employee data from Workday, SAP, PeopleSoft, ADP, Paycor, and Active Directory. Lets communicators build live segments without depending on IT for every send.

**AI personalization via MyNews / News Digest / Omni AI**
MyNews engine prioritizes and personalizes content by role, interests, behaviors, and channel preference.

**Publish once across existing channels**
Omni-channel layer distributes one message across email, Teams, Slack, intranet/SharePoint, and mobile — no duplicate posting.

**Unified analytics and campaign measurement**
Normalizes engagement across email, Teams, SharePoint, Slack, and mobile so comms teams can prove reach and effectiveness.

**Internal-email-native workflow**
Drag-and-drop email building, templates, retargeting, and calendar invites — built specifically for internal comms, not adapted from marketing tools.

### Integrations

| Category | Systems |
|---|---|
| People data / HRIS | Workday, SAP, PeopleSoft, ADP, Paycor, Active Directory |
| Employee channels | SharePoint, Microsoft Teams, Slack, Outlook, Gmail, mobile app, microsites/web |
| Identity / security | Azure AD, Google Workspace, Okta, PingIdentity, OneLogin, Workspace ONE, JumpCloud, Duo, CyberArk (via WorkOS-backed SSO) |
| APIs / content sync | REST API, RSS/Atom, SFTP, CSV/manual uploads, content endpoints |

## Competitive Landscape

### Main Competitors

**Enterprise internal comms:** Staffbase, Simpplr, LumApps, Firstup, Poppulo, Workvivo, Haiilo, AxiosHQ

**Email-first / SMB:** Workshop, ContactMonkey

### How Broadcast Differentiates

Cerkl's strongest positioning is not "we are the company intranet." It is: "We are the personalized delivery and measurement layer across the channels you already have."

- Less emphasis on replacing existing digital workplace tools
- More emphasis on audience sync, personalization, native inbox delivery, cross-channel measurement, low IT friction, and faster launch
- Free Foundations plan is a meaningful commercial differentiator vs. heavier enterprise suites

### What Customers Switch From

Cerkl more often replaces patched-together internal comms stacks than named enterprise suites:
- Outlook/Gmail newsletter workflows
- PDFs and spreadsheets/distribution lists
- Homegrown tools
- Disconnected email-plus-intranet processes

Recurring switch reasons: better design, better scheduling, dynamic targeting, personalization, cross-channel reach, measurable analytics. The dominant pattern is "escape the manual stack," not displacing an enterprise competitor.

### Where Competitors Win

Cerkl is more vulnerable when a buyer wants a broader intranet/home-base platform or stronger overall suite breadth. Staffbase is more often preferred when buyers prioritize roadmap and overall business need fit. Intranet-centric or digital-signage-heavy use cases can favor LumApps or Poppulo. Known product friction points include editor/design limitations, small editing windows.

## Goals & Metrics

### What Success Looks Like

**Marketing KPI: Foundations subscriber growth.**

Converting free subscribers to paid is Product's responsibility. Marketing's job is to grow the number of active Foundations users. Secondary experiments with upsell opportunities (e.g., AI Agents) do not take priority over subscriber growth.

**Customer-facing metrics Cerkl emphasizes:** open rates, click rates, audience reach, personalization, engagement, proof of ROI.

### Pricing Model

- Foundations is free forever — no credit card, no contract, no seat or message pricing
- Paid plans (Foundations+, Omni AI) are audience-based and typically annual
- Not priced per communicator seat or per message on paid tiers

## Strategic Read

Cerkl sells **relevance, reach, and measurement for internal comms** — not a broad employee social network or a general marketing platform. The positioning is sharp. The tradeoff is that larger "digital workplace suite" competitors win when buyers want a fuller home-base/intranet platform rather than a delivery-and-measurement layer.
